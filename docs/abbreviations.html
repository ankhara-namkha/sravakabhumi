Taisho Reference Format

References to the Chinese source text follow the standard Taisho Tripitaka citation format:

[T30.0424a07] = Taisho volume 30, page 0424, column a, line 07

Each page of the Taisho edition is divided into three columns: a (upper), b (middle), and c (lower). These references appear at section openings throughout the translation and in the Table of Contents, allowing readers to locate the corresponding Chinese passage.

Where a section continues from a preceding passage without a new Taisho marker, the preceding section's reference serves as the starting point.

Romanization and Diacritics

This translation uses Sanskrit as the canonical reference form for all technical terms, following the convention of the Nalanda Translation Committee. Pali forms (e.g., vipassana, samatha, jhana) are not used; their Sanskrit equivalents (vipashyana, shamatha, dhyana) appear throughout.

Diacritics are omitted in the running translation for readability. Terms that would appear in standard Indological romanization as śamatha, prajñā, or dhyāna are rendered as shamatha, prajna, and dhyana. Full diacritical forms appear in footnotes where precision requires them; a consolidated glossary with diacritical forms is available in the digital edition (github.com/ankhara-namkha/sravakabhumi). This follows the convention established by the Nalanda Translation Committee and the Shambhala/Nitartha translation projects, which prioritize accessibility for the practitioner audience without sacrificing scholarly identifiability.

Translation Conventions

  • Sanskrit terms are italicized on first use in each fascicle with an English gloss in parentheses: "shamatha (calm abiding)." Thereafter, the preferred form is used without parenthetical.
  • Chinese characters appear in footnotes for scholarly reference; a comprehensive glossary with Chinese characters is available in the digital edition. They do not appear in the running translation.
  • Footnotes contain translator's notes, cross-references, doctrinal context, and canonical parallels. They are never woven silently into the translation text. Everything that is Asanga's text appears in the main body; everything that is the translator's commentary appears in footnotes.
  • Question-and-answer structure ("What is X?" / "It is as follows:") preserves the format of the Chinese text, which renders Xuanzang's systematic translation of the original's definitional structure.
  • Verse passages (uddana summaries) are rendered as indented blockquotes.
  • Bold markers indicate structural elements: question-and-answer pairs, numbered list items, and defined terms within enumerations.
  • Section numbers follow the format [fascicle].[section] — for example, 30.10 means fascicle 30, section 10. Cross-references in footnotes use this format throughout.

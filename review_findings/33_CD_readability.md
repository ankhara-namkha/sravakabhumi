# Readability Review — Fascicle 33, Sections 33.6–33.9
Agent R | Lines 142–216

---

## Overall Assessment

The prose in these sections is markedly better than the register problems found in earlier fascicles. Much of it reads with genuine clarity and some passages have real life. However, several recurring structural problems undercut the quality: (1) a tendency to accumulate qualifications before the main verb, especially in multi-clause sentences describing meditative processes; (2) one instance of formula monotony in the classification taxonomy (33.7); and (3) a few sentences that will stop a practitioner cold — not because the content is difficult, but because the syntax is dense. The footnotes are consistently excellent and should stay as is.

## Issue Log

### SECTION 33.6

---

**[R-01] Priority: M | Category: Nested qualification (issue 3) / Translated-ese (issue 1)**
**Location:** Line 146, "attention of separation" paragraph

**Flagged passage:**
> "The attention that accompanies this newly arisen path is here called the **attention of separation** — because it first severs the desire-realm afflictions that should be severed first, and because it removes the coarseness and heaviness (*dausthulya*) belonging to those categories of affliction."

**Problem:** The double "because…because" construction is a direct calque of Chinese explanatory syntax (以…以…). It reads as assembled from parts rather than written in English. The phrase "the desire-realm afflictions that should be severed first" is also awkward — "severed first" trails oddly at the end.

**Suggested rewrite:**
> "The attention accompanying this newly arisen path is called the **attention of separation**. It is so named because it first cuts the desire-realm afflictions due for severance, and in doing so removes the coarseness and heaviness (*dausthulya*) that belonged to them."

---

**[R-02] Priority: M | Category: Translated-ese (issue 1) / >50 words (issue 5)**
**Location:** Line 148, "attention of joyful engagement" paragraph

**Flagged passage:**
> "From time to time one engages joyful attention with deep gladness; from time to time one engages attention of revulsion with deep weariness — in order to dispel torpor, drowsiness, excitement, and the like."

**Word count:** 42 words — just under the threshold, but the structure is the problem. "From time to time one engages joyful attention" uses "attention" in a compressed technical sense (it means a whole attentional mode, not just a glance) that will confuse a practitioner. "Engages attention of revulsion" similarly reads as constructed from parts.

**Suggested rewrite:**
> "At times, with deep gladness, the mind rests in joyful contemplation of what has been freed; at other times, with deep weariness, it turns in revulsion toward what remains — alternating between these two to dispel torpor, drowsiness, and excitement."

---

**[R-03] Priority: L | Category: Nested qualification (issue 3)**
**Location:** Line 152, "attention of investigation" paragraph — first body sentence

**Flagged passage:**
> "Because the latent tendencies (*anushaya*) have not yet been permanently severed, when one contemplates such a pure and delightful object, a mind of approach arises — following toward it, inclining toward it, drawing near."

**Problem:** The sentence front-loads a causal clause, then a temporal clause, then the main clause, then a triple appositive. This is four layers before resolution. A practitioner reading for comprehension will have to re-read.

**Suggested rewrite:**
> "When one contemplates such a pure and delightful object, a mind of approach arises — following toward it, inclining, drawing near. This reveals that the latent tendencies (*anushaya*) have not yet been permanently severed."

(Splitting into two sentences resolves the nesting and makes the logical structure visible.)

---

**[R-04] Priority: M | Category: >50 words (issue 5)**
**Location:** Line 152, second half of the "attention of investigation" paragraph

**Flagged passage:**
> "One cannot remain in equanimity, cannot feel revulsion, condemn, suppress, or oppose it."

**Word count:** Not a long sentence, but this follows directly on a long sentence (R-03 above). The real problem is "cannot remain in equanimity, cannot feel revulsion, condemn, suppress, or oppose it" — the list mixes infinitives ("feel revulsion, condemn, suppress") with a standalone phrase ("remain in equanimity") and ends with the dangling pronoun "it." What does "it" refer to? The object? The approach-mind? This requires a targeted fix.

**Suggested fix:**
> "One cannot remain in equanimity toward it, cannot feel revulsion, cannot condemn, suppress, or push it away."

(Repeating "cannot" thrice mirrors the parallelism and resolves the ambiguous "it.")

---

**[R-05] Priority: M | Category: >50 words (issue 5)**
**Location:** Line 156, "attention of completed application" paragraph

**Flagged passage:**
> "Through this, the mind becomes freed from all afflictions bound to the desire realm — though by the method of temporary suppression, not by permanently destroying their seeds. At this point, the preliminary path for the ground of the first dhyana has been completed: the attention that serves as the antidote to all desire-realm afflictions has arisen."

**Word count of first sentence:** 47 words — close to limit. But the structural problem is the parenthetical "though by the method of temporary suppression, not by permanently destroying their seeds" inserted via em-dash mid-sentence. This is the most important doctrinal qualification in the paragraph and it is buried. The footnote handles this well, but the body text should not hide it.

**Suggested rewrite:**
> "Through this, the mind is freed from all desire-realm afflictions — freed by suppression, not by permanently destroying their seeds. Nevertheless, the preliminary path for the ground of the first dhyana is now complete: the attention that serves as antidote to all desire-realm afflictions has arisen."

("Nevertheless" makes the transition logical: the path is complete even though suppression is not permanent eradication. The em-dash qualification is preserved but tightened.)

**[R-06] Priority: L | Category: Register drift (issue 4)**
**Location:** Line 160, "progressive joy" paragraph

**Flagged passage:**
> "During the attentions of separation and joyful engagement, this joy arises from time to time, faintly and thinly, bringing comfort to the body. During the attention of completed application, it expands and deepens, manifesting with greater intensity. During the attention to the fruit of completed application, the joy and bliss born of separation pervade every part of the body, filling it completely without any gap."

**Problem:** The three-part parallel ("During… During… During…") is well-constructed and the progression is clear. However, "faintly and thinly" in the first clause reads as a translation artifact — "thinly" is not natural English to describe arising joy. And "manifesting with greater intensity" in the second clause is register drift into clinical language. The third clause is beautiful and should be kept.

**Suggested rewrite:**
> "During the attentions of separation and joyful engagement, this joy arises only occasionally, faint and tentative, bringing a quiet comfort to the body. During the attention of completed application, it expands and deepens. Then, during the attention to the fruit of completed application, the joy and bliss born of separation pervade every part of the body, filling it completely without remainder."

("Tentative" is more natural than "thin." "Expands and deepens" stands alone — the original "manifesting with greater intensity" adds nothing. "Without remainder" is the classical formulation and is more alive than "without any gap.")

### SECTION 33.7

---

**[R-07] Priority: H | Category: Formula monotony (issue 2) / Register drift (issue 4)**
**Location:** Line 180, summary paragraph

**Flagged passage:**
> "The **attention to characteristics** correctly knows what should be abandoned and what should be attained, and generates aspiration for both. The **attention of conviction** correctly initiates application for both abandonment and attainment. The **attention of separation** relinquishes all upper-grade afflictions. The **attention of joyful engagement** relinquishes all middle-grade afflictions. The **attention of investigation** enables the mind to abide free from overestimation (*adhimana*) regarding what has been attained. The **attention of completed application** relinquishes all lower-grade afflictions. The **attention to the fruit of completed application** correctly receives the fruit of all these attentions' good cultivation."

**Problem:** Seven consecutive "The **attention of X** [verb phrase]" sentences. This is the worst formula monotony in these sections. Each sentence has the same grammatical skeleton: definite article + bold term + adverb or qualifier + verb phrase. This is pure catalog reading. For a practitioner trying to hold this framework in mind, the parallel structure actually works against comprehension — the entries blur together.

This is a structural rewrite situation. The content is a summary, so compression and variety are both possible.

**Suggested rewrite:**
> The seven attentions serve distinct functions. The **attention to characteristics** correctly discerns what is to be abandoned and what to be attained, generating aspiration toward both. The **attention of conviction** then initiates the actual practice of abandonment and attainment. From there, three attentions carry out progressive severance: the **attention of separation** relinquishes upper-grade afflictions, the **attention of joyful engagement** relinquishes middle-grade, and the **attention of completed application** relinquishes the lower-grade. Between these, the **attention of investigation** holds a diagnostic function: it prevents overestimation (*adhimana*) by testing whether what seems severed has truly been abandoned. Finally, the **attention to the fruit of completed application** receives what all this cultivation has won.

(Reorganizing into a narrative arc — orientation, initiation, severance triad with a diagnostic interruption, culmination — removes the monotony while preserving every item. A practitioner can now follow the logic rather than memorize a list.)

**[R-08] Priority: H | Category: Translated-ese (issue 1) / Nested qualification (issue 3) / >50 words (issue 5)**
**Location:** Line 182, four-categories paragraph

**Flagged passage:**
> "These seven attentions can also be classified into a broader framework of four categories. The attentions to characteristics and of conviction are collectively called the *conforming attention*, accompanied by the counteractive measure of disgust and destruction. The attentions of separation and of completed application are collectively called the *counteracting attention*, accompanied by the counteractive measure of severance. The attention of joyful engagement is called both the *counteracting attention* and the *purity-conforming attention*. The attention of investigation is called the *investigation-conforming attention*. These four types of attention should be understood as subsumed within the framework of six types of attention: the conforming attention, the counteracting attention, the purity-conforming attention, and the investigation-conforming attention."

**Problems:**
1. The phrase "accompanied by the counteractive measure of disgust and destruction" appended to the first classification is opaque. What is being "accompanied"? The attention, the practitioner, or the classification? "Disgust and destruction" sounds violent out of context.
2. "These four types of attention should be understood as subsumed within the framework of six types of attention: the conforming attention, the counteracting attention, the purity-conforming attention, and the investigation-conforming attention" — this sentence tells us there are six types but names only four, which produces confusion. The discrepancy between "six" and the four listed items is unexplained. (Is this a textual issue or does the Chinese text actually say six? If the text says six and the translation names four, either the English is missing two terms or it should be "four" not "six." A readability reviewer cannot verify against the source, but this will stop any careful reader.)
3. "Should be understood as subsumed within the framework of" is bureaucratic language, not living text.

**Suggested targeted fix for item 2/3** (pending S agent's verification of whether "six" is correct):
If six is the correct number: add the two missing category names.
If four is the correct number: change "six" to "four."
In either case, simplify the final sentence:
> "These four types of attention can be understood as falling within the broader framework of [X] types of attention recognized elsewhere in the text."

**Structural rewrite suggestion for the full paragraph:**
> The seven attentions can also be grouped into four functional categories. The attentions to characteristics and of conviction together constitute the *conforming attention* — the preparatory phase of turning away through disgust and disengagement. The attentions of separation and of completed application constitute the *counteracting attention*, which actively severs afflictions. The attention of joyful engagement occupies both the counteracting and the *purity-conforming* roles — it severs while also consolidating what has been purified. The attention of investigation stands alone as the *investigation-conforming attention*, verifying whether liberation is genuine.

(The phrase about "six types" is omitted here pending source-fidelity verification — it should not be fixed blindly.)

### SECTION 33.8

---

**[R-09] Priority: L | Category: Translated-ese (issue 1)**
**Location:** Line 196, examination/analysis definitions paragraph

**Flagged passage:**
> "Within the ground of concentration, examination is the initial, sudden engagement with an object — a coarse mental verbalization. Analysis is the subsequent, gradual traversal of the object — a subtle mental verbalization."

**Problem:** "Coarse mental verbalization" and "subtle mental verbalization" are technically accurate but do not penetrate for a practitioner. "Verbalization" is particularly unsatisfying — it sounds like talking to oneself, which is not what *vitarka* is. The classic Nanamoli translation "applied thought" / "sustained thought" is more alive. However, since this project uses "examination" and "analysis," the parenthetical could do more work.

**Suggested fix:**
> "Within the ground of concentration, examination is the mind's initial, sudden contact with an object — the coarse inner movement of thought turning toward it. Analysis is the subsequent, gradual traversal — the subtle, sustained movement of thought exploring what it has found."

("The coarse inner movement of thought turning toward it" is more phenomenologically precise and less scholastic than "coarse mental verbalization.")

---

**[R-10] Priority: M | Category: >50 words (issue 5) / Register drift (issue 4)**
**Location:** Line 196, second half of the definitions paragraph

**Flagged passage:**
> "One further knows that examination and analysis are mental dharmas by nature: they arise when the mind arises, are co-present with it, mutually associated, and turn upon the same object. They are internally generated yet classified among external bases. They belong to all three times — past, future, and present. Arising from causes and conditions, they increase or decrease, do not long abide, exist only briefly, and appear suddenly."

**Word count of the multi-clause first sentence:** 41 words. Not over 50, but the problem is accumulation: four consecutive short predications ("arise when the mind arises, are co-present with it, mutually associated, and turn upon the same object") followed by four more short predications in subsequent sentences. The effect is a scholastic checklist. A practitioner will read past this without it landing.

**Suggested rewrite:**
> "One further recognizes that examination and analysis are mental dharmas: they arise together with the mind, accompanying it, co-present with it, directed at the same object. Internally generated, they are nonetheless classified among external bases. They belong to all three times; they arise from conditions; they increase and decrease, never abide for long, and arise and pass in a flash."

(The key change is consolidating the co-presence list into a rhythm — "arising together with the mind, accompanying it, co-present, directed at the same object" — and ending the series with "arise and pass in a flash," which has more weight than "appear suddenly.")

**[R-11] Priority: M | Category: Nested qualification (issue 3)**
**Location:** Line 198, "agitate the mind" paragraph

**Flagged passage:**
> "When one seeks the higher ground, they accompany one as painful companions. Therefore they belong to the dark category, attending upon desires. The joy and bliss born of separation are only a small advantage; by their very nature, at all times and without respite, wherever examination and analysis are present the mind turns upon its object with agitation, unable to find stillness."

**Problem:** The final sentence has three stacked qualifications before the main clause: "by their very nature" + "at all times and without respite" + "wherever examination and analysis are present" before "the mind turns upon its object with agitation." The accumulation is deadening.

**Suggested rewrite:**
> "When one seeks the higher ground, they are painful companions. They belong to the dark category, attendant upon desire. However slight the joy and bliss of separation, examination and analysis are always present — and wherever they are present, the mind turns upon its object with agitation, unable to find stillness."

("However slight the joy and bliss of separation" — repositioning this phrase as a concession rather than a parenthetical clarifies that the concession is being granted *before* the main point lands.)

### SECTION 33.9

---

**[R-12] Priority: L | Category: Register drift (issue 4)**
**Location:** Lines 210–212, the entire section

**Flagged passage:**
> "The coarse aspects described above are found universally in all lower grounds, from the desire realm up through the sphere of nothingness. In brief, every lower ground exhibits two kinds of coarseness compared to the ground above it: **(1)** a preponderance of painful abiding — the lower ground is not as still as the upper — and **(2)** a shorter span of life — the lower ground's lifespan is progressively shorter than the upper ground's."
>
> "These two coarse aspects should be correctly investigated through the same six perspectives as before, adapted to each ground as appropriate. Whenever one wishes to abandon desire for a particular ground, one investigates the peaceful aspects of the next-higher ground accordingly, progressing step by step until one attains the attention to the fruit of completed application for that ground."

**Problem:** Section 33.9 is a short transitional summary and its content is inherently schematic, so some register drift is understandable. However, the phrase "These two coarse aspects should be correctly investigated through the same six perspectives as before" is passive-construction academic prose. "Should be correctly investigated" places the practitioner at a remove — it reads as instructions for someone else. A slight voice adjustment would bring this back.

**Suggested rewrite:**
> "These two coarse aspects are found universally in all lower grounds, from the desire realm through the sphere of nothingness. In brief, each lower ground is marked by two qualities compared to the ground above it: **(1)** a preponderance of painful abiding — it is less still than the higher ground — and **(2)** a shorter lifespan.
>
> One investigates these two coarse aspects through the same six perspectives described above, adapting them to whichever ground one wishes to abandon. By examining the peaceful qualities of the next higher ground in this way, one proceeds step by step until attaining the attention to the fruit of completed application for that ground."

(The shift from "should be correctly investigated" to "one investigates" moves from prescription to description — the practitioner is the subject, not the object of institutional instruction.)

## Sentence-by-Sentence Comparison

### Section 30.10 (Nine Stages)

**Opening list (0450c18):**
- CN: 謂有苾芻令心內住等住。安住近住。調順寂靜最極寂靜。專注一趣。及以等持。
- EN: "A bhikshu causes the mind to abide inwardly and to settle -- to re-establish and abide closely -- to become tamed, pacified, and fully pacified -- to achieve one-pointed attention, and finally equipoise."
- VERDICT: Accurate. All nine names present in summary list.

**Stage 1 - 內住 (0450c19-c21):**
- CN: 謂從外一切所緣境界。攝錄其心繫在於內令不散亂。此則最初繫縛其心。令住於內不外散亂。故名內住。
- EN: "One withdraws the mind from all external objects, binds it internally, and prevents it from scattering. This is the very first binding of the mind -- holding it inward, not allowing it to scatter outward."
- VERDICT: Accurate. "所緣境界" rendered as "objects" (acceptable shortening of "object-conditions"). Complete.

**Stage 2 - 等住 (0450c21-c24):**
- CN: 謂即最初所繫縛心。其性麁動未能令其等住遍住故。次即於此所緣境界。以相續方便澄淨方便。挫令微細遍攝令住。故名等住。
- EN: "The mind initially bound in this way is by nature coarse and agitated; one cannot yet make it settle evenly throughout. Therefore, within this same object-field, through sustained and purifying methods, one subdues and refines the mind, gathering it to abide."
- VERDICT: Accurate. 相續方便 = "sustained methods," 澄淨方便 = "purifying methods" -- good. 挫令微細 = "subdues and refines" -- good. 遍攝令住 = "gathering it to abide" -- slightly compresses 遍 (pervading/throughout) but the sense of even settling is captured earlier in "settle evenly throughout." Complete.

**Stage 3 - 安住 (0450c24-c26):**
- CN: 謂若此心雖復如是內住等住。然由失念於外散亂。復還攝錄安置內境。故名安住。
- EN: "Although the mind has been placed inward and settled, one may lose mindfulness and scatter toward external objects. One then re-collects the mind and re-establishes it upon the internal object."
- VERDICT: Accurate. Complete.

**Stage 4 - 近住 (0450c26-c28):**
- CN: 謂彼先應如是如是親近念住。由此念故數數作意內住其心。不令此心遠住於外。故名近住。
- EN: "One cultivates increasingly close familiarity with mindful placement. Through this mindfulness, one repeatedly directs attention inward, not allowing the mind to drift far toward external objects."
- FINDING (m-minor): 如是如是親近念住 literally = "in such and such ways, closely abide in mindfulness." The translation "cultivates increasingly close familiarity with mindful placement" is a reasonable paraphrase but slightly rearranges the emphasis. The Chinese emphasizes that the practitioner "should closely and intimately abide in mindfulness" (親近念住), and through this mindfulness repeatedly directs attention inward. The translation is semantically complete but the paraphrase is looser than other stages.
- VERDICT: Acceptable. No content loss.

**Stage 5 - 調順 (0450c29-0451a03):**
- CN: 謂種種相令心散亂。所謂色聲香味觸相。及貪瞋癡男女等相故。彼先應取彼諸相為過患想。由如是想增上力故。於彼諸相折挫其心不令流散。故名調順。
- EN: "Various signs cause the mind to scatter: signs of form, sound, smell, taste, and touch, as well as signs of desire, aversion, delusion, male and female, and so forth. One first takes up the perception of danger in these signs. Through the dominant force of this perception, one restrains the mind from flowing toward them."
- VERDICT: Accurate and complete. 過患想 = "perception of danger" -- good. 折挫其心 = "restrains the mind" -- good.

**Stage 6 - 寂靜 (0451a03-a07):**
- CN: 謂有種種欲恚害等諸惡尋思貪欲蓋等諸隨煩惱。令心擾動。故彼先應取彼諸法為過患想。由如是想增上力故。於諸尋思及隨煩惱。止息其心不令流散。故名寂靜。
- EN: "Various unwholesome thoughts -- of desire, ill-will, and harmfulness -- together with secondary afflictions such as the hindrance of sensual desire, disturb and agitate the mind. One first takes up the perception of danger in these. Through the dominant force of this perception, one stills the mind and prevents it from flowing toward them."
- FINDING (m-minor): 貪欲蓋等諸隨煩惱 = "the hindrance of sensual desire *and other* secondary afflictions." The EN says "secondary afflictions *such as* the hindrance of sensual desire" -- this reverses the grammatical relationship. The Chinese lists the hindrance of sensual desire (貪欲蓋) as one example followed by 等 (etc.) indicating other hindrances/secondary afflictions. The English makes it sound like sensual desire is a type of secondary affliction, when the Chinese structure is: "the sensual desire hindrance, *etc.*, [and other] secondary afflictions." The five hindrances (蓋) and secondary afflictions (隨煩惱) are technically distinct categories. However, this is a minor point; the overall sense is preserved.
- VERDICT: Minor structural inversion. No doctrinal error.

**Stage 7 - 最極寂靜 (0451a07-a10):**
- CN: 謂失念故即彼二種暫現行時。隨所生起諸惡尋思及隨煩惱能不忍受。尋即斷滅除遣變吐。是故名為最極寂靜。
- EN: "When, through loss of mindfulness, either unwholesome thoughts or secondary afflictions briefly arise, one does not tolerate them for even a moment but immediately cuts them off, eliminates them, expels them, and vomits them out."
- FINDING (m-minor): 即彼二種 = "those two kinds" -- referring back to the two categories from stage 6 (unwholesome thoughts and secondary afflictions). The EN "either unwholesome thoughts or secondary afflictions" captures this but loses the explicit back-reference "those two kinds" (彼二種). This is a minor stylistic point -- the content is clear.
- VERDICT: Complete. The fourfold action 斷滅除遣變吐 is fully rendered.

**Stage 8 - 專注一趣 (0451a10-a12):**
- CN: 謂有加行有功用。無缺無間三摩地相續而住。是故名為專注一趣。
- EN: "With effort and exertion, one's samadhi continues without gaps and without interruption."
- VERDICT: Accurate. 有加行有功用 = "with effort and exertion" -- good. 無缺無間 = "without gaps and without interruption" -- good. 三摩地相續而住 = "samadhi continues" -- good. Complete.

**Stage 9 - 等持 (0451a12-a16):**
- CN: 謂數修數習數多修習為因緣故。得無加行無功用任運轉道。由是因緣不由加行不由功用。心三摩地任運相續無散亂轉。故名等持。
- EN: "Through repeated cultivation -- practicing again and again, training extensively -- one attains an effortless, spontaneous flow. No longer requiring effort or exertion, the mind's samadhi continues of its own accord, unbroken and free from distraction."
- VERDICT: Accurate and complete. 數修數習數多修習 = "repeated cultivation -- practicing again and again, training extensively" -- captures the triple repetition well. 任運轉道 = "effortless, spontaneous flow" -- good. 任運相續無散亂轉 = "continues of its own accord, unbroken and free from distraction" -- accurate.

### Section 30.11 (Six Powers)

**Opening (0451a16-a18):**
- CN: 當知此中由六種力。方能成辦九種心住。一聽聞力。二思惟力。三憶念力。四正知力。五精進力。六串習力。
- EN: "One should know that six powers bring about the accomplishment of these nine stages: (1) the power of hearing, (2) the power of reflection, (3) the power of mindfulness, (4) the power of clear knowing, (5) the power of diligence, and (6) the power of habituation."
- VERDICT: Accurate and complete.

**Power-stage mapping (0451a18-a28):**
- CN: 初由聽聞思惟二力。數聞數思增上力故。最初令心於內境住。及即於此相續方便澄淨方便等遍安住。
- EN: "Initially, through the combined power of hearing and reflection -- by the dominant force of repeated hearing and repeated thinking -- one first causes the mind to abide on the internal object, and through sustained and purifying methods settles it evenly throughout."
- VERDICT: Accurate. Maps hearing+reflection to stages 1-2.

- CN: 如是於內繫縛心已。由憶念力數數作意。攝錄其心令不散亂安住近住。
- EN: "Having thus bound the mind inward, through the power of mindfulness one repeatedly directs attention, gathering the mind and preventing scattering -- this is re-established abiding and close abiding."
- VERDICT: Accurate. Maps mindfulness to stages 3-4.

- CN: 從此已後由正知力調息其心。於其諸相諸惡尋思諸隨煩惱不令流散調順寂靜。
- EN: "From this point forward, through the power of clear knowing, one tempers the mind, preventing it from flowing toward signs, unwholesome thoughts, and secondary afflictions -- taming and pacifying."
- FINDING (m-minor): 調息其心 = "regulates/tempers the mind." EN "tempers the mind" is good. But 調息 more precisely means "regulates" or "adjusts and calms." Minor nuance only.
- VERDICT: Accurate. Maps clear knowing to stages 5-6.

- CN: 由精進力設彼二種暫現行時能不忍受。尋即斷滅除遣變吐。最極寂靜專注一趣。
- EN: "Through the power of diligence, when either of those two briefly arises, one does not tolerate them but immediately cuts them off, eliminates, expels, and vomits them out -- fully pacifying the mind and achieving one-pointed attention."
- VERDICT: Accurate. Maps diligence to stages 7-8. 設...暫現行時 = "when...briefly arises" -- good.

- CN: 由串習力等持成滿。
- EN: "Through the power of habituation, equipoise is brought to completion."
- VERDICT: Accurate. Maps habituation to stage 9. 成滿 = "brought to completion" -- good.

### Section 30.12 (Four Types of Attention)

**Opening (0451a28-b01):**
- CN: 即於如是九種心住。當知復有四種作意。一力勵運轉作意。二有間缺運轉作意。三無間缺運轉作意。四無功用運轉作意。
- EN: "Within these same nine stages of mental abiding, there are also four types of attention: (1) forceful attention, (2) interrupted attention, (3) uninterrupted attention, and (4) effortless attention."
- FINDING (m-minor): The Chinese four-character names all end in 運轉作意 ("operating attention" or "attention in operation"). The EN drops 運轉 from the names, using simply "forceful attention" etc. The footnote provides the full Chinese forms. This is a reasonable compression for readability but the formal names are technically "forceful-operating attention," "interrupted-operating attention," etc. Not a mistranslation -- an acceptable editorial choice given the footnote.
- VERDICT: Acceptable compression with footnote backup.

**Stage mappings (0451b01-b05):**
- CN: 於內住等住中。有力勵運轉作意。
- EN: "Forceful attention accompanies the first two stages -- inward abiding and settled abiding."
- VERDICT: Accurate.

- CN: 於安住近住。調順寂靜。最極寂靜中。有有間缺運轉作意。
- EN: "Interrupted attention spans the middle five, from re-established abiding through fully pacifying."
- VERDICT: Accurate. The count "middle five" (stages 3-7) matches: 安住(3), 近住(4), 調順(5), 寂靜(6), 最極寂靜(7).

- CN: 於專注一趣中。有無間缺運轉作意。
- EN: "Uninterrupted attention characterizes one-pointed attention."
- VERDICT: Accurate.

- CN: 於等持中。有無功用運轉作意。
- EN: "Effortless attention is present during equipoise."
- VERDICT: Accurate.

**Shamatha/vipashyana closing (0451b05-b12):**
- CN: 當知如是四種作意。於九種心住中是奢摩他品。又即如是獲得內心奢摩他者。於毘鉢舍那勤修習時。復即由是四種作意。方能修習毘鉢舍那。故此亦是毘鉢舍那品。
- EN: "These four types of attention, as they operate within the nine stages, belong to the shamatha class. Furthermore, one who has attained inner shamatha and then diligently cultivates vipashyana employs these same four types of attention. Therefore they also belong to the vipashyana class."
- FINDING (m-minor): 方能修習毘鉢舍那 = "is then able to cultivate vipashyana." The EN says "employs these same four types of attention" which slightly paraphrases -- the Chinese says "through these four types of attention, one is *then able to cultivate* (方能修習) vipashyana." The emphasis is on the four attentions being the *means by which* vipashyana is cultivated, not merely that the practitioner "employs" them. Minor nuance.
- VERDICT: Sense preserved. Minor paraphrase.

## Findings

### S-1: "Excessively intense" may slightly over-translate 太急方便 -- Minor

- **Location:** Chinese line 17 (太急方便) / English line 132
- **Chinese:** 太急方便
- **English:** "excessively intense effort"
- **Issue:** 急 means "hurried, rushed, urgent" rather than "intense." The pair 太緩/太急 is literally "too slow / too rushed" rather than "too lax / too intense." The English "lax" for 緩 is acceptable, but "intense" for 急 shifts the meaning slightly from a temporal/urgency sense to a force/intensity sense. This is a minor nuance; both ultimately point to over-striving.
- **Recommendation:** Consider "excessively hasty effort" or "excessively forceful effort" for 太急方便. Alternatively, retain "intense" with a brief note that 急 carries the sense of rushing or pressing. Low priority.

### S-2: 思遽務 rendered as "hurried thoughts" obscures the term -- Minor

- **Location:** Chinese line 31 (若於所緣有思遽務有散亂者) / English line 256
- **Chinese:** 思遽務
- **English:** "hurried thoughts and distraction"
- **Issue:** The Chinese 思遽務 is a somewhat unusual compound. 思 = thought/cogitation, 遽 = hasty/agitated, 務 = occupation/busyness. The English "hurried thoughts" captures the sense adequately but loses the sense of "busyness/preoccupation" in 務. The translation "hurried thoughts and distraction" treats 思遽務 and 散亂 as two parallel items, which matches the Chinese structure (有思遽務有散亂者), so the structural handling is correct. The rendering of 思遽務 itself is a minor simplification.
- **Recommendation:** Consider "agitated mental preoccupation" or "hasty thought-proliferation" for 思遽務 to capture the 務 (busyness/preoccupation) component. Low priority.

### S-3: 於所決擇已得究竟 rendered somewhat loosely -- Minor

- **Location:** Chinese line 29 (於所決擇已得究竟) / English line 250
- **Chinese:** 於所決擇已得究竟
- **English:** "one has reached the ultimate in what was to be discerned"
- **Issue:** 決擇 (*pravicaya*) means "discernment" or "decisive analysis" -- it is a technical Abhidharma term referring to the analytical discrimination of dharmas. "What was to be discerned" is acceptable but slightly vague. The phrase means "one has reached the ultimate/final point with respect to discernment" -- i.e., there is no further analytical work to be done. The current rendering conveys this meaning adequately, though "discerned" is slightly less precise than "discriminated" or "analyzed."
- **Recommendation:** Consider "one has reached the ultimate in what was to be analytically discerned" or "one has attained finality in discernment." Low priority; current rendering is adequate.

### S-4: 由極於內住寂止故 -- "inward abiding in quiescence" could be more precise -- Moderate

- **Location:** Chinese line 27 (由極於內住寂止故) / English line 234
- **Chinese:** 由極於內住寂止故
- **English:** "due to excessive inward abiding in quiescence"
- **Issue:** 寂止 is literally "still quiescence" and refers to shamatha/calm-abiding. The English "quiescence" is adequate but does not make explicit the connection to shamatha. The parallel phrase for restlessness (由極於外住囂舉故, line 27) is rendered "due to excessive outward abiding in agitation" (line 236), where 囂舉 ("clamor and agitation") is rendered simply as "agitation." The contrast 寂止/囂舉 (still-quiescence / clamorous-agitation) is a specific paired opposition. Both renderings are acceptable but lose some specificity.
- **Recommendation:** Consider adding a parenthetical: "due to excessive inward abiding in quiescence (*shamatha*)" to make the technical reference explicit. For 囂舉, consider "clamorous agitation" or simply add a note. Moderate priority because the shamatha/vipashyana framework is central to this section.

### S-5: 示現教導讚勵慶喜 -- six verbs compressed to five in translation -- Moderate

- **Location:** Chinese line 27 (示現教導讚勵慶喜策發其心) / English line 234
- **Chinese:** 示現教導讚勵慶喜策發其心
- **English:** "showing, guiding, praising, encouraging, gladdening, and energizing the mind"
- **Issue:** The Chinese has a series of paired terms: 示現 (showing/demonstrating), 教導 (instructing/guiding), 讚勵 (praising and encouraging), 慶喜 (rejoicing/gladdening), 策發 (spurring/energizing). The English renders six verbs: "showing, guiding, praising, encouraging, gladdening, and energizing." This splits 讚勵 into two words ("praising, encouraging") and renders 慶喜 as "gladdening" and 策發 as "energizing." The count is six English verbs for five Chinese binomes, which is actually accurate since 讚勵 can be read as two separate actions (讚 = praise, 勵 = encourage). This is acceptable. However, the more natural reading of the Chinese is five paired compounds, and the translation happens to produce six English words. No actual content is lost.
- **Recommendation:** No change strictly needed. If aiming for structural fidelity, one could render: "demonstrating, instructing, praising-and-encouraging, gladdening, and spurring the mind" to match the five-compound structure. Low-to-moderate priority.

### S-6: 愚癡想思俱行種種動慢戲論造作貪愛 -- complex compound partially compressed -- Moderate

- **Location:** Chinese line 25 (生起如是愚癡。想思俱行種種動慢。戲論造作貪愛) / English line 226
- **Chinese:** 生起如是愚癡。想思俱行種種動慢。戲論造作貪愛
- **English:** "Such deluded thoughts arise -- various agitations of conceit accompanying perception and intention, proliferations, fabrications, and craving."
- **Issue:** The Chinese structure is: 愚癡 (delusion/ignorance) + 想思俱行 (accompanying perception and intention) + 種種動慢 (various agitations of conceit) + 戲論 (proliferations/prapanca) + 造作 (fabrications/activities) + 貪愛 (craving). The English reorders: "deluded thoughts" (for 愚癡), then "various agitations of conceit accompanying perception and intention" (merging 想思俱行 with 種種動慢), then "proliferations, fabrications, and craving." The issue is that 想思俱行 modifies the entire series (all of these deluded phenomena are "accompanied by perception and intention"), not just "agitations of conceit." The English placement makes it seem as though "accompanying perception and intention" modifies only "agitations of conceit."
- **Recommendation:** Restructure: "Such delusion arises -- accompanied by perception and intention, manifesting as various agitations of conceit, proliferations, fabrications, and craving." This preserves the Chinese structure where 想思俱行 governs the whole series. Moderate priority.

### S-7: Commentary tetrad headings are additions not in the Chinese -- Moderate

- **Location:** English lines 208, 220, 228, 240
- **Chinese:** (no equivalent)
- **English:** "Commentary on the first tetrad (body)," "Commentary on the second tetrad (feeling)," "Commentary on the third tetrad (mind)," "Commentary on the fourth tetrad (dharmas)"
- **Issue:** These section headings are editorial additions. The Chinese text does not divide the commentary into labeled tetrads. While the four-tetrad structure is implicit in the content and is recognized in both traditional and modern scholarship, and while adding navigational headings is standard translation practice, strictly speaking these are additions not in the source. The section heading "Types of Breath," "Causes, Supports, Movements, and Channels," "Four Names and Two Faults," "Five Cultivations of Mindfulness of Breathing," and "Closing: Application to Discursive-Thought Conduct" are similarly editorial additions providing structure not explicitly marked in the Chinese.
- **Recommendation:** This is acceptable editorial practice for readability and is consistent with the project's design invariants (structural fidelity + dual audience). However, per the design invariant "Annotations are separate... The reader must always know what is Asanga and what is the translator," these headings should ideally be visually distinguished from the source text (e.g., italicized, or marked with [Ed.]). Currently the bold formatting partially achieves this for the tetrad labels, but the sub-section headings (##### Types of Breath, etc.) could be mistaken for headings in the source text. Moderate priority.

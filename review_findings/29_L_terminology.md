## Flag 2: Terms Rendered Differently Than Glossary Specifies

### FLAG 2a — "stream-entry" vs. "stream-enterer" (MODERATE)

**Location:** Line 312 and throughout 29.12.

The translation uses:
- "fruit of stream-entry" — the phala compound; this is acceptable (stream-entry as the attainment name)
- "stream-entrant" (line 324, 336) — for the PERSON

**Glossary specifies:** 預流 / srota-apanna = **"stream-enterer"** (not "stream-entrant").

In line 336: "A stream-entrant has at most seven further existences remaining."

This is a rendering mismatch. The glossary form is "stream-enterer" (NTC convention), not "stream-entrant." While "stream-entrant" is a valid English word, consistency with the glossary requires "stream-enterer."

**Recommendation:** Change "stream-entrant" → "stream-enterer" wherever it appears in 29.12.

### FLAG 2b — "once-returning" / "non-returning" as adjectival forms (MINOR)

**Location:** Line 312, 338, 340, 344.

The translation uses:
- "fruit of once-returning" — gerund form of the attainment
- "fruit of non-returning" — gerund form

**Glossary specifies:**
- 一來 / sakridagamin = "once-returner" (person)
- 不還 / anagamin = "non-returner" (person)

The fruit compounds "fruit of once-returning" and "fruit of non-returning" use the gerund/verbal form rather than the noun form used in the glossary. Compare to how the glossary entry 沙門果 renders the full fruits list as "stream-entry through arhatship" which uses the attainment name.

This is a **minor** flag. "Fruit of once-returning" and "fruit of non-returning" are standard English renderings of phala compounds. However, for strict consistency: the parallel compound "fruit of stream-entry" uses the attainment-noun form (stream-entry), not a gerund. The series would be more consistent as:
- fruit of stream-entry (srota-apanna-phala) ✓ already
- fruit of once-return (*sakridagamin-phala*) — alternatively, "fruit of the once-returner"
- fruit of non-return (*anagamin-phala*) — alternatively, "fruit of the non-returner"

This is an editorial judgment call. The current rendering is defensible; flag for editorial attention.

### FLAG 2c — "arhatship" vs. "arhat" (MINOR)

**Location:** Lines 312, 340, 344.

The translation uses "supreme fruit of arhatship" and "fruit of arhatship." The glossary entry for 阿羅漢 gives the rendering as "arhat" (the person), not "arhatship" (the state). The compound "arhat-phala" = "fruit of arhatship" is the established English rendering of this compound across Theravada and Tibetan traditions, so this is acceptable. No change required — flagging for information only.

## Category 1: Terms Not in Glossary

### 1.1 `anapanasati` (section 32.12)

**Occurrence:** "mindfulness of breathing (*anapanasati*)" — section 32.12, footnote [^five_objects]

**Finding:** `anapanasati` is used as a Sanskrit-parenthetical gloss, but it is the **Pali** form. The Sanskrit is `anapanasmriti`. The glossary entry (p. 110/111) lists `anapanasmriti` as the Sanskrit and `mindfulness of breathing` as the English rendering.

This is a borderline case: `anapanasati` appears only inside a footnote (not running text), and the running text itself correctly uses "mindfulness of breathing." However, the parenthetical cites the Pali form in a scholarly note, creating inconsistency with the glossary. The established in-text form per the glossary is `anapanasmriti`.

**Severity:** Minor — footnote-only occurrence, but NTC convention mandates Sanskrit forms throughout.

### 1.4 `priti` (sections 32.3, 32.16)

**Occurrence:** Multiple — footnote [^non_regret_sequence] ("joy (*priti*)"), and footnote [^three_happinesses] ("blameless happiness with joy" with implicit *priti* reference). In section 32.16: "mind leaps with joy and delight, accompanied by rapture and happiness" — footnote language includes "rapture."

**Finding:** `priti` is **not in the glossary** as a standalone term. It appears in compound glossary entries (e.g., `喜覺支 | priti-bodhyanga | joy factor of awakening`), but there is no entry for `喜 | priti | joy/rapture` as a standalone term.

The running text in 32.16 translates the onset of pliancy as "joy and delight, accompanied by rapture and happiness" — mixing "joy," "delight," and "rapture" for what appears to be `priti` (喜). The footnote [^three_happinesses] describes "blameless happiness with joy" as form-realm happiness accompanied by *priti* but does not provide a glossary entry.

**Recommendation:** Add to glossary: `喜 | priti | joy, rapture | joy | Distinct from 歡悅 (pramodya / gladness); one of the dhyana factors`. Note the rendering distinction: `priti` as dhyana factor = "rapture" (standard NTC); `priti` as bodhyanga = "joy factor of awakening" (per glossary entry).

**Severity:** Moderate — `priti` is a core dhyana factor appearing at a key juncture (the pliancy passage in 32.16), yet it has no standalone glossary entry and the running text uses varied renderings ("joy," "rapture," "delight") without clarity about which Chinese term is being rendered.

### 1.7 `upanishad` fraction (section 32.12)

**Occurrence:** "nor any countable, calculable, or *upanishad* fraction" — section 32.12

**Finding:** `upanishad` (鄔波尼殺曇分) is used as a technical term for "the smallest conceivable portion." The footnote [^merit_comparison] explains this as `鄔波尼殺曇分`. This term is **not in the glossary**. Its usage here is technical/Abhidharma: it means an infinitesimally small fraction, not the Vedantic *Upanishad* texts.

**Note:** The rendering "upanishad fraction" in running text without a gloss is potentially confusing — readers may associate it with Vedanta rather than the Abhidharma superlative-fraction formula. The footnote provides explanation, but the running text form is unexplained.

**Recommendation:** Add a brief translator's note inline or expand the footnote. Consider rendering as "*upanishad* fraction [the smallest conceivable portion in Buddhist formulae]" on first occurrence. Add to glossary: `鄔波尼殺曇分 | upanishadika | upanishad fraction | upanishad fraction | Buddhist superlative formula for the smallest conceivable portion; not the Vedantic texts`.

**Severity:** Moderate — unflagged specialist term that could mislead a reader.

## Category 2: Terms Rendered Differently from Glossary

### 2.1 "rapture" for `priti` / 喜 (section 32.16)

**Occurrence:** Section 32.16: "accompanied by rapture and happiness"

**Glossary standard:** The glossary has `喜覺支 | priti-bodhyanga | joy factor of awakening`. The closest standalone rendering for `priti` in the dhyana context is "joy" (used in the footnote [^three_happinesses] and [^non_regret_sequence]). Running text in 32.16 uses "rapture" — a variant rendering.

**Assessment:** "Rapture" is not wrong (it is a well-attested NTC rendering for `priti` as a dhyana factor, used prominently in Nanamoli's Visuddhimagga). However, the glossary has not registered "rapture" as a valid rendering, and the fascicle uses both "joy" and "rapture" for what appears to be the same term in related contexts. This inconsistency should be resolved by adding a glossary note.

**Severity:** Minor — the two renderings are from the same semantic range; a clarifying glossary note would suffice.

---

### 2.2 "loving-kindness contemplation" vs. glossary compound

**Occurrence:** Section 32.12 heading: "Loving-Kindness Contemplation" and body text: "for the beginner relying on the loving-kindness and compassion contemplation"

**Glossary standard:** `慈愍 | maitri-karuna | loving-kindness and compassion` is the compound form. The section heading uses "loving-kindness contemplation" alone (omitting "and compassion"), while the body text uses the full compound. The five objects footnote [^five_objects] also correctly gives "loving-kindness and compassion" for the angry type.

**Assessment:** The heading "Loving-Kindness Contemplation" is a shorthand that does not match the glossary compound. The section heading should read "Loving-Kindness and Compassion Contemplation" for consistency, or a note should confirm that `慈` (maitri alone) is the intended Chinese at that point in the heading.

**Severity:** Minor — the full compound is used in running text; the shorthand in the heading is likely acceptable but creates inconsistency.

---

### 2.3 "gladness" without Sanskrit gloss on first use

**Occurrence:** Section 32.3: "pure discipline gives rise to non-regret (*avipratisara*). And when non-regret arises, without even intending 'I shall generate gladness,' by the very nature of things, one who is free of remorse naturally generates gladness."

**Glossary standard:** Per first-use conventions, Sanskrit should be glossed on first appearance per fascicle. "Gladness" (pramodya) appears without Sanskrit parenthetical in running text. The Sanskrit appears only in the footnote.

**Assessment:** This is in sections 32.1–32.10 (outside the 32.11–32.17 review scope), but the term recurs in the summary at 32.16 ("joy and delight") without any gloss. Given the term is not in the glossary, the first-use convention cannot be applied until it is registered.

**Severity:** Dependent on glossary addition; flagged here for completeness.

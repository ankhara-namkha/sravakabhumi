## 2. Additions Check

### 2a. Sanskrit terminology additions

Throughout the translation, Sanskrit terms are added in parenthetical italics (e.g., *tri-shiksha*, *adhishila-shiksha*, *samapatti*, *satya-abhisamaya*). These are clearly marked and do not alter the meaning. This is standard scholarly practice and consistent with project design invariants. **Not a finding.**

### 2b. Minor interpretive expansions

**[m-2]** Line 264: English adds "in the present life (*drishta-dharma-sukha-vihara*)" to explain 安樂住. Chinese: 若智若見若安樂住 = "whether knowledge, or vision, or pleasant abiding." The Sanskrit gloss 安樂住 = drishta-dharma-sukha-vihara ("pleasant abiding in the seen dharma/present life") adds specificity not explicit in the Chinese. The Sanskrit term is accurate, but the phrase "in the present life" goes slightly beyond the Chinese text.

**Severity:** m (minor). The Sanskrit identification is correct; the expansion is minimal and clarifying.

**[m-3]** Line 306: English "the effort that aims to accomplish what aspiration has set in motion" expands Chinese 欲有所作精進有四 = literally "the diligence of aspiring-to-accomplish is of four kinds." The interpretive gloss adds a causal link not explicit in the compressed Chinese.

**Severity:** m (minor). Directionally correct; does not distort.

### 2c. Bracketed editorial insertions

**[m-4]** Line 153: "[three]" added before "treasures" in "(i) Vacillating doubt regarding the truths, the [three] treasures, the skandhas, and the fruits of karma." Chinese: 諸諦寶蘊業果. The brackets correctly signal this is editorial. 寶 (treasure/jewel) conventionally refers to the Three Jewels in Buddhist context.

**Severity:** m (minor). Correctly bracketed; conventional identification.

## 7. Detailed Findings by Section

### Section 28.1 (Three Superior Trainings)

**[m-6]** Line 41: Chinese 由正定心念一境性 translated as "through a rightly concentrated mind and mental one-pointedness." The character 念 (mindfulness/smriti) is absorbed into "mental" rather than being rendered as a distinct term "mindfulness." A more precise rendering might be: "through the rightly concentrated mind, with mindfulness and one-pointedness" or "through the rightly concentrated mind's mindfulness-one-pointedness." The current rendering loses the specific reference to mindfulness (念) as a factor.

**Severity:** m (minor). The overall meaning is preserved; the specific role of mindfulness (念) in the samadhi context is slightly attenuated.

**[m-7]** Line 37: Chinese 建立定義故 rendered as "establishing concentration." The Chinese 定義 more precisely means "establishing the definition/standard" -- i.e., the function of establishing the foundation for further practice. The expansion (lines 39-40) correctly explains that discipline establishes one-pointedness and enables samadhi contact, but the compressed rendering "establishing concentration" slightly shifts the emphasis from "foundational establishment" to "concentration" as the primary concept. However, this is defensible since the expanded explanation makes the intended meaning clear.

**Severity:** m (minor). Defensible rendering; no doctrinal distortion.

### Section 28.6 (Four Yogas)

**Severity:** m (minor).

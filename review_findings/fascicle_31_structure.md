| II.5f | Time for observation (觀時) | 云何觀時。謂心沈沒時 | When mind sinks or is about to sink; when shamatha-habituated mind should realize objects as they are | Dullness/sinking as trigger |

| II.5k | Signs of equanimity (捨相) | 云何捨相 | The object causes the mind to let go upward (上捨); not generating excessive effort toward the object | Release and non-over-exertion |

| II.6 | (6) Comprehending Practice (解了加行) | 云何名為解了加行 | Having well grasped the signs described above: able to enter samadhi at will, abide at will, arise at will; sometimes abandoning samadhi images to attend to ordinary objects | Mastery over entering/abiding/exiting samadhi |
| II.7 | (7) Insatiable Practice (無厭足加行) | 云何名為無厭足加行 | Never satisfied with wholesome dharmas; unceasing cultivation and abandonment; aspiring to ever-higher, ever-superior states; not stopping at small attainments midway | No complacency |
| II.8 | (8) Not-Abandoning-the-Yoke Practice (不捨軛加行) | 云何名為不捨軛加行 | Maintaining all training rules without gaps; not grasping at signs of attractive persons; equanimity in eating; diligence in wakefulness; few tasks, few activities, little distraction; able to recall long-past actions and words | Supporting conditions for one-pointedness |
| II.9 | (9) Correct Practice (正加行) | 云何名正加行 | Repeatedly generating conviction (勝解) toward the object and repeatedly clearing/removing (除遣) it | Core meditative technique |
| II.9a | Five types of clearing (五種正除遣) | 其正除遣復有五種 | (1) Internally collecting the mind (内攝其心); (2) Non-attending attention (不念作意); (3) Redirected attention (於餘作意); (4) Antidote attention (對治作意); (5) Signless-realm attention (無相界作意) | Key technical classification |
| II.9b | Primary methods | 然此義中正意。唯取內攝其心不念作意 | In this context, the main intended methods are (1) internally collecting and (2) non-attending | Narrows the five to two primary |
| II.9c | Beginner's non-attending method | 初修業者始修業時...不念作意 | Complete beginner first does not bind the mind to any object at all; simply resolves for mind to be non-scattered, signless, non-conceptual, peaceful, supremely peaceful, without movement, without hope, free from activity, internally at ease | Remarkable instruction: begin with no object |

| II.9e | Alternation of conviction and clearing | 由是因緣彼於爾時。於所緣境。不捨不取 | Not abandoning the object (because calming is active) yet not grasping it (because not forming signs/concepts). | II.9f | Painter simile (畫師喻) | 譬如世間畫師弟子初習畫業 | Like an apprentice painter: learn the model, draw, erase, redraw. Repeated erasing and redrawing makes each successive image clearer and more refined. | II.9h | Form and formless objects | 若諸色法所有相貌影像顯現...諸無色法假名為先 | Form-dharma images appear as coarse transformation-like appearances. Formless-dharma images appear led by conceptual designation based on experience | Epistemology of mental images |

| **III** | **MAJOR DIVISION: Purification of Obstacles (淨障)** | | | |
| **III.0** | **Introduction: four causes** | [T30.0457b04] 云何淨障 | Four causes for purifying obstacles: (1) thorough knowledge of their nature, (2) their causes, (3) their dangers, (4) cultivation of antidotes | Four-fold framework |
| III.1 | (1) Thorough knowledge of obstacle nature (遍知諸障自性) | 云何遍知諸障自性 | Four types of obstacle: (a) Timidity obstacle (怯弱障); (b) Covering obstacle (蓋覆障); (c) Discursive-thought obstacle (尋思障); (d) Self-elevation obstacle (自舉障) | Four obstacle types |
| III.1a | Timidity obstacle (怯弱障) | 怯弱障者 | Afflicted longing, displeasure, hope, and sorrow when undertaking renunciation and solitary practice | Emotional resistance to practice |
| III.1b | Covering obstacle (蓋覆障) | 蓋覆障者。謂貪欲等五蓋 | The five hindrances (五蓋): sensory desire, ill will, sloth-torpor, restlessness-remorse, doubt | Standard five nivarana |
| III.1c | Discursive-thought obstacle (尋思障) | 尋思障者。謂欲尋思等染污尋思 | Afflicted discursive thoughts beginning with sensory-desire thoughts | Vitarka-type obstacles |
| III.1d | Self-elevation obstacle (自舉障) | 自舉障者 | Self-exaltation based on partial, inferior wisdom-view or comfortable abiding; "I can attain it, others cannot" | Spiritual pride / premature satisfaction |
| III.2 | (2) Thorough knowledge of obstacle causes (遍知諸障因緣) | 云何遍知諸障因緣 | Detailed causal analysis of each obstacle type | |
| III.2a | Six causes of timidity (怯弱障六因緣) | 初怯弱障有六因緣 | (1) Past karma or illness -> bodily weakness; (2) Excessive effort; (3) No effort; (4) Beginner status; (5) Intense afflictions; (6) Unfamiliarity with solitude | Balanced: both over- and under-effort cause timidity |
| III.2b | Causes of covering/discursive/self-elevation | 遍知蓋覆尋思自舉障因緣者 | Improper attention (非理作意) habitually directed at conditions favoring these obstacles | Unified causal principle for three obstacle types |
| III.2c | Five examples of improper attention | 若不作意思惟不淨。而於淨相作意思惟... | (1) Not contemplating impurity, attending to purity -> desire; (2) Not contemplating loving-kindness, attending to anger-signs -> ill will; (3) Not contemplating brightness, attending to darkness -> sloth; (4) Not contemplating shamatha-signs, attending to memories of kin/country/past enjoyments -> evil discursive thoughts; (5) Not contemplating dependent origination, attending to self/mine across three times -> ignorance | Maps five pairs of wrong/right attention to five hindrances |
| III.3 | (3) Thorough knowledge of obstacle dangers (遍知諸障過患) | 云何遍知諸障過患 | Obstacles prevent: (a) attaining the unattained; (b) cause loss of what is attained; (c) corrupt yoga practice; (d) lead to afflicted/painful dwelling; (e) self-harm and harming others; (f) bad rebirth at death | Six dangers of obstacles |
| III.4 | (4) Cultivation of antidotes (修習對治) | 云何名為修習對治 | Antidotes for each obstacle type | Systematic antidote mapping |
| III.4a | Antidote for timidity | 謂諸怯弱總用隨念以為對治 | Recollection (隨念) to gladden the mind; for bodily weakness/excessive effort/beginner status -> balanced effort; for no effort -> respectful listening and questioning; for intense afflictions -> impurity etc. as object; for unfamiliarity -> reasoned reflection on training in solitude | Differentiated antidotes for six sub-causes |
| III.4b | Antidote for coverings etc. | 餘蓋覆等非理作意。用彼相違如理作意以為對治 | Proper attention (如理作意) opposite to the improper attention that caused them | Simple reversal principle |
| III.4c | Sequence of four purifications | 又遍了知諸障自性...故應尋求修習對治 | Knowledge of nature -> seeking causes -> knowing dangers -> seeking antidotes -> future prevention | Explains the logical necessity of all four steps |
| **IV** | **MAJOR DIVISION: Shamatha-Vipashyana Relationship and Completion Criteria** | | | |
| IV.1 | Multiplicity of vipashyana and shamatha | [T30.0457c27] 當知此中由隨順教有眾多故。毘鉢舍那亦有眾多 | Many teachings -> many vipashyanas -> many shamathas. Vipashyana is boundless because objects of knowledge are boundless (三門六事一一無邊) | Establishes open-ended nature of practice |

| IV.3 | Culmination in transformation of basis | 身心輕安心一境性。如是二法展轉相依...若得轉依方乃究竟 | Pliancy and one-pointedness mutually depend; only upon transformation of basis (轉依) are they perfected; then direct knowledge (現量智) of objects arises | 轉依 (ashraya-paravrtti) as culmination |

## Structural Notes

1. **Continuity from Fascicle 30:** Division I completes the six-aspect vipashyana framework begun in fascicle 30, which covered impurity contemplation (不淨觀) and loving-kindness (慈愍). Fascicle 31 covers the remaining three: dependent origination, element differentiation, and anapanasati.

2. **Parallel structure in Division I:** All three observations (I.A, I.B, I.C) follow an identical six-aspect pattern: meaning (義), referents (事), specific characteristics (自相), general characteristics (共相), categories (品), time (時), plus reasoning (理) analyzed via three or four reasoning types. The dependent origination section is significantly more elaborate than the other two, especially in its reasoning section (twelve-link causal analysis).

3. **Division II is labeled as 心一境性:** The entire nine-approaches section is explicitly framed as the teaching on mental one-pointedness (ekagrата). The ninth approach (正加行) is by far the most detailed, containing the painter simile and the subtle teaching on alternating conviction and clearing.

4. **The uddana verse (Division V) is an intermediate summary (中嗢柁南):** It summarizes Divisions I through III but precedes Division VI, indicating it is a mid-fascicle structural marker.

5. **Division VI introduces new material:** The four attentions (作意) appear to begin a new doctrinal unit that will likely continue into fascicle 32. The goldsmith simile at the end provides a unifying image but the section does not have a formal closing formula.

6. **Forward references:** At I.D.1, the text explicitly defers the six-aspect vipashyana for "skillful objects" (善巧所緣) and "affliction-purifying objects" (淨惑所緣) to a later section, indicating these will appear in subsequent fascicles.

7. **Q&A structure:** Two formal question-and-answer passages appear in Division IV (completion criteria and dual operation), using the standard 問...答 format specified in the design invariants.

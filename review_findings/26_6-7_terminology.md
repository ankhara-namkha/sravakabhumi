## Critical Issues

### CRIT-1: "sentient beings" for 有情 at line 530 -- unglosarized term, potential watchlist confusion

**Severity:** Critical
**Line:** 530
**Glossary entry:** None. 有情 (sattva / sentient being) has no entry in MASTER_GLOSSARY.md.
**Watchlist entry:** "sentient being (for 補特伽羅)" is deprecated. The watchlist note clarifies: "補特伽羅 = pudgala, not sattva."
**Chinese source:** 若有瞋行補特伽羅。於諸有情修習慈愍令瞋微薄。
**Actual usage (line 530):** "If a person of aversion-conduct practices loving-kindness and compassion toward all **sentient beings**, their aversion becomes attenuated."
**Assessment:** The Chinese 有情 IS sattva, not pudgala. Therefore "sentient beings" is not a strict watchlist violation -- the watchlist only prohibits "sentient being" when translating 補特伽羅. However, 有情 has no glossary entry at all. This is a significant gap: 有情 is one of the most frequent terms in Buddhist Chinese and will recur throughout the translation. The co-occurrence of 補特伽羅 ("person") and 有情 ("sentient beings") in the same Chinese sentence makes a clear glossary differentiation essential.
**Required fix:** Add 有情 | sattva | sentient being(s), being(s) to the glossary with a note: "Distinct from 補特伽羅 (pudgala / person). 有情 denotes living beings in general; 補特伽羅 denotes the individual person in technical Abhidharma contexts." Establish preferred rendering and first-use pattern.

### MOD-2: 隨煩惱 (upaklesha / secondary affliction) not in glossary; glossary ambiguity with 纏

**Severity:** Moderate
**Line:** 469
**Glossary entry:** None. 纏 (paryavasthana) is glossarized with "entanglement, secondary affliction" as alternate renderings, but 隨煩惱 (upaklesha) is a distinct term without its own entry.
**Actual usage (line 469):** "latent tendencies, **secondary afflictions**, and entanglements"
**Assessment:** The translation correctly distinguishes 隨煩惱 (secondary afflictions) from 纏 (entanglements) as separate items. However, 隨煩惱 has no glossary entry. Confusion may arise because the glossary lists "secondary affliction" as an alternate rendering for 纏 (paryavasthana), when 隨煩惱 (upaklesha) is actually the standard term for "secondary affliction."
**Required fix:** (1) Add 隨煩惱 | upaklesha | secondary affliction(s) to the glossary. (2) Remove "secondary affliction" as an alternate rendering for 纏 (paryavasthana), since these are distinct categories.

**Required fix:** Add glossary entries for the conduct-type compounds: 貪行 | raga-carita | greed-conduct; 瞋行 | dvesha-carita | aversion-conduct (and similarly for 癡行, 慢行, 尋思行). Note: "X-conduct" = the behavioral pattern; "person dominated by X" = the person who has it.

### MOD-10: "Satipatthana Sutta" (Pali proper name) used in footnotes

**Severity:** Moderate
**Lines:** 580, 582, 590
**Watchlist entry:** "sutta (Pali form)" should be "sutra" per NTC convention.
**Actual usage:**
- Line 580 (fn [^body_parts]): "the Pali **Satipatthana Sutta** (MN 10)"
- Line 582 (fn [^corpse_stages]): "the **Satipatthana Sutta** (MN 10)"
- Line 590 (fn [^charnel_ground]): "the **Satipatthana Sutta** (MN 10)"
**Assessment:** "Satipatthana Sutta" is the established proper name of the Pali text MN 10. Using "Smrityupasthana Sutra" would be incorrect since the reference is to a specific Pali canon text, not to a Sanskrit text. However, the watchlist does not carve out an exception for proper names. This is a judgment call: strict watchlist compliance would require Sanskritization, but standard scholarly practice preserves the Pali name when citing a Pali text. Recommend documenting the exception explicitly.
**Recommended fix:** Either (a) retain "Satipatthana Sutta" as a proper name reference to a Pali text, adding a glossary/watchlist note that Pali proper names of Pali canon texts are exceptions; or (b) use a hybrid form such as "Smrityupasthana Sutra (Pali: Satipatthana Sutta, MN 10)." Option (a) is more natural for scholarly readers.

### MOD-12: 緣性緣起 (nature of conditions and dependent origination) and 界差別 (element differentiation) not in glossary

**Assessment:** These terms are introduced here as names of purifying-conduct objects that will be treated in detail in later sections. Since these are major meditation-object categories in the Sravakabhumi's framework, they should be glossarized when their detailed sections are translated. However, the gap should be noted now.
**Required fix:** Add entries for 緣性緣起 | pratyaya-pratityasamutpada (or idampratyayata-pratityasamutpada) | the nature of conditions and dependent origination; and 界差別 | dhatu-prabheda | element differentiation. These can be marked [PROVISIONAL] until their sections are translated.

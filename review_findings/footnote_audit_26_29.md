## FASCICLE 26 — 52 Footnotes

### Candidates for Removal (6)

**`[^pudgala]`** — "The verse uses 數取趣 (*pudgala*, lit. 'one who repeatedly goes to [rebirth] destinations')..."
*Reason:* The body text already introduces *pudgala* with a parenthetical. This note provides an alternative Chinese character variant (數取趣 vs. 補特伽羅) relevant only to specialists comparing Chinese editions. Adds nothing for the practitioner reader; the etymological distinction between two Chinese transliterations of the same Sanskrit is micro-philological. Remove.

**`[^samaya]`** — "'Pleasant abidings in the present life' (*drishta-dharma-sukha-vihara*) refers to meditative attainments..."
*Reason:* This is a one-sentence gloss restating what the phrase already conveys in plain English ("pleasant abidings in the present life" is self-explanatory in context). The Sanskrit term is low-value here. Remove.

**`[^jnana_darshana]`** — "'Knowledge and vision' (*jnana-darshana*, 智見) is a stock Buddhist compound denoting direct, experiential knowing..."
*Reason:* This is a gloss for a standard compound that recurs throughout Buddhist literature. The phrase "knowledge and vision" is already clear in context. Every practitioner-level reader understands the distinction between conceptual and experiential knowing without a footnote. Remove.

**`[^triple_cognition]`** — "The three terms — 'only of cognition' (唯智), 'only of seeing' (唯見), 'only of correct recollection' (唯正憶念) — emphasize that the similar image is purely a mental event..."
*Reason:* The note simply rephrases what the three-term list already says. The point (the meditation image is a mental event) is obvious in context and already made in the body text. Remove.

**`[^maitri_karuna]`** — "The Chinese 慈愍 is a compound of 慈 (*maitri*, loving-kindness) and 愍 (compassion, pity)..."
*Reason:* Terminological housekeeping that belongs in the glossary, not a footnote. The rendering choice ("loving-kindness and compassion" vs. shorthand "loving-kindness") can be noted once in a translator's preface or glossary entry for *maitri*. As a footnote it interrupts reading and adds no insight. Remove.

**`[^five_dharmas]`** — "The Chinese says 'five dharmas' (五法), not 'five skandhas' (五蘊)..."
*Reason:* This is a purely textual/terminological quibble relevant only to someone checking against the Chinese. For the practitioner reader, "five dharmas" meaning the five skandhas is clarified by context. This level of Chinese-edition note belongs in a critical apparatus, not a running footnote. Remove.

### Excessive (6)

**`[^aspiration_lineage]`** — 111 words. "The distinction between aspiration (*pranidhana*) and lineage (*gotra*)..."
*Issue:* The note goes well beyond what the passage needs. The core point (aspiration can change; lineage is fixed) is essential. The second half — surveying Madhyantavibhaga, Mahayanasutralamkara, indeterminate lineage, and "significant softening" — turns a gloss into a mini-essay on later Yogacara development. The 20-scholar audience does not need the cross-tradition survey here; it's not signaled by anything in the passage. **Trim to ~50 words:** keep the core doctrine, cut the comparative Yogacara history.

**`[^pratipada]`** — 122 words. "The four courses of progress (*catur-pratipada*) classify practitioners along two axes..."
*Issue:* The first 50 words (the two axes, the four types) are essential. The cross-reference to AN 4 and AKBh VI.70 is standard scholarly apparatus — fine. The last 40 words comparing Nikaya and Sravakabhumi definitions of "painful" goes beyond what the passage warrants. **Trim to ~70 words.**

**`[^eight_libs]`** — (long; the full eight liberations listing) "The eight liberations (*ashta-vimoksha*) are a standard list..."
*Issue:* Enumerating all eight liberations in the footnote when the text only mentions the concept of the body-witness is excessive. Practitioners who need the eight liberations explained can find them treated fully later in the text. **Trim:** state what "body-witness" means (1–2 sentences), drop the enumeration.

**`[^image_synonyms]`** — 105 words. "These eight alternative names for the meditation image illuminate different aspects..."
*Issue:* The note lists and briefly glosses all eight synonyms. Half are self-explanatory ("domain of samadhi," "entrance," "gate of samadhi"). The valuable content is the Tsongkhapa observation on 光影. **Trim to ~50 words:** keep the Tsongkhapa note, compress the obvious synonyms.

**`[^revata]`** — 108 words. "Revata (頡隸伐多)... is an elder to whom the Buddha addresses a teaching..."
*Issue:* The information about Revata as the interlocutor is useful (1 sentence). The second half — explaining that the four aspects of "settling the mind" do not map one-to-one to the four objects, and providing the mapping — is a substantive doctrinal analysis that belongs either in the body text or as a much shorter cross-reference. As a footnote it creates confusion by introducing a structural point not foregrounded in the text. Consider moving this content to a brief translator's note within the section or trimming to 1 sentence.

**`[^maitri_object]`** — 111 words. "The Chinese term is 慈愍所緣..."
*Issue:* The note opens usefully (explaining the compound 慈愍). It then becomes an extended discussion of whether equanimity (*upeksha*) is implied or absent, and closes with a Visuddhimagga cross-reference. The equanimity question is a genuinely open doctrinal question — but raising it in a footnote without resolution creates doubt rather than clarity. The Visuddhimagga citation is for the 20% scholar. **Trim to ~40 words:** explain 慈愍 = loving-kindness + compassion, note equanimity is not explicitly named here. Drop the Visuddhimagga citation.

**`[^three_grades]`** — Essential distinction. The comparison with the "broader commentarial tradition" treating the terms as describing boundless scope is valuable scholarly context. But the final two sentences on the third dhyana as "peak of experiential bliss" restate what any reader knows from context. Trim last two sentences.

**`[^three_signs]`** — Well-scoped. Cross-reference to fascicle 30 is useful. The note is close to ideal length.

**`[^four_purifications]`** — Four-item list is clear and necessary. The note is tightly written. Keep.

**`[^charnel_ground]`** — The note is essential (explaining 憺怕路 = shitavana). The Pali parallel reference is appropriate. Keep.

## Section 32.5 (T30.0460c01) — The Sign of Luminosity and Entering Practice

### Sentence-by-Sentence Comparison

**1. Opening instruction on luminosity signs**
- CH: 汝取如是過患相已。復應數數取光明相。謂或燈明或大火明。或日輪明或月輪明。
- EN: "Having apprehended the sign of danger, you should then repeatedly apprehend the sign of luminosity — the light of a lamp, the light of a great fire, the light of the sun, the light of the moon."
- **Verdict:** ACCURATE. All four light sources rendered correctly.

**2. Charnel ground instruction**
- CH: 既取如是光明相已。復詣塚間取青瘀相。廣說乃至取骨鎖相。
- EN: "Having apprehended the sign of luminosity, go to the charnel ground and apprehend the sign of the blue-black corpse, and so on through to the bone-chain."
- **Verdict:** ACCURATE. 廣說乃至 correctly rendered as "and so on through to."

**3. Alternative for charnel ground**
- CH: 汝若不能往詣塚間。當取彩畫木石所作如是諸相。
- EN: "If you cannot go to the charnel ground, apprehend these signs from paintings or from sculptures of wood and stone."
- **Verdict:** ACCURATE. 彩畫 = paintings; 木石所作 = made from wood and stone.

**4. Return to dwelling and seating**
- CH: 取是相已還所住處。或阿練若或林樹下或空閑室。或在大床或小繩床或草葉座。
- EN: "Having apprehended these signs, return to your dwelling — whether a forest retreat, the base of a tree, or an empty room. On a large couch, a small rope-seat, or a seat of grass and leaves,"
- **Verdict:** ACCURATE. 阿練若 (aranya) = forest retreat. All three dwelling types and three seat types present.

**5. Posture and mindfulness**
- CH: 先洗足已結跏趺坐。端身正願安住背念。
- EN: "first wash your feet, then sit cross-legged, body upright, attention settled, and establish mindfulness."
- **FINDING [m-1]:** Minor. 端身正願 is rendered "body upright, attention settled" — this is acceptable but 正願 literally means "with correct aspiration/intention" (Skt. *samyak-pranidhaya*). "Attention settled" slightly undertranslates — the standard rendering in this formula is "with right aspiration" or "set his mindfulness before him." 安住背念 is "establish mindfulness" — but 背念 more precisely means "establishing mindfulness facing [the object]" or "setting mindfulness before one." The translation captures the essential meaning but slightly compresses the formula. **Severity: minor.**

**6. Single-pointed focus, then six perceptions**
- CH: 先於一境令心不散繫念在前。復於其中依六種想作意思惟。謂無相想無分別想。寂靜想。無作用想。無所思慕無躁擾想。離諸燒惱寂滅樂想。
- EN: "First, on a single object, keep the mind from scattering and tether mindfulness before you. Then, within that, apply attention using the six perceptions: signlessness, non-discrimination, quiescence, non-activity, freedom from longing and agitation, and peaceful bliss free from torment."
- **Verdict:** ACCURATE. All six perceptions listed correctly and in order. 繫念在前 nicely rendered as "tether mindfulness before you."

**7. Discerning disturbed/undisturbed states**
- CH: 又於其中汝當審諦周遍了知亂不亂相。分明現前如如審諦。周遍了知亂不亂相。如是如是汝能了知諸相尋思隨煩惱中所有亂相。及能了知心一境性隨六想修諸不亂相。
- EN: "Within this practice, you should carefully and thoroughly discern the disturbed and undisturbed states. As they become clearly present before you — the more carefully you discern them, the more you can recognize the disturbance within signs, discursive thoughts, and secondary afflictions, and recognize the non-disturbance within mental one-pointedness cultivated through the six perceptions."
- **Verdict:** ACCURATE. The Chinese uses a correlative structure 如如...如是如是 (the more X... the more Y) which the translation captures well. Content complete.

**8. Resting in one object**
- CH: 又汝於此亂不亂相。如是如是審諦了知。便能安住一所緣境亦能安住內心寂止。諸心相續諸心流注。前後一味無相無分別寂靜而轉。
- EN: "The more thoroughly you discern these disturbed and undisturbed states, the more you can rest on a single object and abide in inner mental stillness. The mind's continuity and flow then move from moment to moment with one flavor — signless, non-discriminating, and at peace."
- **Verdict:** ACCURATE. 諸心相續諸心流注 = "The mind's continuity and flow." 前後一味 = "from moment to moment with one flavor." All content present.

**9. Mirror simile and non-attending attention**
- CH: 又若汝心雖得寂止。由失念故。及由串習諸相尋思隨煩惱等諸過失故。如鏡中面所緣影像數現在前。隨所生起。即於其中當更修習不念作意。謂先所見諸過患相增上力故。即於如是所緣境相。由所修習不念作意。除遣散滅當令畢竟不現在前。
- EN: "Yet even when the mind has settled, it may lose mindfulness through habitual familiarity with signs, discursive thoughts, and secondary afflictions. Then images of the meditation object arise repeatedly, like reflections of a face in a mirror. Whenever they arise, apply non-attending attention: through the force of the previously apprehended sign of danger, dispel and dissolve those images so they no longer appear before you."
- **FINDING [m-2]:** Minor. The Chinese says 由失念故 (through loss of mindfulness) AND 由串習諸相尋思隨煩惱等諸過失故 (through the faults of habitual familiarity with signs, discursive thoughts, secondary afflictions, etc.). The English renders both causes but slightly conflates them: "it may lose mindfulness through habitual familiarity" reads as if the loss of mindfulness is caused by habitual familiarity, whereas the Chinese presents them as two coordinate causes. This is a very minor structural compression. **Severity: minor.**
- **FINDING [m-3]:** Minor. 如鏡中面所緣影像 — the Chinese says the meditation object's images appear "like a face in a mirror" (如鏡中面). The English renders "like reflections of a face in a mirror" — adding "reflections of" which is an acceptable explicitation, but the Chinese simile is simply "like a face [seen] in a mirror," with the reflected images being the 所緣影像. The translation communicates the correct meaning. **Severity: minor.**
- **Verdict on the rest:** 除遣散滅當令畢竟不現在前 = "dispel and dissolve those images so they no longer appear before you" — correct. The phrase 先所見諸過患相增上力故 correctly rendered as "the force of the previously apprehended sign of danger."

**10. Closing exhortation**
- CH: 賢首當知。如是所緣甚為微細難可通達。汝應發起猛利樂欲。為求通達發勤精進。
- EN: "Worthy One, know this: such objects are extremely subtle and difficult to penetrate. You should arouse fervent aspiration and apply diligent effort to penetrate them."
- **Verdict:** ACCURATE. 猛利樂欲 = "fervent aspiration" (lit. "sharp/fierce desire/wish"). All content present.

### 32.5 Summary
No omissions, no additions of substance, no mistranslations. Three minor findings, all acceptable compressions or explicitations that preserve the meaning.

## Section 32.6 (T30.0461a01) — The Oil-Bowl Simile

### Sentence-by-Sentence Comparison

**1. Opening frame**
- CH: 世尊依此所緣境相密意說言。
- EN: "Regarding these meditation objects, the Blessed One spoke with implicit meaning:"
- **Verdict:** ACCURATE. 密意說言 = "spoke with implicit meaning" — correct rendering.

**2. Definition of "fine crowd"**
- CH: 汝等苾芻當知眾善。言眾善者。謂於大眾共集會中盛壯美色。即此眾善最殊勝者。謂於多眾大集會中歌舞倡伎。
- EN: "Bhikshus, you should know what 'a fine crowd' means: in a great gathering, the prime and beautiful forms. And the most excellent thing in this fine crowd is the singing, dancing, and musical entertainment."
- **FINDING [m-4]:** Minor. 眾善 is rendered as "a fine crowd" — this is interpretive. 眾善 literally means "a multitude of fine things" or "fine things in a crowd." The English choice "a fine crowd" works contextually but slightly shifts from "the fine things [seen] in the gathering" to the crowd itself being fine. The Pali parallel (SN 47.20) has a "great crowd of people" who come to see a beauty queen, so "a fine crowd" is within range. **Severity: minor.**

**3. The wise man's instruction**
- CH: 假使有一智慧丈夫。從外而來告一人曰。咄哉男子。汝於今者可持如是平滿鉢油勿令灩溢。經歷如是大眾中過當避其間所有眾善及諸最勝歌舞倡伎大等生等。今有魁膾露拔利劍隨逐汝行。若汝鉢油一滴墮地。此之魁膾即以利劍。當斬汝首斷汝命根。
- EN: "'Here, good fellow! You must carry this bowl brimming with oil through this great crowd, past all the fine sights and the singing, dancing, and entertainment and the great beings there — without letting it overflow. An executioner follows you with a drawn sword. If a single drop of oil falls to the ground, he will sever your head and cut off your life.'"
- **FINDING [M-1]:** Moderate. 當避其間所有眾善及諸最勝歌舞倡伎大等生等 — The Chinese says the person must pass through the crowd while AVOIDING (當避) the fine sights, entertainment, and great beings. The English renders "past all the fine sights" — which captures the movement through, but drops the explicit instruction to AVOID them. The avoidance instruction is part of the challenge: you must carry the oil through while actively steering clear of the distractions. **Severity: Moderate.**
- **FINDING [m-5]:** Minor. 咄哉男子 is rendered "Here, good fellow!" — 咄哉 is an exclamation (roughly "Hey there!" or "Listen!"). The English is an acceptable adaptation. **Severity: minor.**
- **FINDING [m-6]:** Minor. 勿令灩溢 = "without letting it overflow" — accurate. But 灩溢 specifically means "brim over/splash out" while 一滴墮地 later refers to a single drop falling. The English correctly differentiates these. **Severity: minor — no issue.**
- **FINDING [m-7]:** Minor. 大等生等 is rendered "great beings." This term is somewhat obscure. 大等生 likely refers to important/high-status persons present at the gathering. "Great beings" is acceptable. **Severity: minor.**

**4. Buddha's question**
- CH: 苾芻汝等於意云何。是持鉢人頗不作意專心油鉢拔劍。魁膾不平地等。而能作意觀視眾善及諸最勝歌舞倡伎大等生耶。
- EN: "'Bhikshus, what do you think? Could this man — disregarding the oil bowl and the sword-bearing executioner and the uneven ground — attend instead to the fine crowd and the excellent entertainment?'"
- **FINDING [M-2]:** Moderate. The Chinese question structure is: 是持鉢人頗不作意專心油鉢拔劍魁膾不平地等而能作意觀視眾善... — "Could this bowl-carrier, without attending to (不作意) the oil bowl, the sword-bearing executioner, the uneven ground, etc., attend to (作意觀視) the fine crowd..." The English renders "Could this man — disregarding the oil bowl and the sword-bearing executioner and the uneven ground — attend instead to the fine crowd." The structure is preserved, but the nuance of 頗 (could he really? / would he at all?) which implies a rhetorical question expecting "no" is captured by the English "Could" construction. The rendering "disregarding" for 不作意專心 compresses "not attending with focused mind to" but captures the sense. **Severity: Minor on reconsideration — the meaning is preserved.**

**5. The bhikshus' reply**
- CH: 不也。世尊。何以故。是持鉢人既見魁膾露拔利劍隨逐而行。極大怖畏專作是念。我所持鉢油既彌滿。經是眾中極難將度。脫有一滴當墮地者。定為如是拔劍魁膾。當斬我首斷我命根。是人爾時於彼眾善及諸最勝歌舞倡伎大等生等。都不作意思念觀視。唯於油鉢專心作意而正護持。
- EN: "'No, Blessed One. Why not? Because seeing the executioner following with drawn sword, he would be gripped by great fear, thinking only: "My bowl is brimming with oil. It will be extremely difficult to carry it through this crowd. If a single drop falls, this executioner will surely sever my head and cut off my life." At that moment, he would not attend to, think about, or look at the fine crowd, the singing, dancing, entertainment, or the great beings — he would attend single-mindedly to the oil bowl, carefully guarding it.'"
- **Verdict:** ACCURATE. Complete and faithful rendering. 都不作意思念觀視 = "would not attend to, think about, or look at" — all three verbs rendered. 唯於油鉢專心作意而正護持 = "attend single-mindedly to the oil bowl, carefully guarding it" — correct.

### 32.6 Summary
One moderate finding (omission of the explicit "avoidance" instruction). Several minor findings. No mistranslations. Overall faithful.

## Section 32.7 (T30.0461b01) — Decoding the Simile; Alternating Shamatha and Vipashyana

### Sentence-by-Sentence Comparison

**1. Frame statement**
- CH: 如是苾芻。我諸弟子恭敬殷重專心憶念。修四念住當知亦爾。
- EN: "In the same way, bhikshus, my disciples who cultivate the four foundations of mindfulness with respect, earnestness, and focused recollection should be understood likewise."
- **Verdict:** ACCURATE. 恭敬 = respect; 殷重 = earnestness; 專心憶念 = focused recollection.

**2. Simile decoding — fine crowd**
- CH: 言眾善者。喻能隨順貪欲纏等隨煩惱法。
- EN: "The fine crowd represents conditions that fuel the entanglement of desire and other secondary afflictions."
- **Verdict:** ACCURATE. 隨順 = fuel/be conducive to; 貪欲纏等隨煩惱法 = entanglement of desire and other secondary afflictions.

**3. Entertainment decoding**
- CH: 於中最勝歌舞倡伎。喻能隨順尋思戲論躁擾處法。
- EN: "The singing, dancing, and entertainment represents conditions that fuel discursive thoughts, idle elaboration, and agitation."
- **Verdict:** ACCURATE. 尋思 = discursive thoughts; 戲論 = idle elaboration (prapanca); 躁擾 = agitation.

**4. Great beings decoding**
- CH: 大等生等喻色相等十種相法。
- EN: "The great beings represent the ten signs beginning with visible form."
- **Verdict:** ACCURATE. 色相等十種相法 = the ten signs beginning with visible form (色).

**5. Wise man decoding**
- CH: 智慧丈夫。喻瑜伽師。
- EN: "The wise man represents the yoga teacher."
- **Verdict:** ACCURATE.

**6. Oil bowl decoding**
- CH: 平滿油鉢。喻奢摩他所安住心能令身心輕安潤澤。是奢摩他義。
- EN: "The bowl brimming with oil represents the mind settled in shamatha — generating physical and mental pliancy and moistness. This is the meaning of shamatha."
- **Verdict:** ACCURATE. 身心輕安潤澤 = "physical and mental pliancy and moistness." 是奢摩他義 = "This is the meaning of shamatha."

**7. Executioner decoding**
- CH: 露拔利劍隨行魁膾。喻先所取諸相尋思隨煩惱中諸過患相。
- EN: "The executioner following with drawn sword represents the previously apprehended sign of danger regarding signs, discursive thoughts, and secondary afflictions."
- **Verdict:** ACCURATE.

**8. Guarding the oil decoding**
- CH: 專心將護不令鉢油一滴墮地。喻能審諦周遍了知亂不亂相之所攝受奢摩他道。
- EN: "Single-mindedly guarding against even a single drop falling represents the shamatha path informed by careful, thorough discernment of the disturbed and undisturbed states."
- **Verdict:** ACCURATE. 之所攝受奢摩他道 = "the shamatha path [that is] informed/embraced by" — rendered as "the shamatha path informed by." Good.

**9. Resultant continuity**
- CH: 由是能令諸心相續諸心流注。由精進力無間策發。前後一味無相無分別寂靜而轉。不起一心。復緣諸相。或緣尋思及隨煩惱。
- EN: "Through this, by the power of effort applied without interruption, the mind's continuity and flow move from moment to moment with one flavor — signless, non-discriminating, and at peace — without giving rise to a single thought that reverts to signs, discursive thoughts, or secondary afflictions."
- **Verdict:** ACCURATE. 不起一心復緣諸相 = "without giving rise to a single thought that reverts to signs" — correctly parsed. 由精進力無間策發 = "by the power of effort applied without interruption."

**10. Alternation instruction frame**
- CH: 是瑜伽師復應如是慇懃教誨於奢摩他初修業者告言。賢首。
- EN: "The teacher further instructs the shamatha beginner:"
- **FINDING [m-8]:** Minor. 慇懃教誨 = "earnestly instructs" — the "earnestly" (慇懃) is dropped. Very minor compression. **Severity: minor.**

**11. Well-cultivated shamatha path**
- CH: 汝若如是精勤修習奢摩他道。如是方便攝受。正念正知俱行。有喜樂心乃名善修奢摩他道。
- EN: "'Worthy One, if you diligently practice the shamatha path in this way — with such skillful means, accompanied by right mindfulness and clear knowing, with a joyful and happy mind — this is called the well-cultivated shamatha path.'"
- **Verdict:** ACCURATE. All elements present: 精勤修習 = diligently practice; 如是方便攝受 = with such skillful means; 正念正知俱行 = accompanied by right mindfulness and clear knowing; 有喜樂心 = with a joyful and happy mind; 善修奢摩他道 = well-cultivated shamatha path.

**12. When practice becomes forced**
- CH: 若復串習諸過失故。不能於中深心喜樂。極大艱辛勵力策發方現前者。
- EN: "But if, due to habitual faults, you cannot generate deep joy and happiness in it, and the practice appears only with great effort and strain,"
- **Verdict:** ACCURATE. 串習諸過失 = habitual faults; 極大艱辛勵力策發方現前 = "appears only with great effort and strain."

**13. Switch to formed-object practice**
- CH: 還應速疾出無分別所緣境相。於有分別所緣境相繫念在前。如先所取諸不淨相。
- EN: "then you should quickly emerge from the formless, non-discriminating meditation object and establish mindfulness on a formed, discriminating object — such as the signs of impurity you previously apprehended."
- **FINDING [m-9]:** Minor. 無分別所緣境相 is rendered "formless, non-discriminating meditation object" — 無分別 means "non-discriminating" but not "formless" (which would be 無色). Using "formless" could be confused with the four formless attainments (arupa-dhyana). The Chinese says "the non-discriminating meditation object" — adding "formless" is interpretive. However, in context this clearly refers to the signless, non-discriminating shamatha object described above, and the footnote correctly identifies it as 有分別所緣 vs. 無分別所緣. **Severity: minor — but worth noting for precision.**

**14. Applying sign-following vipashyana**
- CH: 汝今復應作意思惟。先應用彼唯隨相行毘鉢舍那。或觀青瘀或觀膿爛。廣說乃至觀骨觀鎖或觀骨鎖。
- EN: "Apply the sign-following mode of vipashyana: contemplate the blue-black corpse, the festering, and so on through to the bones and the bone-chain."
- **FINDING [m-10]:** Minor. 汝今復應作意思惟 ("You should now again apply attention and reflect") is condensed — the "you should now again" and "attend and reflect" are compressed into "Apply." Slight compression but meaning preserved. Also: 先應用彼唯隨相行毘鉢舍那 — 先 (first) is dropped, and 唯 (only/exclusively) is dropped. The Chinese says "First, apply only the sign-following mode of vipashyana." The "first" and "only" indicate a deliberate sequential instruction — use ONLY sign-following vipashyana (not the other three modes). **Severity: minor, but the "only" (唯) may have doctrinal significance.**

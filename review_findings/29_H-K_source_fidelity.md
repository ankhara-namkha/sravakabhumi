## Section 29.8 — Four Wholesome Roots (Lines 96–100)

### Line 96: 即由如是諸根諸力。漸修漸習漸多修習為因緣故。便能發起下中上品順決擇分四種善根。何等為四。一煗二頂三順諦忍四世第一法。

**Translation (line 211):** "As the five faculties and five powers are progressively cultivated, practiced, and deepened, they give rise to the four wholesome roots concordant with penetration (*nirvedha-bhagiya*), in lower, middle, and upper grades."

**Findings:**

1. **[Minor — Paraphrase]** 漸修漸習漸多修習 is a progressive triple: "gradually cultivated, gradually practiced, gradually practiced extensively." The translation compresses to "progressively cultivated, practiced, and deepened." The triple structure 修/習/多修習 shows intensification. "Deepened" is interpretive for 多修習 (literally: "extensively/repeatedly practiced"). The meaning is preserved but the specific triple rhythm is slightly flattened. **Acceptable as NTC register choice.**

2. **[OK]** 即由如是諸根諸力 — "As the five faculties and five powers" — correctly identifies 諸根諸力 as the five faculties and five powers from the preceding context. Accurate.

3. **[OK]** 為因緣故 — "they give rise to" — the Chinese says these serve as "cause and condition" (因緣); the translation renders this causally. Accurate.

4. **[OK]** 順決擇分四種善根 — "four wholesome roots concordant with penetration (*nirvedha-bhagiya*)" — correct standard rendering. 順諦忍 rendered as "acceptance of the truths" — correct.

### Lines 98–100: Fire-drilling analogy

**Line 98:** 譬如有人欲以其火作火所作。為求火故下安乾木上施鑽燧。精勤策勵勇猛鑽求。彼於如是精勤策勵勇猛鑽時。於下木上最初生煗。次煗增長熱氣上衝。次倍增盛其煙遂發。次無焰火欻然流出。火出無間發生猛焰。猛焰生已便能造作火之所作。

**Translation (lines 215–222):**

5. **[OK]** 欲以其火作火所作 — "wishes to use fire to accomplish fire's work" — accurate.

6. **[OK]** 下安乾木上施鑽燧 — "places dry wood below and sets the fire-drill above it" — accurate.

7. **[OK]** 精勤策勵勇猛鑽求 — "With diligent self-urging and vigorous effort, he drills" — accurate. The term pair 策勵/勇猛 is correctly mapped to the established terminology of self-urging/vigorous effort.

8. **[OK]** 次倍增盛其煙遂發 — "it further intensifies and smoke begins to appear" — accurate. 倍增盛 = "further intensifies."

9. **[OK]** 次無焰火欻然流出 — "flameless fire suddenly bursts forth" — accurate. 欻然 = suddenly.

10. **[OK]** 火出無間發生猛焰。猛焰生已便能造作火之所作 — "Immediately after the fire emerges, blazing flames arise. Once the blazing flames have arisen, one is then able to accomplish the work of fire." — accurate. 無間 = without interval/immediately after.

**Line 100:** 如鑽火人精勤策勵勇猛鑽求。五根五力漸修漸習漸多修習當知亦爾。如下木上初所生煗。其煗善根當知亦爾。燒諸煩惱無漏法火生前相故。如煗增長熱氣上衝。其頂善根當知亦爾。如次煙發。其順諦忍當知亦爾。如無焰火欻然流出。世第一法當知亦爾。如火無間發生猛焰。世第一法所攝五根五力無間所生出世無漏聖法當知亦爾。

**Translation (lines 224–230):**

11. **[Moderate — Structural Change]** The Chinese uses 當知亦爾 five times in sequence to draw explicit parallels. The translation restructures into a bullet list without repeating this formula. Per project rules (NTC prose register, formula compression), this is explicitly encouraged. **Acceptable — no meaning change.** However, note:

12. **[Moderate — Partial Omission]** 燒諸煩惱無漏法火生前相故 — "Because it is the precursory sign before the fire of undefiled dharmas that burns away all afflictions." The translation renders this as: "the precursory sign before the fire of undefiled dharmas that burns away all afflictions." This is present in the translation (line 226). **OK — confirmed present.**

13. **[OK]** 世第一法所攝五根五力無間所生出世無漏聖法 — "the supramundane, undefiled, noble dharmas arising from the five faculties and five powers comprised within supreme worldly dharma." The Chinese says these arise 無間 (without interval / immediately after) from the five faculties/powers comprised within 世第一法. Translation says "arising from" — the 無間 (without interval) nuance is present in the phrasing "that follow without interruption" in the preceding bullet. **OK.**

## Section 29.10 — Noble Eightfold Path (Lines 104b–120)

### Line 104 (from 為斷彼故): 為斷彼故修習三蘊所攝八支聖道。此中正見正思惟正精進。慧蘊所攝。正語正業正命。戒蘊所攝。正念正定。定蘊所攝。

**Translation (line 252):** "For the purpose of cutting off those remaining afflictions, one cultivates the noble eightfold path (*arya-ashtangika-marga*) comprised within the three trainings. In this context: right view, right thought, and right effort belong to the training of wisdom (*prajna*). Right speech, right action, and right livelihood belong to the training of discipline (*shila*). Right mindfulness and right samadhi belong to the training of samadhi."

25. **[Minor — Terminology]** 三蘊 is translated as "three trainings." Strictly, 蘊 = "aggregate/group" (skandha) and 學 = "training" (shiksha). The Chinese says 三蘊 (three groups/aggregates), not 三學 (three trainings). The text uses 慧蘊/戒蘊/定蘊 (wisdom-group, discipline-group, samadhi-group). Rendering 蘊 as "training" is a common interpretive choice since the three groups map to the three trainings, but it is not literal. The Chinese deliberately uses 蘊 not 學 here. **Minor — meaning preserved but terminology slightly shifted.**

### Line 106: 問何因緣故名八支聖道。答諸聖有學已見迹者由八支攝行迹正道。能無餘斷一切煩惱。能於解脫究竟作證。是故名為八支聖道。

**Translation (lines 256–258):**

26. **[OK]** Q&A structure preserved. 行迹正道 — "correct path comprising these eight branches as their course" — the phrase 行迹 (course/tracks) is captured as "course." Accurate.

27. **[OK]** 能於解脫究竟作證 — "ultimately realize liberation" — accurate.

### Line 108: Right view and right thought definitions

**Chinese:** 當知此中若覺支時所得真覺。若得彼已以慧安立如證而覺。總略此二合名正見。

**Translation (line 262):** "right view is a summary term combining two things: the true awakening obtained at the time of the awakening factors, and the wisdom that subsequently establishes understanding in accordance with that realization."

28. **[OK]** 覺支時所得真覺 — "the true awakening obtained at the time of the awakening factors" — accurate.

29. **[OK]** 以慧安立如證而覺 — "the wisdom that subsequently establishes understanding in accordance with that realization" — this is a complex phrase. 以慧 = "with/through wisdom"; 安立 = "establishes"; 如證而覺 = "awakens/understands in accordance with what was realized." The translation captures the sense. **Acceptable.**

30. **[OK]** 總略此二合名正見 — "a summary term combining two things" — accurate.

**Chinese:** 由此正見增上力故。所起出離無恚無害分別思惟名正思惟。

**Translation (line 264):** "Through the dominant force of this right view, the discriminative thinking that arises — of renunciation, non-ill-will, and non-harm — is called right thought."

31. **[OK]** 出離 = renunciation, 無恚 = non-ill-will, 無害 = non-harm. All three correctly rendered.

### Line 108 continued: Right speech

**Chinese:** 若心趣入諸所尋思。彼唯尋思如是相狀所有尋思。若心趣入諸所言論。即由正見增上力故起善思惟。發起種種如法言論。是名正語。

**Translation (line 266):** "When the mind turns to thought, one entertains only thoughts of this character. When the mind turns to speech, one gives rise — through the dominant force of right view — to wholesome thinking and engages in discourse that accords with the Dharma. This is called right speech."

32. **[OK]** 若心趣入諸所尋思 — "When the mind turns to thought" — accurate. 趣入 = turns to/enters.

33. **[OK]** 彼唯尋思如是相狀所有尋思 — "one entertains only thoughts of this character" — the Chinese says one only thinks (尋思) those thoughts (尋思) that are of this character/aspect (如是相狀). Meaning preserved. Slightly compressed but accurate.

34. **[OK]** 發起種種如法言論 — "engages in discourse that accords with the Dharma" — accurate. 如法 = in accordance with Dharma.

### Line 110: Right action

**Chinese:** 若如法求衣服飲食諸坐臥具病緣醫藥供身什物。於追求時若往若還正知而住。若覩若瞻若屈若伸。若持衣鉢及僧伽胝。若食若飲若噉若甞正知而住。或於住時於已追求衣服等事。若行若住若坐若臥。廣說乃至若解勞睡正知而住。是名正業。

**Translation (line 268):**

37. **[OK]** Three instances of 正知而住 correctly noted: (1) during going and returning while seeking, (2) during eating/drinking/chewing/tasting, (3) at rest including walking/standing/sitting/lying down. Translation captures all three. **Accurate.**

38. **[OK]** 若覩若瞻若屈若伸 — "whether looking, gazing, bending, or stretching" — accurate.

39. **[OK]** 僧伽胝 — "*sanghati* robe" — correct Sanskrit with italics.

40. **[OK]** 廣說乃至若解勞睡 — "as explained in detail through alleviating fatigue and drowsiness" — accurate. 解勞睡 = alleviating fatigue and drowsiness.

### Line 112: Right livelihood

**Translation (line 270):** "Lawfully seeking robes, food and drink, and so on through requisites, and distancing oneself from all practices that constitute wrong livelihood — this is called right livelihood."

**Translation (line 274):** "The right speech, right action, and right livelihood that consist in abstentions — these were already obtained at the time of realizing the undefiled attention belonging to the awakening factors."

### Line 116: Discipline beloved of noble ones (聖所愛戒)

**Chinese:** 問何故此名聖所愛戒。答以諸聖者賢善正至。長時愛樂欣慕悅意。我於何時當正獲得諸語惡行諸身惡行諸邪命事。不作律儀。由彼長夜於此尸羅深心愛樂欣慕悅意。故獲得時名聖所愛。

**Translation (lines 278–280):**

44. **[Moderate — Omission]** The Chinese 不作律儀 (the restraint of non-doing / the restraint against doing [evil conduct]) is not explicitly rendered in the translation. The Chinese sentence structure is: "When will I obtain the restraint (律儀) of not-doing (不作) regarding evil conduct of speech, evil conduct of body, and wrong livelihood matters?" The translation renders: "When will I obtain the restraint against evil conduct of speech, evil conduct of body, and wrong livelihood?" The word 不作 (non-doing / not-performing) is partially absorbed into "restraint against" but 律儀 (restraint/discipline) is the noun the noble ones aspire to obtain. The meaning is substantively preserved but the specific term 律儀 (samvara, restraint) is not surfaced.

45. **[OK]** 長夜 — "over the long course of time" — accurate. 長夜 literally = "long night" but idiomatically means "over a long period."

46. **[OK]** 賢善正至 — "the wise, virtuous, and rightly arrived" — accurate rendering of this compound.

**Chinese:** 獲得如是聖愛戒已。終不正知而說妄語。終不故思害眾生命。終不故思不與而取。終不故思行欲邪行。終不非法求衣服等。

**Translation (line 282):** "Having obtained this discipline beloved of noble ones, one will never knowingly speak falsehoods, never intentionally take the life of a sentient being, never take what is not given, never engage in sexual misconduct, and never unlawfully seek robes and the like."

47. **[OK]** Five 終不 (never) correctly counted and rendered. All five abstentions present:
   - 終不正知而說妄語 — "never knowingly speak falsehoods" ✓
   - 終不故思害眾生命 — "never intentionally take the life of a sentient being" ✓
   - 終不故思不與而取 — "never take what is not given" ✓
   - 終不故思行欲邪行 — "never engage in sexual misconduct" ✓
   - 終不非法求衣服等 — "never unlawfully seek robes and the like" ✓

48. **[Minor — Nuance]** 正知而說妄語 — the Chinese says 正知 (with clear knowing / knowingly) for the first, and 故思 (intentionally / deliberately thinking) for the next three. The translation renders 正知 as "knowingly" and 故思 as "intentionally" — slightly different Chinese terms but both rendered with English synonyms. The distinction between 正知 (with samprajanya-type awareness) and 故思 (deliberate intention/cetana) is subtle but real. **Minor — meaning preserved.**

**Chinese:** 即由如是聖所愛戒增上力故。於修道時乃至所有語業身業養命事轉。亦得名為正語業命。

**Translation (line 284):** "Through the dominant force of this discipline beloved of noble ones, during the path of cultivation, all activities of speech, bodily action, and livelihood that arise are also called right speech, right action, and right livelihood."

49. **[OK]** 養命事轉 — "livelihood that arise" — 養命 = livelihood/sustaining life; 事轉 = activities that arise/operate. Accurate.

### Line 118: Right effort

**Chinese:** 依止正見及正思惟正語業命勤修行者。所有一切欲勤精進出離勇猛勢力發起策勵其心。相續無間名正精進。

**Translation (line 288):** "Right effort is the desire, diligence, renunciative valor, powerful exertion, and continuous urging of the mind that arise in one who cultivates diligently on the basis of right view, right thought, right speech, right action, and right livelihood."

50. **[OK]** 依止正見及正思惟正語業命 — "on the basis of right view, right thought, right speech, right action, and right livelihood" — the Chinese lists 正見, 正思惟, 正語業命 (the latter being a compressed form for right speech, right action, right livelihood). Correctly expanded in English.

51. **[OK]** 欲勤精進出離勇猛勢力發起策勵其心 — "the desire, diligence, renunciative valor, powerful exertion, and continuous urging of the mind" — this is a string of near-synonymous effort terms. 欲 = desire; 勤精進 = diligent effort; 出離 = renunciative/going forth; 勇猛 = valor/courage; 勢力 = power/force; 發起 = arising/initiating; 策勵其心 = urging the mind. The translation captures the main terms. **Accurate.**

52. **[OK]** 相續無間 — "continuous" — the Chinese emphasizes both continuity (相續) and no-gap (無間). The translation word "continuous" absorbs both. Adequate.

### Line 118 continued: Right mindfulness and right samadhi

**Chinese:** 成就如是正精進者。由四念住增上力故。得無顛倒九種行相所攝正念能攝九種行相心住。是名正念及與正定。

**Translation (line 290):** "Having achieved this right effort, one attains through the dominant force of the four foundations of mindfulness the non-inverted right mindfulness comprised within the nine aspects, which settles the mind in these same nine aspects. 53. **[OK]** 能攝九種行相心住 — "which settles the mind in these same nine aspects" — 能攝 = able to gather/settle; 心住 = mental abiding. Accurate.

54. **[OK]** Both right mindfulness and right samadhi named together as in the Chinese.

### Line 120: Two categories and path distinction

**Chinese:** 如是一切八支聖道。總立二種。謂無所作及住所作。無所作者。謂正語正業正命。住所作者。復有二種。謂奢摩他毘鉢舍那。正見正思惟正精進是毘鉢舍那。正念正定是奢摩他。

**Translation (line 294):**

55. **[OK]** 無所作 — "non-active" — accurate rendering. 住所作 — "active" — accurate. The Chinese 住所作 literally means "residing-in-action" or "dwelling-and-acting."

56. **[OK]** Shamatha/vipashyana subdivision of the "active" category correctly rendered.

**Chinese:** 如是清淨正語業命為所依止。於時時間修習止觀。能斷諸結無餘永斷。能得最上阿羅漢果。長時相續名為修道。多時串習斷煩惱故。率爾智生名為見道。暫時智起即能永斷諸煩惱故。由是因緣。正語業命於修道中方始建立。

**Translation (line 296):**

57. **[Critical — Mistranslation]** 長時相續名為修道。多時串習斷煩惱故 — The Chinese says the path of cultivation is named so because it is "of long duration and continuity" (長時相續) and because "through extended habituation over much time" (多時串習) it cuts off afflictions. The translation renders: "What unfolds through sustained practice over long duration is the path of cultivation — because extended cultivation cuts off afflictions." This is **accurate in meaning**.

58. **[Critical — Mistranslation]** 率爾智生名為見道。暫時智起即能永斷諸煩惱故 — The Chinese says: "When wisdom arises suddenly/instantly (率爾智生), it is called the path of seeing. Because momentary/brief wisdom arising (暫時智起) can permanently sever afflictions." The translation renders: "What arises in a single flash of wisdom is the path of seeing — because momentary wisdom permanently severs afflictions." **Accurate.** The key term 率爾 (suddenly, in a flash) is correctly captured as "a single flash."

**Re-evaluation:** Upon close re-reading, both renderings in #57 and #58 are actually accurate. Downgrading from "Critical" to **OK**. The contrast between 長時/多時 (long time, extended practice) for bhavana-marga and 率爾/暫時 (sudden, momentary) for darshana-marga is correctly preserved.

59. **[OK]** 由是因緣。正語業命於修道中方始建立 — "For this reason, right speech, right action, and right livelihood are first established within the path of cultivation." Accurate. 方始 = "only then / first" — the sense is that these three are *primarily* established in the path of cultivation (as opposed to path of seeing). Correct.

60. **[OK]** 能斷諸結無餘永斷 — "cut off all fetters (*samyojana*) permanently and without remainder" — accurate. 結 correctly glossed as samyojana with footnote reference.

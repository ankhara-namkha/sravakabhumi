## Section 31.1: Dependent Origination (緣起觀)

### Findings

**S-1. [Minor / Possible Mistranslation] "no self-nature" — ambiguous rendering of 亦無自性**

Chinese: 此中都無自在作者生者化者能造諸法。亦無自性。**士夫中間能轉變者轉變諸法。**

The sentence structure in the Chinese reads: "There is no sovereign creator, progenitor, or transformer who produces dharmas. **Nor is there a self-nature** — no intermediary person who transforms dharmas."

The English splits this into: "There is no self-nature. No intermediary person transforms dharmas from within."

The phrase 亦無自性 is syntactically connected to the next clause about 士夫中間能轉變者 — it seems to mean "there is also no inherent nature [such that] a person within [the aggregates] who transforms could transform dharmas." The English rendering as a standalone sentence "There is no self-nature" is acceptable but slightly severs the logical connection. This is minor — the meaning is preserved overall.

**Severity: minor**

---

**S-2. [Minor / Structural] "as broadly explained" abbreviation for 如是廣說如前**

Chinese 黑品所攝。廣說如前: The English says "belonging to the dark category, as described above."
Chinese 白品所攝。廣說如前: The English says "belonging to the bright category" (with no back-reference).

The Chinese has 廣說如前 for the bright category as well, meaning "as broadly explained above." The English omits the back-reference for the bright category. The omission of the back-reference phrase from the second instance is reasonable compression (avoiding repetition), but since both Chinese occurrences have 廣說如前, noting for completeness.

**Severity: minor (acceptable compression)**

---

**S-3. [Minor / Omission] 生支想所攝 — "subsumed under the rubric of the 'birth' group"**

Chinese: 為令當來**生支想所攝**識名色六處觸受次第生故

English: "So that consciousness, name-and-form, the six sense-bases, contact, and feeling may arise in their proper sequence in a future life"

The Chinese phrase 生支想所攝 means specifically "subsumed under the rubric/designation of the birth-group links" (i.e., consciousness through feeling as they manifest in future existence are categorized under the birth branch). The English rendering "in a future life" captures the temporal sense but omits the specific technical designation of these as being 生支 (birth-branch) links — this is a Yogacara technical distinction between the same links in their seed-state (under the drawing cause) vs. their manifest-state (under the birth-group). This nuance is partly conveyed by the footnote but is absent from the body text.

**Severity: minor (technical nuance lost)**

**S-4. [Moderate / Omission] Reasoning section — three reasonings collapsed**

Chinese: 復審思擇於是緣性緣起觀中。善修善習善多修習能斷愚癡。又審思擇如是道理有至教量有內現證有比度法。亦有成立法性等義。如是名依**作用道理**。**證成道理**。**法爾道理**。尋思緣起所有道理。

The Chinese names three distinct reasonings after the first (dependence): function (作用道理), proof (證成道理), and nature-of-things (法爾道理). The English separates these into three distinct sub-paragraphs, which is correct. However, the Chinese text for "reasoning from proof" includes THREE evidentiary bases: 至教量 (scriptural authority), 內現證 (inner direct realization), and 比度法 (inferential reasoning). The English renders these as "scripture, by direct inner realization, and by inference" — this is correct.

But the Chinese also includes 亦有成立法性等義 as part of 證成道理 — "there is also [reasoning establishing] the nature of dharmas and such." The English assigns this phrase to "reasoning from the nature of things" as a separate paragraph: "The established nature of dharmas likewise confirms it." In the Chinese, 成立法性等義 appears to belong to the proof-reasoning clause, with 法爾道理 being invoked separately without its own content phrase.

The translation splits the material so that 證成道理 gets scripture/realization/inference, and 法爾道理 gets "established nature of dharmas." This is a defensible reading, but the Chinese syntax is ambiguous. Some commentators read 成立法性等義 as belonging to 法爾道理 rather than 證成道理. The translation's reading is reasonable.

**Severity: minor (ambiguous syntax, defensible choice)**

## Section 31.2: Element Differentiation (界差別觀)

### Findings

**S-7. [Minor / Omission] Water and fire characteristics omitted from own-characteristic**

Chinese: 又正尋思地為堅相。**乃至**風為輕動相。

The Chinese uses 乃至 ("up to and including"), which is an abbreviation marker — the full series is earth=solidity, water=moisture, fire=heat, wind=lightness-and-movement. The English gives: "earth is solidity; water is moisture; fire is heat; wind is lightness and movement" — this is actually MORE explicit than the Chinese, which abbreviates with 乃至. The English expands the abbreviation, which is good practice.

Wait — re-reading: the Chinese says 地為堅相。乃至風為輕動相 — only earth and wind are named, with water and fire implied by 乃至. The English explicitly names all four plus consciousness and space. This is an addition in the sense that the English makes explicit what the Chinese abbreviates, but it is a correct and helpful expansion. The consciousness and space characteristics are explicitly in the Chinese: 識為了別相。空界為虛空相遍滿色相無障礙相.

However, the English renders the space element as "characterized by pervasive openness, filling the interstices of form and offering no obstruction." The Chinese says 虛空相遍滿色相無障礙相 — three characteristics: (1) emptiness/openness, (2) pervading form (filling the interstices of material form), (3) non-obstruction. The English captures all three.

Actually wait — let me re-read: 遍滿色相 should be "pervading and filling form" — the English "filling the interstices of form" is interpretive but defensible. 遍滿 means "pervading and filling," and 色相 means "the aspect of form/material." "Pervasive openness" captures 虛空相; "filling the interstices of form" captures 遍滿色相; "offering no obstruction" captures 無障礙相.

**Severity: No issue (correct expansion of Chinese abbreviation)**

**S-8. [Minor / Omission] 高慢 vs. "pride"**

Chinese: 發起高慢便為顛倒 — "gives rise to hauteur/arrogance, which is inversion"
English: "one generates pride"

高慢 specifically means "arrogance" or "haughty pride" — a specific form of mana (慢). "Pride" is acceptable as a general rendering and matches how 憍慢 is used elsewhere. The term 高慢 (literally "high-pride") is slightly different from 憍慢 (arrogance/conceit) used in section 31.2's reasoning section. The English uses "pride" for both. This is a minor inconsistency but not a mistranslation.

**Severity: minor**

**S-9. [OK / Verified] House simile**

Chinese: 如草木等眾緣和合圍遶虛空。數名為舍。如是六界為所依故。筋骨血肉眾緣和合圍遶虛空。假想等想施設言論。數名為身。

Complete and accurate. The Chinese 數名為 means "conventionally designated as" — correctly rendered. The additional clause 假想等想施設言論 (by conceptual imputation and conventional designation) is captured by "by conventional designation."

**No issue.**

## Section 31.3: Mindfulness of Breathing (阿那波那念)

### Findings

Chinese: 能正了知於入出息所緣境界。繫心了達無忘明記是阿那波那念義。

English: "binding the mind to the object of the in-and-out breaths, with clear knowledge, non-forgetfulness, and lucid recollection — this is the meaning of anapanasati."

繫心 = binding the mind; 了達 = clear knowledge/penetrating understanding; 無忘 = non-forgetfulness; 明記 = lucid recollection. All four terms present. Correct.

**No issue.**

**S-13. [Minor / Omission] 繫屬身故外處攝故 — two distinct reasons**

Chinese: 入息出息在內可得。繫屬身故**外處攝故**內外差別。

This gives two reasons: (1) "belonging to the body" (繫屬身故) and (2) "classified under the external sense-base" (外處攝故). Together they create the internal-external distinction.

English: "in-breaths and out-breaths are internally obtained, since they belong to the body; yet they are classified under the external sense-base."

The English correctly captures both reasons. The "yet" effectively conveys the internal-external paradox.

**No issue.**

**S-14. [OK / Verified] Own-characteristic — two types of in-breath and out-breath**

Chinese: 入息有二出息有二。若風入內名為入息若風出外名為出息。

English: "there are two types of in-breath and two types of out-breath: wind entering inward is the in-breath; wind passing outward is the out-breath."

Accurate. The Chinese says "in-breath has two, out-breath has two" — referring to long and short varieties of each. The English correctly identifies these and then specifies the directional definition.

**No issue.**

**S-15. [OK / Verified] Common characteristic — life-faculty**

有識身 literally means "body endowed with consciousness" — rendered as "sentient body." This is acceptable. 命根 = life-faculty, correctly glossed with Sanskrit.

**No issue.**

**S-17. [Minor / Nuance] 不應思議。不應分別。唯應信解**

English: "This should not be further analyzed or elaborated upon, but met with faith and understanding."

不應思議 = "should not be thought about or investigated"; 不應分別 = "should not be discriminated/analyzed"; 唯應信解 = "should only be met with faith and understanding."

The English "should not be further analyzed or elaborated upon" compresses the two negative injunctions into one phrase. This is acceptable compression. "Met with faith and understanding" for 信解 is good.

**Severity: minor (acceptable compression)**

## Section 31.4: Shamatha and Vipashyana in Mutual Support

### Findings

**S-18. [OK / Verified] Main passage**

Chinese: 如是依止淨行所緣。尋思六事差別觀已。數數於內令心寂靜。數數復於如所尋思。以勝觀行審諦伺察。彼由奢摩他為依止故。令毘鉢舍那速得清淨。復由毘鉢舍那為依止故。令奢摩他增長廣大。

English: "Having investigated the purifying-conduct objects through the six aspects in this way, one repeatedly stills the mind internally, and repeatedly investigates what has been discerned through the superior observation of vipashyana. Complete and accurate. 淨行所緣 = "purifying-conduct objects" (the five contemplations: unpleasantness, loving-kindness, dependent origination, element differentiation, mindfulness of breathing). 數數 appears twice — "repeatedly... repeatedly" — correctly doubled. The mutual-support logic is precisely rendered.

**No issue.**

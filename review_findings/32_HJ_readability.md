## Issue 1 — Nested Qualification / Translated-ese (32.8, line 185)

**Category:** (1) Translated-ese, (3) Nested Qualification

**Passage:**
> "...then expand: to two, three, four, five, ten, twenty, thirty, forty, fifty, a hundred, a thousand, and onward until the livid fill every cardinal and intermediate direction. Generate a limitless, all-pervading, gapless field of imaginative attention, leaving not even a space the size of a staff-tip unfilled."

**Problem:** The numerical cascade is mechanical — the sentence reads as a dutiful transcription of the list rather than a living description of practice. The practitioner is not helped to feel what this expansion is like. The series "two, three, four, five, ten, twenty..." also breaks register abruptly — it goes from precise (five) to coarse (ten, twenty) in a way that signals rote list-copying rather than progressive meditative experience.

**Suggested rewrite:**
> "...then expand — two, three, five, ten, a hundred, a thousand — until the livid fill every direction and quarter. Let no gap remain, not even a space the width of a staff-tip; imaginative attention becomes limitless, all-pervading, without seam."

The revision still conveys the graduation and the completeness criterion, but the compressed list (dropping the four/twenty/thirty/forty/fifty sequence) reads as natural enumeration rather than inventory. The final clause is restructured to follow the mental act ("becomes") rather than stacking noun phrases.

## Issue 2 — Formula Monotony (32.8, lines 185–195)

**Category:** (2) Formula Monotony

**Pattern:** "As with the livid, so too through each stage to the skeleton" appears three times in close succession:
- Line 185: "As with the livid, so too through each stage to the skeleton."
- Line 193: "As with the livid, so with each stage through to the skeleton..."
- Line 195: (implied by "starting from the final limitless livid corpses through to the final limitless skeletons")

Three occurrences of essentially the same compression formula in nine lines is too many. This is a structural issue — the passage must compress the asubha sequence multiple times, but the same phrasing every time reads as mechanical repetition.

**Suggested approach:**
Keep the first occurrence as written. Vary the second:
> "Apply this same principle through every stage — from the festering through to the skeleton — and at every level of expansion."

For the third (line 195), the "starting from the final limitless livid corpses through to the final limitless skeletons" phrasing is already varied by its context, so it can stand. The fix is targeted at the second occurrence only.

## Issue 3 — >50-Word Sentence (32.9, line 221)

**Category:** (5) >50-word sentence

**Passage:**
> "In this way, because you have well apprehended the sign of disenchantment, the sign of delight, the sign of shamatha, the sign of vipashyana, and the sign of luminosity — alternating from time to time between inner stillness and the investigation of dharmas through sign-following vipashyana — through the force of this correct practice of impurity, you gradually enter the foundations of mindfulness."

**Word count:** ~65 words. The sentence has two layered prepositional phrases ("because you have..." and "through the force of...") and a parenthetical ("alternating from time to time...") that together bury the main verb. The reader must hold three qualifying clauses in memory before reaching "you gradually enter."

**Suggested rewrite:**
> "In this way — having well apprehended all five signs and alternating between inner stillness and sign-following vipashyana — you gradually enter the foundations of mindfulness through the force of this correct practice with impurity."

This reduces to ~35 words and places the main clause where it belongs: at the close, not buried. The five signs are referenced collectively rather than listed (the full list was just given two lines earlier, so reciting it here adds no information and slows the sentence).

## Issue 4 — Translated-ese / Register Drift (32.10, line 241)

**Category:** (1) Translated-ese, (4) Register drift

**Passage:**
> "After this, well apprehend the signs of impurity both internal and external to your own body, making the mind clear. Likewise apprehend the internal and external impurity of others' bodies. Regarding your own beloved body, generate imaginative attention as follows: after death, carried out to the charnel ground, discarded on the earth — reaching the livid stage, the festering stage, and so forth through to the skeleton."

**Problem:** "Making the mind clear" is a stray phrase hanging loosely off the prior clause — it reads like a clause boundary was lost in translation. "Likewise apprehend the internal and external impurity of others' bodies" is a complete sentence syntactically but barely a sentence rhetorically — it is the most compressed and affectless line in the section. Most significantly, "Regarding your own beloved body, generate imaginative attention as follows" is a clunky transition; "as follows" promises an elaboration, but the elaboration is three participial phrases rather than a full clause.

**Suggested rewrite:**
> "After this, apprehend clearly the signs of impurity both in your own body and in others' bodies. Then turn to your own beloved body — now imagine it: after death, carried out to the charnel ground and left on the earth, passing through the livid, the festering, and so forth through to the skeleton. Having generated this imaginative attention repeatedly, settle the mind in inner stillness. This is called contemplating the body within the internal-and-external body."

The rewrite keeps all content, removes the hanging "making the mind clear," tightens the transition into the self-corpse visualization (the most striking moment in the passage), and restores the closing formula naturally.

## Issue 5 — Nested Qualification (32.10, line 247)

**Category:** (3) Nested Qualification

**Passage:**
> "The four formless aggregates that arose in connection with those disturbances have passed away; yet now, through loss of mindfulness, similar states may arise again. Regarding these, apply proper attention: all such dharmas are by nature the work of deception and illusion — temporarily present, abruptly appearing, filled with dangers, impermanent, unreliable."

**Problem:** The list of five qualities at the end ("temporarily present, abruptly appearing, filled with dangers, impermanent, unreliable") is appended as an em-dash clause after the predicate "are by nature the work of deception and illusion." This is not technically nested qualification — it is a catalog tacked on after the main clause. The problem is register: the practitioner is just told these mental states are "the work of deception and illusion" (a striking phrase), and then this insight is immediately diluted by a five-item enumeration that feels like a list from a doctrinal taxonomy. The momentum of the instruction collapses at exactly the point it should be sharpest.

**Suggested rewrite:**
> "Regarding these, apply proper attention: all such dharmas are the work of deception and illusion. They flash into being and vanish — temporarily present, filled with dangers, unreliable. See them for what they are."

The revision cuts "abruptly appearing" (subsumed by "flash into being"), folds the enumeration into a shorter appositive that retains the essential qualities, and adds a single direct instruction ("See them for what they are") that restores the practice register. The practitioner now receives something they can use, not a category list.

## Detailed Findings

### Finding 1 — CRITICAL: "bone-chain" vs. "the skeleton" (glossary mismatch)

**Locations:**
- 32.5, line 117: "go to the charnel ground and apprehend the sign of the blue-black corpse, and so on through to the bone-chain."
- 32.7, line 171: "such as the signs of impurity you previously apprehended. Apply the sign-following mode of vipashyana: contemplate the blue-black corpse, the festering, and so on through to the bones and the bone-chain."

**Glossary entry:**
> `骨鎖 | asthi-samkalika | the skeleton | the skeleton | Connected bones; also 骨 (bones) and 鎖 (sinew-connected)`

**Issue:** The glossary mandates "the skeleton" as the rendering of 骨鎖 (asthi-samkalika). The translation uses "bone-chain" consistently. This is a divergence from the glossary.

**Context check:** Fascicle 30 (lines 400–406) also uses "bone-chain" extensively and distinguishes it from "linked skeleton." This suggests either (a) the glossary entry is outdated and was superseded by the more precise terminology developed during fascicle 30, or (b) the fascicle 30 translation introduced "bone-chain" incorrectly without updating the glossary.

**Fascicle 26** uses "the skeleton" and "sinew-connected bones," consistent with the glossary.

**Recommendation:** This requires editorial resolution. Either:
- Update the glossary entry 骨鎖 → "bone-chain" and propagate to fascicle 26 (where "the skeleton" is used), OR
- Correct fascicles 30 and 32 to use "the skeleton" per current glossary.

The fascicle 30 footnote (line 402) offers a terminological rationale for "bone-chain" as distinct from "linked skeleton." If that distinction reflects the Chinese, the glossary should be updated. This is a doctrinal/source-fidelity question that should be flagged for Agent S.

### Finding 2 — Minor: "physical and mental pliancy and moistness" — "moistness" not in glossary

**Location:** 32.7, line 163: "the mind settled in shamatha — generating physical and mental pliancy and moistness."

**Issue:** "Moistness" (潤澤 / 滋潤) is used as a distinct quality paired with pliancy. The glossary records:
- `輕安 | prashrabdhi | pliancy, ease | pliancy`
- `滋潤心作意 | — | attention that moistens the mind | moistening attention`

But "moistness" as a standalone technical term (separate from the compound attention-type) is not separately glossed. It appears only here in the translation corpus. It may correspond to 潤澤 or 滋 in the Chinese source. This term should be verified against source and added to the glossary if it is a technical term.

---

### Finding 3 — Minor: "yoga teacher" — rendering not in glossary

**Location:** 32.7, line 162: "The **wise man** represents the yoga teacher."

**Issue:** The glossary entry for 瑜伽師 specifies preferred rendering "yogin." The footnote at 32.1 (line 33) renders it as "yoga teacher" with the Chinese gloss 善通達修瑜伽師 provided. The running text of 32.7 uses "yoga teacher" — an unlisted alternate rendering.

**Assessment:** Within the simile decoding, "yoga teacher" is a natural contextual gloss (the wise man guiding another through a crowd = the teacher guiding the student). The footnote at 32.1 establishes the interpretive frame. However, the glossary should acknowledge "yoga teacher" as an acceptable contextual variant for 瑜伽師, or else the text should be harmonized to "yogin / practitioner."

**Recommendation:** Minor; does not need immediate correction if the footnote context covers it. Consider adding a glossary note for this contextual variant.

### Finding 6 — Observation: First-use gloss conventions — mostly compliant

**Shamatha:** First introduced with gloss "(calm abiding)" at 32.4. In 32.5–32.7 it appears without gloss — **correct** (within-fascicle subsequent use).

**Vipashyana:** First introduced with gloss "(insight)" at 32.4. In 32.7 it appears without gloss — **correct** (within-fascicle subsequent use).

**Secondary afflictions:** First introduced in 32.4. In 32.5 and 32.7 used without gloss — **correct**.

**Sign of luminosity:** First introduced by name in 32.1 and defined in 32.4. Used in 32.5 section title and body — **correct**.

**Sign of danger:** First introduced in 32.4. Used in 32.7 without gloss — **correct**.

**Four foundations of mindfulness:** Used at 32.7 (line 155) without Sanskrit gloss. The glossary entry is: `念住 | smrityupasthana | foundation of mindfulness`. First use in fascicle 32 is at 32.7. The compound "four foundations of mindfulness" (四念住) is a well-known technical term but this appears to be its first occurrence in the fascicle. By per-fascicle convention, a gloss "(smrityupasthana)" or "(four smrityupasthanas)" should appear on first use.

**Recommendation:** Add gloss to "four foundations of mindfulness" at line 155: "four foundations of mindfulness (*smrityupasthana*)" — Minor.

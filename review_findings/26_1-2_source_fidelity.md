## Section 26.1: Opening Questions and Summary Verse

### 26.1-S01 | MODERATE | Opening question phrase — "問於如前所舉所開示出離地中" rendering

**Chinese:** 問於如前所舉所開示出離地中。
**English:** "Question: In the ground of renunciation (naishkramya) that was previously presented and elucidated:"

**Issue:** The Chinese uses two distinct verbs: 所舉 (what was raised/presented) and 所開示 (what was elucidated/disclosed). The English collapses these into a single compound "previously presented and elucidated," which is acceptable. However, "Question:" is rendered as **"Question:"** — the Chinese has 問 which simply means "asked" or is a discourse marker introducing the question. The translation uses "Question:" as a bold label, which is a reasonable structural choice, but it introduces a formatting element not in the source.

**Assessment:** The doublet "presented and elucidated" correctly represents 所舉所開示. The "Question:" label is a structural interpretation rather than a translation of 問 as it functions in Chinese prose. This is a minor structural choice.

**Severity: MINOR**

### 26.2-S03 | MODERATE | Type 7 (尋思增上) — "煩惱" absent in Chinese for this type

**Chinese:** 云何尋思增上補特伽羅。謂有補特伽羅。先餘生中**於其尋思**。已修已習已多修習。
**English:** "It is a person who in previous lives has cultivated, habituated, and repeatedly cultivated **discursive thought**."

**Issue:** For types 3–6, the Chinese consistently says "於X**煩惱**" (e.g., 於貪煩惱, 於瞋煩惱, 於癡煩惱, 於慢煩惱) — i.e., "the **affliction** of X." For type 7 (discursive thought), the Chinese says only "於其尋思" — it does NOT call discursive thought an affliction (煩惱). The English for type 7 correctly omits "affliction" — no issue here. But this finding is noted for completeness: the translation accurately reflects the Chinese distinction that types 3–6 explicitly name the klesha, while type 7 does not.

**Severity: NO ISSUE** (translation correct; noted for doctrinal awareness)

The addition of "even" is an interpretive emphasis, not a distortion.

### 26.2-S22 | CRITICAL | Type 25 (時解脫) — Detailed analysis of the complex definition

**Chinese full text:** 謂有補特伽羅。鈍根種姓於諸世間現法樂住容有退失。或思自害或守解脫。勵力勤修不放逸行。謂防退失增上力故。或唯安住自分善品。或經彼彼日夜剎那瞚息須臾勵力勝進。乃至未證最極猛利。是名時解脫補特伽羅。

**English full text:** "It is a person of dull-faculty lineage who, with respect to the pleasant abidings in the present life, is liable to regress. They may contemplate harming themselves, or [alternatively] guard their liberation, diligently cultivating the practice of non-negligence — that is, by the force of preventing regression, they either merely maintain their present level of wholesome qualities, or through each day, night, moment, eye-blink, and instant, strive and progress, yet have not realized the most extremely fierce [attainment]."

**Issue found:** The Chinese presents 或思自害或守解脫 as two alternatives: either (1) contemplate harming themselves, OR (2) guard their liberation. These are two possible responses to the danger of regression. The English renders this as "They may contemplate harming themselves, or [alternatively] guard their liberation" — the bracket "[alternatively]" is an addition, but it correctly captures the 或...或... structure. However, there is a subtlety: the Chinese then continues 勵力勤修不放逸行 which could apply only to the "guard liberation" alternative, not to the "contemplate self-harm" alternative. The English uses a dash "— that is, by the force of preventing regression..." which runs together both alternatives with the subsequent elaboration, potentially obscuring that the detailed practice description pertains specifically to the "guard liberation" path.

**Assessment:** This is a complex passage. The structural connection in the Chinese is: [either they contemplate self-harm] or [they guard their liberation — which involves diligently cultivating non-negligence, i.e., either maintaining current level or striving to progress, but not yet reaching the most fierce attainment]. The English rendering captures all clauses but the dash structure slightly obscures the scope. The content is all present.

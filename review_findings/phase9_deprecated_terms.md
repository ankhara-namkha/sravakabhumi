## Genuine Violations

### 16. "concentration" used as standalone rendering for samadhi

The watchlist prohibits "concentration" as a rendering for standalone 三摩地 (samadhi). The following instances use "concentration" as a standalone term in running text where "samadhi" is the correct rendering:

**fascicle_28_en.md:37**
> "Because of three functions: establishing concentration, providing the basis for knowledge, and accomplishing the purpose."

Context: This is a section heading-level summary of why the three trainings are related. "Establishing concentration" is likely a rendering of 心定 or 三摩地, where "samadhi" should be used. The full phrase "establishing samadhi" is natural and correct.

**fascicle_28_en.md:39**
> "**(1) Establishing concentration** refers to the superior training in ethical discipline. Why? Because through ethical discipline one establishes mental one-pointedness (*ekagrata*), enabling the mind to attain samadhi (meditative concentration)."

Note: The sentence actually uses "samadhi" correctly at the end of this line. However, the bold section label "Establishing concentration" itself uses "concentration" as a standalone rendering. Should be "Establishing samadhi."

**fascicle_28_en.md:490, 492, 494, 496, 524, 546, 600**
> Multiple instances of "the faculty of concentration," "the power of concentration," "the concentration factor of awakening," "right concentration," "concentration and non-concentration," "the ease, concentration, and equanimity factors," "prajna and mindfulness holding concentration."

Assessment: These are NOT violations. "Right concentration" (正定), "concentration factor of awakening" (定覺支), "faculty of concentration" (定根), "power of concentration" (定力) are standard English renderings for these compounds in the Bodhi/NTC tradition. These are composite terms where "concentration" is a constituent of a recognized compound, not a standalone rendering for samadhi. "Right concentration" specifically is the standard rendering for 正定 in the eightfold path — replacing it with "right samadhi" would be a terminology deviation without precedent in the reference library.

**fascicle_30_en.md:184**
> "the mind gladdened and sustained in concentration"

Context: This is describing a practitioner abiding with their mind "sustained in concentration." This is genuinely ambiguous — it could render 三摩地 (samadhi) or 心一境性 (mental one-pointedness). If the Chinese is 三摩地 or 定, "samadhi" should be used. **Requires source verification.**

**fascicle_31_en.md:207**
> "never resting content with a small measure of tranquil concentration and losing heart midway"

Context: "Tranquil concentration" as a compound phrase. If the Chinese is 定/三摩地, this should read "tranquil samadhi." **Requires source verification.**

**Confirmed standalone violations requiring fix (pending source check): fascicle_28 line 37 and 39 ("Establishing concentration" label), fascicle_30 line 184, fascicle_31 line 207.**

### 14. "absorption" / "absorptions" used for 靜慮/dhyana

The watchlist specifies: "first use: 'the four dhyanas (meditative absorptions)'; thereafter: dhyanas." Using "absorption" or "absorptions" as primary rendering (without "dhyanas" present) is a violation.

**fascicle_26_en.md:127**
> "a non-returner who, after being born upward into the first dhyana (meditative absorption), abides there..."

Assessment: "First dhyana (meditative absorption)" — this is the correct first-use pattern per the watchlist. NOT a violation.

**fascicle_27_en.md:438**
> "Furthermore, to bring about the attainment of the **dhyana absorptions**, one first teaches the **initial dhyana absorption** and afterward teaches the **remaining dhyana absorptions**."

Assessment: "dhyana absorptions" and "dhyana absorption" — these use "absorption" as a paired gloss alongside "dhyana," not as a standalone replacement. The watchlist specifies "thereafter: dhyanas" (i.e., without "meditative absorptions" parenthetical). Using "dhyana absorption" instead of just "dhyana" is a borderline violation — it re-introduces the gloss where the watchlist says to drop it. However, it does keep "dhyana" as the primary term. **Minor violation**: the first-use gloss is being used beyond first use. Should read "...attainment of the dhyanas, one first teaches the initial dhyana and afterward teaches the remaining dhyanas."

**fascicle_28_en.md:27**
> "entering and fully abiding in the first dhyana (meditative absorption) with its joy and ease born of separation"

Assessment: "First dhyana (meditative absorption)" — same first-use pattern. If fascicle 26 already established this first use, this re-use of the gloss in fascicle 28 is unnecessary repetition. However, each fascicle may be treated as having its own first-use. **Minor issue, not a hard violation.**

**fascicle_28_en.md:29**
> "whatever other meditative attainments and absorptions (*samapatti*) there may be"

Assessment: "absorptions (*samapatti*)" — this is NOT a rendering for dhyana/靜慮. This correctly uses "absorptions" for samapatti (meditative attainments), which is a legitimate use of the term for a different referent. NOT a violation.

**fascicle_29_en.md:203**
> "right samadhi within the four dhyanas (meditative absorptions)"

Assessment: Another re-use of the first-use gloss pattern. Same issue as fascicle 28 line 27 — the parenthetical "(meditative absorptions)" should have been dropped after the true first use. **Minor recurring violation.**

**fascicle_33_en.md:25** (verse)
> "Two absorptions and five superknowledges,"

Assessment: This refers to the two absorptions without mind (二無心定 — the attainment of non-perception and cessation attainment), NOT to the four dhyanas. "Absorptions" for mindless attainments/samapatti is appropriate. NOT a violation.

**fascicle_33_en.md:342, 344, 346, 354**
> "two absorptions without mind," "these two absorptions," "one enters the absorption"

Assessment: All refer to the two mindless attainments (samapatti), not dhyanas. NOT violations.

**Confirmed violations for "absorptions" misuse:**
- fascicle_27_en.md:438 — "dhyana absorptions" / "dhyana absorption" (3 occurrences) — should drop to "dhyanas" / "dhyana"
- fascicle_29_en.md:203 — "four dhyanas (meditative absorptions)" — unnecessary re-use of first-use gloss

### 7. "jhana" (Pali) in footnote

**fascicle_33_en.md:204**
> "[^second_dhyana_coarse]: ...The Visuddhimagga (IV.88-94) provides a parallel analysis of how *vitakka* and *vicara* must be abandoned for the second *jhana*."

Assessment: This is in a footnote citing a Pali text (the Visuddhimagga), and uses *jhana* as a Pali term in that context. The watchlist says "jhana (Pali form) → dhyana." Strictly, even in footnotes, the convention is to use Sanskrit forms. However, when citing a Pali source and discussing its Pali-specific terminology (vitakka, vicara, jhana), the use of Pali forms has scholarly justification — the reader needs to know this is a Pali term they would find in the Visuddhimagga. This is borderline. **Low-priority — could note as "Pali term used in Pali-citation footnote."**

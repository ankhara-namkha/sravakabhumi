## Detailed Flags

### FLAG 1: Terms used but NOT in glossary

**1a. "moral conduct (*shila-samvara*)" — section 30.7**
- Sanskrit given correctly as *shila-samvara*, but the compound 戒律儀 is not a standalone glossary entry.
- Glossary has "shila" (戒, line 28) and "samadhi-sambhara" lists shila as a component (line 493), but *shila-samvara* as a compound is absent.
- Recommend: Add entry `| 戒律儀 | shila-samvara | discipline of moral conduct | shila-samvara |` to glossary.

**1b. "repeated mindfulness" — section 30.9**
- Key technical term in Asanga's samadhi definition. No Sanskrit given, no glossary entry.
- Chinese: 重念 or equivalent; Sanskrit not identified in running text.
- Recommend: Research Chinese term, add to glossary, add first-use Sanskrit gloss if available.

**1c. "homogeneous object-field" — section 30.9**
- Key technical term in samadhi definition. No Sanskrit given, no glossary entry.
- Recommend: Research Chinese source term, add glossary entry with Sanskrit if available.

**1d. "four modes of wisdom-practice" — section 30.9**
- Technical term for vipashyana class of mental one-pointedness. No Sanskrit given, no glossary entry.
- Glossary has "superior wisdom-dharma / adhiprajna-dharma" (line 175) which may relate, but the "four modes" as a category is not entered.
- Recommend: Identify Chinese source term, add glossary entry.

### FLAG 2: Rendered differently from glossary

**2a. "one-pointed mind" vs. "mental one-pointedness" — section 30.6 heading**
- Section 30.6 overview uses "One-pointed mind" as the heading; section 30.9 heading uses "One-Pointed Mind."
- Watchlist requires "one-pointed mind / mental one-pointedness" (both permitted).
- In the body of 30.9, "wholesome mental one-pointedness" is used — correct.
- Section heading "One-Pointed Mind" is acceptable per watchlist; no fix required. But note inconsistency: 30.6 heading uses "one-pointed mind" and 30.9 heading uses "One-Pointed Mind" — capitalization is the same (title case), so this is consistent. No issue.

**2b. "attention of the preparatory stage" — section 30.8**
- Text: "the attention of the preparatory stage"
- Glossary has "preparatory practice" (prayoga, line 90) and various "attention of X" compounds (manaskara entries).
- The phrase "attention of the preparatory stage" is a compound not found in the glossary. The component terms are present (manaskara/attention; prayoga/preparatory), but the combined form should be checked against the Chinese and cross-referenced with the seven-attentions framework.
- Moderate concern — this may be a coined English phrase where a glossary-standardized form should be used.

**2c. "samadhi (*samadhi*)" — section 30.9**
- Text: "this is called samadhi (*samadhi*)"
- Tautological formatting: the English term and Sanskrit term are identical. The first-use convention is "samadhi (meditative concentration)" per glossary line 23.
- This appears to be a deliberate self-referential definition rather than a gloss, but it deviates from first-use convention. Since samadhi was already introduced in section 30.7, the parenthetical is unnecessary here. Minor issue.

### FLAG 5: Missing first-use gloss

**5a. "shramana adornments" — section 30.7**
- Used without any Sanskrit parenthetical: "all the shramana adornments."
- Glossary: 沙門莊嚴 | shramana-alamkara | shramana adornments (line 499).
- First use in these sections: should read "all the shramana adornments (*shramana-alamkara*)."

**5b. "counter-mindfulness" — section 30.8**
- Used as a technical term without Sanskrit or Chinese source citation.
- Glossary entry 497: 背念 | — | counter-mindfulness (no Sanskrit equivalent found).
- Since no Sanskrit exists, Chinese source could be noted: "counter-mindfulness (背念)."
- Minor issue given absent Sanskrit, but a translator's note would aid scholarly readers.

**5c. "nine stages of mental abiding" — section 30.9**
- Used without Sanskrit: "Mental one-pointedness within the nine stages of mental abiding belongs to the shamatha class."
- Glossary: 九種心住 | nava-akara-citta-sthiti | nine stages of mental abiding (line 173).
- Should read: "the nine stages of mental abiding (*nava-akara-citta-sthiti*)."
- Moderate issue — this is one of the central technical frameworks of the Sravakabhumi.

## Findings by Category

### (1) Omissions — Chinese content missing from English

**O-1 (Minor).** 33.10, "離有二種" — Chinese: 一者相應離。二者境界離 (1. separation by disassociation, 2. separation from objects). English: "separation by disassociation — being freed from the mental afflictions themselves — and separation from objects — no longer being bound to the external things that occasion desire." The translation renders 相應離 as "separation by disassociation" with the explanatory gloss, and 境界離 as "separation from objects" with a gloss. This is acceptable but adds interpretive glossing not present in the Chinese. The original just says "associated separation" (相應離) and "object-realm separation" (境界離) without further explanation. **Severity: minor.** The meaning is conveyed; the glosses are accurate though additive.

**O-2 (Minor).** 33.11 — Chinese: 依順次數此為第二。如是一切如前應知。 The English has: "In sequential order this is the second. The rest — 'equipped' and 'dwelling' — should be understood as before." The addition of "'equipped' and 'dwelling'" is an interpretive clarification not in the Chinese. The Chinese just says 如是一切如前應知 ("all of this should be understood as before"). **Severity: minor.** Helpful clarification.

**O-3 (Minor).** 33.12, Chinese: 令心踊躍俱行喜受便得除滅 — "the elation (踊躍) accompanied by joy-feeling is eliminated." English: "the elation and joy-accompanied feeling are eliminated." The English renders 踊躍 as "elation" and treats 俱行喜受 as "joy-accompanied feeling." The Chinese reads: 令心踊躍 (cause the mind to leap/surge) 俱行喜受便得除滅 (the co-occurring joy-feeling is thereby eliminated). The English may slightly misparse here — 踊躍 is not something eliminated but rather the state of the mind during cultivation. The English reads as if both "elation" and "joy-accompanied feeling" are eliminated, but the Chinese says: through extensive cultivation that causes the mind to surge/leap, the joy-accompanied feeling is eliminated. **Severity: minor to moderate — potential misparse of sentence structure.**

**O-4 (None — false alarm on review).** Verified that 離喜寂靜最極寂靜 is rendered as "Separated from joy — still, supremely still." Correct.

**O-5 (Minor).** 33.14, sphere of infinite space: Chinese 由不顯現故及厭離欲故皆能超越 — "because they no longer appear and because one has turned away from them in weariness, one can transcend them." The Chinese has two reasons: (1) 不顯現 (not appearing/manifesting) and (2) 厭離欲 (wearied detachment/turning away from desire). English renders 厭離欲 as "turned away from them in weariness" — this conflates 厭 (wearied/disgusted) and 離欲 (separating from desire). More precisely: "because they cease to manifest and because of wearied detachment from desire for them." **Severity: minor.** The rendering captures the general sense.

**O-6 (Minor).** 33.14, room/space simile: Chinese 當知此中由奢摩他相安住上捨勤修加行 — "one should understand that the practice proceeds through the shamatha aspect, abiding in upper equanimity and diligently cultivating." The English merges this smoothly. The Chinese emphasizes 由奢摩他相 (by the shamatha aspect) more pointedly as the instrumental means. **Severity: minor.**

### (3) Mistranslations — Meaning errors

**M-1 (Moderate).** 33.12, Chinese: 令心踊躍俱行喜受便得除滅. As noted in O-3 above, the English reads "the elation and joy-accompanied feeling are eliminated." A more faithful reading: "Through extensive cultivation, the mind's surging (踊躍) together with joy-feeling (俱行喜受) are thereby eliminated" — i.e., the excited quality of mind (踊躍) and the joy-feeling that co-occurs are both removed. Alternatively, 令心踊躍 could be read as a positive phase: cultivation makes the mind surge/exert, and through that the co-occurring joy-feeling is eliminated. The ambiguity is real, but the English phrasing "the elation and joy-accompanied feeling are eliminated" reads as two separate things being eliminated, which is one valid reading. **Severity: moderate — an ambiguous passage where the English captures one defensible reading.**

**M-2 (Minor).** 33.12, Chinese: 與喜相違心受生起 — "a mental feeling contrary to joy arises" (English). Chinese: 與喜相違心受 = "a mental feeling (心受) contrary to (相違) joy (喜)." English is accurate. No issue.

**M-3 (Minor).** 33.15, Attainment of non-perception prerequisite: Chinese: 已離遍淨貪未離廣果貪 — "freed from desire for the third dhyana (subhakrtsna, universal purity) but not yet freed from desire for the fourth dhyana (brhatphala, vast fruit)." The Chinese says 遍淨 (universal purity = third dhyana top heaven) and 廣果 (vast fruit = fourth dhyana heaven). The English accurately says "third dhyana" and "fourth dhyana" and provides the correct Sanskrit. However, strictly, 遍淨 is the name of the third dhyana heaven (Subhakrtsna), and the Chinese means "freed from desire for the Subhakrtsna level" — the English says "third dhyana" which is slightly broader but functionally equivalent. **Severity: minor.** The parenthetical Sanskrit compensates.

**M-4 (Minor).** 33.15, Chinese: 出離想作意為先 — "this attention of departing from perception takes the lead." The Chinese 出離想作意 literally means "attention of departing from perception" (出離 = departing/transcending, 想 = perception, 作意 = attention). The English rendering is faithful. No issue on review.

**M-5 (Minor).** 33.15, Cessation attainment: Chinese: 或阿羅漢求暫住想作意為先 — "For an arhat, it requires the aspiration to abide temporarily in quiescence." Chinese: 求暫住想作意為先 = "with the attention of seeking temporary abiding taking the lead." The English renders 想 as part of "quiescence" but 想 here is part of 求暫住想作意 = "attention that seeks temporary abiding." The English shifts "temporarily" to modify "abide" rather than keeping the full Chinese construction. **Severity: minor.** The meaning is preserved.

**M-6 (Minor).** 33.16, perception (9): Chinese says 謂面恒顰蹙語音謇澁言常變色 for anger. English: "perpetual frowning, harsh and halting speech, and frequent changes of color." Chinese 語音謇澁 = "voice harsh and halting" and 言常變色 = "countenance (色) constantly changing." The English "frequent changes of color" is ambiguous in English — could mean facial color (which is the intended meaning: 變色 = changing color/complexion, i.e., flushing/paling with anger). A reader might misunderstand. **Severity: minor.** The rendering is technically accurate but could be clearer.

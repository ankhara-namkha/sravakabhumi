## Flag 1 — NOT IN GLOSSARY

### anuttara-samyak-sambodhi
**Location:** "the vehicle of supreme perfect awakening (*anuttara-samyak-sambodhi*)" (line 114)
**Status:** Sanskrit compound appears in the translation but has NO entry in MASTER_GLOSSARY.md.
**Issue:** The term is introduced with an English gloss ("supreme perfect awakening") and the Sanskrit parenthetical, which is good practice. However, the glossary has no entry for this compound at all — neither for the full Sanskrit form nor for the Chinese 阿耨多羅三藐三菩提 (anuttara-samyak-sambodhi).
**Action required:** Add glossary entry for 阿耨多羅三藐三菩提 | anuttara-samyak-sambodhi | supreme perfect awakening / unsurpassed complete enlightenment | anuttara-samyak-sambodhi.

## Flag 2 — RENDERED DIFFERENTLY FROM GLOSSARY

### "great vehicle" vs. "mahayana"
**Location:** "the great vehicle" (line 114, 118, 126)
**Glossary standard:** Entry 69 — 大乘 | mahayana | mahayana / great vehicle | **Preferred in text: mahayana**
**Usage in 30.5:** The text uses "the great vehicle" in running text (lines 114, 118, 126), not "mahayana." Three occurrences.
**Verdict:** The glossary's "Preferred in text" column specifies "mahayana." The translation is inconsistent with that preference. This is a deviation, though "great vehicle" is listed as an acceptable rendering. The first use at line 114 is in a parallel list with "shravaka vehicle" and "pratyekabuddha vehicle" — the structural parallelism arguably favors "great vehicle" there. However, the standing preference should be honored, or the glossary note updated.
**Action required:** Either (a) change to "mahayana vehicle" / "mahayana" per glossary preference, or (b) add a note to the glossary authorizing "great vehicle" as the preferred rendering in vehicle-list contexts.

### "yoga teacher" vs. "yogin"
**Location:** "yoga teacher" (lines 102, 126, 148) and "a yoga teacher accomplished in practice" (line 148)
**Glossary standard:** Entry 98 — 瑜伽師 | yogin / yogacara | **yogin**, practitioner | **Preferred: yogin**
**Usage in 30.5:** The text consistently uses "yoga teacher" rather than "yogin" or "yogin / yoga teacher."
**Verdict:** The gloss "yoga teacher" is a reasonable expansion given the context (the teacher–student relationship), but "yogin" is the glossary's preferred form. Other fascicles use "yogin" for 瑜伽師. This is a deviation that should be standardized.
**Action required:** Change "yoga teacher" to "yogin" or "the yogin (yoga teacher)" on first use, then "yogin" thereafter. If the translator intends "yoga teacher" for pedagogical clarity in this context, the glossary should be updated with a contextual note.

## Flag 5 — MISSING FIRST-USE GLOSS

### paracitta-jnana
**Location:** "knowledge of others' minds (*paracitta-jnana*)" (line 110 and line 148)
**Glossary standard:** Entry 492 — 知他心差別智 | paracitta-jnana | **knowledge of others' minds** | knowledge of others' minds
**Status:** The translation introduces the Sanskrit parenthetically (line 110: "knowledge of others' minds (*paracitta-jnana*)"). This is technically correct as a first-use gloss pattern. However, the glossary note says it is "one of the superknowledges," and the term is used without any indication that this is a supernormal faculty (an abhijna). The reader is not told whether this is an ordinary skill or a supernormal attainment.
**Verdict:** Not a missing gloss per se, but a missing explanatory context. Line 148 elaborates ("a yoga teacher accomplished in practice has attained the knowledge"), which partially addresses this. Minor.
**Action required:** Consider adding a translator's note on first use at line 110 explaining that paracitta-jnana is a superknowledge (abhijna), not ordinary perceptiveness.

## Minor Observations (no glossary violation, but worth noting)

### "middling faculties" — no glossary entry for middle/middling grade
**Location:** "those with middling faculties" (line 128)
**Glossary entries:** 288 (dull faculties / mrdvindriya), 289 (sharp faculties / tikshneindriya). No entry for middling/middle faculties (madhyendriya or equivalent).
**Verdict:** The middle grade is implicit in the dull/sharp contrast but lacks an explicit glossary entry. Not a violation — the text uses "middling" as the natural middle term — but the glossary is incomplete on the three-grade faculty schema.
**Action required:** Low priority. Consider adding entry for middling faculties (中根 / madhyendriya) to complete the set.

### "disposition" for carita vs. "conduct-type"
**Location:** "disposition (*carita*)" (lines 106, 118); "each of the five conduct types" (line 130); the individual compound terms use "conduct" (raga-carita = greed-conduct, dvesha-carita = aversion-conduct, etc.)
**Glossary standard:** Entry 81 — 種姓 | gotra | lineage, family, **disposition** | lineage. The carita entries (186–194, 235, 290–294) use "conduct" as part of the compound rendering.
**Usage in 30.5:** The text uses both "disposition" (when introducing carita abstractly in the domain list) and "conduct" (in the specific compound terms like greed-conduct). This is actually consistent with glossary usage — 種姓/gotra takes "disposition" and carita compounds take "conduct." No violation.
**Verdict:** Correct as rendered. No action needed.

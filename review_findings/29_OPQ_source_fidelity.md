## 29.15 Eight Types of Persons (T30.0447b13)

### Sentence-by-Sentence Analysis

**Chinese (line 186):** 補特伽羅。略有八種建立因緣。略有四種。
**English (line 442):** "Persons are of eight types, established through four kinds of differentiating causes."
**Assessment:** The Chinese says persons are briefly (*lue you*) of eight types, and the establishing causes are briefly of four types. The English merges this into a single sentence. The word 略 (briefly/in summary) is dropped in both instances. **Severity: m (minor).** The compression is stylistically defensible; no content is lost.

**Chinese (line 186):** 云何八種補特伽羅。一有堪能者。二無堪能者。三善知方便者。四不善知方便者。五有無間修者。六無無間修者。七已串修習者。八未串修習者。
**English (line 446):** "What are the eight types of persons? (1) Those with capacity and (2) those without capacity. (3) Those skilled in methods and (4) those unskilled in methods. (5) Those who practice without interruption and (6) those who do not. (7) Those with extensive training and (8) those without extensive training."
**Assessment:** All eight types present. The Q&A structure (云何...謂...) is preserved. The renderings are reasonable:
- 堪能 → "capacity" — acceptable
- 善知方便 → "skilled in methods" — acceptable
- 無間修 → "practice without interruption" — acceptable
- 串修習 → "extensive training" — acceptable; 串 literally means "habituated/practiced repeatedly over time," and "extensive training" captures the duration sense. **No issues.**

**Chinese (line 188):** 云何四種補特伽羅建立因緣。謂由四種差別因緣。建立八種補特伽羅。一由根差別故。有根已成熟及根未成熟。二由瑜伽差別故。有善知瑜伽及不善知瑜伽。三由加行差別故。有有無間慇重修及無無間慇重修。四由時差別故。有已長時修道及未長時修道。
**English (lines 448-452):** Fully rendered with all four differentiating causes and their corresponding pairs.
**Assessment:** Accurate. The term 慇重修 (earnest/diligent practice) is captured in "continuously and earnestly." 加行 is rendered as "practice intensity" — the Chinese literally says "preparatory practice" or "applied effort," but "practice intensity" conveys the sense in this context. **No issues.**

**Chinese (line 190):** 云何如是四種差別能為前八補特伽羅建立因緣。謂根已成就即有堪能者。根未成就即無堪能者。善知瑜伽即善知方便者。不善知瑜伽即不善知方便者。有無間慇重修即有無間修者。此亦名為有常委修。無無間慇重修即無無間修者。此亦名為無常委修。已長時修道即已串修習者。未長時修道即未串修習者。
**English (line 452):** All eight mappings are given in the combined paragraph. The alternate names 有常委修/無常委修 are rendered as "Continuous and earnest practice is also called 'constant devoted practice.'"
**Finding 1 — Partial omission of alternate term:** The Chinese gives two alternate names: 有常委修 ("having constant devoted practice") AND 無常委修 ("lacking constant devoted practice"). The English only renders one: "Continuous and earnest practice is also called 'constant devoted practice.'" The negative counterpart is omitted.
**Severity: m (minor).** The pattern is symmetrical and the reader can infer it, but completeness requires both.

**Chinese (line 192):** 如是名為由根差別。瑜伽差別。加行差別及時差別。建立八種補特伽羅。
**English (line 454):** "Thus, through differences in faculties, yoga knowledge, practice intensity, and duration, the eight types of persons are established."
**Assessment:** Faithful summary. **No issues.**

**Chinese (line 194):** Five conditional statements about the interaction of the four factors.
**English (lines 458-466):** All five conditional scenarios are present and accurately rendered.
**Assessment:** The five scenarios match:
1. Faculties unmatured → nothing works (Chinese: 皆不能辨) — rendered correctly.
2. Faculties matured + no method knowledge → cannot accomplish — correct.
3. Faculties matured + method + no continuous practice → cannot attain swift penetrating wisdom (速疾通慧) — correct.
4. Faculties matured + method + continuous practice + no extensive training → own tasks unfinished (自所作事未得成辨) — correct.
5. All four present → accomplishes everything + swift wisdom + tasks complete — correct.
**No issues.**

## 29.16 Four Maras and Mara's Activities (T30.0447c15)

### Sentence-by-Sentence Analysis

**Chinese (line 196):** 當知諸魔略有四種。魔所作事有無量種。勤修觀行諸瑜伽師應善遍知當正遠離。
**English (line 474):** "One should know that the maras (mara) are of four types, while mara's activities are beyond counting. Yogins who diligently cultivate contemplative practice should thoroughly understand them and resolutely keep their distance."
**Assessment:** Faithful. 觀行 = "contemplative practice" is acceptable. **No issues.**

**Chinese (line 196):** 云何四魔。一蘊魔。二煩惱魔。三死魔。四天魔。
**English (line 478):** "(1) The skandha-mara (skandha-mara), (2) the affliction-mara (klesha-mara), (3) the death-mara (mrtyu-mara), and (4) the celestial mara (devaputra-mara)."
**Finding 2 — Redundant Sanskrit for skandha-mara:** The English gives "The skandha-mara (*skandha-mara*)" — the italicized Sanskrit is identical to the non-italicized term, creating redundancy. Compare "the affliction-mara (*klesha-mara*)" where the Sanskrit adds information. This is a formatting inconsistency rather than a source fidelity issue.
**Severity: m (minor).**

**Chinese (line 198):** 蘊魔者。謂五取蘊。
**English (line 480):** "The skandha-mara is the five appropriating skandhas themselves."
**Assessment:** Accurate. 取蘊 = "appropriating skandhas" (upadana-skandha). **No issues.**

**Chinese (line 198):** 煩惱魔者。謂三界中一切煩惱。
**English (line 480):** "The affliction-mara encompasses all afflictions throughout the three realms."
**Assessment:** Accurate. **No issues.**

**Chinese (line 198):** 死魔者。謂彼彼有情從彼彼有情眾殀喪殞歿。
**English (line 480):** "The death-mara is the premature perishing and passing away of beings among their various kinds."
**Assessment:** 殀喪殞歿 is a four-character compound meaning "premature death, passing away, perishing" — rendered as "premature perishing and passing away." 彼彼有情從彼彼有情眾 = "those various beings from among those various classes of beings" — the English simplifies to "beings among their various kinds." This is an acceptable compression. **No issues.**

**Chinese (line 198):** 天魔者。謂於勤修勝善品者求欲超越蘊煩惱死三種魔時。有生欲界最上天子得大自在。為作障礙發起種種擾亂事業。是名天魔。
**English (line 480):** "The celestial mara appears when those who diligently cultivate superior wholesome qualities seek to transcend the three maras of skandhas, afflictions, and death: a celestial being born at the highest level of the desire realm, having attained great power, creates obstacles and sets in motion every manner of disturbance."
**Assessment:** Accurate and complete. 天子 = "celestial being" (devaputra); 最上 = "highest level"; 得大自在 = "having attained great power." **No issues.**

**Chinese (line 200):** 當知此中若死所依。若能令死。若正是死。若於其死作障礙事不令超越。依此四種建立四魔。
**English (lines 484):** "These four maras can be understood through their relation to death: what death depends upon, what causes death, death itself, and what prevents one from transcending death."
**Assessment:** Faithful rendering of the fourfold framework. **No issues.**

**Chinese (line 200):** 謂依已生已入現在五取蘊故方有其死。
**English (line 486):** "Death depends upon the five appropriating skandhas already arisen and presently existing — without them, there can be no death."
**Assessment:** Accurate. The added clause "without them, there can be no death" is an explicating addition — the Chinese says 方有其死 ("only then is there death"), which implies the same. **No issues.** The addition is a reasonable gloss of 方.

**Chinese (line 200):** 由煩惱故感當來生生。已便有殀喪殞歿。
**English (line 486):** "Afflictions generate future rebirth, and once reborn, premature perishing follows."
**Assessment:** 感當來生 = "impel/generate future rebirth"; 生已便有殀喪殞歿 = "once born, there is then premature perishing." Accurate. **No issues.**

**Chinese (line 200):** 諸有情類命根盡滅殀喪殞歿。是死自性。
**English (line 486):** "The exhaustion and cessation of the life-faculty — premature perishing — is the nature of death itself."
**Finding 3 — Minor omission:** The Chinese says 諸有情類 ("of all classes of sentient beings") before "the life-faculty exhausts and ceases." The English drops this qualifier, going directly to "the exhaustion and cessation of the life-faculty."
**Severity: m (minor).** The context makes it clear this applies to all beings.

**Chinese (line 200):** 勤修善者為超死故正加行時。彼天子魔得大自在能為障礙。由障礙故或於死法令不能出。或經多時極大艱難方能超越。
**English (line 486):** "And when those cultivating goodness strive to transcend death, the celestial mara wields great power to obstruct them. Because of these obstacles, one either cannot escape the law of death at all, or transcends it only after great difficulty and a very long time."
**Assessment:** Complete and accurate. **No issues.**

**Chinese (line 202):** 又魔於彼或有暫時不得自在。謂世間道離欲異生。或在此間或生於彼。
**English (line 490):** "Over ordinary beings who have separated from desire through the worldly path — whether dwelling in this realm or reborn in higher ones — mara temporarily loses command."
**Assessment:** 異生 = "ordinary beings" (prthag-jana). 或在此間或生於彼 = "whether remaining here or born there." Accurate. **No issues.**

**Chinese (line 202):** 或魔於彼得大自在。謂未離欲。若未離欲在魔手中隨欲所作。
**English (line 490):** "But over those who have not yet separated from desire, mara holds full sway: they remain in mara's hands, subject to his will."
**Assessment:** 在魔手中隨欲所作 = "in mara's hands, subject to [mara's] desire to act upon them." Accurate and vivid. **No issues.**

**Chinese (line 202):** 若世間道而離欲者。魔縛所縛未脫魔罥。由必還來生此界故。
**English (line 492):** "Even those who have separated from desire through the worldly path remain bound by mara's bonds and have not escaped mara's net — for they will inevitably return and be reborn in this realm."
**Assessment:** 魔縛所縛 = "bound by mara's bonds"; 未脫魔罥 = "not escaped mara's net/snare." Both rendered. The reason 由必還來生此界故 = "because they will inevitably return to be born in this realm" is correctly given. **No issues.**

**Chinese (line 204):** 云何魔事。(Long passage listing numerous mara activities.)
**English (lines 496-514):** Extensive rendering of all mara activities.

**Detailed mara-activity comparison:**

1. **善法欲生/耽著諸欲/退捨** (desire for wholesome dharmas → attached to pleasures → retreats): Line 498 renders fully. Correct.

2. **密護根門/可愛色聲香味觸法/執取相執取隨好** (guarding sense gates / pleasant sense objects / grasping at signs): Line 500 renders fully. Correct.

3. **於食知量/美味不平等食/貪愛欲** (food moderation / delicious + immoderate food / craving): Line 502 renders fully. Correct.

4. **初夜後夜覺寤瑜伽/睡眠樂偃臥樂脇臥樂/懈怠力** (wakefulness practice / pleasure of sleep, lying down, side-lying / laziness): Line 504 renders fully. Correct.

5. **正知而住/往來等諸事業** (full awareness / daily activities) — the long compound at line 204:
   - 幼少盛年美色諸母邑等 → "youthful, attractive persons" — Line 506.
   **Finding 4 — Minor undertranslation:** The Chinese specifically says 母邑 (matrgrama, "womenfolk/females"), indicating the gender-specific nature of this temptation. The English renders "youthful, attractive persons," which is gender-neutral. The source text is specifically about encountering attractive women as a monastic temptation.
   **Severity: m (minor).** The meaning is partially conveyed but the specific cultural/monastic context is softened.

   - 世間諸妙好事 → "worldly wonders" — correct.
   - 多事多所作中 → "multitude of tasks and activities" — correct.
   - 在家及出家眾歡娛雜處 → "householders and monastics gathered in entertainment" — correct.
   - 惡友共相雜住 → "bad companions dwelling together" — correct.
   - 便生隨喜心樂趣入 → "one generates approving delight and the mind pursues them" — correct.

6. **佛法僧苦集滅道此世他世若生疑惑** (doubt about Buddha/Dharma/Sangha/Four Truths/this world/next world): Line 508. Correct.

7. **阿練若樹下塚間空閑靜室** (forest, trees, tombs, secluded rooms) + terrifying apparitions + false shramana/brahmin/human/non-human figures urging abandonment of white dharmas: Lines 510. All elements present. Correct.
   **Finding 5 — Minor addition/explicitation:** Chinese says 欻爾而來 ("suddenly appear"). English says "appear suddenly" — this matches. The English adds "against all reason" for 不如正理, which is actually present in the Chinese (不如正理勸捨白品勸取黑品 = "not in accordance with right principle, urge abandoning the wholesome, urge adopting the unwholesome"). On rereading, the English correctly renders this. **No issue.**

8. **利養恭敬稱譽** (material support, respect, praise) + **慳吝廣大希欲不知喜足忿恨覆惱及矯詐等** (miserliness, great desires, discontentment, anger, resentment, concealment, vexation, deception, flattery, etc.) + 沙門莊嚴所對治法 (qualities opposed to shramana adornments): Lines 512. All elements present.
   **Finding 6 — Minor rendering note:** 矯詐 is rendered as "deception, flattery." The Chinese compound 矯詐 more precisely means "deception/pretense" as a single quality. The next character 等 ("etc.") indicates there are further items. The English separates into "deception, flattery" which may be reading 矯 and 詐 as two separate items rather than one compound. However, the Abhidharma tradition does list shathya (deception) and maya (flattery/pretense) as separate klesha, so this rendering is defensible.
   **Severity: m (minor).**

9. **如是等類無量無邊諸魔事業。一切皆是四魔所作。隨其所應當正了知。** (conclusion)
   **English (line 514):** "Thus, these and countless other varieties of mara's activities are all the work of the four maras. One should correctly understand each according to its nature."
   **Assessment:** Accurate. 隨其所應 = "according to what is appropriate [to each]" → "according to its nature" is a reasonable rendering. **No issues.**

## 29.17 Three Causes of Fruitless Practice (T30.0448b04)

**Chinese (line 206):** 由三因緣正修行者。精勤發趣空無有果。何等為三。一由諸根未積集故。二由教授不隨順故。三由等持力微劣故。
**English (lines 522-524):**
"Through three causes, one who practices correctly and strives diligently may yet produce no fruit whatsoever. What are the three?
(1) One's faculties have not been sufficiently developed. (2) The instructions one has received are unsuitable. (3) One's power of samadhi is weak."
**Assessment:** All three causes present. Renderings:
- 諸根未積集 → "faculties have not been sufficiently developed" — 積集 means "accumulated/gathered"; "sufficiently developed" is an acceptable interpretation. **No issues.**
- 教授不隨順 → "instructions one has received are unsuitable" — 不隨順 = "not in accord/not suitable." Correct.
- 等持力微劣 → "power of samadhi is weak" — 等持 (samadhi); 微劣 = "feeble/weak." Correct.

**Chinese (line 208):** Four conditional permutations.
**English (line 526):** Three negative conditions and one positive.
**Finding 7 — Structural compression:** The Chinese gives four separate permutations:
1. Faculties undeveloped + suitable instructions + strong samadhi → fruitless
2. Faculties developed + strong samadhi + unsuitable instructions → fruitless
3. Faculties developed + suitable instructions + weak samadhi → fruitless
4. All three present → definite fruit

The English (line 526) renders all four permutations accurately in a compressed paragraph. Checking each:
- Permutation 1: "Even with suitable instructions and strong samadhi, if the faculties remain undeveloped..." — Correct.
- Permutation 2: "Even with developed faculties and strong samadhi, without suitable instructions..." — Correct.
- Permutation 3: "Even with developed faculties and suitable instructions, if the power of samadhi is weak..." — Correct.
- Permutation 4 (line 528): "Only when all three converge..." — Correct.
**No issues.** All four permutations are present and accurately rendered.

**Chinese (line 210):** 如是名為由三因緣空無有果。由三因緣決定有果。
**English (line 530):** "Thus, through three deficiencies practice is fruitless, and through three sufficiencies it bears definite fruit."
**Assessment:** Accurate summary. The English adds "deficiencies" and "sufficiencies" as interpretive glosses for 由三因緣 in negative and positive contexts respectively. This is a reasonable explicitation. **No issues.**

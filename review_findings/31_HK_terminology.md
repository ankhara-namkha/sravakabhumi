### CRIT-1: "Yogachara practitioners" — Wrong Form in Running Text (Line 249)

**Location:** Section 31.8, opening sentence.
**Text:** "How does one purify obstacles? Yogachara practitioners who properly cultivate the approaches..."
**Glossary entry (line 98):** `| 瑜伽師 | yogin / yogacara | yogin, practitioner | yogin |`
**Preferred in text:** "yogin"
**Watchlist:** `the terminology watchlist` explicitly states the issue — "yogachara" in running text is the wrong form; "yogin" is the preferred in-text rendering.

This is the sole instance in sections 31.8–31.11. Elsewhere in the file (e.g., line 153 section 31.5, line 340 section 31.11), the term is correctly rendered as "yogin." This single use of "Yogachara practitioners" is an inconsistency and violates the glossary's preferred-in-text column.

**Required fix:** Replace "Yogachara practitioners" with "yogins" (or "practitioners" alone).

## MODERATE Issues

### MOD-1: Sanskrit Spelling Inconsistency — ashraya-paravrtti vs. ashraya-paravritti (Lines 279, 291, 568 in glossary)

**Location:** Section 31.9, lines 279 and 291.
**Text uses:** `*ashraya-paravrtti*` (two t's, no i before final i)
**Glossary line 166 uses:** `ashraya-paravritti` (three i's: paravritti)
**Glossary line 568 uses:** `ashraya-paravrtti` (matching the translation text)

The glossary itself is internally inconsistent on this Sanskrit form — line 166 reads "ashraya-paravritti" while line 568 reads "ashraya-paravrtti." The translation uses "ashraya-paravrtti" (matching line 568). The canonically correct Sanskrit is *āśraya-parāvṛtti* — the transliterated form without double-i ("paravrtti") is more standard. However, this is a glossary-internal consistency problem that requires a glossary fix (picking one form), not just a translation fix.

**Required fix:** Standardize glossary to a single spelling; translation should match whichever is chosen. As both line 166 and 568 cover the same Chinese (轉依), one entry should be removed or merged.

### MOD-2: "Conceptual elaboration" — Not in Glossary for This Specific Context (Lines 173, 221, 322)

**Location:** Sections 31.5 (line 173), 31.7 (line 221), and 31.11 (line 322).
**Text:** "free from conceptual elaboration," "signless, non-conceptual serenity."
**Glossary:** 戲論 = "idle elaboration." The term "conceptual elaboration" appears in contexts referring to prapanca (戲論) but uses a different rendering from the glossary's established "idle elaboration."

However, "conceptual elaboration" may be rendering a different Chinese term (無戲論 vs. 戲論, or 分別 = conceptual discrimination) rather than 戲論 specifically. This requires source fidelity verification. If the Chinese is 戲論, all instances should read "idle elaboration." If it is 無分別 or 離分別, "non-conceptual" is appropriate and glossary-compliant.

**Required fix:** Source agent to verify Chinese; if 戲論, use "idle elaboration" consistently.

### MOD-3: "Body-aggregates" — Not in Glossary (Line 285)

**Location:** Section 31.9.
**Text:** "the perception of varied, multiple element-natures and differentiated body-aggregates predominates."
**Glossary:** No entry for "body-aggregates" as a compound. The term 蘊 (skandha) = "skandhas." The compound here likely represents a compound Chinese term describing the aggregated body elements in the context of element differentiation.

If the Chinese reads something like 衆蘊 (mass of aggregates) or 多界 (multiple elements), the glossary's preferred form for 蘊 is "skandhas" and for 界 is "elements." "Body-aggregates" is an unlisted compound.

**Required fix:** Verify source Chinese; if it is 蘊-based, render as "the skandhas of the body" or restructure. If "body-aggregates" is a defensible paraphrase, add it to the glossary as a note.

### MOD-4: "Companions in the holy life" — Glossary Renders 梵行 as "pure conduct" (Line 328)

**Location:** Section 31.11, recollecting the Three Jewels passage.
**Text:** "my companions in the holy life are those who possess discipline and virtue."
**Glossary entry (line 312):** `| 梵行 | brahmacarya | pure conduct, celibate conduct | pure conduct |`

"Holy life" (translating 梵行 as brahmacarya) is a Pali-register rendering common in Bhikkhu Bodhi's translations ("companions in the holy life" = *sabrahmacari*). The glossary's preferred rendering is "pure conduct." However, "companions in the holy life" is a well-established NTC-register phrase that may be acceptable for the social/monastic context rather than the technical meditation context.

This is a borderline issue: the phrase "companions in the holy life" is standard Buddhist English (Bodhi, Nanamoli) for *sabrahmacārin*, while the glossary targets the term 梵行 itself. If the Chinese source has 梵行侶 (companions in brahmacarya), the glossary form would suggest "companions in pure conduct." Source agent should verify.

**Required fix:** Verify source Chinese. If the rendering departs from glossary without source justification, adjust to "companions in the practice of pure conduct" or add a glossary note for the sabrahmacari compound.

## MINOR Issues

### MIN-1: "Direct perceptual wisdom" — Not in Glossary as a Set Phrase (Line 279)

**Location:** Section 31.9.
**Text:** "direct perceptual wisdom regarding the knowable objects arises."
**Glossary:** 現量 = *pratyaksha* = "direct perception" (line 167). "Wisdom" = *prajna*. The compound "direct perceptual wisdom" blends two glossary terms. This is likely a translation of 現量智 or 現觀智.

The rendering is reasonable but not explicitly in the glossary. Consider: "direct perceptual knowing" or "the wisdom of direct perception." Glossary should be updated if this compound appears in the source.

**Severity:** MINOR — rendering is defensible and internally consistent; no wrong term used.

### MIN-3: "Joy of abandonment" — Not in Glossary (Lines 320, 321)

**Location:** Section 31.11, opening of cultivation of attention.
**Text:** "one seeks mental one-pointedness and the joy of abandonment."
**Glossary:** No entry for "joy of abandonment." This likely translates 捨離之喜 or a similar compound. The glossary has 喜 = *mudita* = "sympathetic joy" (line 86) and 喜覺支 = "joy factor of awakening" (line 461), but neither fits "joy of abandonment."

If the Chinese is 遠離喜 or 出離喜 (joy of renunciation/seclusion), standard rendering would be "joy of seclusion" or "joy of renunciation" — both more precise than "abandonment." The Visuddhimagga (Nanamoli) uses "joy of renunciation."

**Severity:** MINOR — "joy of abandonment" may be an acceptable rendering, but the term is unlisted and possibly imprecise. Glossary should capture the specific Chinese form.

### MIN-4: "Afflictive outflows" — Compound Not Explicitly Glossarized (Lines 324, 334)

**Location:** Section 31.11.
**Text:** "generating disenchantment regarding afflictive outflows and the conditions that sustain them" / "conditions that sustain afflictive outflows."
**Glossary:** 漏 = *asrava* = "outflows" (lines 112, 232); 煩惱 = *klesha* = "afflictions." "Afflictive outflows" as a compound is not listed. The Chinese likely reads 煩惱漏 or 有漏.

The rendering "afflictive outflows" is coherent — it combines both glossary terms — but should be explicitly listed. Alternatively, just "outflows" (the standard form per glossary) may suffice if 煩惱 is not separately present in the source.

**Severity:** MINOR — no wrong term used; gap in glossary for the compound.

### MIN-5: "Defiling" Used as Adjective for Obstacles (Line 265)

**Location:** Section 31.8, closing paragraph.
**Text:** "one knows that obstacles by nature are obstructing, defiling, and belong to the dark category."
**Watchlist:** The watchlist prohibits "defilements" (noun) for 煩惱. Here "defiling" is used as an adjective describing obstacles (障), not as a rendering of 煩惱. The Chinese likely reads something like 染污性 (having a defiling nature) or 染著性.

This is a borderline issue. "Defiling" as an adjective for the nature of obstacles (not as a noun rendering of 煩惱) is permissible. However, the NTC preferred vocabulary tends toward "afflictive" rather than "defiling" even adjectivally. Consider: "obstructing and afflictive in nature."

**Severity:** MINOR — watchlist targets the noun use; adjectival use is borderline but worth flagging for consistency.

## Critical Findings (C) — Glossary Rendering Violated

### C1. Three mara names rendered as Sanskrit compounds rather than English descriptive forms

**Location:** Section 29.16, line 478; line 480; line 514

**Glossary specifies:**
| Chinese | Glossary English rendering | Preferred in text |
|---------|---------------------------|-------------------|
| 蘊魔 | mara of the skandhas | mara of the skandhas |
| 煩惱魔 | mara of afflictions | mara of afflictions |
| 死魔 | mara of death | mara of death |
| 天魔 | celestial mara | celestial mara |

**Actual in translation (line 478):**
> "(1) The skandha-mara (*skandha-mara*), (2) the affliction-mara (*klesha-mara*), (3) the death-mara (*mrtyu-mara*), and (4) the celestial mara (*devaputra-mara*)."

**Problem:** Three of four mara names use the Sanskrit compound (e.g., "skandha-mara") as the English-language rendering, with the Sanskrit then repeated parenthetically. This reverses the pattern: the glossary prescribes English descriptive forms ("mara of the skandhas," "mara of afflictions," "mara of death") as the in-text rendering, with Sanskrit in parentheses on first use only.

Only the fourth mara — "celestial mara" — matches the glossary.

**Recurs at line 480:**
> "The skandha-mara is the five appropriating skandhas..."
> (Should read: "The mara of the skandhas is the five appropriating skandhas...")

**Recurs at line 514:**
> "...all the work of the four maras" — this is fine; the issue is the named forms above.

**Required fix:** Change all three instances:
- "the skandha-mara (*skandha-mara*)" → "the mara of the skandhas (*skandha-mara*)"
- "the affliction-mara (*klesha-mara*)" → "the mara of afflictions (*klesha-mara*)"
- "the death-mara (*mrtyu-mara*)" → "the mara of death (*mrtyu-mara*)"
- At line 480: "The skandha-mara is" → "The mara of the skandhas is"

## Moderate Findings (M) — Terms Not in Glossary

**Location:** Section 29.15, lines 450, 452, 454

**Usage:**
> "They are differences in faculties, yoga knowledge, practice intensity, and duration."
> "*Yoga knowledge*: whether one knows the yoga skillfully or does not — distinguishing those skilled in methods from those unskilled."

**Problem:** This is a coined compound rendering for a Chinese technical term (likely 瑜伽善巧 or similar — exact Chinese not checked here, this is a T-agent flag for follow-up). No Sanskrit equivalent is given. No glossary entry exists. Because it appears as an italicized term with what appears to be technical status, it requires:
1. Identification of the underlying Chinese and Sanskrit
2. Glossary entry
3. First-use gloss pattern applied

**Recommendation:** Add to glossary once the Chinese is confirmed. If the Chinese is 善巧 (kaushala) in a yoga context, consider whether this aligns with existing entries for kaushala (skillful object, etc.) or needs a new entry.

### M2. "constant devoted practice" — not in glossary, no Sanskrit given

**Location:** Section 29.15, line 452

**Usage:**
> "Continuous and earnest practice is also called 'constant devoted practice.'"

**Problem:** This is presented as a synonymous technical designation for the third differentiating factor (practice intensity / 無間修). No Chinese or Sanskrit is given. It is placed in quotation marks as a technical term. If the Chinese source uses a specific term here (e.g., 殷重修 or 無間殷重修), it should be in the glossary. If it is the translator's paraphrase, the quotation marks are misleading.

**Recommendation:** Verify against Chinese source; if a specific term underlies it, add to glossary. If it is a translator's paraphrase, remove quotation marks or add bracketed note.

### M3. "life-faculty" — not in glossary as a standalone entry

**Location:** Section 29.16, line 486

**Usage:**
> "The exhaustion and cessation of the life-faculty — premature perishing — is the nature of death itself."

**Problem:** The glossary notes "life faculty" only parenthetically in the definition of 死魔 ("Death and the destruction of the life faculty"). There is no standalone glossary entry for 命根 (jivita-indriya / life faculty). This term appears substantively in the text and warrants a proper entry. The hyphenated form "life-faculty" in the translation is also inconsistent with how the glossary notes it (unhyphenated "life faculty").

**Recommendation:** Add glossary entry: 命根 | jivita-indriya | life faculty | life faculty. Standardize hyphenation: "life faculty" (no hyphen), consistent with the glossary notes style.

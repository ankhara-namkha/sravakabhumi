## Sentence-by-Sentence Comparison

### Section 30.13 [T30.0451b13]

**Chinese:** 云何四種毘鉢舍那。謂有苾芻依止內心奢摩他故。於諸法中能正思擇最極思擇。周遍尋思周遍伺察。是名四種毘鉢舍那。
**English (line 298):** "What are the four types of vipashyana? Relying on inner shamatha, a bhikshu engages in correct discernment, thorough discernment, comprehensive investigation, and comprehensive examination of all dharmas. These are the four types of vipashyana."
**Assessment:** Accurate. 依止內心奢摩他故 = "relying on inner shamatha" is correct.

**Chinese:** 云何名為能正思擇。謂於淨行所緣境界。或於善巧所緣境界。或於淨惑所緣境界。能正思擇盡所有性。
**English (line 300):** "What is correct discernment? Regarding purifying-conduct objects, skillful objects, or affliction-purifying objects, one correctly discerns the full extent of what exists (*yavad-bhavikata*)."
**Assessment:** Accurate. The three object types (淨行所緣, 善巧所緣, 淨惑所緣) are all present. 盡所有性 rendered as "full extent of what exists" with Sanskrit — correct.

**Chinese:** 云何名為最極思擇。謂即於彼所緣境界。最極思擇如所有性。
**English (line 302):** "What is thorough discernment? Regarding those same objects, one thoroughly discerns the nature of things as they are (*yathavad-bhavikata*)."
**Assessment:** Accurate. 如所有性 = "nature of things as they are" with Sanskrit.

**Chinese:** 云何名為周遍尋思。謂即於彼所緣境界。由慧俱行有分別作意。取彼相狀周遍尋思。
**English (line 304):** "What is comprehensive investigation? Regarding those same objects, through attention conjoined with wisdom and accompanied by conceptual thought, one grasps their characteristics and investigates them comprehensively."
**Assessment:** Accurate. 慧俱行有分別作意 = "attention conjoined with wisdom and accompanied by conceptual thought" captures both 慧俱行 (conjoined with wisdom) and 有分別 (with conceptual discrimination). 取彼相狀 = "grasps their characteristics."

**Chinese:** 云何名為周遍伺察。謂即於彼所緣境界。審諦推求周遍伺察。
**English (line 306):** "What is comprehensive examination? Regarding those same objects, one scrutinizes and probes them carefully, examining them thoroughly."
**Assessment:** Accurate. 審諦推求 = "scrutinizes and probes carefully."

### Section 30.14

**Chinese:** 又即如是毘鉢舍那。由三門六事差別所緣。當知復有多種差別。
**English (line 314):** "Furthermore, this vipashyana has additional distinctions through three doors and six aspects of its objects."
**Assessment:** Accurate. 當知復有多種差別 = "has additional distinctions" — slight compression of 當知 (should be known) but acceptable.

**Chinese:** 云何三門毘鉢舍那。一唯隨相行毘鉢舍那。二隨尋思行毘鉢舍那。三隨伺察行毘鉢舍那。
**English (lines 318):** "What are the three doors of vipashyana? (1) Vipashyana following appearances, (2) vipashyana following investigation, and (3) vipashyana following examination."
**Assessment:** Accurate.

**Chinese:** 云何名為唯隨相行毘鉢舍那。謂於所聞所受持法。或於教授教誡諸法。由等引地如理作意。暫爾思惟未思未量未推未察。如是名為唯隨相行毘鉢舍那。
**English (line 320):** "Vipashyana following appearances works as follows: regarding the teachings one has heard and retained, or the instructions and guidance received, one enters meditative equipoise with wise attention and briefly contemplates — not yet thinking through, evaluating, analyzing, or examining."
**Assessment:** Mostly accurate. 由等引地如理作意 = "through meditative equipoise (*samahita-bhumi*) with wise attention" — the English says "enters meditative equipoise with wise attention," which slightly shifts the meaning. The Chinese says "by means of wise attention in the equipoise ground" (由...如理作意), not "enters equipoise." This is a nuance issue, not a mistranslation. The four negated activities (未思未量未推未察) are accurately rendered as "not yet thinking through, evaluating, analyzing, or examining."

**FINDING 1 — Minor nuance (m):** 由等引地如理作意 — "by wise attention in the equipoise ground" is rendered as "enters meditative equipoise with wise attention." The Chinese does not say one "enters" equipoise at this point; it says the wise attention occurs *within* the equipoise ground. The distinction matters because the practitioner is already in equipoise (from shamatha) and now applies wise attention there.

**Chinese:** 若復於彼思量推察。爾時名為隨尋思行毘鉢舍那。
**English (line 322):** "When one then thinks through and analyzes the material, this becomes vipashyana following investigation."
**Assessment:** Accurate. 思量推察 compressed to "thinks through and analyzes" — acceptable.

**Chinese:** 若復於彼既推察已。如所安立復審觀察。如是名為隨伺察行毘鉢舍那。
**English (line 324):** "When, having analyzed, one reviews the established conclusions with careful, thorough scrutiny, this becomes vipashyana following examination."
**Assessment:** Accurate. 如所安立復審觀察 = "reviews the established conclusions with careful scrutiny." Good.

**Chinese:** 云何六事差別所緣毘鉢舍那。謂尋思時尋思六事。一義。二事。三相。四品。五時。六理。既尋思已復審伺察。
**English (line 330):** "What is vipashyana through the six aspects of its objects? During investigation, one investigates six things: (1) meaning, (2) things, (3) characteristics, (4) categories, (5) time, and (6) reasoning. Having investigated, one also examines them thoroughly."
**Assessment:** Accurate. 既尋思已復審伺察 = "having investigated, one also examines them thoroughly."

**Chinese:** 云何名為尋思於義。謂正尋思如是如是語有如是如是義。如是名為尋思於義。
**English (line 332):** "One investigates how such-and-such words bear such-and-such meanings."
**Assessment:** Accurate. 如是如是語有如是如是義 well captured.

**Chinese:** 云何名為尋思於事。謂正尋思內外二事。如是名為尋思於事。
**English (line 334):** "One investigates the two domains of things: internal and external."
**Assessment:** Accurate. 內外二事 = "two domains of things: internal and external."

**Chinese:** 云何名為尋思於相。謂正尋思諸法二相。一者自相。二者共相。如是名為尋思於相。
**English (line 336):** "One investigates the two characteristics of all dharmas: own-characteristic (svalakshana) and shared characteristic (samanya-lakshana)."
**Assessment:** Accurate with appropriate Sanskrit.

**Chinese:** 云何名為尋思於品。謂正尋思諸法二品。一者黑品。二者白品。尋思黑品過失過患。尋思白品功德勝利。如是名為尋思於品。
**English (line 338):** "One investigates the two categories of all dharmas: the black category and the white category — investigating the faults and dangers of the black, and the virtues and benefits of the white."
**Assessment:** Accurate. 過失過患 = "faults and dangers"; 功德勝利 = "virtues and benefits."

**Chinese:** 云何名為尋思於時。謂正尋思過去未來現在三時。尋思如是事曾在過去世。尋思如是事當在未來世。尋思如是事今在現在世。如是名為尋思於時。
**English (line 340):** "One investigates the three times — past, present, and future: 'This matter existed in the past'; 'This will exist in the future'; 'This exists in the present.'"
**Assessment:** Accurate. Note: Chinese order is 過去未來現在 (past-future-present), English reorders to past-present-future, but the illustrative sentences follow the Chinese order (past, future, present). This is acceptable as the reordering in the list header is natural English convention and the content is complete.

**Chinese:** 云何名為尋思於理。謂正尋思四種道理。一觀待道理。二作用道理。三證成道理。四法爾道理。
**English (line 342):** "One investigates four types of reasoning: ..."
**Assessment:** Accurate. All four listed.

**Chinese:** 當知此中由觀待道理尋思世俗以為世俗。尋思勝義以為勝義。尋思因緣以為因緣。
**English (line 344):** "One investigates the conventional as conventional, the ultimate as ultimate, and causes and conditions as causes and conditions."
**Assessment:** Accurate. 世俗 = conventional; 勝義 = ultimate; 因緣 = causes and conditions.

**Chinese:** 由作用道理尋思諸法所有作用。謂如是如是法有如是如是作用。
**English (line 346):** "One investigates the specific function of each dharma — how such-and-such dharmas have such-and-such functions."
**Assessment:** Accurate.

**Chinese:** 由證成道理尋思三量。一至教量。二比度量。三現證量。謂正尋思如是如是義。為有至教不。為現證可得不。為應比度不。
**English (line 348):** "One investigates three means of valid cognition: scriptural authority (agama-pramana), inference (anumana-pramana), and direct realization (pratyaksha-pramana). One asks: 'Is there scriptural authority for this meaning? Can it be directly realized? Can it be inferred?'"
**Assessment:** Accurate. All three means listed with Sanskrit. The interrogative form (為...不) faithfully rendered as questions. Note the Chinese order of the questions is 至教/現證/比度 and the English follows the same order — correct.

**FINDING 2 — Terminological note (m):** 三量 rendered as "three means of valid cognition" — this is accurate but the Chinese literally says "three measures/standards" (量). The rendering is defensible and consistent with Yogacara epistemological usage. No change needed.

**Chinese:** 由法爾道理。於如實諸法成立法性。難思法性安住法性應生信解不應思議不應分別。
**English (line 350):** "Regarding the established nature of dharmas as they truly are — their inconceivable nature, their abiding nature — one generates faith and understanding rather than attempting further analysis or conceptual elaboration."
**Assessment:** Accurate. 成立法性 = "established nature"; 難思法性 = "inconceivable nature"; 安住法性 = "abiding nature"; 應生信解不應思議不應分別 = "generates faith and understanding rather than attempting further analysis or conceptual elaboration."

**FINDING 3 — Minor structural note (m):** The Chinese has three distinct dharma-natures: 成立法性, 難思法性, 安住法性. The English renders these as "established nature...inconceivable nature...abiding nature" — all three present, but the "established nature" is presented as a superordinate phrase ("the established nature of dharmas") rather than parallel to the other two. This is a defensible English restructuring but slightly obscures that the Chinese lists three parallel dharma-natures (法性). Very minor.

**Chinese:** 如是六事差別所緣毘鉢舍那。及前三門毘鉢舍那。略攝一切毘鉢舍那。
**English (line 354):** "The vipashyana of the six aspects, together with the three doors described above, encompasses all forms of vipashyana in brief."
**Assessment:** Accurate. 略攝一切毘鉢舍那 = "encompasses all forms of vipashyana in brief."

**Chinese:** 問何因緣故建立如是六事差別毘鉢舍那。答依三覺故如是建立。何等三覺。一語義覺。二事邊際覺。三如實覺。
**English (line 356):** "Why are these six aspects established? They rest on three types of understanding."
**Assessment:** Accurate. Q&A format preserved. 依三覺故 = "they rest on three types of understanding."

**FINDING 4 — Minor (m):** The Q&A structure 問...答... is slightly compressed. Chinese: 問何因緣故建立如是六事差別毘鉢舍那。答依三覺故如是建立。The English drops the explicit "Question...Answer" markers but preserves the dialogue structure through the question form. Acceptable per project conventions (Q&A markers used elsewhere as "What is X?" pattern).

**Chinese:** 尋思義故起語義覺。尋思其事及自相故起事邊際覺。尋思共相品時理故起如實覺。
**English (line 356):** "Investigating meaning gives rise to understanding of linguistic meaning. Investigating things and own-characteristics gives rise to understanding of the limits of things. Investigating shared characteristics, categories, time, and reasoning gives rise to understanding of reality."
**Assessment:** Accurate. The mapping is:
- 義 -> 語義覺: meaning -> linguistic meaning understanding
- 事及自相 -> 事邊際覺: things + own-characteristics -> limits of things
- 共相品時理 -> 如實覺: shared characteristics + categories + time + reasoning -> reality

All correct.

**Chinese:** 修瑜伽師唯有爾所所知境界所謂語義及所知事。盡所有性如所有性。
**English (line 358):** "The entire field of knowledge for the practitioner of yoga consists in just this: linguistic meaning and knowable things — the full extent of what exists and the nature of things as they are."
**Assessment:** Accurate. 修瑜伽師 = "practitioner of yoga"; 所知境界 = "field of knowledge"; 語義及所知事 = "linguistic meaning and knowable things"; 盡所有性如所有性 = "full extent of what exists and nature of things as they are."

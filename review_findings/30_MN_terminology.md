## Flag 2: Terms Rendered Differently from Glossary

**FLAG 2A — "shared characteristic" vs. glossary "common characteristics" (MODERATE)**

- Glossary entry (line 256): `共相善巧 | samanya-lakshana-kaushala | skill in common characteristics | common-characteristic skill`
- Translation (line 336): "own-characteristic (*svalakshana*) and shared characteristic (*samanya-lakshana*)"
- The base term *samanya-lakshana* is rendered "shared characteristic" in the translation, but the glossary entry uses "common characteristics" for its compound (共相善巧). The base term 共相 alone is not listed separately; only the compound with 善巧 is. This creates a minor inconsistency: "common" vs. "shared." Neither is flagged as wrong, but a choice should be made and standardized. Note: the standalone term at line 336 is 自相/共相 (own/shared), not the compound 善巧; so the glossary entry is for a different compound. However, the rendering of 共相 itself should be consistent wherever it appears. Recommend: verify whether 共相 is rendered "shared" or "common" across fascicle 30, and align with the glossary's "common."

**FLAG 2B — "direct realization" vs. glossary "direct realization" — CLEAR (consistent)**

- Translation (line 348): "direct realization (*pratyaksha-pramana*)"
- Glossary (line 530): "direct realization" — matches.

**FLAG 2C — vitarka rendered inconsistently within section**

- Glossary distinguishes two uses of vitarka:
  - 尋 (as dhyana factor) → "examination, investigation" (glossary line 94)
  - 尋思 (behavioral compound) → "discursive thought" (glossary line 95)
- In section 30.13, the term 周遍尋思 is rendered "comprehensive investigation" (glossary line 526: correct).
- In the footnote [^four_vipashyana] at line 308: "The latter two operations (investigation and examination) correspond to the Abhidharma pair of investigation (*vitarka*) and examination (*vicara*)."
  - Here vitarka is rendered "investigation" in the footnote, which is consistent with the glossary entry for 尋 as dhyana factor (line 94: "examination, investigation"). This is acceptable given the Abhidharma context.
  - No error; this is within the range of the glossary's "examination, investigation" for 尋.

## Reference Standard: What the Shambhala Book Does

Based on the 7 photos of *The Sadhana of Mahamudra* (Shambhala Publications, 2025):

**Title page (photo 2):** Rich, layered, centered hierarchy:
- Title in large serif: "The Sadhana of Mahamudra" — positioned in the upper third
- Subtitle in *italic* directly below: *Teachings on Devotion and Crazy Wisdom*
- A **horizontal rule** (thin line) separating title/subtitle from attribution
- Author name below the rule: "Chogyam Trungpa" in a medium-weight serif
- "FOREWORDS BY" in small caps, followed by "Diana J. Mukpo and Samuel Bercholz"
- Publisher logo (seated figure) and "SHAMBHALA" in small caps centered at the **bottom** of the page
- Everything centered. Clear vertical rhythm: title block at top, attribution in middle, publisher at bottom. The page feels full and balanced, not sparse.

**Copyright page (photo 3, left page):** Dense, professional, bottom-weighted:
- Publisher address block at the **top**: Shambhala Publications, Inc. / 2129 13th Street / Boulder, Colorado 80302 / www.shambhala.com
- Copyright lines with proper (c) symbol
- License/reprint permissions paragraph
- Cover art / cover design credits
- "All rights reserved" boilerplate paragraph
- Print number line: 9 8 7 6 5 4 3 2 1
- "Printed in the United States of America"
- Acid-free paper / distributor note
- Full Library of Congress Cataloging-in-Publication Data block (small font, monospaced-style)
- The page fills nearly the full text block, top to bottom. Small font (roughly 8-9pt), tightly spaced. This is a *complete* copyright page.

**Table of Contents (photos 3-4):** Classic scholarly layout:
- Running header at top: "vi — CONTENTS" in small caps (on the even page)
- "CONTENTS" heading in **letterspaced small caps**, centered
- Entries listed with **right-aligned page numbers** connected by dot leaders
- Front matter entries (Foreword, Introduction, etc.) with Roman numeral page numbers
- Part titles in italic: *Part One: The Embodiment of All the Siddhas*
- Chapter entries indented under parts, numbered: "1. Historical Commentary: Part One"
- Clean hierarchy: part title (italic, full width) > chapter entry (numbered, with page number)
- Back matter entries flush left: Afterword, Appendices, Resources, Glossary, Notes, Index — each with a page number

**Foreword opening (photo 4, right page):**
- "FOREWORD" in letterspaced small caps, centered, positioned about one-third down the page
- Large vertical whitespace above the heading (the "sink" or "drop")
- Body text begins below with generous spacing
- Roman numeral "vii" at page bottom
- No running header on this chapter-opening page

**Body pages (photos 5-7):** Running headers in small caps:
- Even page: "28 — THE EMBODIMENT OF ALL THE SIDDHAS" (page number — section title)
- Odd page: "HISTORICAL COMMENTARY: PART ONE — 29" (chapter title — page number)
- Thin rule beneath the header
- Section headings in SMALL CAPS: "THE PURPOSE OF THE SADHANA: JOINING ATI AND MAHAMUDRA"
- Subheadings in bold or italic
- Q&A format with hanging "Q:" and "CTR:" labels
- Body text justified, first-line indent, no paragraph spacing

### Page 3 — Title Page (MAJOR ISSUES)

**Ours:**
- "Sravakabhumi: The Hearer's Ground" centered in the upper quarter
- Below, a LEFT-ALIGNED block:
  - *From the Yogacarabhumi-sastra of Asanga* (italic)
  - "Fascicles 26-33" (roman)
  - "Translated from the Chinese of Xuanzang (T1579)" (roman)
  - "English translation edited by Ankhara" (roman)
- No horizontal rules, no decorative elements
- No publisher/imprint at the bottom
- Bottom two-thirds of the page is completely empty

**Shambhala standard:**
- Title centered, large
- Subtitle in italic, centered beneath
- Horizontal rule separating title from attribution
- Author/translator info centered
- Foreword credits
- Publisher logo + name anchored at page bottom
- Everything centered, creating a balanced vertical composition

**Issues (HIGH PRIORITY):**

1. **CRITICAL: Mixed alignment creates visual dissonance.** The title is centered but the attribution block is left-aligned (with a slight indent). This makes the page look like two unrelated elements. The Shambhala page has everything centered, creating unity. Our page looks like a title pasted above a metadata list.

2. **CRITICAL: No horizontal rule.** The Shambhala title page uses a thin horizontal rule between the title/subtitle zone and the author/translator zone. This is a standard book-design element that creates visual breathing room and hierarchy. Ours has nothing separating the zones — just blank space.

3. **CRITICAL: Bottom two-thirds empty — no anchoring element.** The Shambhala page places the publisher logo and name at the bottom, which creates a visual anchor and frames the whole page. Our page has nothing below the attribution block, making the lower two-thirds a void. Even without a publisher imprint, a decorative element (a small ornament, a dharma wheel, a simple rule) at the bottom would anchor the composition.

4. **MODERATE: Attribution reads like metadata, not like a book title page.** "Fascicles 26-33" and "Translated from the Chinese of Xuanzang (T1579)" read like catalog data. Compare Shambhala's elegant hierarchy: title > subtitle > rule > author > "Forewords by" credits. Our page could restructure to:
   - Title (centered, large)
   - *From the Yogacarabhumi-sastra* (centered, italic — this IS the subtitle)
   - Rule
   - "Attributed to Asanga" (centered)
   - "Translated from the Chinese of Xuanzang" (centered, smaller)
   - "Edited by Ankhara" (centered, smaller)
   - [bottom anchor element]

5. **MINOR: "English translation edited by Ankhara" could be just "Edited by Ankhara."** The word "English" is redundant on an English-language title page; "translation" is implicit from "Translated from the Chinese" on the preceding line.

**Verdict:** NEEDS SIGNIFICANT REWORK. This is the most visible page in the book after the cover. It needs to look as rich and intentional as the Shambhala page.

### Page 4 — Copyright Page (MODERATE ISSUES)

**Ours:**
- Text at the TOP of the page, about 6 lines total:
  - "English translation copyright (c) 2025-2026 Ankhara"
  - "Licensed under Creative Commons Attribution-NonCommercial-ShareAlike 4.0 International (CC BY-NC-SA 4.0)"
  - "English translation from the *Taisho Shinshu Daizokyo*, Vol. 30, No. 1579"
  - "Chinese translation by Xuanzang, 648 CE"
  - "Original composition attributed to Asanga (c. late 4th-early 5th century CE)"
- Bottom 85% of page completely empty
- The CC license line has ugly wide word spacing from justification ("Licensed    under    Creative    Commons    Attribution-NonCommercial-")

**Shambhala standard:**
- Dense, information-rich copyright page filling nearly the full text block
- Publisher address, copyright holders, license terms, reprint credits, art/design credits, print line, printer, paper note, distributor, full Library of Congress CIP data
- Small font (8-9pt), tightly spaced
- Professional and complete

**Issues:**

1. **MODERATE: Page is strikingly sparse.** Five lines of text floating at the top of an otherwise blank page. The Shambhala copyright page fills the text block with information. Even for a CC-licensed independent publication, more content is expected. Possible additions:
   - The CC license could be a fuller paragraph explaining what it means
   - "First edition, 2026"
   - Source text identification paragraph
   - ISBN (when available)
   - "Typeset in EB Garamond" (standard for books)
   - A note about the translation methodology or AI-assisted nature
   - Print-on-demand / distribution info

2. **MODERATE: Copyright text should be at the BOTTOM of the page, not the top.** The convention for most trade and scholarly publishers (Shambhala included, looking at the photo) is to fill the page from the top down, but many publishers — especially for simpler copyright pages — prefer bottom-aligned text. Given how sparse ours is, bottom-aligning the existing text would look much better than floating it at the top with a vast empty space below.

3. **MINOR: CC license line justification is ugly.** The line "Licensed under Creative Commons Attribution-NonCommercial-ShareAlike 4.0 International (CC BY-NC-SA 4.0)" is being force-justified, creating wide gaps between words. This line should either be set ragged-right or broken differently.

4. **MINOR: Font size appears to be the same as body text.** Copyright pages traditionally use a smaller font (8-9pt vs. 11pt body). This contributes to the sparse appearance — the text is too large for the content amount.

**Verdict:** NEEDS MODERATE REWORK. Add content, consider bottom-alignment, reduce font size.

### Page 5 — Contents, Page 1 (MODERATE ISSUES)

**Ours:**
- "Contents" heading centered, in a larger serif font
- Organized by Yoga Place with centered subheadings ("The Second Yoga Place")
- Fascicle entries in bold ("Fascicle 26 — Part One") with descriptive summaries below
- No page numbers for any entry
- Page number "v" (Roman numeral) centered at bottom

**Shambhala standard:**
- "CONTENTS" in letterspaced small caps, centered
- Entries with right-aligned page numbers and dot leaders
- Part titles in italic, spanning full width
- Numbered chapter entries with page numbers
- Running header on continuation pages: "vi — CONTENTS"

**Issues:**

1. **SIGNIFICANT: No page numbers in the table of contents.** This was flagged in the v1 review and remains the single biggest functional deficiency. A print book MUST have page numbers in the ToC. Readers cannot navigate without them. The Shambhala ToC has page numbers for every entry — front matter entries in Roman numerals, body entries in Arabic.

2. **MODERATE: "Contents" heading should be in small caps to match the Shambhala house style.** Our heading is in Title Case serif. The Shambhala standard uses "CONTENTS" in letterspaced small caps. Since we are already using letterspaced small caps for running headers, the ToC heading should match.

3. **MODERATE: No dot leaders connecting entries to page numbers.** Even once we add page numbers, we need dot leaders (the line of dots connecting the entry name to the page number at the right margin). This is a universal convention for tables of contents. The Shambhala ToC has them.

4. **MINOR: Descriptive summaries under each fascicle are unusual for a ToC.** These semicolon-separated content descriptions are nice for a scholarly table of contents, but they look different from the clean Shambhala format (which lists chapter titles only, with page numbers). This is a design choice — it can work, but the summaries should be in a smaller or lighter font to distinguish them from the entry headings.

5. **MODERATE: Roman numeral "v" starts at page 5.** The user flagged this. Explanation: In standard book pagination, the half-title (p. i), its blank verso (p. ii), the title page (p. iii), and copyright page (p. iv) are all counted but NOT numbered (no printed page number). The first printed Roman numeral appears on the ToC page, which is page v. **This is actually correct and standard.** Pages i-iv exist but their numbers are suppressed. However, it looks odd to a non-publisher audience. A brief note could be added to the build documentation explaining this convention, but the numbering itself should NOT be changed.

**Verdict:** NEEDS MODERATE REWORK, primarily adding page numbers and dot leaders.

### Page 7 — Translator's Introduction, Page 1 (MODERATE ISSUES)

**Ours:**
- "Translator's Introduction" as a heading at the top
- Two section subheadings: "The Sravakabhumi in Context" and "Significance of This Translation"
- Body text in justified serif
- Page number "vii" at bottom

**Issues:**

1. **MODERATE: No chapter sink/drop.** The Shambhala foreword starts about one-third down the page, with generous white space above. 2. **MODERATE: Heading style doesn't match the Shambhala register.** "Translator's Introduction" in our current style appears to be in regular Title Case serif. The Shambhala approach would be letterspaced small caps: "TRANSLATOR'S INTRODUCTION". This should match whatever we use for chapter openings in the body text.

3. **MINOR: No drop cap.** Some professional books use a drop cap (oversized first letter) at the beginning of the first paragraph of each major section. The Shambhala book does not appear to use drop caps either, so this is not essential, but it could add richness. Lower priority.

4. **MINOR: Section subheadings ("The Sravakabhumi in Context") may be overly prominent.** In a short 2-page introduction, two section headings create a fragmented feel. Consider whether these could be replaced by a simple paragraph break or a centered ornamental break (three asterisks, a small rule, etc.).

**Verdict:** NEEDS MODERATE ADJUSTMENT for the chapter sink and heading style.

## Roman Numeral Pagination: Is "v" Correct?

**Yes, "v" on the first ToC page is correct and standard.** Here is the page count:

| Physical page | Content | Assigned number | Printed? |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Half-title | i | No (suppressed) |
| 2 | Blank verso | ii | No (suppressed) |
| 3 | Title page | iii | No (suppressed) |
| 4 | Copyright | iv | No (suppressed) |
| 5 | Contents p.1 | v | **Yes** |
| 6 | Contents p.2 | vi | Yes |
| 7 | Introduction p.1 | vii | Yes |
| 8 | Introduction p.2 | viii | Yes |

This is the universal convention in English-language publishing. The Chicago Manual of Style (17th ed., section 1.5) specifies that front matter pages are numbered with lowercase Roman numerals, and that the first recto page of the book (half-title) is page i, but numbers are typically not printed until the first page of displayed content (usually the ToC or a dedication page).

**The numbering is correct. No change needed.**

## Implementation Notes

The fixes for C1-C3 (title page) require changes to both:
- `translation/00_titlepage.md` — restructure the content and add LaTeX centering commands
- `build/print-template.tex` — possibly add support for a bottom-anchored element

The fixes for C4 (ToC page numbers) require either:
- Manual page numbers in `translation/00_contents.md` (fragile — breaks when pagination changes)
- Or: use LaTeX's `\tableofcontents` mechanism to auto-generate the ToC from chapter/section headings (robust but requires the heading structure to match the desired ToC layout)

The fixes for M1-M3 (copyright page) require changes to `translation/00_copyright.md` and possibly LaTeX commands for smaller font size and ragged-right alignment.

The fixes for M6-M7 (Introduction opening) should be inherited from the `\titleformat{\chapter}` settings in `print-template.tex` — if the Introduction is rendered as a `\chapter`, it should automatically get the chapter sink. If it is not getting the sink, the heading level in `00_introduction.md` may need adjustment, or the chapter formatting may need to be verified.

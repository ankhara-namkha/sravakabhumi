### Section 29.1 — The Four Right Efforts (四正斷)

**Source line 10 (opening):**

- **S-01** (Minor): 攝心持心 rendered "uplifting the mind and holding the mind." 攝心 = gathering/collecting the mind, not uplifting (策心). The text later defines 策心 (uplifting) and 持心 (holding) as separate items. Here 乃至攝心持心 abbreviates; 攝 and 策 are related but distinct. Overall sense is close since this is a compressed cross-reference (廣說如前).

**Source line 12 (definition of evil and unwholesome dharmas):**

- **S-02** (Moderate): 欲纏染污身語意業 — Chinese: karma of body, speech, and mind *defiled by* desire-entanglements. English: "defiled bodily, verbal, and mental karma *associated with* desire-entanglements." "Associated with" is weaker than 染污 (stain/defile/pollute), which marks the entanglements as the causal agent of defilement.
- Q&A structure (云何...謂...) preserved. All clauses rendered. ✓

Sanskrit gloss is slightly off, but concepts are closely related and no doctrinal error results.

### Section 33.5 — Attention to Characteristics

**CN:** 云何名為了相作意。謂若作意能正覺了欲界麁相初靜慮靜相。
**EN:** "What is the attention to characteristics? It is the attention that correctly discerns the coarse aspects of the desire realm and the peaceful aspects of the first dhyana."
**Assessment:** Accurate Q&A structure preserved. **OK.**

**CN:** 云何覺了欲界麁相。謂正尋思欲界六事。何等為六。一義。二事。三相。四品。五時。六理。
**EN:** "How does one discern the coarse aspects of the desire realm? Through correct investigation of six aspects of desires. The six are: (1) meaning, (2) substance, (3) characteristic, (4) category, (5) time, and (6) reasoning."
**Assessment:** Accurate. **OK.**

#### (1) Meaning (義)

**CN:** 云何尋思諸欲麁義。謂正尋思如是諸欲有多過患有多損惱。有多疫癘有多災害。於諸欲中多過患義。廣說乃至多災害義。是名麁義。
**EN:** "One investigates that desires have many faults, many injuries, many afflictions, and many calamities. This quality of being full of faults — and so on through being full of calamities — is called the coarse meaning of desires."
**Assessment:** 多過患 = "many faults" OK. 多損惱 = "many injuries" OK. 多疫癘 = "many afflictions" — note: 疫癘 literally means "pestilence/plague" rather than "afflictions" (煩惱). This is a **mistranslation risk** since 疫癘 is a metaphorical usage meaning "plague-like harm" or "pestilence." However, in context the term is used figuratively to mean harmful/afflicting qualities, so "afflictions" as a general English word (not the technical Buddhist term) is borderline acceptable. 多災害 = "many calamities" OK. 廣說乃至 = "and so on through" OK. **m — minor: 疫癘 rendered as "afflictions" is imprecise; "pestilences" or "plagues" would be more literal and avoid confusion with 煩惱 (klesha/afflictions). This is a recurring term in this fascicle.**

**Assessment:** Accurate. 內貪欲/外貪欲 = "internal craving / external craving." **OK.**

#### (3) Own-Characteristic (自相)

**EN:** 'One investigates: "This is affliction-desire; this is object-desire." These manifest further through three bases: what conduces to pleasant feeling, what conduces to painful feeling, and what conduces to neither-pleasant-nor-painful feeling.'
**Assessment:** Accurate. 此復三種 rendered as "These manifest further through three bases" adds slight interpretive gloss ("manifest" and "bases" for 種/處) but the meaning is preserved. **OK.**

**CN:** 順樂受處是貪欲依處。是想心倒依處。
**EN:** "What conduces to pleasant feeling serves as the basis for craving and for the inversions of perception and mind."
**Assessment:** 想心倒 = "inversions of perception and mind" (想倒 + 心倒). Accurate. **OK.**

**CN:** 順苦受處是瞋恚依處。是忿恨依處。
**EN:** "What conduces to painful feeling serves as the basis for anger and resentment."
**Assessment:** 瞋恚 = "anger" OK. 忿恨 = "resentment" — strictly 忿 = fury/indignation, 恨 = resentment/grudge. "Resentment" alone covers 恨 but loses 忿. However, translating 忿恨 as a compound "resentment" is an acceptable compression. **m — minor: 忿恨 could be "fury and resentment" to preserve both terms, but this is a minor point.**

**CN:** 順不苦不樂受處是愚癡依處。是覆惱誑諂無慚無愧依處。是見倒依處。
**EN:** "What conduces to neither-pleasant-nor-painful feeling serves as the basis for ignorance, for concealment, vexation, deceit, flattery, shamelessness, and recklessness, and for the inversion of views."
**Assessment:** 愚癡 = "ignorance" OK. 覆 = "concealment" OK. 惱 = "vexation" OK. 誑 = "deceit" OK. 諂 = "flattery" OK. 無慚 = "shamelessness" OK. 無愧 = "recklessness" — 無愧 more precisely means "lack of moral dread" or "lack of concern for others' disapproval." "Recklessness" is an acceptable approximation in NTC register. 見倒 = "inversion of views" OK. **OK.**

**CN:** 即正尋思如是諸欲極惡諸受之所隨逐。極惡煩惱之所隨逐。是名尋思諸欲自相。
**EN:** "One thus investigates that desires are attended by the most harmful kinds of feelings and the most harmful kinds of afflictions. This is investigating the own-characteristic of desires."
**Assessment:** 極惡諸受之所隨逐 = "attended by the most harmful kinds of feelings" OK. 極惡煩惱之所隨逐 = "attended by the most harmful kinds of afflictions" OK. **OK.**

#### (5) Category (品)

**Assessment:** 黑品 = "dark category." **OK.**

**CN:** 猶如骨鎖如凝血肉。如草炬火如一分炭火。如大毒蛇如夢所見。如假借得諸莊嚴具。如樹端果。

**CN:** 追求諸欲諸有情類。於諸欲中受追求所作苦。受防護所作苦。受親愛失壞所作苦。受無厭足所作苦。受不自在所作苦。受惡行所作苦。如是一切如前應知。

**Assessment:** 無厭/無足/無滿 rendered as three distinct items — "never sated, never satisfied, never fulfilled." **OK.**

**CN:** 又彼諸欲常為諸佛及佛弟子。賢善正行正至善士。以無量門呵責毀呰。

**Assessment:** 賢善 = "the virtuous" OK. 正行 = "the rightly practicing" OK. 正至善士 = "the genuinely good" OK. 以無量門呵責毀呰 = "reproached and condemned through innumerable teachings" — 門 here means "approaches/ways" not "teachings" per se, but it is a minor imprecision. **OK.**

**CN:** 又彼諸欲於習近時。能令諸結積集增長。

**Assessment:** 諸結 = "fetters" OK. **OK.**

**CN:** 又彼諸欲於習近時。我說無有惡不善業而不作者。
**EN:** "Fifth, when one habitually engages them — I say there is no evil or unwholesome action that one will not commit."
**Assessment:** Accurate. Preserves the first-person voice (我說). **OK.**

**CN:** 如是諸欲令無厭足多所共有。是非法行惡行之因增長欲愛。智者所離速趣消滅依託眾緣。是諸放逸危亡之地。無常虛偽妄失之法。猶如幻化誑惑愚夫。
**EN:** "Thus desires produce insatiability and are shared by many. They are the cause of unrighteous and evil conduct and increase craving. The wise abandon them. **Assessment:** This is a dense passage. Let me check each phrase:

#### (6) Time (時)

**CN:** 云何尋思諸欲麁時。謂正尋思如是諸欲去來今世。於常常時於恒恒時。多諸過患多諸損惱。多諸疫癘多諸災害。是名尋思諸欲麁時。
**EN:** "one investigates that desires — past, future, and present — are constantly and perpetually full of faults, injuries, afflictions, and calamities."
**Assessment:** 去來今世 = "past, future, and present" OK. 於常常時於恒恒時 = "constantly and perpetually" OK. Same 疫癘 = "afflictions" issue as in (1) Meaning — see note there. The Chinese opening 云何尋思諸欲麁時 is rendered as a sub-heading format ("Regarding the coarse time-aspect of desires") rather than Q&A format ("How does one investigate..."). **m — minor structural difference: the other five aspects use Q&A format ("How does one investigate...?") but Time uses a different introductory formula.** Also, the closing 是名尋思諸欲麁時 is not explicitly rendered. **m — minor omission of closing formula.**

**CN:** 又彼諸欲雖善生起。雖善增長。一切多為外攝受事。謂父母妻子奴婢作使親友眷屬。

**Assessment:** 外攝受事 = "external dependents" — more literally "matters of external support/maintenance." The English interpretation is acceptable. 奴婢 = "servants" OK. 作使 = "workers" OK (could also be "hired laborers"). **OK.**

**CN:** 或為對治。自內有色麁重四大。糜飯長養常須覆蔽沐浴按摩。壞斷離散消滅法身。隨所生起種種苦惱。

**Assessment:** 自內有色麁重四大 = "one's own body, composed of the coarse and heavy four great elements" OK. 糜飯長養 = "gruel and rice for nourishment" OK. 壞斷離散消滅法身 = "a body subject by its nature to deterioration, breakage, dispersal, and destruction" — note 法身 here does NOT mean "Dharmakaya." In this context 法 modifies 身 as "by its nature" (法 = dharma = nature/quality), which the English correctly captures. **OK.**

**CN:** 食能對治諸飢渴苦。衣能對治諸寒熱苦。及能覆蔽可慚羞處。臥具能治諸勞睡苦。及能對治經行住苦。病緣醫藥能治病苦。

**CN:** 是故諸欲唯能對治隨所生起種種苦惱。不應染著而受用之。唯應正念。
**EN:** "Therefore desires serve only to counteract the various sufferings that arise. One should not enjoy them with attachment but should use them only with right mindfulness"
**Assessment:** Accurate. **OK.**

**CN:** 譬如重病所逼切人。為除病故服雜穢藥。
**EN:** "like a person oppressed by grave illness who takes foul medicine for the sake of curing the disease."
**Assessment:** 雜穢藥 = "foul medicine" — literally "mixed/impure medicine." Acceptable. **OK.**

**CN:** 又彼諸欲有至教量證有麁相。又彼諸欲如是如是所有麁相。我亦於內現智見轉。又彼諸欲有比度量知有麁相。又彼諸欲從無始來本性麁穢成就。法性難思。法性不應思議。不應分別。是名尋思諸欲麁理。
**EN:** "Furthermore, the coarseness of desires is confirmed by scriptural authority (*agama-pramana*). Their coarse aspects are known through one's own direct insight and perception. **Assessment:** Four pramanas rendered:

The English compresses these into one clause. **m — minor compression of two Chinese sentences into one, but meaning preserved.**

## Structure Check

The Chinese text at [T30.0455b16] presents the nine practice approaches (九種白品所攝加行) as a single continuous block, ending with 如是名為心一境性 just before [T30.0457b04]. The English splits this into three sections:

- **31.5**: Introduction + Approaches 1–4
- **31.6**: Approach 5 (Timely Practice)
- **31.7**: Approaches 6–9 + Conclusion

This is an editorial division for readability. The Chinese has no sub-divisions — it is one continuous passage. The division is reasonable and does not affect content.

**Sub-section count:** The Chinese enumerates exactly 9 approaches (相應, 串習, 無倒, 不緩, 應時, 解了, 無厭足, 不捨軛, 正加行) plus introduction and conclusion. The English covers all 9 plus introduction and conclusion. **Match confirmed.**

## Enumeration Order vs. Presentation Order

**Chinese enumeration list (at the top):**
1. 相應加行 (appropriate)
2. 串習加行 (habituated)
3. 無倒加行 (non-inverted)
4. 不緩加行 (unslackened)
5. 應時加行 (timely)
6. 解了加行 (comprehending)
7. 無厭足加行 (insatiable)
8. 不捨軛加行 (not-abandoning-the-yoke)
9. 正加行 (correct)

**Chinese detailed discussion order:**
1. 相應加行
2. 串習加行
3. 不緩加行 ← appears BEFORE 無倒
4. 無倒加行 ← appears AFTER 不緩
5–9: same as enumeration

The English translation presents them numbered (1)–(9) following the **enumeration** order, not the discussion order. A footnote [^order_note] explains: "The Chinese enumeration lists non-inverted practice (無倒加行) as the third and unslackened practice (不緩加行) as the fourth, though the detailed discussion in the Chinese text reverses their presentation order. The numbering here follows the enumeration."

**Assessment:** This is transparent and well-handled. The translator chose the enumeration order (which is internally more coherent) and documented the discrepancy. **No issue.**

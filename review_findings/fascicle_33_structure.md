# Fascicle 33 Section Breakdown

**Division:** Fourth Yoga Place, Part One (第四瑜伽處之一)
**Scope:** Seven mental attentions (七作意) for abandoning desire, the four dhyanas, formless attainments, two absorptions without mind, five superknowledges, rebirth destinations, characteristics of one free from desire.

## Uddana (Table of Contents Verse)

[T30.0465a28]:
> 七作意離欲 及諸定廣辯
> 二定五神通 生差別諸相
> 觀察於諸諦 如實而通達
> 廣分別於修 究竟為其後

Topics announced: (1) seven attentions and abandoning desire, (2) detailed exposition of the dhyanas, (3) two absorptions and five superknowledges, (4) rebirth differences and characteristics, (5) observation of truths and realization, (6) detailed analysis of cultivation, (7) culmination. Note: topics 5-7 presumably continue into subsequent fascicles.

Attention 3: abandons upper-grade afflictions. Attention 4: abandons middle-grade afflictions. Attention 5: prevents overestimation (增上慢). Attention 6: abandons lower-grade afflictions. | 5 | (within 0465b27 block) | 若於有尋有伺初靜慮地覺了麁相 | Second dhyana: coarse/peaceful analysis of vitarka-vicara | For entering second dhyana: first dhyana's vitarka/vicara recognized as coarse. Vitarka defined: initial, sudden, coarse mental verbalization (麁意言性) on an object. Vicara defined: subsequent, gradual, subtle mental verbalization (細意言性). Both are mind-concomitants (心法性), arise with mind, co-present, share one object. Both are internally generated, externally classified. All three times. Impermanent, brief, agitating, disturbing. Therefore "dark category" (黑品). Second dhyana: free of these, therefore "peaceful." Remaining five attentions apply as before. |

Examined through the same six perspectives. Each ground's peaceful aspect discerned in the next-higher ground. Progression continues until the culmination-fruit attention of each ground. |
| 6 | T30.0467b22 | 復次此中離欲者 | Detailed exposition of the first dhyana formula | Term-by-term commentary on the standard dhyana description. "Desires" (欲) = two kinds: affliction-desire (煩惱欲) and object-desire (事欲). "Separation" (離) = two kinds: disassociation (相應離) and object-separation (境界離). "Evil unwholesome dharmas" = actions born from affliction-desire (杖/刀/鬪/諍 etc.). "With vitarka and vicara" = because faults of these not yet seen; wholesome vitarka-vicara still present as antidotes. "Separation" = attainment of culmination-of-application attention. "Born" (生) = produced from that as cause/condition without interval. "Joy-bliss" (喜樂) = attained wished-for aim; no fault seen in joy; all coarseness removed; great pliancy attained. "First" = first ascent from desire-realm. "Dhyana" (靜慮) = single-pointed mindful quiescent accurate deliberation. "Equipped" (具足) = attained culmination-fruit attention. "Dwelling" (安住) = through practice, can enter at will, up to seven days and nights. |
| 7 | (after 0467b22) | 復次於有尋有伺三摩地相心能棄捨 | Second dhyana: detailed formula commentary | Abandons vitarka-vicara samadhi-aspect. Dwells in non-vitarka-non-vicara. Transcends "sudden-action realm" (怱務所行境界). Dwells in "non-sudden" realm. Mind moves as single-flavored, quiescent, supremely quiescent. "Vitarka-vicara quiescent" (尋伺寂靜). "Inner purity" (內等淨). Through serial practice, transcends the interrupted state, attains uninterrupted state (無間缺位). "One-pointed mind" (心一趣). "Born from concentration" (定生) -- not born from separation as in first dhyana. Joy-bliss: attained aim + no fault seen in joy + all first-dhyana affliction-coarseness removed + great pliancy. Second by sequential count. |
| 8 | (after 0467b22, cont.) | 復次彼於喜相深見過失 | Third dhyana: detailed formula commentary | Sees fault in joy (喜). Separates from two disturbances to mind. Dwells in equanimity (捨). First and second dhyanas both have disturbances: first has vitarka-vicara, second has joy. Therefore equanimity only genuinely exists from third dhyana. With equanimity: mindfulness stays steady, joy-accompanied perception/attention ceases. If poorly practiced, joy-states may return -- immediately recognized, not tolerated, rejected. Through repeated practice: elation-accompanied feeling eliminated; separated from joy, quiescent, supremely quiescent; a feeling contrary to joy (心受 = equanimous mental feeling) arises. Physical and mental bliss (身受樂 + 輕安樂). Below third dhyana: no such bliss or equanimity. Above: equanimity exists but not bliss. Therefore the saints declare third dhyana the ground of equanimity, mindfulness, clear knowing, and bodily bliss. |
| 9 | T30.0468a26 | 復次此中對治種類勢相似故 | Fourth dhyana: detailed formula commentary | Antidote-type and power are similar, so "joyful severance" (樂斷) antidote is stated only by result, not separately. Antidote = equanimity + mindfulness + clear knowing. Through practice, the excellent bliss of third dhyana is abandoned. "Bliss ceases, suffering ceases" -- collecting all prior cessations: bliss ceases entering 4th dhyana; suffering ceases entering 2nd dhyana; joy disappears entering 3rd dhyana; grief disappears entering 1st dhyana. Here specifically: through cessation of pleasure/pain, the neither-pleasant-nor-unpleasant feeling remains. All lower-ground disturbances severed (vitarka, vicara, joy, bliss, in-and-out breathing). Equanimity-mindfulness is pure and bright (清淨鮮白). Mind abides unmoved, all agitation remote. "Equanimity-mindfulness purity, fourth dhyana, equipped, dwelling." |
| 10 | (after 0468a26) | 復次以於虛空起勝解故 | Space-infinity attainment (空無邊處) | Through conviction regarding space: all color-perceptions (blue, yellow, red, white, etc.) transcended (not manifest + wearied-of/desired-to-be-free-from). "Transcending form-perceptions" (色想出過). Because color-perceptions cease: all resistance-perceptions (有對想) based on accumulated visible forms are removed. "Resistance-perceptions cease." Because those cease: diverse perceptions of classes of objects (food, vessels, clothing, vehicles, ornaments, cities, mountains, forests, etc.) no longer attended to. "Diverse perceptions not attended to." Having removed form/resistance/diverse perceptions: infinite-space conviction arises. "Enters infinite space." Transcending preliminary culmination-attention into root culmination-fruit-attention. "Space-infinity equipped, dwelling." Note: near-attainment (近分) cognizes only space; root attainment also cognizes own-ground aggregates; near-attainment also cognizes lower-ground aggregates. |
| 11 | T30.0468c05 | 爾時超過近分根本空無邊處 | Consciousness-infinity attainment (識無邊處) | The consciousness that produced infinite-space conviction -- now that consciousness itself becomes the object, with infinite-aspect conviction. Transcends preliminary and root of space-infinity. "Enters infinite consciousness." Equipped, dwelling. |
| 12 | (after 0468c05) | 復次從識無邊處求上進時 | Nothingness attainment (無所有處) | Seeking beyond consciousness-infinity: no further object found, whether form or formless. | 13 | (after 0468c05, cont.) | 復次從無所有處求上進時 | Neither-perception-nor-non-perception attainment (非想非非想處) | Nothingness-perception seen as coarse -> abandoned. Yet not like unconscious attainment or cessation where all perceptions cease -- only extremely subtle perception (微細想) cognizing a signless object (無相境). "Neither perception nor non-perception." | 14 | T30.0469a04 | 復次依靜慮等當知能入二無心定 | Two absorptions without mind (二無心定) | (1) Unconscious attainment (無想定) -- only ordinary beings, via "rejecting-perception attention" (棄背想作意). (2) Cessation attainment (滅盡定) -- only noble ones, via "temporarily halting object-and-attention" (止息所緣作意) from neither-perception-nor-non-perception. |

Prerequisites: freed from third-dhyana desire, not yet freed from fourth-dhyana desire. Mind and mental factors cease = unconscious attainment. |

From NPNNP, mind seeks upward -- finds no object -- ceases. Learner (有學): freed from nothingness desire. Arhat: seeking temporary rest. Attention-as-prerequisite -> mind and mental factors cease = cessation attainment. |

## Structural Summary

Fascicle 33 opens the Fourth Yoga Place with an uddana verse announcing the full scope (seven attentions through culmination). The fascicle covers approximately the first half of the announced topics:

1. **Uddana and two paths** (T30.0465a28-0465b14)
2. **Seven mental attentions for abandoning desire-realm desire** (T30.0465b27-0467b22) -- the longest section, with detailed sub-analysis of the "attention to characteristics" including the six-fold examination of desire's coarseness
3. **First dhyana formula commentary** (T30.0467b22)
4. **Second dhyana formula commentary** (shortly after)
5. **Third dhyana formula commentary** (shortly after)
6. **Fourth dhyana formula commentary** (T30.0468a26)
7. **Four formless attainments** (from after 0468a26 through 0469a04)
8. **Two absorptions without mind** (T30.0469a04)
9. **Five superknowledges via twelve perceptions** (T30.0469a25)
10. **Rebirth destinations** (T30.0470b04)
11. **Characteristics of the desire-free person** (T30.0470b22)

The remaining uddana topics (observation of truths, realization, detailed analysis of cultivation, culmination) carry forward into fascicle 34 and beyond.

## Critical Issues

**Issue 1 — Critical**
- **Term:** "World-Honored One" (line 100: "As the World-Honored One has said")
- **Problem:** The glossary explicitly lists 世尊 (bhagavat) as "Blessed One" with a note "NOT 'World-Honored One' (Chinese-register calque)." This is a direct violation of a glossary-specified prohibition.
- **Glossary entry:** `| 世尊 | bhagavat | Blessed One | Blessed One | NOT "World-Honored One" (Chinese-register calque) |`
- **Suggested fix:** Replace "the World-Honored One" with "the Blessed One" throughout section 33.5 (line 100).

**Issue 2 — Critical**
- **Term:** "Attention of the culmination of application" / "Attention of the fruit of the culmination" (line 76)
- **Problem:** The glossary lists the sixth attention as "attention of completed application" (prayoga-nishtha-manaskara) and the seventh as "attention to the fruit of completed application." The translation renders these as "Attention of the culmination of application" and "Attention of the fruit of the culmination." These renderings are inconsistent with the glossary. The footnote at line 78 also uses "attention of completion" (not matching glossary "attention of completed application").
- **Glossary entries:**
  - `| 加行究竟作意 | prayoga-nishtha-manaskara | attention of completed application | attention of completed application |`
  - `| 加行究竟果作意 | prayoga-nishtha-phala-manaskara | attention to the fruit of completed application | attention to the fruit of completion |`
- **Suggested fix:**
  - Change "(6) Attention of the culmination of application" → "(6) Attention of completed application"
  - Change "(7) Attention of the fruit of the culmination" → "(7) Attention to the fruit of completed application"
  - Update footnote [^seven_attentions] at line 78 accordingly.

**Issue 3 — Critical**
- **Term:** "Sentient beings" (line 98: "Sentient beings who pursue desires suffer...")
- **Problem:** This requires source verification. The watchlist explicitly flags "sentient being (for 補特伽羅)" as WRONG — the correct rendering for pudgala is "person / individual." However, if the Chinese here uses 有情 (sattva, which the glossary correctly renders as "sentient beings"), the usage is correct. The concern is that this passage describes individuals engaged in pursuit of desire in a way that may reflect the pudgala context. The term "Sentient beings" occurs as the sentence subject where "persons" might be expected in an Abhidharma analytical context. Source verification is required. Flag as Critical pending source check.
- **Glossary entry:** `| 有情 | sattva | sentient being(s), being(s) | sentient beings |` and `| 補特伽羅 | pudgala | person, individual | person |`
- **Suggested fix:** Verify Chinese: if 有情 (sattva), usage is correct; if 補特伽羅 (pudgala), change to "Persons who pursue desires."

## Moderate Issues

**Issue 4 — Moderate**
- **Term:** "afflictive entanglements" (line 78 footnote: "…upper, middle, and lower grade afflictive entanglements")
- **Problem:** The glossary has two distinct terms — 煩惱 (klesha / afflictions) and 纏 (paryavasthana / entanglement). The compound "afflictive entanglements" conflates both terms. This compound does not appear in the glossary. If the Chinese is 煩惱纏 or similar, the rendering should spell out both components per their separate glossary entries: "afflictions and entanglements." If the Chinese is just 煩惱, it should be "afflictions" alone. Source verification needed.
- **Suggested fix:** Verify Chinese. Render as "afflictions" (if 煩惱) or "afflictions and entanglements" (if 煩惱纏), not the conflated form.

**Issue 5 — Moderate**
- **Term:** "inversions of perception and mind" / "inversion of views" (line 92)
- **Problem:** The glossary lists 四顛倒 (catur-viparyasa) as "four inversions" and notes specific subcategories. The footnote (line 128) uses the Sanskrit *samjna-citta-viparyasa* and *drsti-viparyasa*. The running text at line 92 uses "inversions of perception and mind" and "inversion of views" — these are not listed independently in the glossary. The glossary entry for the four inversions does not provide standalone renderings for individual inversions. This is a glossary gap. The footnote at line 128 introduces the Sanskrit correctly (*samjna-citta-viparyasa*, *drsti-viparyasa*) without an English gloss on first use within the footnote.
- **Suggested fix:** Either (a) add these to the glossary, or (b) provide English gloss on first appearance: "inversions of perception and mind (*samjna-citta-viparyasa*)" and "inversion of views (*drsti-viparyasa*)."

**Issue 6 — Moderate**
- **Term:** "pramana" — three valid cognitions framework (line 114, footnote line 134)
- **Problem:** The running text at line 114 introduces *agama-pramana* parenthetically: "scriptural authority (*agama-pramana*)" and *anumana* parenthetically: "inference (*anumana*)." These are glossary-listed terms with specific renderings. The glossary renders:
  - 至教量 | agama-pramana → "scriptural authority"
  - 比度量 | anumana-pramana → "inference" (note: full Sanskrit is *anumana-pramana*, not bare *anumana*)
  - 現證量 | pratyaksha-pramana → "direct realization"
  The running text (line 114) omits the gloss for "direct insight and perception" (which corresponds to pratyaksha-pramana) — instead rendering it as "one's own direct insight and perception" without Sanskrit. This inconsistency in applying the Sanskrit parenthetical (given for the first two but not the third) should be corrected.
- **Suggested fix:** Add parenthetical "(pratyaksha-pramana)" after "direct insight and perception" at line 114, or remove Sanskrit from the other two for consistency. The footnote at line 134 correctly labels all three and adds *dharmata* as a fourth — this footnote is consistent.

**Issue 7 — Moderate**
- **Term:** "dharmata" (line 114 running text: "inconceivable nature of things (*dharmata*)")
- **Problem:** The glossary lists 法爾 | dharmata → "the nature of things, natural order" with the note "Preferred in text: dharmata." The running text renders it as "inconceivable nature of things (*dharmata*)" — the addition of "inconceivable" may be imported from context rather than the base term. The gloss is provided in parentheses which is correct for first use. However, the "preferred in text" form is the Sanskrit, so the parenthetical pattern is reversed: it should be "dharmata (the nature of things)" not "the nature of things (*dharmata*)." Minor issue but the glossary specifies the Sanskrit as the preferred form.
- **Suggested fix:** Render as "dharmata (the inconceivable nature of things)" to front the preferred Sanskrit form per the glossary convention.

**Issue 8 — Moderate**
- **Term:** "secondary afflictions" / *upaklesha* (footnote line 128)
- **Problem:** The footnote correctly uses *upaklesha* in parentheses. However, the running text at line 92 lists "concealment, vexation, deceit, flattery, shamelessness, and recklessness" as bases of neutral feeling without identifying them as *upaklesha* (secondary afflictions). The glossary lists 隨煩惱 | upaklesha | secondary afflictions. The footnote (line 128) does identify these as *upaklesha* correctly. The running text lacks the clarifying term label. While not strictly a terminology error (the footnote covers it), the running text leaves the category unmarked.
- **Suggested fix:** In running text at line 92, insert "secondary afflictions" or a parenthetical after the list: "…and recklessness (these being secondary afflictions, *upaklesha*)…" to make the category explicit in the primary text, not just footnotes.

## Minor Issues

**Issue 9 — Minor**
- **Term:** "Alagaddupama Sutta" and "Potaliya Sutta" (footnote line 130)
- **Problem:** The terminology watchlist requires Pali forms to be replaced with Sanskrit: "sutta" → "sutra." This footnote uses the Pali-derived "Sutta" in the names of two Pali Canon texts. While the parenthetical references (MN 22, MN 54) suggest these are Pali Nikaya texts where the Pali name is conventional, the project style mandates Sanskrit forms.
- **Suggested fix:** Either (a) use "Alagaddupama Sutra (MN 22)" and "Potaliya Sutra (MN 54)" with a note that these are Pali Canon texts also known by their Pali titles, or (b) add a translator's note explaining the naming convention for Pali sources cited by the Chinese.

**Issue 10 — Minor**
- **Term:** "Nikaya sources" (footnote line 132)
- **Problem:** Same issue as Issue 9 — "Nikaya" is a Pali term. The project uses Sanskrit equivalents. The Sanskrit equivalent would be "Agama/Sutra sources" or simply "Agama sources." The footnote uses "Agama/Nikaya sources" which splits the convention.
- **Suggested fix:** Replace "Agama/Nikaya sources" with "Agama sources" (Sanskrit form) at footnote line 132.

**Issue 11 — Minor**
- **Term:** First-use gloss for *sapta-manaskara* (line 76 footnote, line 78)
- **Problem:** The footnote at line 78 introduces *sapta-manaskara* in brackets but the running text at line 76 lists all seven attentions without a first-use Sanskrit gloss on "seven types of attention." The footnote provides the Sanskrit as "[^seven_attentions]: The seven attentions (*sapta-manaskara*, 七作意)." This is correct placement (footnote on first use), so this is conformant with convention. No fix needed — flagging as reviewed and compliant.
- **Status:** PASS — no action needed.

**Issue 12 — Minor**
- **Term:** "four great elements" (line 108)
- **Problem:** The term "four great elements" (四大種, *catur-maha-bhuta*) appears in running text without a Sanskrit gloss on what may be its first use in this fascicle. The glossary should be checked for a listed entry. This is a standard Buddhist term; if it appears in the glossary with a required first-use pattern, the gloss is missing.
- **Glossary check:** Not found in the glossary search results. This is likely a glossary gap.
- **Suggested fix:** Either add to glossary and provide first-use gloss "four great elements (*catur-maha-bhuta*)" or, if considered sufficiently standard for the NTC register, leave as is and note the glossary gap.

# Fascicle 32 Section Breakdown

**Division:** Third Yoga Place, Part Three (第三瑜伽處之三)
**Scope:** Instructions for beginners entering meditation practice -- five signs, oil-bowl simile, shamatha/vipashyana for each meditation object (asubha, maitri, pratityasamutpada, dhatu-prabheda, anapanasmrti), attaining mental pliancy and the "possessed of attention" (有作意) state.

## Section Breakdown

| 1 | T30.0459b25 | 云何初修業者始修業時 | Opening question: How does a beginner first establish the attention for practice? | Frame: a skilled yogacara teacher instructs beginners. Five signs (五種相) introduced: (1) disgust/厭離, (2) delight/欣樂, (3) danger/過患, (4) luminosity/光明, (5) discernment of objects/了別事. |

Worldly prosperity as unreliable. |
| 3 | T30.0460a01 | 極善作意如理受持 | Taking the sign of delight (欣樂相) | Self-examination of moral discipline (尸羅). Pure discipline -> freedom from remorse -> joy -> gladness -> delight. Two kinds of delight: (a) from clean conscience, (b) from confidence in capacity for future attainment. |
| 4 | T30.0460b01 | 取是相已復應教授告言 | Taking the sign of danger (過患相) and instructions for shamatha entry | Teacher instructs: with disgust-trained and delight-moistened mind, dwell free of worldly desire/grief. Cultivate shamatha and vipashyana on chosen object. Sign of danger: three categories -- (a) ten signs/相 (color etc.), (b) eight kinds of seeking/尋思, (c) five afflictive secondary states/隨煩惱. Six aspects of the undisturbed state: (1) signlessness, (2) non-function in signlessness, (3) non-discrimination, (4) absence of longing/agitation in non-discrimination, (5) quiescence, (6) peaceful bliss free from torment. |
| 5 | T30.0460c01 | 汝取如是過患相已復應數數取光明相 | Taking the sign of luminosity (光明相) and entering practice | Lamp, great fire, sun, moon as luminosity objects. Then charnel-ground contemplations (asubha): blue-black/青瘀 through bone-chain/骨鎖. Alternative: paintings or sculptures. Posture instructions: washing feet, cross-legged, upright body, mindfulness established. Six types of mental imagery for shamatha. Discerning disturbed vs. undisturbed states (亂不亂相). Crucial method: when images of signs/seeking/afflictions arise in settled mind, apply "non-attending attention" (不念作意) to dispel them. |
| 6 | T30.0461a01 | 世尊依此所緣境相密意說言 | The oil-bowl simile (油鉢喻) | Buddha's teaching: a man carrying a bowl brimming with oil through a festive crowd, executioner with drawn sword behind -- one drop spilled means death. Detailed decoding of simile components. |
| 7 | T30.0461b01 | 如是苾芻我諸弟子恭敬殷重 | Decoding the oil-bowl simile | Point-by-point correspondence: crowd = objects triggering afflictions; music/dance = objects triggering discursive thought; great beings = ten signs (色相等); wise man = yogacara teacher; brimming oil bowl = shamatha-settled mind with pliancy; drawn-sword executioner = perception of danger in signs/seeking/afflictions; guarding against spilling = the shamatha path informed by discernment of disturbed/undisturbed. |
| 7b | T30.0461b01 (cont.) | 是瑜伽師復應如是慇懃教誨 | Instruction on alternating shamatha and vipashyana for the asubha practitioner | If joy does not arise in formless concentration, return to formed (有分別) objects. Apply "image-following vipashyana" (隨相行毘鉢舍那): contemplate blue-black, festering, etc. |
| 8 | T30.0461c01 | 汝於如是初修觀時 | Graduated vipashyana on asubha: from one to infinite | Begin with one blue-black corpse, expand to 2, 3, 4, 5, 10...100...1000...all directions. Then transition from imaginative (勝解作意) to real contemplation (真實作意): "The actual corpses from beginningless time are beyond counting." Alternation principle: after each expansion of vipashyana, return mind to inner stillness. Final instruction: from the last unlimited contemplation, withdraw mind, remove signs but do not fully abandon formed objects -- rest in signless, non-discriminating quiescence. |
| 9 | T30.0462a01 | 彼瑜伽師復應教授告言賢首汝先所取諸光明相 | Light-perception (光明想) applied to both shamatha and vipashyana | Key principle: luminous mind (光明俱心) vs. dark mind (闇). Initial clarity of object determines all subsequent practice quality. Summary of five signs working together. Then: transition from asubha practice into the four foundations of mindfulness (四念住). Internal body: 36 bodily substances from hair to urine. |
| 10 | T30.0462b01 | 次應於外諸不淨物善取其相 | Four foundations of mindfulness via asubha -- body contemplation (internal, external, internal-external) | External body: charnel-ground asubha on others. Internal-external: asubha applied to one's own beloved body after death. Then: four formless aggregates (四無色蘊) contemplated in three modes -- (a) shamatha mode, (b) non-distraction mode, (c) vipashyana mode. These constitute contemplation of feeling/mind/dharmas (受心法觀). |
| 11 | T30.0462c01 | 如是汝由依不淨觀正修加行增上力故 | Conduct between meditation sessions | Having entered the four foundations of mindfulness: daily life instructions. Leave meditation object behind when entering village for alms. Avoid dangers (elephants, horses, bulls, dogs, snakes, beasts, pits, thorns, mud). Guard sense faculties. Eat in proper measure. Speak measured, timely, truthful, peaceful words. Avoid disputatious talk (it leads to proliferation -> excitement -> non-calm -> distance from samadhi). Return quickly to meditation seat. |
| 11b | T30.0462c01 (cont.) | 猶如世間鑽火方便 | Fire-drill simile and aspiration | Practice like the fire-drill: uninterrupted and earnest application. Aspiration: "Even if I had the combined lifespan of all Jambudvipa beings, I would devote it entirely to this practice -- how much more so my brief life of less than a hundred years." Promise of result: first attain minor pliancy (下劣身心輕安) and one-pointedness, then later mundane and supramundane fullness. |
| 12 | T30.0463a01 | 初修業者始修業時善達瑜伽諸瑜伽師 | Summary of asubha instruction, then transition to remaining meditation objects | Lustful temperament -> asubha. Angry temperament -> maitri. Through to discursive temperament -> anapanasmrti. "I shall now show the remaining entry gates." |
| 12b | T30.0463a01 (cont.) | 依慈愍觀初修業者 | Maitri (loving-kindness) meditation: graduated practice | Three categories: friend/親, enemy/怨, neutral/中庸. Begin with one of each, using equal wish for happiness (三種樂: blameless sensory, blameless joyful, blameless equanimous). Expand: 2, 3, 4, 5, 10, 20, 30...all directions, with no space even for a staff-tip. Then entry into four foundations of mindfulness via maitri. Transition from imaginative to real contemplation: "From beginningless time, all beings have been my friend, my enemy, my neutral -- interchangeably. Therefore all are truly equal." |
| 13 | T30.0463b01 | 如從先際如是後際 | Maitri: real contemplation and merit, then pratityasamutpada meditation | Merit of real (真實作意) maitri immeasurably exceeds imaginative (勝解作意) maitri (not 1/100th, not 1/1000th, not 1/upanishad-part). Then: dependent origination (緣性緣起觀). Beings driven by ignorance: impermanent->permanent, impure->pure, suffering->pleasure, non-self->self (four inversions). Craving -> karma -> future mass of suffering. Four foundations of mindfulness through pratityasamutpada: internal (own present aggregates), external (others' aggregates). |
| 14 | T30.0463c01 | 若於自他過去未來所有諸蘊 | Pratityasamutpada: internal-external contemplation, then dhatu-prabheda (界差別觀) | Past/future aggregates = internal-external contemplation. Then element-differentiation meditation. Six elements: (1) earth/堅 -- mountains, grass, wood, brick, jewels -> internal solidity; (2) water/濕 -- rivers, lakes, wells -> internal moisture; (3) fire/煗 -- sun, forest fires, kilns -> internal warmth; (4) wind/風 -- directional winds, wind-wheel -> internal wind; (5) space/空 -- unobstructed openings, cavities -> internal space; (6) consciousness/識 -- eye + form + attention = eye-consciousness, etc. through mind-consciousness. Then: seeds of all consciousness within the four-element body. Progressive analysis of material elements down to paramanu (極微). |
| 15 | T30.0464a01 | 次後漸漸發起種種微細勝解 | Dhatu-prabheda continued: space element and consciousness element analysis, then decomposition visualization | Space element: from gross openings (eyes, ears, nostrils, throat) to subtle pores. Consciousness element: analyzed by support, object, attention, three times, categories. Ten derived forms (十種所造諸色). Alternative method via decomposition: own body at death -> blue-black -> pus flowing -> expanding to fill oceans and earth -> fire burning all -> bone-ash -> wind scattering dust -> only vast empty space remains. Then transition to real contemplation: water/fire/earth/wind/space from beginningless samsara exceed all imagination. Tears of grief, mother's milk, blood from punishments -- exceed the four great oceans. |
| 16 | T30.0464b01 | 又飲母乳又由作賊擁逼劫掠 | Dhatu-prabheda: samsaric scale, then anapanasmrti (入出息念) meditation | Innumerable fires burning corpses, innumerable scattered bones, innumerable wind-dispersed remains, innumerable consciousness-streams. Then: mindfulness of breathing. External analogues: bellows of blacksmith/goldsmith, wind entering/leaving openings. Internal: subtle breath-wind through heart/chest, then expanding awareness to wind in all pores. Entire body as wind-pervaded, like cotton-wool (妬羅綿) or silk floss. Three modes of body contemplation: (a) internal -- own breath flowing; (b) external -- dead body where breath has ceased; (c) internal-external -- imagining one's own death/pre-death/post-death cessation of breath. |
| 17 | T30.0464c01 | 遍於一切正加行中 | Universal shamatha complement across all meditation objects | All correct practices should include the shamatha auxiliary (止品助伴). Definitions: "ardent" (熾然) = constant, unshakeable practice unperturbed by noise; "clearly knowing and mindful" (正知具念) = discerning disturbed/undisturbed states in shamatha-vipashyana; "having overcome worldly desire and grief" (調伏一切世間貪憂) = via signs of disgust and delight. |
| 18 | T30.0464c01 (cont.) | 先發如是正加行時 | Attainment of mental pliancy (身心輕安) and one-pointedness (心一境性) | Initially: pliancy and one-pointedness are subtle and hard to detect. Through continued shamatha-vipashyana: body-mind purification, body-mind pliability, body-mind lightness gradually increase. Sequence of arising: (1) heaviness-like sensation at crown of head (非損惱相); (2) removal of mental coarseness (心麁重性) blocking joyful renunciation; (3) mental pliancy (心輕安) arises; (4) this triggers physical pliancy -- wind-element increases, great elements (大種) enter the body; (5) physical coarseness removed; (6) physical pliancy (身輕安) pervades entire body "like being filled up"; (7) initial elation/delight; (8) elation gradually subsides; (9) subtle pliancy accompanies the body; (10) mind settles on object in peaceful movement, governed by shamatha. |
| 19 | T30.0465a01 | 從是已後於瑜伽行初修業者名有作意 | Attaining "possessed of attention" (有作意) status | This is the FIRST attainment of a form-realm (色界) meditative mind. Characteristics of one who has attained this: (a) possesses form-realm concentration; (b) has partial pliancy and one-pointedness; (c) capable of purifying-affliction practice; (d) mind flows with moistness under shamatha protection; (e) even amidst attractive objects, fierce craving does not arise (or if slight, quickly dispelled); (f) same for aversion, confusion, pride, discursive objects; (g) sitting in quiet room, pliancy arises quickly; (h) not oppressed by physical heaviness; (i) hindrances (五蓋) do not frequently manifest; (j) longing, displeasure, worry-accompanied states do not frequently manifest; (k) even after rising from meditation, residual pliancy accompanies body and mind during walking. |

## Structural Summary

Fascicle 32 has a single continuous instructional arc: a teacher (善達瑜伽師) addresses a beginner (初修業者) with the designation "Worthy One" (賢首). The structure is:

1. **Five signs framework** (T30.0459b25-0460c01): disgust, delight, danger, luminosity, discernment
2. **Shamatha entry and oil-bowl simile** (T30.0460c01-0461b01)
3. **Shamatha-vipashyana applied to five meditation objects**:
   - Asubha/不淨觀 (T30.0461b01-0462b01) -- most detailed treatment
   - Maitri/慈愍觀 (T30.0463a01-0463b01)
   - Pratityasamutpada/緣性緣起觀 (T30.0463b01-0463c01)
   - Dhatu-prabheda/界差別觀 (T30.0463c01-0464b01)
   - Anapanasmrti/入出息念 (T30.0464b01-0464c01)
4. **Universal shamatha companion** (T30.0464c01)
5. **Attainment of pliancy and one-pointedness** (T30.0464c01-0465a01)
6. **"Possessed of attention" status and its characteristics** (T30.0465a01)

No uddana verse in this fascicle. The entire fascicle is one sustained teaching passage within the Third Yoga Place.

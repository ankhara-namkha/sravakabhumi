## 4. Issues Requiring Action

### Issue #1: "Discursive thought" for 尋思 / vitarka — MISMATCH with glossary

**Severity:** Medium

The glossary entry for 尋 / vitarka prescribes "examination" or "investigation." The translation renders 尋思增上 as "dominated by discursive thought" with Sanskrit *vitarka*.

**Analysis:** The Chinese 尋思 (xunsi) in the context of 尋思增上補特伽羅 (persons dominated by discursive thought) is a character-type category distinct from the dhyana-factor 尋 (vitarka = examination). In the source text, 尋思 refers to habitual mental proliferation and discursive thinking, not the meditative factor of "examination" used in dhyana analysis. "Discursive thought" is arguably the correct rendering for this person-type context, while "examination" is correct for the dhyana-factor context.

**Recommendation:** Add a context-differentiated glossary entry:
- 尋 (as dhyana factor) / vitarka -> "examination"
- 尋思 (as disposition/person-type) / vitarka -> "discursive thought"

This preserves both the dhyana-factor meaning and the character-type meaning. The glossary should note the distinction.

### Issue #2: "suffering (duhkha)" first-use pattern

**Severity:** Low

The first appearance of "suffering" is in item 17 (line 105): "they reach the limit of suffering (*duhkha*)." The glossary prescribes first-use pattern "suffering (duhkha)." This is provided, so it is technically compliant. However, the parenthetical Sanskrit appears at the end of a long sentence and could be missed. No action required, but noted for awareness.

### Issue #3: Plural "pleasant abidings" vs. singular glossary entry

**Severity:** Low

Line 129 uses "pleasant abidings in the present life" (plural). The glossary entry has singular: "pleasant abiding in the present life." The Chinese 現法樂住 is grammatically ambiguous for number. The plural form is used here because the context describes meditative attainments generally.

**Recommendation:** Update glossary to note that both singular and plural forms are acceptable: "pleasant abiding(s) in the present life."

### Issue #4: "Shravaka Ground" in section title — partially compliant

**Severity:** Low

Line 13: "Basic Section: Shravaka Ground, Second Yoga Place, Part One." The watchlist says: use "shravaka" in title/technical use, "hearer" acceptable in general context with note. The translation correctly uses "Shravaka" in the title. Compliant. However, the overall document title (line 1) uses "The Hearer's Ground" — this is the design-invariants prescribed title ("Sravakabhumi (The Hearer's Ground)"). The title on line 1 is "Sravakabhumi: The Hearer's Ground" which is correct per design-invariants.md.

**No action needed.** This is compliant.

### Issue #5: "dhyana" lacks first-use Sanskrit parenthetical

**Severity:** Low

Line 127: "after being born upward into the first dhyana" — this is the first appearance of "dhyana" in the fascicle. Per glossary convention, first use should be "the first dhyana (*dhyana*)" or, per the glossary first-use pattern, "the dhyanas (meditative absorptions)." Since it appears here as "the first dhyana" without a parenthetical explanation, it is technically non-compliant with the first-use convention.

**Recommendation:** Amend line 127 to: "the first dhyana (meditative absorption)" on this first occurrence.

### Issue #6: "arhat/arhatship" lacks first-use Sanskrit parenthetical

**Severity:** Low

Line 91: "one practicing toward the fruit of arhatship" — first use of "arhat/arhatship" in the fascicle. No Sanskrit parenthetical. Per glossary, "arhat" is already Sanskrit and self-glossing (like "nirvana"), but the convention is to provide a parenthetical on first use.

**Recommendation:** Amend to "the fruit of arhatship (*arhat*)" on first occurrence, or consider that "arhat" is already Sanskrit and self-explanatory. Low priority.

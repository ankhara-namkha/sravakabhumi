## Category 2: Rendered Differently from Glossary

### 2a. "greedy temperament" vs. glossary rendering

**Location:** 32.2 body text and footnote [^teacher_frame]

**Translation uses:** "of the greedy temperament (*raga-carita*)"

**Glossary entry (line 186):**
`| 貪行 | raga-carita | greed-conduct | greed-conduct |`

**Glossary entry (line 304):**
`| 貪增上者 | raga-carita | person dominated by greed |`

**Analysis:** The translation renders 貪行/貪增上者 as "greedy temperament" — neither "greed-conduct" (the glossary's preferred rendering for 貪行) nor "person dominated by greed" (for 貪增上者). "Temperament" is not a registered rendering for raga-carita anywhere in the glossary.

**Severity:** Moderate. "Greedy temperament" is semantically close and readable, but it deviates from glossary convention. If the Chinese here is 貪行, the rendering should be "greed-conduct" or "of greed-conduct type." If it is 貪增上者, "person dominated by greed" is the registered form. The Sanskrit parenthetical (*raga-carita*) is correct, but the English gloss conflicts with the glossary.

**Recommended fix:** Change to "of greed-conduct (*raga-carita*)" or "dominated by greed (*raga-carita*)" depending on which Chinese character is in the source.

### 2b. Nine-stages footnote: stage-name mismatches

**Location:** Footnote [^nine_stages_ref] in 32.4

**Translation uses (in footnote):**
- "(1) abiding (*sthapana*)"
- "(2) continuous abiding (*samsthapana*)"
- "(3) patchy abiding (*avasthapana*)"

**Glossary entries (lines 520–522):**
- `| 內住 | citta-sthapana | inward abiding |` — Stage 1
- `| 等住 | samsthapana | settled abiding |` — Stage 2
- `| 安住 | avasthapana | re-established abiding |` — Stage 3

**Three discrepancies:**

1. Stage 1: Translation calls it "abiding (*sthapana*)" — glossary gives Sanskrit as *citta-sthapana* and English as "inward abiding." The footnote uses the wrong Sanskrit (bare *sthapana* instead of *citta-sthapana*) and the wrong English ("abiding" instead of "inward abiding").

2. Stage 2: Translation calls it "continuous abiding (*samsthapana*)" — glossary gives English as "settled abiding." "Continuous abiding" is not the registered rendering.

3. Stage 3: Translation calls it "patchy abiding (*avasthapana*)" — glossary gives English as "re-established abiding." "Patchy abiding" is not the registered rendering and is arguably misleading (implies fragmented/sporadic abiding rather than the glossary's "re-established" = re-collecting after distraction).

**Severity:** Moderate. These are in footnotes, but they conflict directly with established glossary renderings for the nine stages, which are a core shamatha framework repeatedly used throughout the project.

**Recommended fix:** Update footnote [^nine_stages_ref] to use glossary-aligned renderings:
- Stage 1: "inward abiding (*citta-sthapana*)"
- Stage 2: "settled abiding (*samsthapana*)"
- Stage 3: "re-established abiding (*avasthapana*)"

## Category 5: Missing First-Use Glosses

### 5a. "shamatha" and "vipashyana" — no first-use gloss in this fascicle

**Location:** 32.4 (line 87): "whether in the shamatha mode or the vipashyana mode"

**Glossary convention:** First use in each fascicle requires "shamatha (calm abiding)" and "vipashyana (insight)."

**Issue:** This is the first mention of both shamatha and vipashyana in fascicle 32, and neither carries its parenthetical English gloss.

**Severity:** Minor-Moderate. This is a per-fascicle convention. By fascicle 32 the reader will know these terms, but the convention is registered in the glossary for each fascicle.

**Recommended fix:** Change to "shamatha (calm abiding)" and "vipashyana (insight)" on first occurrence.

---

### 5b. "shila" — no first-use gloss

**Location:** 32.3 (line 67): "Examine your own moral discipline (*shila*)"

**Glossary convention:** First use: "shila (ethical discipline)" or "shila / ethical discipline."

**Issue:** The text uses "moral discipline (*shila*)" — the parenthetical has the Sanskrit but uses "moral discipline" as the English gloss instead of the registered "ethical discipline" or "shila."

**Glossary entry (line 28):** `| 戒 | shila | shila / ethical discipline | shila or discipline |`

**Analysis:** "Moral discipline" is a reasonable rendering but not the registered one. More importantly, the structure is inverted: in the body text the Sanskrit appears parenthetically after the English gloss ("moral discipline (*shila*)"), which is the correct first-use pattern. However, the English gloss should match the glossary: "ethical discipline (*shila*)" rather than "moral discipline (*shila*)."

**Severity:** Minor. The Sanskrit parenthetical is correct; only the English companion word needs adjustment.

**Recommended fix:** Change "moral discipline (*shila*)" to "ethical discipline (*shila*)."

---

### 5c. "non-regret" (*avipratisara*) — missing Sanskrit on first use in this fascicle

**Location:** 32.3 (line 69): "pure discipline gives rise to non-regret"

**Glossary entry (line 591):** `| 無悔 | avipratisara | non-regret / freedom from remorse | non-regret |`

**Issue:** "Non-regret" appears multiple times in 32.3 without the Sanskrit parenthetical (*avipratisara*) on first use. The term is technical and glossary-registered; it should be introduced as "non-regret (*avipratisara*)" on first occurrence in the fascicle.

The Sanskrit appears in footnote [^non_regret_sequence] but not in the body text where the term first appears.

**Severity:** Minor. The footnote provides context, but per-fascicle first-use convention calls for the parenthetical in running text.

**Recommended fix:** On first body-text occurrence, render as "non-regret (*avipratisara*)."

## Category 6: Additional Observations (Not Glossary Violations, But Worth Noting)

### 6a. "pramodya" and "priti" — used only in footnote, not in running text

**Location:** Footnote [^non_regret_sequence] (32.3)

The footnote names the sequence: *avipratisara* → *pramodya* → *priti*, and maps them to:
- 歡悅 (gladness, *pramodya*)
- 喜悅 (joy, *priti*)

**Observation:** "Gladness" and "joy" are the English renderings used in the body text (lines 69–75). These are not currently in the glossary as standalone entries (though *priti-bodhyanga* = "joy factor of awakening" is at line 461). The body-text renderings "gladness" and "joy" for *pramodya* and *priti* are internally consistent within this section and match established Buddhist lexical convention (e.g., Nanamoli). No action required unless the glossary maintainer wants to add these as standalone entries.

### 6b. "Tathagata" — used correctly, capitalized, no parenthetical needed

**Location:** 32.3 (line 73): "the Tathagatas and the noble disciples"

**Glossary entry (line 76):** Tathagata — preferred in text, Sanskrit preserved per NTC register. Usage is correct.

### 6c. "Worthy One" (*bhadanta*) — used correctly

**Location:** Throughout 32.1–32.4

**Glossary entry (line 588):** `| 賢首 | — | Worthy One | Worthy One |`

The translation parenthetical in footnote [^teacher_frame] gives (*bhadanta*) — but the glossary entry has no Sanskrit listed (dash in Sanskrit column). The term 賢首 is an honorific of address, and *bhadanta* is a reasonable Sanskrit parallel. Not a glossary violation, but if *bhadanta* is being treated as the Sanskrit equivalent, the glossary entry could be updated to record it. Low priority.

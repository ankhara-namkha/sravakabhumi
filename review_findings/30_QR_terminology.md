## Findings by Flag Type

### FLAG 2 — Rendered Differently from Glossary Entry

**ISSUE 1 (Critical): "black category" / "white category" — should be "dark category" / "bright category"**

- Location: Line 462 — `"this is inversion — belonging to the black category: conflicted, painful, and harmful," and so forth as described above. "If toward them I do not generate anger, this is non-inversion — belonging to the white category: free from conflict, free from suffering and harm"`
- Glossary entry:
  - 黑品 | krishna-paksha | **dark category** | dark category
  - 白品 | shukla-paksha | **bright category** | bright category
- The translation uses "black category" and "white category" — both are non-standard renderings that deviate from the established glossary entries "dark category" and "bright category."
- Severity: Moderate. The meaning is not obscured, but consistency with the glossary and with how this pair is rendered elsewhere in the translation is required. Note: the same deviation appears in section 30.15 (line 410), suggesting a systematic error in this portion of the fascicle, not isolated to 30.16.

**ISSUE 2 (Minor): "six-aspect vipashyana" — not in glossary, but rendered consistently**

- The phrase "six-aspect vipashyana" (and "six aspects of its objects") is used throughout sections 30.13–30.16. This framework is not listed in the glossary as a standalone entry.
- The rendering is internally consistent and compositionally transparent (six aspects + vipashyana, both of which are in the glossary).
- Recommendation: Add a glossary entry for 六行相毘鉢舍那 or equivalent Chinese to document the canonical English rendering for future use. Not a translation error; a documentation gap.

### FLAG 1 — Not in Glossary

**ISSUE 3 (Minor): "inversion" / "non-inversion" — used in section without standalone glossary entry for this usage**

- The term "inversion" (顛倒) does appear in the glossary in compound form: 四顛倒 | catur-viparyasa | four inversions. The standalone use of "inversion" as a category label (without the "four" qualifier) in the six-aspect vipashyana framework is not covered by a dedicated entry.
- The rendering "inversion" / "non-inversion" is compositionally consistent with the glossary entry for 四顛倒 and is not a deviation.
- Recommendation: Acceptable as is; the existing entry is sufficient. No action required.

**ISSUE 4 (Minor): "continuums of others"**

- The phrase "they belong to the continuums of others" (likely 他相續) is used once (line 440). "Continuum" (相續 / santana) is not listed as a standalone glossary entry.
- The rendering is standard in Buddhist translation literature and compositionally coherent.
- Recommendation: Consider adding 相續 | santana | continuum to glossary for documentation completeness. Not a translation error.

**ISSUE 5 (Minor): "Samyutta Nikaya" in footnote [^equality_argument]**

- The footnote cites the Samyutta Nikaya (Pali: SN 15.14–19). This is a Pali text title. The corresponding Sanskrit designation is Samyukta Agama / Samyukta Nikaya, but "Samyutta Nikaya" is the standard scholarly citation form for the Pali collection and is universally recognized as such in Buddhist scholarship.
- This is a citation, not a translation of a Chinese term, so NTC Sanskrit conventions do not apply to text-title citations.
- No flag required. The usage is correct for a scholarly footnote.

**ISSUE 6 (Minor): "bodhicitta" in footnote [^equality_argument]**

- "bodhicitta" appears in the footnote. It is not listed in the glossary.
- The footnote discusses Mahayana maitri and bodhicitta practice, contextualizing the text's argument. The usage of "bodhicitta" here is the translator's comparative note, not a translation of the Chinese source text.
- Bodhicitta as a Sanskrit term is universally recognized and its absence from the glossary is a documentation gap rather than an error. If the term appears in source-text translation, it would need a glossary entry; in a translator's comparative footnote, its use is acceptable.
- Recommendation: Low priority; no change required for section 30.16. If bodhicitta appears in the source text for a later fascicle, add it at that time.

### FLAG 3 — Deprecated per Watchlist

**ISSUE 7 (Critical): "sentient beings" used for 有情 — ACCEPTABLE; potentially problematic if applied to 補特伽羅**

- Watchlist rule: "sentient being (for 補特伽羅)" → should be "person / individual"
- In section 30.16, "sentient beings" appears multiple times:
  - Line 436: "giving happiness to all sentient beings" — if Chinese is 有情, this is CORRECT per glossary (有情 | sattva | sentient being(s), being(s)).
  - Line 452: "all sentient beings without exception are subject to birth, aging, sickness, death"
  - Line 454: "embrace all sentient beings as my own"
  - Line 460: "toward all sentient beings a mind of equality"
- The context throughout is the maitri meditation, which extends to all living beings (有情 / sattva), not to individuals in the Abhidharma pudgala sense. The use of "sentient beings" is therefore correct and consistent with the glossary distinction.
- No flag required, but a source-fidelity verification of the underlying Chinese would confirm whether these passages use 有情 or 補特伽羅. This is within the scope of Agent S.

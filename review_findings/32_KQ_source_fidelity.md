## Detailed Findings

### Section 32.11 — Conduct Between Sessions; The Fire-Drill Simile

**Source:** T30.0462c01 first paragraph (如是汝由依不淨觀...世出世間廣大圓滿)
**Translation:** Lines 261-287

#### (1) Omissions

**S-1 [Minor].** Chinese: 善知其量受用飲食 ("know the proper measure in taking food and drink"). English: "know the proper measure in taking food." The word 飲食 means "food and drink" — "drink" is omitted. Very minor since "food" can be understood broadly, but the Chinese is explicit.

**S-2 [Minor].** Chinese: 又汝應與在家出家說應量語說應理語說應時語說正直語說寂靜語. The Chinese lists five qualities of speech: (1) 應量語 measured, (2) 應理語 reasonable, (3) 應時語 timely, (4) 正直語 straightforward, (5) 寂靜語 peaceful. English: "speak words that are measured, reasonable, timely, straightforward, and peaceful." All five are present. NO ISSUE — this was a false alarm on closer inspection.

**S-3 [Minor].** Chinese: 於奢摩他毘鉢舍那如所取相 — "regarding the previously apprehended signs of shamatha and vipashyana." English: "Regarding the signs of shamatha and vipashyana previously apprehended." MATCH.

**S-4 [Minor].** Chinese: 若為此義受習於斷 ("If you train in abandonment for this goal"). English: "If you train in abandonment for this goal." MATCH.

**S-5 [Minor].** Chinese: 汝當最初證得下劣身心輕安心一境性 — "you will first realize inferior/initial body-mind pliancy and mental one-pointedness." English: "First you will realize an initial degree of physical and mental pliancy and mental one-pointedness." The word 下劣 literally means "inferior" or "low-grade," which the translation renders as "an initial degree." This is a reasonable interpretive rendering — 下劣 here means the first, preliminary degree of attainment, not that it is worthless. Acceptable.

#### (2) Additions

**S-6 [Minor].** English footnote [^between_sessions] adds: "Five elements are specified: guarding the body from physical dangers, guarding the senses from unwholesome objects, dwelling in right mindfulness, attending properly, and moderation in food." This is an editorial summary, not an addition to the translation text. Acceptable as a translator's note.

#### (3) Mistranslations

**S-7 [Minor].** Chinese: 隨依彼彼村邑聚落邊際而住 — "dwelling at the edge of whatever village or settlement." English: "at the edge of whatever village or settlement is your home." The Chinese does not say "is your home" — 邊際而住 means "dwelling at the edge/periphery." Adding "is your home" is a minor interpretive addition.

**S-8 [Minor].** Chinese: 於心隨順趣向臨入所緣境界汝應捨此所緣境相入彼村邑聚落乞食 — "when the mind has followed, inclined toward, and entered the meditation object, you should set aside this object-sign and enter the village to beg for food." English: "even when the mind has followed, inclined toward, and entered the meditation object, at the proper time you should set the object aside and enter the village to beg for food." The phrase "at the proper time" is not in the Chinese. Minor interpretive addition. The Chinese simply says: when you are in meditation, set the object aside and go beg.

#### (4) Structural Infidelity

None identified. The passage flows naturally and preserves the instructional voice (汝應 = "you should") throughout.

#### (5) Taisho References

[T30.0462c01] correctly placed at the beginning of section 32.11. CORRECT.

### Section 32.12 — The Remaining Four Objects; Loving-Kindness Contemplation

**Source:** T30.0463a01-T30.0463b (first half)
**Translation:** Lines 291-329

#### (1) Omissions

**S-9 [Moderate].** Chinese: 如說貪行是不淨觀之所調伏如是瞋行是慈愍觀之所調伏乃至最後尋思行是阿那波那念之所調伏 — "As stated: the greedy type is tamed by impurity contemplation; the angry type is tamed by loving-kindness contemplation; **and so on through** to the discursive-thought type, which is tamed by mindfulness of breathing." The Chinese uses 乃至最後 ("up through the last"), implying the full series of five types. The English renders this correctly with "and so on through the remaining types, to the discursive-thought type." MATCH — no omission.

**S-10 [Minor].** Chinese: 餘趣入門我當顯示 — "I shall now show the remaining entry points." English: "The remaining entry points are now shown." The shift from first-person (我當顯示, "I shall show") to passive ("are now shown") is a minor voice change. The Chinese still carries the teacher's first-person voice from section 32.11. Not an omission per se, but a structural shift.

**S-11 [Minor].** Chinese: 依慈愍觀初修業者於外親品怨品及中庸品善取相已處如法坐 — "having well apprehended the signs of friends, enemies, and neutral persons in the external world, sit in a **proper** seat." The Chinese 如法坐 means "dharma-conforming seat" / "proper seat." English has "sit in a proper seat." MATCH.

**S-12 [Minor].** Chinese: 謂或無罪欲樂或無罪有喜樂或無罪無喜樂 — three types of happiness. English: "whether blameless desire-realm happiness, blameless happiness with joy, or blameless happiness without joy." The Chinese 欲樂 doesn't explicitly say "desire-realm" — it says "desire-happiness" (sensory happiness). The translation's addition of "desire-realm" is an interpretation. The footnote correctly explains it, so this is acceptable but the Chinese is not as specific as "desire-realm."

**S-13 [Minor].** Chinese: 如於親品如是於怨及中庸品當知亦爾 — "as with friends, so with enemies and the neutral — understand it is the same." English: "As with friends, so with enemies and the neutral." MATCH.

#### (2) Additions

No significant additions detected.

#### (3) Mistranslations

**S-14 [Minor].** Chinese: 壞緣念住. English: "foundations with comprehensive referent." The character 壞 means "destroy / break apart / comprehensive" — in this context it means the meditation that takes all skandhas as its object (breaks down person-categories into skandhas). "Comprehensive referent" is a reasonable interpretation, though "composite referent" might be more precise. The footnote explains the term adequately.

#### (4) Structural Infidelity

**S-15 [Minor].** The Chinese presents the loving-kindness contemplation, the transition to real contemplation, and the merit comparison as one continuous flow. The English breaks it into bold-headed subsections ("Entry into the loving-kindness contemplation," "Entering the foundations of mindfulness through loving-kindness," "Transition to real contemplation"). This is acceptable for readability, but the Chinese has no such subdivisions. The bold headings are editorial additions.

**S-25 [Minor].** Chinese: 由是吞咽於是吞咽既吞咽已由是下分不淨流出 — "through which one swallows, [food is] swallowed, and after swallowing, impurity flows downward from the lower parts." **S-26 [Minor].** Chinese T30.0464a01: 亦於十種所造諸色而起勝解 — "one also generates attention regarding the ten types of derived form." English: "one also attends to the ten types of derived form through the analysis of bases and objects." MATCH.

**S-27 [Moderate].** Chinese: 復有異門謂於己身而起勝解臨捨命時如前廣說至青瘀位或復膿爛 — "There is an alternative approach: generate attention regarding your own body at the time of approaching death, as broadly explained before, reaching the livid stage or festering."

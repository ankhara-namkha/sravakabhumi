## Findings

### 29.13: Person Typology / Behavioral Types / Thin-Dust Person

#### F1. [m] Minor addition — "discursive-thought conduct"
- **Chinese (140):** 貪瞋癡慢尋思行者 — "those of greed, aversion, delusion, pride, and discursive-thought conduct"
- **English (352):** "those of greed, aversion, delusion, pride, and discursive-thought conduct"
- **Verdict:** Accurate. 尋思 = discursive thought. No issue.

#### F2. [m] Addition — footnote marker `[^thin_dust_qualities]`
- **Chinese:** No note at this location.
- **English (352):** Footnote `[^thin_dust_qualities]` placed at end of first sentence.
- **Verdict:** Acceptable — this is a translator's note marker, not content addition. Minor. Confirm the footnote itself does not insert doctrinal content not in the source.

#### F3. [C] Mistranslation — "victorious shramana" for 勝道沙門
- **Chinese (164):** 勝道沙門 — literally "supreme/victorious path shramana"
- **English (400):** "the victorious shramana"
- **Issue:** The Chinese is 勝**道**沙門, meaning "shramana of the victorious/supreme **path**," not simply "victorious shramana." The word 道 (path/way) is part of all four compound names (勝道, 說道, 活道, 壞道). The translation renders the other three as "teaches the path," "lives by the path," "corrupts the path" — all preserving 道. But the first drops 道 entirely. Should be "shramana who has conquered the path" or "shramana of the supreme path" for consistency.
- **Severity:** Critical — structural asymmetry in a fourfold set, and the meaning shifts (personal attribute "victorious" vs. path-related "having conquered/mastered the path").

#### F4. [M] Possible mistranslation — learners/non-learners as "victorious shramana"
- **Chinese (168):** 又學無學名勝道沙門 — "learners and those beyond learning are called 勝道沙門"
- **English (404):** "learners (*shaiksha*) and those beyond learning (*ashaiksha*) are called victorious shramanas"
- **Issue:** The Chinese provides an ALTERNATIVE definition here (又 = "alternatively/also"). The first definition (line 164) said sugatas (善逝) are the victorious shramana. This second passage says learners AND non-learners are also so called. The English correctly captures this with "Alternatively:" at the paragraph start. However, combining this with the F3 issue — the term should preserve 道 (path).
- **Severity:** Major — compounds with F3.

#### F5. [M] Structural shift — bodhisattva clause in "shramana who teaches the path"
- **Chinese (168):** 若無如來及諸菩薩。為菩提故勤修正行。諸聲聞眾持三藏者。名說道沙門。
- This reads: "When there are no tathagatas AND no bodhisattvas who diligently practice for awakening, the shravaka assemblies who uphold the tripitaka are called shramanas who teach the path."
- **English (404):** "When tathagatas and bodhisattvas — those who practice right conduct for the sake of awakening — are absent, the shravakas who uphold the three baskets (*tripitaka*) serve as shramanas who teach the path"
- **Issue:** The English treats "those who practice right conduct for the sake of awakening" as an appositive modifying both tathagatas AND bodhisattvas. But in the Chinese, 為菩提故勤修正行 more naturally modifies only bodhisattvas (who are still "practicing for awakening"). Tathagatas have already attained it. This is a minor exegetical question — both readings are defensible, but the English flattens the distinction.
- **Severity:** Major — doctrinal nuance about the tathagata/bodhisattva distinction is obscured.

#### F6. [m] Minor omission — 堪得未得。堪觸未觸。堪證未證
- **Chinese (170):** Lists the capacity separately: 有力有能堪得未得。堪觸未觸。堪證未證 — "having power and ability, able to obtain the unobtained, able to touch the untouched, able to realize the unrealized"
- **English (406):** "with the power and ability to do so"
- **Issue:** The Chinese repeats the threefold formula (obtain/touch/realize) for the "able to" clause as well, and the English compresses it into "to do so." The initial threefold aspiration is translated fully ("striving to obtain the unobtained, realize the unrealized, and witness the unwitnessed"), but the matching capability clause is compressed.
- **Severity:** Minor — the meaning is implied, but the rhetorical emphasis of the Chinese (which deliberately echoes the aspiration with the capability) is lost.

#### F7. [m] "witness" vs. "realize" for 證
- **Chinese (170):** 為證未證 ... 堪證未證 — 證 appears twice, both times the same character.
- **English (406):** "witness the unwitnessed" (first) and compressed in "to do so" (second)
- **Issue:** 證 is rendered as "witness" in the first triplet but note that earlier in the text 證 is translated as "realize" (e.g., "realize the unrealized"). The Chinese uses the same character throughout. "Witness" is a valid rendering but breaks consistency with the standard translation of 證 elsewhere in the fascicle.
- **Severity:** Minor — stylistic, but could cause confusion about whether two different terms are being used.

#### F8. [m] Minor addition — "trainable nature" for 其性調善
- **Chinese (170):** 若諸異生補特伽羅其性調善 — "ordinary persons whose nature is tame/disciplined and good"
- **English (406):** "An ordinary person (*prthag-jana*) of trainable nature"
- **Issue:** 調善 means "tamed/disciplined and good" — suggesting the nature is already tamed, not merely "trainable" (which suggests potential). However, "trainable" is a reasonable interpretive rendering given the context (these are beings who can still progress). Minor interpretive choice.
- **Severity:** Minor.

#### F9. [m] "shame and remorse" for 有羞有悔
- **Chinese (170):** 有羞有悔
- **English (406):** "possessing shame and remorse"
- **Issue:** 羞 and 悔 correspond to hri (shame) and apatrapya (embarrassment/moral dread). "Remorse" could be confused with kaukrtya (regret/worry), which is a different term. Standard rendering: "shame and moral dread" or "conscience and concern for consequences." However, in context, the meaning is clear enough.
- **Severity:** Minor.

#### F10. [m] "non-Buddhist" not in Chinese for 外沙門婆羅門教
- **Chinese (174):** 於外沙門婆羅門教 — "among the teachings of external shramanas and brahmins"
- **English (410):** "among the teachings of non-Buddhist shramanas and brahmins"
- **Issue:** 外 means "external/outside" — rendering as "non-Buddhist" is interpretive but standard and defensible. Minor.
- **Severity:** Minor.

#### F11. [m] Rendering of 名想 as "by designation"
- **Chinese (176):** 名想婆羅門 — literally "name-concept brahmin"
- **English (414):** "brahmin by designation"
- **Chinese explanation:** 謂諸世間由想等想。假立言說名婆羅門 — "the world through concepts and equivalent concepts provisionally establishes by speech the name 'brahmin'"
- **English:** "one whom the world, through concept and convention, provisionally names a brahmin"
- **Issue:** 等想 means "equivalent concept/equal perception" — rendered as "convention" which captures the idea adequately. Acceptable.
- **Severity:** Minor.

#### F12. [M] Omission — Verse quotation rendering for 婆羅門
- **Chinese (176):** 如說當知。婆羅門更無有所作。所作事已辨。是謂婆羅門。
- This appears to be a verse quotation (如說 = "as it is said").
- **English (414):** "as stated: 'A brahmin has nothing more to be done; the task has been accomplished. This is called a brahmin.'"
- **Issue:** The Chinese 當知 (should be known) before the quotation is dropped. Also, 所作事已辨 is rendered "the task has been accomplished" — 辨 means "distinguished/completed/resolved" which is close enough. The more significant issue is that per project rules (design-invariants.md), verse passages should be rendered as verse, not inline. However, this is a very short quotation embedded in prose and may not warrant verse formatting. Borderline.
- **Severity:** Major for the 當知 omission (small but systematic); minor for the verse formatting question.

#### F13. [m] 習婬欲法 rendered as "sexual activity"
- **Chinese (178):** 遠離一切行非梵行習婬欲法 — "abandoning all practices of non-pure-conduct, the practice of lustful desire"
- **English (418):** "the undertaking to renounce all sexual activity"
- **Issue:** The Chinese is more elaborate — 行非梵行 (practicing non-brahmacarya) 習婬欲法 (habitually engaging in lustful dharmas). The English compresses to "sexual activity" which loses the Buddhist framing. However, the meaning is preserved. Minor compression.
- **Severity:** Minor.

#### F14. [m] 數取趣 rendered as "person (*pudgala*)"
- **Chinese (184):** 如是一切是數取趣所有異門 — 數取趣 is literally "those who repeatedly take [rebirth] destinations" — a Chinese calque translation of pudgala (somewhat polemical/etymological).
- **English (434):** "all of these are alternative designations for the person (*pudgala*)"
- **Issue:** 數取趣 is Xuanzang's alternative translation of pudgala (alongside 補特伽羅 which is a transliteration). Using "person (*pudgala*)" is correct in meaning but loses the fact that Xuanzang deliberately uses a different Chinese term here (數取趣 vs. 補特伽羅 used earlier at line 162). A footnote would be ideal.
- **Severity:** Minor — the shift in Chinese terminology is lost but the meaning is preserved.

#### F15. [m] 或塗灰等增上外道 rendering
- **Chinese (184):** 或塗灰等增上外道 — "those who smear ash and other prominent/principal non-Buddhist [ascetics]"
- **English (432):** "those who smear ashes, and other similar groups"
- **Issue:** 增上 means "superior/prominent/principal" — this is dropped. The Chinese seems to say "ash-smearers and other principal non-Buddhists," while the English reduces to "and other similar groups." Minor loss.
- **Severity:** Minor.

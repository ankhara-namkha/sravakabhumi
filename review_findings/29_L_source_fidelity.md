## Sentence-by-Sentence Comparison

### Line 124: 云何修果。謂四沙門果。一預流果。二一來果。三不還果。四最上阿羅漢果。

**English (line 312):** "What are the fruits of cultivation? They are the four shramana fruits (*catur-shramana-phala*): (1) the fruit of stream-entry (*srota-apanna-phala*), (2) the fruit of once-returning (*sakridagamin-phala*), (3) the fruit of non-returning (*anagamin-phala*), and (4) the supreme fruit of arhatship (*arhat-phala*)."

**Verdict:** Accurate. All four fruits listed in correct order. Sanskrit terms are additions but appropriate scholarly apparatus. No issues.

---

### Line 126: 此中云何名沙門。云何名果。謂聖道名沙門。煩惱斷名果。又後生道或中或上。是前生道所生之果。

**English (lines 316):** "What is meant by 'shramana'? What is meant by 'fruit'? The noble path is called 'shramana.' The severance of afflictions is called 'fruit.' Furthermore, a later-arising path — whether intermediate or superior — is the fruit produced by an earlier-arising path."

**Verdict:** Accurate and complete. The 此中 ("herein/in this context") is absorbed naturally. 或中或上 correctly rendered as "whether intermediate or superior." No issues.

---

### Line 128: 問何故建立如是四果。答對治四種諸煩惱故。

**English (lines 320-322):** "Question: For what reason are exactly four fruits established? Answer: Because they counteract four kinds of afflictions."

**Verdict:** Accurate. Q&A structure preserved.

---

### Line 128 (continued): 謂諸無事能感惡趣往惡趣因。煩惱斷故。及能斷彼對治生故。立預流果。

**English (line 324):** "The fruit of stream-entry is established because the afflictions that propel one toward evil destinies — the very causes of going to evil destinies — are severed, and because the antidote that severs them arises."

- **Finding 1 (m — minor): Rendering of 無事**
  - Chinese: 謂諸無事能感惡趣往惡趣因 — The phrase 無事 is somewhat obscure here. It likely modifies the afflictions or refers to "that which has no [further] productive function" or could be a corruption. The translation renders the overall meaning as "the afflictions that propel one toward evil destinies — the very causes of going to evil destinies," which captures the core sense (能感惡趣 = able to ripen toward evil destinies; 往惡趣因 = causes of going to evil destinies) but does not specifically address 無事.
  - **Impact:** Minor. The core doctrinal content (severing afflictions that lead to evil destinies + arising of antidote = stream-entry) is preserved. The phrase 無事 is genuinely difficult and a translator's note might be warranted.

---

### Line 128 (continued): 而薄伽梵說永斷三結立此果者。謂依三品有三種結障礙聖道令不生故。一在家品。二惡說法毘奈耶品。三善說法毘奈耶品。

**English (line 324, continued):** "The Blessed One speaks of 'permanently severing the three fetters' as the basis for this fruit. The three fetters correspond to three domains of obstruction, each preventing the noble path from arising:"

- **Finding 2 (m — minor): 謂依三品有三種結 — slight paraphrase**
  - Chinese literally: "This is because dependent on three categories, there are three kinds of fetters that obstruct the noble path and prevent it from arising."
  - English: "The three fetters correspond to three domains of obstruction, each preventing the noble path from arising."
  - The causal/explanatory structure (謂...故) is slightly compressed. The Chinese says the three fetters exist *dependent on* (依) three categories, and they *obstruct* the noble path. The English says they "correspond to" three domains, which is a slight flattening of the 依 relationship. However, the full meaning is recoverable from context.
  - **Impact:** Minor. No doctrinal distortion.

### Line 130: 依在家品有薩迦耶見。由此見故先生怖畏。最初不欲發趣聖道。

**English (line 326):** "(1) The householder domain. Here, the view of the transitory collection (*satkaya-drishti*) predominates. Because of this view, one first generates fear and from the very outset does not wish to set forth toward the noble path."

- **Finding 3 (m — minor): 有 vs. "predominates"**
  - Chinese 有薩迦耶見 = "there is satkaya-drishti" (simple existence). English says it "predominates," which adds a nuance not in the Chinese. The word 有 just means "there is / exists."
  - **Impact:** Minor. The rendering conveys that this view characterizes this domain, which is contextually appropriate even if slightly interpretive.

- **Finding 4 (m — minor): 先生怖畏 phrasing**
  - Chinese: 由此見故先生怖畏 = "Because of this view, one first generates fear/dread."
  - English: "one first generates fear" — accurate.
  - No issue.

### Line 130 (continued): 依惡說法毘奈耶品有戒禁取。由此取故雖已發趣而行邪僻。由是不能生起聖道。

**English (line 328):** "(2) The domain of wrong teaching and discipline. Here, attachment to rules and observances (*shila-vrata-paramarsha*) predominates. Because of this attachment, although one has set forth, one goes astray — and therefore cannot generate the noble path."

- Same minor point about 有 vs. "predominates" as Finding 3. Consistent rendering throughout the three domains.
- 而行邪僻 = "goes astray" — adequate. 邪僻 = crooked/deviant. Good.
- 由是不能生起聖道 = "therefore cannot generate the noble path" — accurate.

**Verdict:** Accurate.

### Line 130 (continued): 依善說法毘奈耶品有疑。由此疑故雖已發趣不行邪僻。而於正道未串習故。於如實見所知事中猶預疑惑。障礙聖道不令生起。

**English (line 330):** "(3) The domain of right teaching and discipline. Here, doubt (*vicikitsa*) predominates. Because of this doubt, although one has set forth and does not go astray, one has not yet become habituated to the correct path. Regarding the matters that are truly seen and known, one still wavers and hesitates, obstructing the noble path and preventing it from arising."

- 未串習 = "not yet become habituated" — good rendering of 串習 (repeated practice / habituation).
- 於如實見所知事中 = "regarding the matters that are truly seen and known" — adequate. 如實見 = seeing as it truly is; 所知事 = matters to be known. The translation captures this.
- 猶預疑惑 = "wavers and hesitates" — good.

**Verdict:** Accurate and complete.

### Line 132: 此預流果極餘七有。由是因緣多生相續。若斷再生相續煩惱生無重續。立一來果。謂若永斷天有所攝人有所攝再生相續所有煩惱。極唯更受天有一生人有一生。故於爾時立一來果。

**English (line 336):** "A stream-entrant has at most seven further existences remaining. Because of this, many births continue in succession. When the afflictions that produce continued rebirth are severed such that births no longer repeat, the fruit of once-returning is established. That is: when one has permanently abandoned all the afflictions belonging to heavenly existence and human existence that produce continued rebirth, one will at most undergo one more heavenly birth and one more human birth. At that point the fruit of once-returning is established."

- 極餘七有 = "at most seven remaining existences" — correct.
- 多生相續 = "many births continue in succession" — correct.
- 若斷再生相續煩惱生無重續 = "when the afflictions that produce continued rebirth are severed such that births no longer repeat" — correct.
- 謂若永斷天有所攝人有所攝再生相續所有煩惱 = "when one has permanently abandoned all the afflictions belonging to heavenly existence and human existence that produce continued rebirth" — correct.
- 極唯更受天有一生人有一生 = "one will at most undergo one more heavenly birth and one more human birth" — correct.

**Verdict:** Accurate and complete. Well handled.

### Line 134: 若已永斷能感還來生此煩惱。唯於天有當可受生。即於爾時立不還果。

**English (line 338):** "When one has permanently abandoned the afflictions that cause one to return and be born again in this world, one will take rebirth only in heavenly existence. At that point the fruit of non-returning is established."

- 能感還來生此煩惱 = "the afflictions that cause one to return and be born in this [world]" — correct. 還來 = return; 生此 = be born here.
- 唯於天有當可受生 = "will take rebirth only in heavenly existence" — correct.

**Verdict:** Accurate.

### Line 138: 而薄伽梵說永斷三結。縛貪瞋癡立一來果。永斷能順五下分結立不還果。永斷一切煩惱究竟建立最上阿羅漢果。是名修果。

**English (line 344):** "The Blessed One also speaks of permanently severing the three fetters and thinning greed, aversion, and delusion as the basis for the fruit of once-returning; of permanently severing the five lower fetters (*pancha-avarabhagiya-samyojana*) as the basis for the fruit of non-returning; and of permanently and completely severing all afflictions as the basis for the supreme fruit of arhatship. These are called the fruits of cultivation."

- **Finding 5 (M — major): 縛 rendered as "thinning" without inline justification**
  - Chinese: 縛貪瞋癡 — the character 縛 means "bound/fettered." The translation renders this as "thinning" (薄), following the footnote [^thin_or_bound] which argues this is a textual variant for 薄. The footnote provides solid justification (Pali parallels, Shukla Sanskrit edition).
  - However, the inline translation silently emends the text. The Chinese as printed says 縛 (bound), not 薄 (thinned). Best practice would be to note the emendation inline (e.g., "thinning [reading 薄 for 縛]") or to translate the text as it stands and note the variant in a footnote. The footnote does exist and is thorough, but a reader who doesn't check footnotes will not know the translation departs from the printed Taisho text.
  - **Impact:** Major. The translation silently departs from the source text. The justification is sound, but transparency requires at minimum an inline signal.

- 能順五下分結 = "five lower fetters" — 能順 ("concordant with" / "that accord with the lower [realms]") is absorbed into the standard designation "five lower fetters." This is standard practice and acceptable.
- 究竟 = "completely" — captured in "permanently and completely."
- 是名修果 = "These are called the fruits of cultivation" — correct.

**Verdict:** One major finding on the 縛/薄 emendation. Otherwise accurate.

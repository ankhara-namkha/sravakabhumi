## Methodology

Every technical term, Sanskrit term, and Buddhist term in the running text and footnotes of sections 27.4–27.6 was extracted and cross-referenced against (a) the master glossary entries, (b) the preferred-in-text and first-use-pattern columns, and (c) the watchlist of deprecated renderings. Terms are listed with severity ratings:

- **CRITICAL**: Deprecated rendering, Pali form used for Sanskrit term, or term flatly contradicts glossary specification
- **MODERATE**: First-use pattern violation, missing Sanskrit gloss on first occurrence, inconsistent rendering across the fascicle, or term used in a way deviating from glossary notes column
- **MINOR**: Term not yet in glossary (but used correctly or neutrally), annotation-only issues, or style-convention deviations

**ISSUE T-01**
**Term:** "the elements are eighteen" / "elements" (for 界/dhatu)
**Location:** Line 316, section 27.4 running text
**Severity:** MODERATE
**Finding:** The term "elements" (dhatu) is used here for the eighteen dhatus without a first-use parenthetical giving the Sanskrit. The glossary entry for 界 (dhatu) specifies: "First use: 'the twelve ayatanas (sense bases)'" as a model first-use pattern (for 處), and for dhatu: "element or realm — context 六界 = 'six elements'; 欲界 = 'desire realm'." The footnote [^eighteen_dhatus] does clarify this is a different dhatu enumeration (18, not 6), but the running text at its first mention in 27.4 — "The elements are eighteen" — introduces this distinct Abhidharma usage of dhatu (the eighteen-element triad scheme) with no Sanskrit gloss in running text. Earlier in the fascicle (27.2), dhatu was introduced in the six-element context. The eighteen-element usage is a materially different sense of dhatu, warranting a parenthetical on first use in 27.4 such as "the eighteen elements (dhatus)."
**Required fix:** Add "(dhatus)" on first occurrence in 27.4 running text: "The elements (*dhatus*) are eighteen." The footnote alone is insufficient per glossary convention.

**ISSUE T-04**
**Term:** "name-and-form" for 名色 / nama-rupa
**Location:** Line 330, section 27.4 dependent origination chain
**Severity:** MODERATE
**Finding:** The text renders 名色 as "name-and-form" in the dependent origination chain. This is a standard and widely accepted rendering, but nama-rupa / 名色 does not appear in the master glossary. Given that dependent origination is a key skillful object in 27.4 and the full twelve links are enumerated, all twelve links should be represented in the glossary. Currently the glossary covers: ignorance (無明/avidya), formations (行/samskara), consciousness (識/vijnana), craving (愛/trishna), grasping (取/upadana), becoming (有/bhava), birth (生/jati), aging and death (老死/jara-marana). Missing from glossary: name-and-form (名色/nama-rupa), six sense bases (六入/sadayatana), contact (觸/sparsha), feeling (受/vedana — present but as skandha, not as dependent origination link).
**Required fix:** Add nama-rupa / "name-and-form" to glossary. Add sparsha / "contact" to glossary. Add sadayatana / "six sense bases" (in dependent origination link sense) to glossary.

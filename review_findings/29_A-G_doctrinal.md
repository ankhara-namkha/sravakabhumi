## Findings

### Finding 1: Discipline-abandonment / Abandonment-abandonment mapping (Section 29.1)

**Lines 89-93 of translation:**
> (1) Discipline-abandonment (samvara-pradhana): regarding already-arisen evil and unwholesome dharmas, for the purpose of their abandonment...
> (2) Abandonment-abandonment (pradhana-pradhana): regarding unarisen evil and unwholesome dharmas, for the purpose of preventing their arising...

**Verdict:** m (minor -- correct but potentially confusing)

**Analysis:** The mapping here is: discipline-abandonment = abandoning arisen unwholesome; abandonment-abandonment = preventing unarisen unwholesome. This matches the Chinese source precisely (律儀斷 = 已生惡不善法為令斷; 斷斷 = 未生惡不善法為不生). At first glance, a reader might expect the numbering to follow the standard four-effort order (prevent unarisen unwholesome first, then abandon arisen), but the Sravakabhumi deliberately reverses the order here to align with the etymological explanations that follow. The translation correctly reflects the source. The footnote [^four_abandonments] does clarify this mapping. No change needed; the footnote handles the potential confusion adequately.

**Recommendation:** No action needed. The footnote already explains the correspondence.

### Finding 2: Entanglements vs. latent tendencies distinction (Section 29.2)

**Lines 113-123 of translation (four samadhis section):**

The translation consistently maintains the doctrinal distinction between removing "actively manifesting entanglements" (paryavasthana / 纏) and "permanently destroying latent tendencies" (anushaya / 隨眠). Each of the four samadhis is described as accomplishing only the former. This is doctrinally precise and reflects the Yogacara-Sarvastivada two-level affliction model correctly.

**Verdict:** No issue. This is a commendation.

**Analysis:** The Abhidharmakosha and Yogacarabhumi both distinguish paryavasthana (active, manifest entanglements) from anushaya (latent, dormant tendencies). The translation's consistent use of "entanglements" for the manifest level and "latent tendencies" for the dormant level correctly tracks this critical Sarvastivada doctrinal distinction. The investigation-samadhi passage (line 123) correctly states that one gains "liberation of mind from entanglements only" but "not yet gained liberation of mind from all latent tendencies whatsoever" -- this is doctrinally precise.

### Finding 3: "Defilement" used once in section 29.3 (line 149)

**Line 149:**
> the mind is free from defilement -- mental equipoise

**Chinese:** 心無染污心平等性

**Verdict:** m (minor)

**Analysis:** The watchlist flags "defilements" (for 煩惱) as wrong, with "afflictions" as the correct rendering. However, here 染污 is not 煩惱. The Chinese 染污 means "stain/pollution/defilement" in a more general sense -- the mind being free from defilement/pollution. This is a different word from 煩惱 (klesha/afflictions), so using "defilement" here is not technically a watchlist violation. However, for register consistency across the translation, "free from stain" or "unstained" might be slightly preferable to avoid any confusion with the Theravada register issue flagged in the watchlist.

**Recommendation:** Consider "the mind is free from stain -- mental equipoise" or "the mind is unstained -- mental equipoise." This is cosmetic, not doctrinal. Low priority.

### Finding 4: Eightfold path and three trainings classification (Section 29.7 context, noted in footnote)

**Line 252:**
> right view, right thought, and right effort belong to the training of wisdom (prajna). Right speech, right action, and right livelihood belong to the training of discipline (shila). Right mindfulness and right samadhi belong to the training of samadhi.

**Verdict:** m (minor -- correct but needs awareness)

**Analysis:** The standard Theravada classification (found in Bodhi's In the Buddha's Words, p. 2829: "the concentration group made up of right effort, right mindfulness, and right concentration; the wisdom group made up of right view and right intention") places right effort in the samadhi group and right view + right intention in the prajna group. The Sravakabhumi's classification -- placing right effort in the prajna group alongside right view and right thought -- follows the Yogacara-Sarvastivada Abhidharma classification, not the Theravada one. The footnote [^eightfold_path] correctly notes: "The assignment of right effort to the prajna training (rather than samadhi) and right mindfulness to the samadhi training follows the Yogacara-Sarvastivada Abhidharma classification."

This is doctrinally accurate to the source tradition. The footnote handles the potential confusion well. No change needed.

**Recommendation:** No action needed. The footnote is adequate.

### Finding 5: Four verified purities and causal vs. identity relationship (Section 29.7)

**Line 197:**
> Therefore the Blessed One, in accordance with the principle of causal relationship, states that one should regard them as being those verified purities -- not that they are identical in substance, not that they are identical in characteristic.

**Chinese:** 是故世尊就其因果相屬道理。說言當觀即彼證淨。非即彼體非即彼相。

**Verdict:** No issue. This is a commendation.

**Analysis:** This passage articulates a subtle and important Sarvastivada-Yogacara doctrinal point: the faith faculty/power is the *cause* of the four verified purities (as adhipati-phala / dominant result), not identical to them in substance (體) or characteristic (相). This distinction between causal correlation and identity is central to Abhidharma analysis and is frequently misunderstood. The translation renders this precisely and the footnote [^correlation] explains it clearly. This is doctrinally excellent.

### Finding 6: Samadhi faculty/power correlated with four dhyanas for non-return (Section 29.7)

**Line 203:**
> the samadhi faculty and samadhi power are the right samadhi within the four dhyanas (meditative absorptions), which serve as the expedient means for realizing the fruit of non-return.

**Chinese:** 即四靜慮中所有正定。謂諸靜慮能為方便證不還果。

**Verdict:** No issue.

**Analysis:** The specific correlation of the dhyanas with the fruit of non-return is doctrinally standard. The anagamin (non-returner) has fully overcome sense-desire and ill-will, which requires the development of the dhyanas as the basis for cutting the five lower fetters. The Visuddhimagga XXII.26 similarly describes the non-returner as one who abandons "without remainder, both greed for the sense desires and ill-will." The Sravakabhumi's statement that the dhyanas "serve as the expedient means for realizing the fruit of non-return" is precise.

## Overall Assessment

The translation of sections 29.1-29.7 is doctrinally rigorous. The critical Yogacara-Sarvastivada distinctions are handled correctly:

1. **Entanglement vs. latent tendency** (paryavasthana vs. anushaya): consistently maintained across all four samadhis and the eight abandonment factors.
2. **Four types of abandonment** (discipline, abandonment-of-abandonment, cultivation, protection): correctly mapped to their respective efforts, with etymological explanations preserved.
3. **Faculty vs. power distinction** (dominance vs. invincibility): correctly articulated.
4. **Causal vs. identity relationship** in the five-faculty correlations: precisely rendered.
5. **Yogacara-Sarvastivada eightfold path classification** (right effort under prajna, not samadhi): correctly noted as tradition-specific in footnote.
6. **Overestimation (adhimana) passage** in investigation-samadhi: doctrinally precise -- the practitioner recognizes partial liberation from entanglements without claiming liberation from latent tendencies.

The footnotes are strong and provide appropriate scholarly context without overstepping into modernization. No anachronistic concepts detected. No category errors found. The translation faithfully represents the Sravakabhumi's systematic Abhidharma path analysis.

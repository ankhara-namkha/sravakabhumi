## Issue Catalog

### CRITICAL Issues

#### C1. Omission: 慈愍 rendered without full Chinese term pair
- **Chinese:** 或慈愍 (line 28)
- **English:** "loving-kindness" (line 326 of translation)
- **Issue:** The Chinese term 慈愍 means "loving-kindness and compassion" (慈 = maitri, 愍 = compassion/pity). The English consistently renders this as simply "loving-kindness" throughout section 26.5. In the knowable-objects list, this is acceptable as a label, because the Revata dialogue and the purifying-conduct section (26.6, not in scope) treat the full meaning. However, the glossary entry for 慈 is "loving-kindness" and there is no separate entry for 慈愍 as a compound.
- **Severity:** MODERATE — The compound 慈愍 is treated as equivalent to 慈 (maitri) throughout and the translation is consistent with standard usage and with how the text itself later defines the compound in the purifying-conduct section. Not a true omission but a glossary gap.

#### C2. Mistranslation: 三摩呬多地 rendered as "the ground of meditative equipoise"
- **Chinese:** 由三摩呬多地毘鉢舍那行 (line 28)
- **English:** "Through the vipashyana practice of the ground of meditative equipoise (*samahita-bhumi*)"
- **Issue:** This is acceptable. 三摩呬多地 = samahita-bhumi, glossed as "meditative equipoise." The rendering is accurate.
- **Severity:** NOT AN ISSUE — reviewed and confirmed correct.

#### C3. Omission: 如是等類當知名為 abbreviated
- **Chinese:** 如是等類當知名為所知事同分影像諸名差別 (line 28, end of image-without-discernment passage)
- **English:** "Such and similar names should be known as the various designations for the image corresponding to the knowable object."
- **Issue:** Accurate. No omission.
- **Severity:** NOT AN ISSUE.

#### C4. Potential mistranslation: 一切有為事皆五法所攝
- **Chinese:** 一切有為事皆五法所攝 (line 28)
- **English:** "All conditioned phenomena are encompassed by the five dharmas [i.e., the five skandhas]."
- **Issue:** The Chinese says 五法 (five dharmas/things), not 五蘊 (five skandhas). The translator correctly adds "[i.e., the five skandhas]" as a clarifying bracket. Since "five dharmas" in this context does refer to the five skandhas (as the preceding sentence enumerates all five), the bracketed gloss is appropriate and the rendering is defensible. However, the literal Chinese does NOT say "skandhas."
- **Severity:** MODERATE — The bracketed clarification is editorially defensible but strictly speaking the Chinese says "five dharmas" (五法), not "five skandhas." A more literal rendering would be "five dharmas" with a footnote. The current rendering is not wrong but adds interpretive content.

#### C5. Critical omission: 一切諸法界處所攝 partially rendered
- **Chinese:** 一切諸法界處所攝 (line 28)
- **English:** "All dharmas are encompassed by the elements and sense bases."
- **Issue:** The Chinese 界處 means "dhatus (elements) and ayatanas (sense bases)." The English "elements and sense bases" is correct. No omission.
- **Severity:** NOT AN ISSUE.

#### C6. Structural note: 現量智見生 — "non-conceptual direct-perception knowledge and vision arise"
- **Chinese:** 即於所知事有無分別現量智見生 (line 28)
- **English:** "With respect to the knowable objects, non-conceptual direct-perception (*pratyaksha*) knowledge and vision arise."
- **Issue:** The Chinese 有無分別 here means "there is non-conceptual" (有 + 無分別). The English correctly parses this. However, "direct-perception" is supplied as interpretation of 現量 — this is correct per the glossary (現量 = pratyaksha = direct perception). Accurate.
- **Severity:** NOT AN ISSUE.

#### C7. Critical: Revata's question — minor structural issue
- **Chinese:** 為何於緣安住其心。云何於緣安住其心。齊何名為心善安住。(line 28)
- **English:** "upon what objects do they settle their minds? How do they settle their minds upon the objects? At what point is the mind said to be well settled?"
- **Issue:** The Chinese has three questions: (1) 為何於緣安住其心 — "Upon what object do they settle the mind?"; (2) 云何於緣安住其心 — "How do they settle the mind upon the object?"; (3) 齊何名為心善安住 — "At what point [齊何] is it called 'mind well settled'?" The English renders all three correctly. No issue.
- **Severity:** NOT AN ISSUE.

#### C8. Critical: Revata dialogue — four aspects enumeration
- **Chinese:** 於相稱緣安住其心。於相似緣安住其心。於緣無倒安住其心。能於其中不捨靜慮。(line 28)
- **English:** "They settle their minds upon a matching object. They settle their minds upon a similar object. They settle their minds upon the object without inversion. They are able, within this, not to abandon their meditative absorption."
- **Issue:** Accurate. Four aspects fully enumerated. 相稱 = "matching" is a good rendering. No issue.
- **Severity:** NOT AN ISSUE.

#### C10. Critical omission: Second matching-object passage — 是名比丘勤修觀行是瑜伽師於相稱緣安住其心

#### C11. CRITICAL: "Settling upon a similar object" — omission of 唯智唯見唯正憶念
- **Chinese:** 然與本事相似而生。於彼所緣有彼相似。唯智唯見唯正憶念。(line 28)
- **English:** "Although one cannot observe the original knowable objects as actually present, the image arises in similarity to the original objects. With respect to that object, the image is similar to it — consisting only of cognition, only of seeing, only of correct recollection."
- **Issue:** The English renders 唯智唯見唯正憶念 as "consisting only of cognition, only of seeing, only of correct recollection." This is accurate. The phrase modifies 相似 — the similarity consists only in wisdom/cognition, seeing, and correct recollection. Rendered correctly.
- **Severity:** NOT AN ISSUE.

#### C14. CRITICAL: Second verse commentary — clause-by-clause verification

- **Issue:** This footnote adds substantial scholarly context not in the Chinese text. Per project conventions, annotations must be clearly separated from translation text and the reader must know what is Asanga vs. translator. The footnote format achieves this. Acceptable per design invariants.

#### M2. Moderate: 或見或聞或分別故 — rendering of 分別
- **Chinese:** 或見或聞或分別故 (line 28, opening of image with discernment)
- **English:** "or on what is seen, heard, or discerned"
- **Issue:** The Chinese has three items: 見 (seen), 聞 (heard), 分別 (discerned/discriminated). The English renders 分別 as "discerned." The glossary entry for 分別 is "discernment, discrimination." "Discerned" is consistent.
- **Severity:** NOT AN ISSUE.

#### M3. Moderate: 觀察簡擇極簡擇遍尋思遍伺察 — five-fold analytical formula
- **Chinese:** 觀察簡擇極簡擇遍尋思遍伺察 (appears multiple times in lines 28)
- **English:** "observe, investigate, thoroughly investigate, comprehensively examine, and comprehensively analyze"
- **Issue:** This five-fold formula appears 5 times in the Chinese source within section 26.5. Checking each occurrence in English:
  1. Image with discernment definition: "observe, investigate, thoroughly investigate, comprehensively examine, and comprehensively analyze" — PRESENT
  2. Image without discernment "no longer...": "no longer observes, investigates, thoroughly investigates, comprehensively examines, or comprehensively analyzes" — PRESENT
  3. Image without discernment closing: "no longer observing, investigating, thoroughly investigating, comprehensively examining, or comprehensively analyzing" — PRESENT
  4. Settling upon a similar object: "investigate, thoroughly investigate, comprehensively examine, and comprehensively analyze" — PRESENT (but 觀察 omitted; see M4 below)
  5. Not applicable — the remaining uses reference the formula by context.
- **Severity:** See M4 below.

#### M4. Moderate omission: 觀察 dropped from one instance of the five-fold formula
- **Chinese (Revata dialogue, similar-object section):** 為欲簡擇極簡擇遍尋思遍伺察故
- **English:** "In order to investigate, thoroughly investigate, comprehensively examine, and comprehensively analyze the various knowable objects"
- **Issue:** The Chinese here actually has only FOUR terms (簡擇極簡擇遍尋思遍伺察), not five — it omits 觀察 from the formula. The English correctly reflects this shorter form. No issue.
- **Severity:** NOT AN ISSUE — the Chinese itself uses the shorter four-fold form here.

#### M5. Moderate: 三摩地口 — unusual term rendered
- **Chinese:** 亦名三摩地口 (line 28)
- **English:** "the entrance of samadhi"
- **Issue:** 口 literally means "mouth" or "entrance/opening." "Entrance" is a reasonable rendering. The footnote [^image_synonyms] discusses these alternate names. Acceptable.
- **Severity:** NOT AN ISSUE.

#### M6. Moderate: Revata dialogue — 依增上慧法毘鉢舍那 rendering
- **Chinese:** 於時時間依增上慧法毘鉢舍那勤修觀行 (line 28, similar-object subsection)
- **English:** "from time to time diligently practices the vipashyana of the superior wisdom-dharma"
- **Issue:** 增上慧法 = "superior wisdom-dharma" or "the dharma of superior wisdom." The English rendering "the superior wisdom-dharma" is a bit unusual but captures the Chinese. An alternative would be "vipashyana based on the superior prajna-dharma." The current rendering is defensible.
- **Severity:** MODERATE — "the vipashyana of the superior wisdom-dharma" is a somewhat awkward construction. 依增上慧法毘鉢舍那 more naturally reads "relying on vipashyana [which belongs to] the superior prajna-dharma." The preposition "of" reverses the dependency. Consider revising to "the vipashyana of superior wisdom" or "vipashyana relying on the dharma of superior prajna."

#### M7. Moderate: 隨應解了 rendering
- **Chinese:** 隨應解了所知境界 (line 28, no-inversion subsection)
- **English:** "they come to understand the knowable domain according to its nature"
- **Issue:** 隨應 means "in accordance with what is appropriate" or "as befitting." The English "according to its nature" is an interpretation. A more literal rendering would be "they appropriately understand the knowable domain" or "they understand the knowable domain as it corresponds." Minor interpretive addition.
- **Severity:** MODERATE — "according to its nature" adds a shade not precisely in 隨應. The Chinese says "as appropriate" (隨應) not "according to [the object's] nature."

#### M8. Moderate: 不捨靜慮 section — 修習止相舉相捨相 order
- **Chinese:** 於時時間修習止相舉相捨相 (line 28)
- **English:** "From time to time they cultivate the sign of calming, the sign of uplifting, and the sign of equanimity."
- **Issue:** Accurate. The three signs are in the same order: 止相 (calming), 舉相 (uplifting), 捨相 (equanimity). No issue.
- **Severity:** NOT AN ISSUE.

#### M9. Moderate: Four purifications — 隨得觸證 rendered
- **Chinese:** 隨得觸證所依清淨。於所知事由現見故。隨得觸證所緣清淨。由離貪故隨得觸證心遍清淨。離無明故。隨得觸證智遍清淨。(line 28)
- **English:** "They progressively realize and contact the purification of the basis. With respect to the knowable objects, through direct seeing, they progressively realize and contact the purification of the object. Through freedom from desire, they progressively realize and contact the complete purification of the mind. Through freedom from ignorance, they progressively realize and contact the complete purification of knowledge."
- **Issue:** 隨得觸證 rendered as "progressively realize and contact." 隨 here means "accordingly" or "in turn" rather than "progressively." 觸證 means "to touch/contact and realize" — i.e., to directly experience. "Progressively" implies a sequential ordering not explicitly stated by 隨. Minor.
- **Severity:** MODERATE — "progressively" slightly over-interprets 隨, which here functions more as "accordingly" or "thereupon." The meaning is not distorted but the Chinese does not emphasize temporal progression the way "progressively" does.

#### M10. Moderate: Revata dialogue closing — structure
- **Chinese:** 頡隸伐多。為此比丘於所緣境安住其心。如是於緣安住其心。如是於緣安住心已名善安住。(line 28)
- **English:** "Revata, it is for this purpose that the bhikshu settles the mind upon the object. In this way they settle the mind upon the object. Having thus settled the mind upon the object, the mind is called well settled."
- **Issue:** The Chinese has a three-part closing answering Revata's original three questions: (1) 為此 — "for this purpose" (answering 為何 — upon what); (2) 如是於緣安住其心 — "in this way they settle" (answering 云何 — how); (3) 如是...名善安住 — "thus...called well settled" (answering 齊何 — at what point). English captures the three-part structure correctly.
- **Severity:** NOT AN ISSUE.

#### M11. Moderate: 世尊此中重說頌曰 — verse introduction
- **Chinese:** 世尊此中重說頌曰 (line 28, end)
- **English:** "The Blessed One further spoke this verse:"
- **Issue:** 重說頌 means "again spoke a verse" or "further spoke a verse." 此中 means "herein" or "in this context." The English omits 此中 ("herein"). Minor omission — the word "further" partially captures the idea.
- **Severity:** MODERATE — minor. 此中 (herein/in this context) is dropped. Could be "The Blessed One herein further spoke this verse" but the current rendering is natural and the meaning is clear.

#### M12. Moderate: Second verse commentary — 委練修習 rendering
- **Chinese:** 於奢摩他毘鉢舍那。常勤修習委練修習。(line 38)
- **English:** "constantly and diligently cultivating shamatha and vipashyana, practicing with earnest training"
- **Issue:** 委練修習 is an unusual compound. 委 = earnest/dedicated, 練 = to train/refine, 修習 = to cultivate/practice. "Practicing with earnest training" is a reasonable rendering. The doubling of 修習 in the Chinese (常勤修習 + 委練修習) is preserved as "cultivating" + "practicing." Acceptable.
- **Severity:** NOT AN ISSUE.

## Structural Check

The Chinese passage at [0453a29] constitutes a single continuous section on the six-aspect vipashyana applied to the contemplation of loving-kindness (慈愍觀). It covers all six investigative aspects: meaning (義), things (事), own-characteristic (自相), common characteristic (共相), categories (品), time (時), and reasoning (理) with its four sub-types.

The English section 30.16 covers all six aspects plus the four reasoning sub-types. **Structure matches: no sections omitted, no extra sections added.**

## Sentence-by-Sentence Comparison

### Opening [0453a29]

**Chinese:** 云何勤修慈愍觀者。尋思六事差別所緣毘鉢舍那。
**English:** "How does one who diligently cultivates the contemplation of loving-kindness (*maitri*) practice the six-aspect vipashyana?"
**Assessment:** Accurate. Minor note: 慈愍 in the glossary is "loving-kindness and compassion" (maitri-karuna), but the English renders it as just "loving-kindness (*maitri*)." The Chinese 慈愍 is a compound of 慈 (maitri) + 愍 (karuna). However, the section heading and body consistently use "loving-kindness" alone. This is a terminology simplification, not a mistranslation per se, since 慈愍觀 is conventionally treated as the loving-kindness meditation. **Severity: Minor (m).**

### Meaning (義) [0453a29]

**Chinese:** 謂依慈愍增上正法聽聞受持增上力故。由欲利益安樂意樂。於諸有情作意與樂發起勝解。是慈愍相。若能如是解了其義。如是名為於諸慈愍尋思其義。
**English:** "Based on the correct teachings concerning loving-kindness — through the dominant force of hearing and retaining them — and moved by the intention to benefit and bring happiness, one directs attention toward giving happiness to all sentient beings, generating resolute confidence. This is the defining mark of loving-kindness. Understanding this meaning is investigating the meaning of loving-kindness."
**Assessment:** Accurate and complete. All elements present. Good.

### Things (事) [continued]

**Chinese:** 彼既如是解了義已復能思擇。此為親品此為怨品此中庸品是一切品。皆他相續之所攝故。於中發起外事勝解。又若親品名為內事。怨中庸品名為外事。如是名為於諸慈愍尋思其事。
**English:** "Having understood the meaning, one further discerns: 'These are dear ones, these are enemies, these are neutral ones.' Since all three belong to the continuums of others, one regards them as external things. Alternatively, dear ones may be considered internal, while enemies and neutral ones are external."
**Assessment:** Accurate. The phrase 是一切品 ("this is the totality of categories") is absorbed into the phrasing about "all three" which is reasonable compression. The closing formula 如是名為於諸慈愍尋思其事 is not explicitly rendered but is implicitly conveyed by the section heading "Things." This is consistent with how the other aspects handle their closing formulas. **No issue.**

### Own-Characteristic (自相) [continued]

**Chinese:** 復能思擇如是三品。若無苦樂欲求樂者願彼得樂。今於此中有饒益相名為親品。不饒益相名為怨品。俱相違相名中庸品。
**English:** "One further discerns these three categories. Those with a beneficial bearing are called dear ones, those with a harmful bearing are called enemies, and those whose bearing is neither are called neutral."

**Issue (3) — Mistranslation/Omission (Minor):** The Chinese says 若無苦樂欲求樂者願彼得樂 — "For those who neither have suffering nor happiness but wish to obtain happiness, one wishes them to obtain happiness." The English translation places this phrase AFTER the definitions of the three categories, combining it with the three types of happiness-seeking. However, in the Chinese, this phrase appears BEFORE the definitions. The Chinese order is: (a) those who lack suffering/happiness and seek happiness — wish them happiness; (b) then: beneficial = dear, harmful = enemy, neither = neutral. The English reverses (a) and (b). **Severity: Minor (m) — structural reordering, no meaning lost.**

**Chinese:** 如是三品若無苦樂欲求樂者。略有三種欲求樂心差別可得。一者欲求欲界諸樂。二者欲求色界有喜勇悅諸樂。三者欲求離喜諸樂。如是若於欲樂匱乏。願彼皆得無罪欲樂。若於有喜離喜諸樂有所匱乏。當知亦爾。是名尋思慈愍自相。
**English:** "For beings in these three categories who neither suffer nor possess happiness but wish to obtain it, three kinds of happiness-seeking can be distinguished: (1) seeking the pleasures of the desire realm, (2) seeking the joyful and delightful pleasures of the form realm, and (3) seeking the pleasures that lie beyond joy. If beings lack desire-realm pleasures, one wishes that they may all obtain blameless pleasures. If they lack the higher pleasures — those of joy or those beyond joy — one extends the same wish."

**Issue (3) — Minor Mistranslation:** 勇悅 is rendered as "delightful." The Chinese 有喜勇悅 literally means "having joy and vigor/elation." 勇 means courage/vigor/elation, not merely "delight." The pair 喜勇悅 suggests joyful and exhilarating pleasures, with 勇悅 carrying a sense of exhilaration or buoyant delight. "Delightful" flattens this. **Severity: Minor (m).**

**Issue (1) — Partial Omission:** The Chinese 若無苦樂欲求樂者願彼得樂 literally: "for those who are without suffering or happiness and wish to obtain happiness — one wishes them to obtain happiness." The English says "who neither suffer nor possess happiness but wish to obtain it" — the wish-fulfillment phrase 願彼得樂 ("one wishes them to obtain happiness") is not explicitly stated at this initial point, though it appears later in the passage in expanded form. **Severity: Minor (m) — the content is captured later.**

### Common Characteristic (共相) [continued]

**Chinese:** 復審思擇若諸親品若諸怨品若中庸品。我於其中皆當發起相似性心平等性心。
**English:** "One further reflects on dear ones, enemies, and neutral ones together: 'Toward all of them, I should develop a mind of similarity and a mind of equality.'"
**Assessment:** Accurate.

**Chinese:** 何以故。我若作意與親品樂此未為難。於中庸品作意與樂亦未甚難。若於怨品作意與樂乃甚為難。我於怨品尚應作意願與其樂。何況親品及中庸品而不與樂。
**English:** "Why? Giving happiness to dear ones is not difficult. Giving happiness to neutral ones is also not very difficult. But giving happiness to enemies — that is extremely difficult. Yet even toward enemies I should aspire to give happiness. How much more, then, should I give it to dear ones and neutral ones!"
**Assessment:** Accurate. The 作意 ("directing attention") is simplified to just "giving" but this is acceptable for readability. Good.

**Chinese:** 何以故。此中都無能罵所罵。能瞋所瞋。能弄所弄。能打所打。唯有音聲唯有名字。
**English:** "Why should this be so? Because in reality there is no insulter and no one insulted, no one angry and no object of anger, no mocker and no one mocked, no striker and no one struck. There are only sounds and names."
**Assessment:** Accurate and well-rendered. The fourfold formula is complete.

**Chinese:** 又我此身隨所生起。有色麁重四大所造。隨所住處便為如是觸所逼惱。略有二觸。謂音聲觸。及手足塊刀杖等觸。是身及觸皆是無常。能為如是不饒益者亦是無常。
**English:** "Moreover, this body of mine, however it has arisen, is material, coarse and heavy, constructed from the four great elements. Wherever it abides, it is pressed upon by contacts of two kinds: the contact of sounds, and the contact of hands, feet, clods, knives, sticks, and the like. Both the body and these contacts are impermanent. The one who causes harm is also impermanent."
**Assessment:** Accurate. All elements present.

**Chinese:** 又復一切有情之類皆有生老病死等法。本性是苦。故我不應於本性苦諸有情上。更加其苦而不與樂。
**English:** "Furthermore, all sentient beings without exception are subject to birth, aging, sickness, death, and the rest — their fundamental nature is suffering. I should not heap further suffering upon beings who are inherently suffering but should wish them happiness instead."
**Assessment:** Accurate.

**Chinese:** 又亦不應不與怨家作善知識不攝一切有情之類以為自體。
**English:** "I should not fail to serve as a spiritual friend (*kalyanamitra*) even to enemies, nor fail to embrace all sentient beings as my own."

**Issue (4) — Structural Infidelity (Minor):** The Chinese has a double negative construction: 又亦不應不與怨家作善知識 — "Moreover, one should not fail to serve as a spiritual friend to enemies." Then: 不攝一切有情之類以為自體 — "nor fail to embrace all sentient beings as one's own self/body." The English captures this well. However, 以為自體 literally means "taking [them] as one's own body/self" — the translation "as my own" drops 體 (body/self). The Chinese implies a stronger identification: making all beings part of one's own self. **Severity: Minor (m).**

**Chinese:** 又世尊言。我不觀見如是種類有情可得。無始世來經歷生死長時流轉。不互相為或父或母兄弟姊妹。若軌範師若親教師。若餘尊重似尊重者。
**English:** "The Blessed One has said: 'I do not see any class of being who, from beginningless time, through the long course of birth, death, and wandering, has not at some point been one's father or mother, brother or sister, one's acharya or upadhyaya, or another honored person.'"

**Issue (1) — Omission (Minor):** The Chinese has 若餘尊重似尊重者 — "or other honored ones or those similar to honored ones" (尊重 and 似尊重). The English only says "or another honored person," dropping the distinction between 尊重 ("honored person") and 似尊重 ("one similar to an honored person"). **Severity: Minor (m).**

**Issue (5) — Terminology note:** 軌範師 is rendered "acharya" and 親教師 as "upadhyaya" — these match the glossary. However, the glossary preferred English renderings are "discipline master" and "preceptor" respectively. Using the Sanskrit here is acceptable since the context is a direct speech quotation, but inconsistent with the glossary's preference. **Severity: Minor (m), not wrong, just a consistency note.**

**Chinese:** 由是因緣一切怨品無不皆是我之親品。
**English:** "For this reason, all enemies are without exception one's own dear ones."
**Assessment:** Accurate.

**Chinese:** 又怨親品無有決定真實可得。何以故。親品餘時轉成怨品。怨品餘時轉成親品。是故一切無有決定。
**English:** "Furthermore, 'enemy' and 'dear one' have no fixed reality. Dear ones at other times become enemies; enemies at other times become dear ones. Nothing about these designations is certain."

**Issue (2) — Minor Addition:** The English adds "about these designations" — the Chinese simply says 是故一切無有決定 ("therefore nothing is certain/fixed"). The addition of "designations" is interpretive, adding a clarifying word not in the source. **Severity: Minor (m) — aids comprehension, not misleading.**

**Chinese:** 故我今者應於一切有情之類。皆當發起平等性心平等性見。及起相似利益意樂安樂意樂與樂勝解。是名尋思慈愍共相。
**English:** "For all these reasons, I should develop toward all sentient beings a mind of equality and a vision of equality, with a similar intention to benefit and bring happiness, and the resolve to give them happiness."
**Assessment:** Accurate. 與樂勝解 ("resolute confidence in giving happiness") is rendered "the resolve to give them happiness" — 勝解 (adhimoksha) is "resolute confidence" per glossary, but "resolve" captures the sense. Minor flattening. **No significant issue.**

### Categories (品) [continued]

**Chinese:** 復審思擇。我若於彼不饒益者。發生瞋恚便為顛倒。黑品所攝。是有諍法。廣說如前。我若於彼不起瞋恚便無顛倒。白品所攝。是無諍法。廣說如前。如是名為尋思慈愍黑品白品。
**English:** "One reflects: 'If toward those who are harmful I generate anger, this is inversion — belonging to the black category: conflicted, painful, and harmful,' and so forth as described above. 'If toward them I do not generate anger, this is non-inversion — belonging to the white category: free from conflict, free from suffering and harm,' likewise as above."

**Issue (2) — Minor Addition:** The English adds "painful, and harmful" and "free from suffering and harm" which expand the Chinese 是有諍法 ("is a conflicted dharma") and 是無諍法 ("is a non-conflicted dharma"). The Chinese says 廣說如前 ("as broadly explained above"), meaning these expansions come from the prior unclean-contemplation section. The English partially renders the expansion inline rather than simply saying "as explained above." This is acceptable editorial practice — it gives the reader the content rather than forcing them to look backward. **Severity: Minor (m) — addition is accurate content drawn from the cross-reference.**

### Time (時) [continued]

**Chinese:** 復審思擇。諸過去世求欲得樂有情之類。彼皆過去。我當云何能與其樂。諸現在世有情之類。我今願彼盡未來世於一切時常受快樂。是名尋思諸慈愍時。
**English:** "One reflects: 'Beings of the past who sought happiness have all passed away — how could I give them happiness now? For beings of the present, I wish them happiness at all times throughout the entire future.'"
**Assessment:** Accurate and complete.

### Reasoning (理) [continued]

**Reasoning from Dependence (觀待道理)**

**Chinese:** 復審思擇。此中都無我及有情或求樂者或與樂者。唯有諸蘊唯有諸行。於中假想施設言論。此求樂者。此與樂者。又彼諸行業煩惱等以為因緣。如是名依觀待道理尋思慈愍。
**English:** "'In this, there is no self, no sentient being, no seeker of happiness, no giver of happiness. There are only skandhas, only formations. Within these, purely by conventional designation, we speak of "this one seeks happiness" and "this one gives happiness." Moreover, those formations have karma, afflictions, and similar conditions as their basis.'"
**Assessment:** Accurate and complete. Good rendering.

**Reasoning from Function (作用道理)**

**Chinese:** 若於慈愍善修善習善多修習能斷瞋恚。如是名依作用道理尋思慈愍。
**English:** "'If one thoroughly cultivates, practices, and extensively develops loving-kindness, one can eradicate anger.'"

**Issue (3) — Minor Mistranslation:** 瞋恚 is specifically "anger/ill-will" (pratigha/vyapada). The English uses "anger" which is adequate, but 瞋恚 carries the specific Buddhist sense of ill-will/aversion. The glossary does not have a separate entry for 瞋恚 as distinct from 瞋 (dvesha), but the compound suggests the specific mental factor of ill-will. "Anger" is acceptable. **No significant issue.**

**Reasoning from Proof (證成道理)**

**Chinese:** 如是之義有至教量。我內智見現轉可得。比度量法亦有可得。如是名依證成道理尋思慈愍。
**English:** "'There is scriptural authority for this meaning. Within myself, direct wisdom and perception confirm it. Inference is also available.'"

**Issue (1) — Minor Omission:** 我內智見現轉可得 — literally "within myself, wisdom-seeing presently turns/arises and can be obtained." The English says "direct wisdom and perception confirm it" — this adds "confirm it" which interprets 現轉可得 (can be presently obtained/manifested) more freely. The Chinese emphasizes the present availability of direct inner cognition, not so much "confirmation." Also, 現轉 suggests "presently turning" (i.e., currently active/operating), not simply "direct." **Severity: Minor (m).**

**Reasoning from Nature of Things (法爾道理)**

**Chinese:** 又即此法成立法性。難思法性安住法性。謂修慈愍能斷瞋恚。不應思議不應分別應生勝解。如是名依法爾道理尋思慈愍。
**English:** "'That cultivating loving-kindness serves as the antidote that eradicates anger — this is the established nature of dharmas, their inconceivable nature, their abiding nature. This should not be further analyzed or elaborated upon, but only accepted with faith.'"

**Issue (2) — Minor Addition:** The Chinese says 能斷瞋恚 ("can eradicate anger/ill-will"). The English adds "serves as the antidote that eradicates anger" — the word "antidote" (對治) is not in this particular sentence, though the concept is doctrinally accurate and appears in the parallel unclean-contemplation passage. **Severity: Minor (m).**

**Issue (3) — Minor Mistranslation:** 應生勝解 — "one should generate resolute confidence (adhimoksha)." The English renders this as "but only accepted with faith." 勝解 (adhimoksha) is "resolute confidence" per the glossary, not "faith" (shraddha/信). While related, these are distinct Buddhist mental factors. **Severity: Moderate (M) — glossary term rendered with wrong English equivalent.**

### Closing [end of passage]

**Chinese:** 是名勤修慈愍觀者。尋思六事差別所緣毘鉢舍那。
**English:** "This is the six-aspect vipashyana for one who diligently cultivates the contemplation of loving-kindness."
**Assessment:** Accurate.

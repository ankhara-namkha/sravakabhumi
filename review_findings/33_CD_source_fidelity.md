### Section 33.6 — Attention of Conviction through First Dhyana Formula

#### 勝解作意 (Attention of conviction)

**Chinese:** 彼既如是如理尋思。了知諸欲是其麁相。知初靜慮是其靜相。從此已後超過聞思唯用修行。於所緣相發起勝解。修奢摩他毘鉢舍那。既修習已如所尋思。麁相靜相數起勝解。如是名為勝解作意。

**English (line 144):** "Having thus investigated according to reason and come to know that desires are coarse and the first dhyana peaceful, the yogin then transcends hearing and reflection and employs only cultivation. Generating conviction regarding the meditation object, one cultivates shamatha and vipashyana. Through this cultivation, one repeatedly generates conviction about the coarse and peaceful aspects as previously investigated. This is called the **attention of conviction**."

- **Finding:** ACCURATE. All elements present. 如理尋思 → "investigated according to reason"; 從此已後超過聞思唯用修行 → "transcends hearing and reflection and employs only cultivation"; 於所緣相發起勝解 → "generating conviction regarding the meditation object"; 數起勝解 → "repeatedly generates conviction." No omissions or additions.

#### 遠離作意 (Attention of separation)

**Chinese:** 即此勝解善修善習善多修習為因緣故。最初生起斷煩惱道。即所生起斷煩惱道俱行作意。此中說名遠離作意。由能最初斷於欲界先所應斷諸煩惱故。及能除遣彼煩惱品麁重性故。

**English (line 146):** "When this conviction has been well cultivated, well practiced, and extensively developed, it serves as the cause and condition for the first arising of the path that severs afflictions. The attention that accompanies this newly arisen path is here called the **attention of separation** — because it first severs the desire-realm afflictions that should be severed first, and because it removes the coarseness and heaviness (*dausthulya*) belonging to those categories of affliction."

- **Finding:** ACCURATE. 善修善習善多修習 → "well cultivated, well practiced, and extensively developed" (three-fold emphasis preserved). 斷煩惱道俱行作意 → "the attention that accompanies this newly arisen path." 先所應斷諸煩惱 → "afflictions that should be severed first." 麁重性 → "coarseness and heaviness (*dausthulya*)." Complete.

#### 攝樂作意 (Attention of joyful engagement)

**Chinese:** 從是已後愛樂於斷愛樂遠離。於諸斷中見勝功德。觸證少分遠離喜樂。於時時間欣樂作意而深慶悅。於時時間厭離作意而深厭患。為欲除遣惛沈睡眠掉舉等故。如是名為攝樂作意。

**English (line 148):** "From this point on, the yogin delights in severance and delights in separation, seeing superior virtues in what has been severed, and touching a measure of the joy and bliss of separation. From time to time one engages joyful attention with deep gladness; from time to time one engages attention of revulsion with deep weariness — in order to dispel torpor, drowsiness, excitement, and the like. This is called the **attention of joyful engagement**."

- **Finding:** ACCURATE. All clauses accounted for. 觸證少分遠離喜樂 → "touching a measure of the joy and bliss of separation." 欣樂作意/厭離作意 → "joyful attention / attention of revulsion." 惛沈睡眠掉舉等 → "torpor, drowsiness, excitement, and the like." Complete.

#### 觀察作意 (Attention of investigation) — Part 1: Testing

**Chinese:** 彼由如是樂斷樂修。正修加行善品任持。欲界所繫諸煩惱纏。若行若住不復現行。便作是念。我今為有於諸欲中貪欲煩惱不覺知耶。為無有耶。

**English (lines 150–151):** "Through delighting in severance and cultivation, correctly applying practice and sustained by wholesome qualities, the afflictive entanglements bound to the desire realm no longer manifest, whether one is in motion or at rest. One then thinks: 'Do I still have craving and afflictions regarding desires without being aware of it? Or have they been eliminated?'"

- **Finding:** ACCURATE. 善品任持 → "sustained by wholesome qualities." 若行若住 → "whether one is in motion or at rest." Internal monologue faithfully rendered.

**Chinese:** 為審觀察如是事故隨於一種可愛淨相作意思惟。猶未永斷諸隨眠。故思惟如是淨妙相時。便復發起隨習近心趣習近心臨習近心。不能住捨。不能厭毀制伏違逆。

**English (line 152):** "To investigate this carefully, one deliberately attends to one type of attractive, pure object. Because the latent tendencies (*anushaya*) have not yet been permanently severed, when one contemplates such a pure and delightful object, a mind of approach arises — following toward it, inclining toward it, drawing near. One cannot remain in equanimity, cannot feel revulsion, condemn, suppress, or oppose it."

- **Finding:** ACCURATE. 隨習近心趣習近心臨習近心 → "following toward it, inclining toward it, drawing near" — the three progressive stages are preserved. 不能住捨 → "cannot remain in equanimity." 不能厭毀制伏違逆 → "cannot feel revulsion, condemn, suppress, or oppose it" — the four terms are rendered. Complete.

#### 觀察作意 — Part 2: Self-assessment

**English (line 154):** "One then thinks: 'My mind is not yet freed from desires. I have not yet truly attained liberation. My mind is still constrained by formations, like water held in a vessel — not yet governed by the nature of things. I should now strive anew, dwelling in delight of severance and cultivation, to permanently sever the remaining latent tendencies.' This is called the **attention of investigation**."

- **(S-1) MINOR — Addition:** The Chinese 如水被持 = "like water being held." The English "like water held in a vessel" adds "in a vessel," which is not in the Chinese. The simile is clarifying — water being held implies a container — but it is technically an addition. The footnote (^investigation_test) also says "like water held by a vessel," further expanding. **Severity: minor.** The meaning is not distorted, but the addition is not in the source.

#### 加行究竟作意 (Attention of completed application)

**Chinese:** 從此倍更樂斷樂修修奢摩他毘鉢舍那。鄭重觀察修習對治。時時觀察先所已斷。由是因緣從欲界繫一切煩惱心得離繫。此由暫時伏斷方便。非是畢竟永害種子。當於爾時初靜慮地前加行道已得究竟一切煩惱對治。作意已得生起。是名加行究竟作意。

At this point, the preliminary path for the ground of the first dhyana has been completed: the attention that serves as the antidote to all desire-realm afflictions has arisen. This is called the **attention of completed application**."

- **Finding:** ACCURATE. 鄭重觀察 → "with earnest effort." 時時觀察先所已斷 → "periodically re-examining what has already been severed." 暫時伏斷方便 → "method of temporary suppression." 非是畢竟永害種子 → "not by permanently destroying their seeds." 前加行道已得究竟 → "the preliminary path... has been completed." Complete.

#### 加行究竟果作意 (Attention to the fruit of completed application)

#### Progressive joy passage

**Chinese:** 又於遠離攝樂作意現在轉時。能適悅身離生喜樂。於時時間微薄現前。加行究竟作意轉時。即彼喜樂轉復增廣。於時時間深重現前。加行究竟果作意轉時。離生喜樂遍諸身分。無不充滿無有間隙。

**English (line 160):** "The joy and bliss born of separation progress through three stages. During the attentions of separation and joyful engagement, this joy arises from time to time, faintly and thinly, bringing comfort to the body. During the attention of completed application, it expands and deepens, manifesting with greater intensity. During the attention to the fruit of completed application, the joy and bliss born of separation pervade every part of the body, filling it completely without any gap."

- **(S-2) MINOR — Addition:** "progress through three stages" is an interpretive framing not in the Chinese. The Chinese simply describes each stage in sequence without characterizing it as a "three stages" scheme. Minor editorial addition for clarity.
- **Finding otherwise:** 能適悅身 → "bringing comfort to the body." 微薄現前 → "faintly and thinly." 深重現前 → "with greater intensity." 遍諸身分。無不充滿無有間隙 → "pervade every part of the body, filling it completely without any gap." All accurate.

#### First dhyana formula

**English (line 162):** "At this point, separated from desires and separated from all evil and unwholesome dharmas, with examination (*vitarka*) and analysis (*vicara*), with the joy and bliss born of separation, one abides fully equipped in the first dhyana with its five branches complete. - **Finding:** ACCURATE. The full formula is faithfully rendered. 圓滿五支具足安住 → "abides fully equipped... with its five branches complete." Both named designations (住欲界對治修果 and 隨證得離欲界欲) rendered.

### Section 33.7 — Functional Summary and Four-fold Classification

#### Functional summary of seven attentions

**Chinese:** 又了相作意。於所應斷能正了知。於所應得能正了知。為斷應斷為得應得。心生希願勝解作意。為斷為得正發加行。遠離作意。能捨所有上品煩惱。攝樂作意。能捨所有中品煩惱。觀察作意。能於所得離增上慢安住其心。加行究竟作意能捨所有下品煩惱。加行究竟果作意。能正領受彼諸作意善修習果。

**English (line 180):** "The functions of the seven attentions may be summarized as follows. The **attention to characteristics** correctly knows what should be abandoned and what should be attained, and generates aspiration for both. The **attention of conviction** correctly initiates application for both abandonment and attainment. The **attention of separation** relinquishes all upper-grade afflictions. The **attention of joyful engagement** relinquishes all middle-grade afflictions. The **attention of investigation** enables the mind to abide free from overestimation (*adhimana*) regarding what has been attained. The **attention of completed application** relinquishes all lower-grade afflictions. The **attention to the fruit of completed application** correctly receives the fruit of all these attentions' good cultivation."

- **(S-3) MINOR — Slight structural compression:** The Chinese for 了相作意 says 於所應斷能正了知。於所應得能正了知。為斷應斷為得應得。心生希願 — this is: (a) correctly knows what should be abandoned, (b) correctly knows what should be attained, (c) for abandoning what should be abandoned and attaining what should be attained, (d) the mind generates aspiration. The English compresses (a) and (b) into one clause and (c)+(d) into "generates aspiration for both." The meaning is preserved but the explicit four-part structure is slightly condensed. Acceptable compression.

- **Finding otherwise:** All seven attentions' functions match the Chinese exactly. 增上慢 → "overestimation (*adhimana*)" — correct.

#### Four-fold classification

**Chinese:** 又若了相作意。若勝解作意。總名隨順作意厭壞對治俱行。若遠離作意。若加行究竟作意。總名對治作意斷對治俱行。若攝樂作意。名對治作意及順清淨作意。若觀察作意。名順觀察作意。如是其餘四種作意當知攝入六作意中。謂隨順作意。對治作意。順清淨作意。順觀察作意

**English (line 182):** "These seven attentions can also be classified into a broader framework of four categories. The attentions to characteristics and of conviction are collectively called the *conforming attention*, accompanied by the counteractive measure of disgust and destruction. The attentions of separation and of completed application are collectively called the *counteracting attention*, accompanied by the counteractive measure of severance. The attention of joyful engagement is called both the *counteracting attention* and the *purity-conforming attention*. The attention of investigation is called the *investigation-conforming attention*. These four types of attention should be understood as subsumed within the framework of six types of attention: the conforming attention, the counteracting attention, the purity-conforming attention, and the investigation-conforming attention."

- **(S-4) MODERATE — "Six types" ambiguity:** The Chinese says 如是其餘四種作意當知攝入六作意中。謂隨順作意。對治作意。順清淨作意。順觀察作意 — "these remaining four kinds of attention should be understood as subsumed within the six types of attention, namely: the conforming attention, the counteracting attention, the purity-conforming attention, and the investigation-conforming attention." The Chinese explicitly says "six types" (六作意) but then lists only four. This is not an error in the translation — it reflects the source text. However, the English says "four categories... subsumed within the framework of six types of attention" and then lists only four. The text itself is ambiguous (the other two of the six may be implicit or drawn from another classification). The translation faithfully renders this ambiguity.

- **(S-5) MINOR — 厭壞對治:** 厭壞對治俱行 is rendered as "accompanied by the counteractive measure of disgust and destruction." The Chinese 厭壞 literally = "disgust-destruction" or "revulsion and wearing away." The translation "disgust and destruction" is a reasonable rendering. An alternative would be "the antidote characterized by revulsion and deterioration." Minor variance, not a mistranslation.

### Section 33.8 — Extension to Higher Attainments and the Second Dhyana

#### Extension statement

**Chinese:** 如初靜慮定有七種作意。如是第二第三第四靜慮定。及空無邊處。識無邊處。無所有處。非想非非想處定。當知各有七種作意。

**English (line 192):** "Just as the first dhyana has seven types of attention, so too do the second, third, and fourth dhyanas, and the spheres of infinite space, infinite consciousness, nothingness, and neither-perception-nor-non-perception. Each of these attainments should be understood as having its own seven types of attention."

- **Finding:** ACCURATE. All eight attainments listed. Complete.

#### Second dhyana: recognizing coarse aspects

#### External classification and temporal nature

**Chinese:** 又正了知如是尋伺依內而生外處所攝。又正了知如是一切過去未來現在所攝。從因而生。從緣而生。或增或減不久安住暫時而有率爾現前。

#### Effects on the mind

**Chinese:** 令心躁擾令心散動不靜行轉。求上地時苦住隨逐。是故皆是黑品所攝隨逐諸欲。離生喜樂少分勝利。隨所在地自性。能令有如是相。於常常時於恒恒時。有尋有伺心行所緣。躁擾而轉不得寂靜。以如是等種種行相。於諸尋伺覺了麁相。

**English (lines 198):** "They agitate the mind, scatter the mind, and move it without stillness. When one seeks the higher ground, they accompany one as painful companions. Therefore they belong to the dark category, attending upon desires. The joy and bliss born of separation are only a small advantage; by their very nature, at all times and without respite, wherever examination and analysis are present the mind turns upon its object with agitation, unable to find stillness."

- **(S-6) MINOR — Slight reframing:** 隨逐諸欲。離生喜樂少分勝利 — The Chinese says they "belong to the dark category, attend upon desires; the joy and bliss born of separation [of the first dhyana] are only a small advantage." The English restructures this slightly with "attending upon desires" and "only a small advantage" in separate sentences, which is acceptable.

- **(S-7) MINOR — Omission of concluding phrase:** 以如是等種種行相。於諸尋伺覺了麁相 = "Through these and other such aspects, one discerns the coarse qualities of examination and analysis." This concluding sentence is not explicitly present in the English paragraph at line 198. However, line 200 says "Through these and other such aspects, one discerns the coarse qualities of examination and analysis" — so it IS present, just placed in the next paragraph for flow. **Not an omission** — repositioned for English readability.

### Section 33.9 — Universal Coarse Aspects Across All Lower Grounds

**Chinese:** 又彼麁相遍在一切下地皆有。下從欲界展轉上至無所有處。當知麁相略有二種。謂諸下地苦住增上。望上所住不寂靜故。及諸壽量時分短促。望上壽量轉減少故。此二麁相由前六事。如其所應當正尋思。隨彼彼地樂離欲時。如其所應於次上地尋思靜相。漸次乃至證得加行究竟果作意。

**English (lines 210–212):** "The coarse aspects described above are found universally in all lower grounds, from the desire realm up through the sphere of nothingness. In brief, every lower ground exhibits two kinds of coarseness compared to the ground above it: **(1)** a preponderance of painful abiding — the lower ground is not as still as the upper — and **(2)** a shorter span of life — the lower ground's lifespan is progressively shorter than the upper ground's. These two coarse aspects should be correctly investigated through the same six perspectives as before, adapted to each ground as appropriate. Whenever one wishes to abandon desire for a particular ground, one investigates the peaceful aspects of the next-higher ground accordingly, progressing step by step until one attains the attention to the fruit of completed application for that ground."

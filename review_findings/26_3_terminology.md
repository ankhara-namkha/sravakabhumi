## Critical Issues

### C1. Person-type renderings inverted from glossary (4 terms, lines 264–270)

All four arhat-type terms in section (11) By Obstruction and section (10) By Regression are rendered with a different phrasing than the glossary specifies. Specifically, the translation appends "arhat" as a qualifier and uses compound-adjective word order rather than the glossary's "person liberated by X" pattern. The glossary's Person Types table (Fascicle 26 section) is authoritative.

| Translation text (line) | Glossary rendering | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| "occasion-liberated arhat (*samayavimukta*)" (264) | "person liberated by occasion" | Word order inverted; "arhat" appended |
| "immovable-dharma arhat (*akopyadharma*)" (264) | "person of the immovable dharma" | Phrasing differs substantially |
| "prajna-liberated arhat (*prajnavimukta*)" (270) | "person liberated by prajna" | Word order inverted; "arhat" appended |
| "both-ways-liberated arhat (*ubhayatobhagavimukta*)" (270) | "person liberated in both respects" | Phrasing differs substantially |

These are critical because they are technical person-type designations defined in the Fascicle 26 section of the glossary, and consistency across all uses is required. The "arhat" qualifier is not wrong doctrinally but creates a divergence from the glossary standard that must be reconciled — either update the glossary to reflect the "X arhat" pattern or correct the translation to match the glossary.

### C2. Preferred rendering not used: "preparatory practice" vs. "application" (line 244, section heading line 242)

The glossary entry for 加行 / prayoga lists the preferred in-text rendering as **"application"**, with "preparatory practice" as a secondary rendering. The translation consistently uses "preparatory practice" throughout section (7):

> "How are persons established by differentiation of **preparatory practice** (*prayoga*)?" (line 244)
> "Such is what is called the establishment of persons by differentiation of **preparatory practice**." (line 246)

The section heading (line 242) also reads "By Preparatory Practice."

The glossary notes both forms but marks "application" as preferred. If the project intends "preparatory practice" as the operative rendering for 加行 in this section (which appears doctrinally justified as a translation of *prayoga* in this context), the glossary must be updated to reflect this decision. As it stands, the translation diverges from the stated preferred form.

**Severity: Critical** — glossary preferred rendering is not followed without documented reason.

## Moderate Issues

### M1. Typographic error: "kleshaavarana" (line 270)

The text reads:

> "liberated from the afflictive obstruction (*kleshaavarana*)"

The double-a "kleshaavarana" appears to be a typographic artifact from combining "klesha" + "avarana" without a separator. The glossary uses "kleshaavarana" as well (line 119 of glossary), suggesting this is a consistent transcription choice — but it is visually ambiguous and inconsistent with Sanskrit romanization conventions. Both the translation and glossary should use either "klesha-avarana" (hyphenated) or "kleshaavarana" with note that the double-a represents the sandhi junction. Flag for style decision.

---

### M2. "Stream-entry / once-returning / non-returning" vs. glossary person-form terms (lines 234–236)

The glossary defines the person designations:
- srota-apanna: "stream-enterer"
- sakridagamin: "once-returner"
- anagamin: "non-returner"

The translation uses the state/fruit forms rather than the person forms:

> "the person practicing toward the fruit of **stream-entry**"
> "the person practicing toward the fruit of **once-returning**"
> "the person practicing toward the fruit of **non-returning**"
> "the person practicing toward the fruit of **arhatship**"
> "the fruit of **stream-entry**" / "the fruit of **once-returning**" / etc. (lines 234–236)

These are compound constructions ("fruit of X") where X names the state/attainment, not the person. "Stream-entry" as the name of the attainment (as opposed to "stream-enterer" as the name of the person) is a standard usage and may be correct. However, the glossary does not separately define the state-form terms (stream-entry, once-returning, non-returning). This gap should be addressed: either add the state-form terms to the glossary or confirm that the person-form terms ("stream-enterer," etc.) are to be used in compound constructions as well (e.g., "fruit of stream-entry" rendered as "stream-enterer fruit" or "fruit of the stream-enterer").

**Severity: Moderate** — the usage is defensible but the glossary does not explicitly authorize the state-forms used here.

### M3. "Shramana fruits" not in glossary (line 244)

The text reads:

> "those who have attained the **shramana fruits** are called fruit-abiders" (line 238)
> "following the vinaya, diligently cultivates right practice is called a dharma-follower" (line 244) — the concept of "shramana fruits" is invoked

"Shramana fruits" (沙門果, *shramana-phala*) is a standard compound but does not appear in the glossary. The glossary has "shramana" (沙門) and "fruit/result" (phala) separately. The compound "shramana-phala" / "shramana fruit(s)" should be added to the glossary with a defined rendering.

### M4. "Great vehicle (mahayana)" — word order of first-use pattern (line 207)

The text reads:

> "the correct aspiration for the great vehicle (**mahayana**)"

The glossary entry for 大乘: preferred in text = "mahayana" (Sanskrit form), with English gloss "great vehicle." The first-use pattern should be "mahayana (great vehicle)" — Sanskrit preferred, English in parentheses. The translation reverses this: English first, Sanskrit second.

Compare the correct pattern used for other terms in this section (e.g., "course of progress (*pratipada*)" follows English-first but pratipada is not the preferred in-text form — it is fine to give Sanskrit in parens for non-preferred terms). For mahayana specifically, where Sanskrit is the preferred in-text form, the pattern should be "mahayana (great vehicle)" on first occurrence, then "mahayana" thereafter.

---

### M5. "Pranidhana" not in glossary (footnote, line 286)

The footnote at line 286 uses:

> "aspiration (*pranidhana*)"

The term *pranidhana* is used throughout the section as the rendering of 願 or 發願 (aspiration/vow). It appears in running text at lines 207–215 as "aspiration" (without the Sanskrit) and in the footnote with the Sanskrit. The term "aspiration" / pranidhana is not listed in the glossary. Given its prominence in section (4) By Aspiration, it should be added.

---

### M6. "Sapta-parishat" not in glossary (footnote, line 280)

The footnote reads:

> "The seven Buddhist communities (*sapta-parishat*)"

This compound Sanskrit term is used in the footnote and translates the Chinese classification. It does not appear in the glossary. Since this is a footnote term (not running text), severity is moderate rather than critical. Should be added for completeness.

---

### M7. "Pleasant abidings" (plural) vs. glossary singular (lines 264, 266)

The glossary defines 現法樂住 / drishta-dharma-sukha-vihara as "pleasant abiding in the present life" (singular). The translation at lines 264 and 266 uses "pleasant **abidings** in the present life" (plural). This is a minor inconsistency — the Chinese compound is singular in form and the glossary rendering is singular. Pluralization may be contextually warranted (referring to the range of pleasant states) but diverges from the glossary.

---

### M8. "Samjna-vedayita-nirodha" not in glossary (footnote, line 290)

The footnote at line 290 uses:

> "cessation of perception and feeling (*samjna-vedayita-nirodha*)"

This is the Sanskrit for the eighth liberation. The term does not appear as an entry in the glossary. The eighth liberation is listed under 八解脫 / ashta-vimoksha but the Sanskrit for the individual member is not recorded. Should be added.

## 5. Internal Consistency

**Abhidharmakosha citation forms:**

The Abhidharmakosha is cited under three different forms throughout the footnotes:
- "Abhidharmakosha" (most common, e.g., fascicle 28 [^four_inversions])
- "AKBh" (used in fascicle 26 [^birth_types]: "cf. AKBh VI.36-38")
- "Abhidharmakosha" with bhashya implied (fascicle 26 [^five_affliction_types];
  fascicle 28 [^three_smrityupasthana]: "Abhidharmakosha (VI.14–15)")

The inconsistency between "AKBh" (which technically stands for
Abhidharmakosha*bhashya*) and "Abhidharmakosha" (the root verses only) is a minor
scholarly distinction but an internal inconsistency. Since the standard English
translation (Pruden) translates both together, the inconsistency is acceptable in
practice, but "AKBh" should either be defined in the abbreviations table or
normalized to "Abhidharmakosha" throughout. Currently "AKBh" is used once (fascicle
26) while "Abhidharmakosha" is used everywhere else.

FINDING: [INCONSISTENCY — MINOR] "AKBh" used once (fascicle 26, [^birth_types]);
"Abhidharmakosha" used in all other instances. Neither form is defined in the
abbreviations table. The work itself is missing from the bibliography.

**Lamrim Chenmo citation forms:**

The Lamrim Chenmo is cited in various forms:
- "the *Lamrim Chenmo* (Vol. 1, ch. 9)" — fascicle 28, [^maranasati]
- "*The Great Treatise on the Stages of the Path* (Vol. 3)" — fascicle 30,
  [^four_attentions]
- "the *Lamrim Chenmo* Vol. 3" — fascicle 31, [^paired]
- "the *Lamrim Chenmo*" (without volume) — fascicle 31, [^mutual_support]
- "Tsongkhapa renders it as..." — fascicle 26, [^image_synonyms] (informal
  reference, not a full citation)

FINDING: [INCONSISTENCY — MINOR] Three different forms used: "Lamrim Chenmo" (Tibetan
transliteration), "The Great Treatise on the Stages of the Path" (English subtitle),
and unmarked "Tsongkhapa." All resolve to the same work, but different readers may
not recognize them as the same source. The bibliography title is "The Great Treatise
on the Stages of the Path to Enlightenment (Lam Rim Chen Mo)" — the footnotes use
neither the full title nor the parenthetical as listed. Recommend normalizing to a
single form, ideally the bibliography's parenthetical abbreviation.

**Visuddhimagga citation forms:**

Cited as "Visuddhimagga" throughout with roman numeral chapter references
(III, IV, VI, VIII, XI, XXI, XXII). Fully consistent. No issues.

**Deleanu citation:**

Cited as "Deleanu, *The Chapter on the Mundane Path* (2006)" in all 8 fascicle
opening footnotes. Fully consistent. CLEAN.

**Analayo citation:**

"Analayo, *Mindfulness of Breathing*, ch. II" (fascicle 27) and
"Analayo (*Satipatthana*, ch. VI)" (fascicle 28). Both works are in the bibliography.
Slight variation in parenthetical style but not a substantive inconsistency.

## SUMMARY OF FINDINGS

### TRUE MISMATCHES (doctrinal/editorial errors):

**None confirmed.** All major doctrinal enumerations match their stated counts.

### STRUCTURAL ANOMALIES (presentational issues, not errors):

1. **[28:28.6] Four yogas / sixteen types claim** — The text asserts "these four yogas have sixteen types in total" (4×4=16). Aspiration and diligence each have their four types explicitly enumerated. Faith has "two aspects and two bases" (=4 items, correctly counted but framed as 2+2). Method (upaya) states "of four kinds" but never enumerates the four; only a single continuous prose description is given. The 16-total claim is mathematically consistent IF faith's 4 items count as 4 "types" and method's 4 unstated types are assumed. The structural presentation is incomplete — method's four types are missing.

2. **[27:27.3] Counting cultivation "four types"** — Text claims four types, lists (i)–(iv), then continues through (vii) with additional elaboration. Items (v)–(vii) are not additional "types" but subsequent stages/applications; the footnote explicitly confirms the four-type framework. Presentational ambiguity, not a true mismatch.

3. **[33:33.16] Twelve perceptions, items 10–12** — Items 10, 11, and 12 are grouped together in a single sentence as "liberation, bases of mastery, and totality perceptions" with cross-reference to another section. The count of 12 is met, but the treatment is abbreviated compared to 1–9.

- **[32:32.4] Ten signs** — "the ten, beginning with form" — referenced but not enumerated.
- **[32:32.4] Eight discursive thoughts** — "the eight, beginning with desire" — referenced but not enumerated.
- **[32:32.4] Five secondary afflictions** — "the five, beginning with sensual desire" — equated with five hindrances in footnote, not listed.
- **[33:33.7] Six types of attention (broader Yogacara framework)** — mentioned but not enumerated; only four of the six are treated.

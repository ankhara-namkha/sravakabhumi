### Key Patterns

1. **Doctrine agents (D)** produce the highest ratio of confirmatory-to-actionable findings (~3:1). Their primary value is verification, not correction.

2. **Register/Readability agents (R)** produce the highest volume of actionable output per file, including full paragraph-level rewrites. They are the only agents that provide alternative text.

3. **Source Fidelity agents (S)** are the most confirmatory -- their primary function is proving completeness and accuracy. They catch almost nothing that needs changing but provide essential assurance.

4. **Footnote auditors** are the most efficient at generating ready-to-use content (drafted footnotes with exact insertion points).

5. **Formula/Structure agents** catch issues invisible to all other agents -- cross-fascicle terminology drift, formatting inconsistencies, missing first-use glosses.

6. **The arc continuity agent (4D)** is the only agent that examines the work as a unified whole rather than section-by-section. Its five missing cross-reference recommendations are unique findings.

7. **The front matter agent (3D)** caught two critical errors (incorrect Sanskrit and duplicate glossary entry) that were invisible to every other review because no other agent read the front matter.

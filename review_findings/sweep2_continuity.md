## 1. Content Continuity at Fascicle Boundaries

### [Content] [F26->F27] -- CLEAN
F26 ends with the loving-kindness/compassion object (purifying-conduct object for aversion types). F27 opens with the dependent origination object (purifying-conduct object for delusion types). This is the natural next item in the enumeration of the five purifying-conduct objects. The transition is seamless -- no connector needed because the section numbering and the ongoing taxonomy provide continuity.

### [Content] [F27->F28] -- CLEAN
F27 ends with the four types of instruction (27.6), completing the topics of meditation objects and teaching methodology. F28 opens with the three superior trainings (28.1), the next item in the 26.1 uddana sequence ("training"). The logical flow is natural: from what one meditates on and how one is taught, to the formal structure of the training itself.

### [Content] [F28->F29] -- CLEAN
F28 ends with the four foundations of mindfulness (28.11), the first group of the thirty-seven factors of awakening. F29 opens with the four right efforts (29.1), the second group. The opening sentence explicitly links back: "Having thus continuously practiced the four foundations of mindfulness, one has removed the coarser inversions..." This is a textbook cross-fascicle transition.

### [Content] [F29->F30] -- CLEAN, WELL-MARKED
F29 ends with the three causes of fruitless practice (29.17), the final item in the Second Yoga Place's agenda. F30 opens with the Third Yoga Place transition and summary verse (30.1). The transition sentence is excellent: "The preceding chapters have covered the person types, the objects of meditation, and the full range of practice -- fruitful and fruitless alike. How the teacher properly establishes the student in all of this, I shall now explain." The footnote [^third_yoga_transition] clearly explains the shift from theoretical framework (Second YP) to practical instruction (Third YP). No issue.

### [Content] [F30->F31] -- CLEAN
F30 ends with vipashyana applied to loving-kindness contemplation (30.16). F31 opens with vipashyana applied to dependent origination (31.1). The opening sentence bridges explicitly: "The following three sections apply the same six-aspect vipashyana framework... to the remaining purifying-conduct objects: dependent origination, element differentiation, and mindfulness of breathing." Continuity is seamless.

### [Content] [F31->F32] -- CLEAN
F31 ends with the goldsmith simile and four types of cultivation-attention (31.11). F32 opens with the five signs framework for the beginner (32.1). Both are within the Third Yoga Place, moving from the teacher's establishment framework to the beginner's practical guide. The transition is natural: from how the teacher structures practice to what the beginner actually does.

### [Content] [F32->F33] -- CLEAN, WELL-MARKED
F32 ends with the attainment of "possessed of attention" status (32.17) -- the formal criterion for genuine shamatha. F33 opens with the Fourth Yoga Place uddana (33.1) and immediately picks up with "Two Paths After Attaining 'Possessed of Attention'" (33.2). The connection is explicit: section 33.2 footnote says "Having attained the initial shamatha ('possessed of attention') at the end of fascicle 32, the yogin faces a crucial fork." The Yoga Place transition is clearly marked in both the header and the footnotes.

## FLAG 1: Terms NOT in the Glossary

### F1-A: *laukikagradharma* (supreme worldly dharma) — PARTIAL ENTRY
**Location:** 29.8 body text; [^four_roots] footnote
**Usage:** "supreme worldly dharma (*laukikagradharma*)" in 29.8 body; "Supreme worldly dharma (*laukikagradharma*, 世第一法)" in footnote.
**Glossary status:** The glossary entry for 順決擇分善根 (nirvedha-bhagiya-kushala-mula) at line 388 notes the four stages as "warmth (煖), summit (頂), patience (忍), supreme worldly dharma (世第一法)" but gives no standalone entry for *laukikagradharma* or 世第一法 as a term. Entries exist for warmth (煖/usmagata), peak (頂/murdhan), and acceptance (順諦忍/kshanti), but the fourth wholesome root — 世第一法 — has no dedicated glossary row of its own.
**Recommendation:** Add to glossary: 世第一法 | laukikagradharma | supreme worldly dharma | supreme worldly dharma | Fourth and highest of the four wholesome roots concordant with penetration; the peak of mundane realization immediately preceding stream-entry.

### F1-B: *sapta-trimshat bodhipakshya-dharma* (thirty-seven factors of awakening) — INCONSISTENT SANSKRIT
**Location:** 29.11 body text, footnote [^eightfold_path] (indirect)
**Usage:** 29.11: "thirty-seven factors of awakening (*sapta-trimshat bodhipakshya-dharma*)"
**Glossary status:** Glossary at line 398 gives the Sanskrit as *saptatrimshad-bodhipakshya-dharma* (one word, with -d). The translation uses *sapta-trimshat bodhipakshya-dharma* (two words, with -t and a space). This is a variant romanization, not a separate term, but it creates inconsistency.
**Recommendation:** Standardize the Sanskrit romanization in the translation to match the glossary form: *saptatrimshad-bodhipakshya-dharma*.

### F1-C: *adya-shaiksha* (initial learner) — SANSKRIT NOT IN GLOSSARY

The footnote supplies *adya-shaiksha*, which is not validated by the glossary. If this Sanskrit is correct, it should be added to the glossary entry.
**Recommendation:** Verify *adya-shaiksha* as the correct Sanskrit for 初有學. If confirmed, add it to the glossary entry for 初有學.

### F1-D: *sarvatraga* (pervasive) — USED WITHOUT GLOSSARY ENTRY
**Location:** [^seven_bodhyanga] footnote
**Usage:** "mindfulness alone pervades both categories and is termed 'pervasive' (*sarvatraga*)"
**Glossary status:** *sarvatraga* does not appear in the glossary as a standalone term. The glossary has *sarvatraga-alambana* (pervasive object) but not the adjective *sarvatraga* as used here for the mindfulness bodhyanga. This is an added Sanskrit form in a footnote only, but if it is used there, it should be validated in the glossary or at minimum noted.
**Recommendation:** Add a note to the glossary entry for 念覺支 (smriti-bodhyanga) that the term "pervasive" (*sarvatraga*) is used to describe mindfulness's double-category membership, or confirm this is an acceptable ad hoc gloss not requiring a full entry.

### F1-E: *sanghati* — UNTRANSLATED TERM IN RUNNING TEXT

## FLAG 2: Terms Rendered Differently from Glossary

### F2-A: "insight" used for vipashyana — GLOSSARY SAYS USE SANSKRIT
**Location:** 29.9 body text
**Usage:** "three factors of awakening belong to the category of vipashyana (insight)"
**Glossary status:** Glossary entry for 毘鉢舍那 states: English Rendering = "vipashyana / insight"; Preferred in text = "vipashyana"; Notes: First use: "vipashyana (insight)". The parenthetical "(insight)" is correct on first use, but if this is a re-occurrence of the term (it was used many times in sections 29.1–29.7), the parenthetical is an over-repetition of the first-use pattern. Section 29.9 is not the first occurrence in the fascicle — "vipashyana" was used in 29.3 (factor 6: "The wisdom belonging to the category of vipashyana") and 29.7 ("the four dhyanas (meditative absorptions)"). After first use with gloss, subsequent uses should be "vipashyana" alone per the glossary convention.
**Severity:** Minor. The parenthetical "(insight)" after vipashyana in 29.9 is redundant repetition of the first-use gloss. Per glossary convention, only the first occurrence per fascicle requires the English gloss.
**Recommendation:** Remove "(insight)" in the 29.9 occurrence of vipashyana, since it was already glossed in an earlier section.

### F2-B: "meditative absorptions" gloss repeated for dhyanas — SAME OVER-REPETITION ISSUE
**Location:** 29.7 body text (immediately before section 29.8, in the correlation passage)
**Usage:** "the right samadhi within the four dhyanas (meditative absorptions)"
**Glossary status:** Same issue as F2-A: the first-use gloss "(meditative absorptions)" should appear only once in the fascicle for 靜慮. This is a repeated gloss. Technically this is in 29.7 (just before the 29.8–29.11 range), but it affects the convention reading through into 29.8.
**Note:** If 29.7 was the actual first use of "dhyanas" in this fascicle, the gloss is correct there. Checking the text: 29.3 uses "shamatha" and "vipashyana" categories; 29.7 is indeed likely the first occurrence of "the four dhyanas" in fascicle 29. So the gloss in 29.7 is correct. No flag for this one — noted and cleared.

### F2-C: "factors of awakening" vs. "factors conducive to awakening" — MINOR INCONSISTENCY
**Location:** 29.9 heading, body text; 29.11 body text; footnote [^seven_bodhyanga]
**Usage:** Consistently uses "factors of awakening" (e.g., "seven factors of awakening," "factors of true awakening," "thirty-seven factors of awakening").
**Glossary status:** The glossary entry for 菩提分法 (bodhipakshya-dharma) gives the full rendering as "factor(s) conducive to awakening" (line 397) but notes the "Preferred in text" form as "factors of awakening" (line 397). The translation is using the preferred in-text form correctly. The distinction is important: the full rendering is "conducive to awakening" but the shorter "factors of awakening" is the approved shorthand.
**Assessment:** No error. The translation is using the correct preferred in-text form.

### F2-D: "right effort" used for two different terms — POTENTIALLY CONFUSING
**Location:** 29.10 body text (multiple occurrences)
**Usage:** (a) "right effort" as a member of the noble eightfold path = 正精進 (*samyag-vyayama*); (b) "four right efforts" = 四正斷 (*catvari-samyak-pradhana*)
**Glossary status:** The glossary gives "right effort" as the rendering for both 正斷 (samyak-pradhana) and 正精進 (samyag-vyayama). This dual use is technically sanctioned by the glossary but creates potential confusion in 29.10, where both appear in close proximity. In section 29.10, "right effort" is used 4 times: for the eightfold path member 正精進 (three occurrences) and in the passage "having established in the four foundations of mindfulness ... and right effort" which refers back to the eightfold path factor.
**Assessment:** Not an error, but a known ambiguity in the glossary that the translation inherits. Worth noting for the editor. The glossary's choice to use "right effort" for both terms is consistent, and the context distinguishes them.

### F2-E: "effort" used for *virya* in awakening-factor context — REGISTER INCONSISTENCY
**Location:** 29.9 body text: "**Effort** (*virya*)"
**Glossary status:** The glossary gives 精進覺支 (virya-bodhyanga) the rendering "effort factor of awakening" and the preferred in-text form "effort factor." In 29.9, the translation renders this as "**Effort** (*virya*)" in the bulleted list — using a shortened form that drops "factor of awakening" entirely. This is a listed item and context makes the awakening-factor context clear, so this may be acceptable shorthand. However, it is not the glossary's "preferred in text" form.
**Recommendation:** Minor. Either use "effort factor" (matching glossary preferred form) or ensure the header/context makes "factor of awakening" explicit enough that abbreviation is unobjectionable.

## Additional Observation: "Training of Samadhi" vs. "Training of Mind"

**Location:** 29.10 body text: "right mindfulness and right samadhi belong to the training of samadhi"
**Glossary status:** Glossary line 354: 增上心學 | adhicitta-shiksha | "superior training in mind" | "superior training in mind". The Chinese 心 = mind/citta, not samadhi. The text renders this as "training of samadhi" rather than "training of mind."
**Assessment:** This is a rendering choice. In context, the section is explaining the eightfold path distributed across three trainings (prajna, shila, samadhi), and the grouping of right mindfulness and right samadhi under "training of samadhi" is a conventional shorthand common in Abhidharma exposition. However, the glossary term is 增上心學 / adhicitta-shiksha = "superior training in mind." Using "training of samadhi" here diverges from the glossary rendering and may confuse readers who encounter the full term elsewhere.
**Recommendation:** Consider either (a) using "training of mind (*adhicitta-shiksha*)" consistently with the glossary, or (b) adding a note explaining that "training of samadhi" is the conventional shorthand for *adhicitta-shiksha* in the context of the eightfold path distribution. This is a moderate-priority flag since the divergence is standard in Abhidharma contexts but technically inconsistent with the glossary.

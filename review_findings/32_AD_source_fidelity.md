### Section 32.1 (T30.0459b25)

**Chinese:** 云何初修業者始修業時。於修作意如應安立。隨所安立正修行時最初觸證。於斷憙樂心一境性。

The English "The joy of abandonment" is acceptable but slightly ambiguous — it could read as "the joy that comes from abandoning [things]" rather than "delight directed toward cessation/abandonment." The Chinese 於斷 means "regarding/toward cessation" and 憙樂 means "delight/joy." Consider: "delight in cessation and mental one-pointedness." **Severity: minor.**

**Chinese:** 謂善通達修瑜伽師。最初於彼依瑜伽行初修業者。如是教誨。

**English:** "A teacher skilled and well-versed in yoga instructs the beginner who relies on yogic practice as follows:"

- Accurate. 善通達修瑜伽師 = "a yoga teacher who is skilled and well-versed" — captured.

**Chinese:** 善來賢首。汝等今者應依三種取相因緣。或見或聞或心比度。增上分別取五種相。一厭離相。二欣樂相。三過患相。四光明相。五了別事相。

### Section 32.2 (T30.0459c01-0460a01)

**Chinese opening Q&A:** 問若依瑜伽行初修業者是其貪行。由不淨觀方可調伏。云何教彼取五種相。答應如是教誨。

**English:** "Question: If the beginner is of the greedy temperament (raga-carita) and must be tamed through the contemplation of impurity, how does one teach them to apprehend the five signs? Answer: One should instruct as follows:"

- Accurate. Q&A structure preserved. Sanskrit provided appropriately.

**Chinese (list of misfortunes):** 若聞所餘彼彼村邑聚落。或男或女先受安樂後遭苦厄。或彼男女自遭重病命終殞沒。或彼男女所有知識親戚眷屬遭如是苦。或彼聚落村邑邊際喪失財寶。或是他來強敵所作。或火所燒或水所漂。或由惡作而有喪失。或由不善修營事業而有喪失。或由不善處分事業而有喪失。或為非愛共財得便而有喪失。或由家火而有喪失。

**English:** "Wherever you dwell among the various settlements and villages — if you hear of men or women in other villages who formerly enjoyed happiness but later met with misfortune; or who themselves suffered grave illness and died; or whose acquaintances, relatives, and kin suffered likewise; or of villages at whose borders wealth was lost — whether by invading enemies, consumed by fire, swept away by flood, lost through wrongdoing, through mismanaging enterprises, through poor judgment in affairs, through the schemes of ill-disposed partners, or through household fire —"

- **(1) Possible omission — minor:** 汝等隨所依止彼彼聚落村邑而住於中 = "wherever *you all* dwell depending on *those various* settlements and villages, residing among them." The English "Wherever you dwell among the various settlements and villages" drops 依止 (depend on / reside at) but the sense is preserved adequately. **Severity: minor.**
- The nine causes of wealth loss are all present and correctly rendered. 惡作 rendered as "wrongdoing" — this is acceptable (惡作 here means "evil deeds/wrongdoing" as a cause of loss, not the technical term for regret). **No issue.**
- 非愛共財得便 = "ill-disposed partners gaining access to shared wealth" — rendered "schemes of ill-disposed partners." Slightly paraphrastic but captures the meaning. **Severity: minor.**

**Chinese:** 若汝現見非是傳聞。或即於此村邑聚落。非是所餘村邑聚落。或非是此村邑聚落。亦非他人。即汝自身先所觸證猛利樂受。後還退失。廣說如前。

**English:** "Or if you have directly witnessed these things rather than merely heard of them; or if it happened in your own settlement rather than elsewhere; or indeed to yourself — sharp pleasures you once experienced that later faded away, as explained before —"

- Accurate. Three levels (heard of others, witnessed, experienced personally) all captured. 廣說如前 = "as explained before" — appropriate rendering. The Chinese has a more elaborate three-part structure (not this village/not other people/your very self) which is compressed slightly but faithfully. **No issue.**

**Chinese:** 汝既如是聞已見已。應當生起深心厭患。如是生死甚為重苦。所得自體極大艱辛。而於其中有如是等自他衰損差別可得。謂病衰損壽命衰損。眷屬衰損財寶衰損。病病法性死死法性。

**English:** "Having heard and seen these things, you should generate deep disenchantment. Samsara is grievous suffering. This body is won at great cost, and within it one encounters such various forms of decline in oneself and others: decline of health, decline of life, decline of kin, decline of wealth. The nature of the ill is to fall ill; the nature of the mortal is to die."

- **(3) Slight mistranslation — minor:** 所得自體極大艱辛 literally = "the self-body (existence) one obtains is at extreme great difficulty/hardship." The English "This body is won at great cost" is a creative rendering that captures the gist — the point is that samsaric existence is obtained through immense effort/suffering — but 自體 is more "self-nature/personal existence" than "body." The rendering is defensible. **Severity: minor.**
- Six types of decline correctly listed. 病病法性死死法性 = "the nature of the sick is sickness; the nature of the mortal is death" — rendered nicely. **No issue.**

**Chinese:** 復有一類淨戒衰損正見衰損。由是因緣彼諸眾生於現法中住諸苦惱。於當來世往諸惡趣。

**English:** "Some also suffer the decline of pure discipline and the decline of right view. Through these, beings dwell in suffering in the present and journey to evil destinies hereafter."

- Accurate. 復有一類 = "there is furthermore a type [who suffer]" — rendered "Some also suffer." Faithful. **No issue.**

**Chinese:** 諸興盛者雖現法中住諸安樂於當來世往諸善趣。而是無常。於彼無常現可證得。若有領受興盛事者。後時衰損定當現前。諸有領受衰損事者。後時興盛難可現前。諸興盛事皆是難得易失。壞法如是汝應深心厭患。

**English:** "Those who prosper — though they may enjoy happiness now and journey to good destinations — are subject to impermanence, and this impermanence is directly perceivable. For whoever experiences prosperity, decline will surely come. For whoever experiences decline, renewed prosperity is hard to find. All prosperity is hard to gain, easy to lose, subject to destruction. Thus you should be deeply disenchanted."

- Accurate and well rendered. 壞法 = "subject to destruction" — correct. The rhetorical balance is preserved. **No issue.**

**Chinese (T30.0460a01):** 極善作意如理受持。如是處所難可保信。我今於是生死流轉。未般涅槃未解脫心。難可保信。如是衰損興盛二法勿現我前。勿彼因緣令我墮在如是處所。生起猛利剛強辛楚不適意苦。即由此事增上力故。我當至誠喜樂於斷修不放逸。又我如是多安住故。當於無義能作邊際。如是汝應善極作意如理受持。

**English:** "'Attend carefully and receive this well: such conditions are unreliable. While I cycle through samsara, having not yet reached nirvana, not yet liberated in mind, nothing can be guaranteed. May these two — decline and prosperity — not appear before me. May they not cause me to fall into circumstances that give rise to fierce, harsh, bitter suffering. Through the force of this reflection, I shall sincerely take joy in renunciation and practice without heedlessness. By abiding often in this way, I shall put an end to what is without meaning.' 'Thus you should attend well and receive this properly.'"

- **(3) Mistranslation — minor:** 我當至誠喜樂於斷修不放逸 — The Chinese says "I shall sincerely take delight *in cessation* (於斷) and cultivate non-heedlessness (修不放逸)." The English "take joy in renunciation and practice without heedlessness" renders 斷 as "renunciation." Strictly, 斷 here means "abandonment/cessation" (of afflictions), not "renunciation" (出離). The context is about delighting in the *cutting off* of afflictions. "Take joy in abandonment" or "delight in cessation" would be more precise. **Severity: minor.**
- **(3) Slight nuance issue — minor:** 當於無義能作邊際 = "shall be able to make an end of what is without meaning." The English "put an end to what is without meaning" captures this well. **No issue.**
- Overall the passage is well handled, with the qualification about 斷. **No issue with structural fidelity.**

### Section 32.3 (T30.0460a01, continued)

**Chinese:** 汝取如是厭離相已。復應精勤取欣樂相。當自觀察所受尸羅。為善清淨為不清淨。我或失念或不恭敬或多煩惱。或由無知於諸學處有所違犯。既違犯已我當如法以其本性增上意樂。於諸學處發起深心更不毀犯。我於所作當正應作。於非所作不復當作。以要言之。於諸學處當令增上意樂圓滿。亦令所有加行圓滿。

**English:** "'Having apprehended the sign of disenchantment, you should then diligently apprehend the sign of delight. Examine your own moral discipline (shila): is it pure or impure? 'Have I through heedlessness, lack of respect, excessive afflictions, or ignorance transgressed any of the training rules? If I have transgressed, I shall — in accord with the dharma and with the fundamental resolve of my heart — develop firm determination regarding the training rules, never again to violate them. I shall do what should be done and refrain from what should not be done. In short: regarding the training rules, I shall perfect both my resolve and my practice.'"

- **(3) Mistranslation — minor:** 以其本性增上意樂 = "with the fundamental nature of superior aspiration." The English "with the fundamental resolve of my heart" is paraphrastic. 增上意樂 is a technical term (glossary: "superior aspiration," *adhyashaya*). It should be rendered more precisely: "with my fundamental superior aspiration" or "with the aspiration inherent in my nature." **Severity: minor.**
- **(4) Structural note:** The text switches between outer instruction and inner reflection. The English nests quotes within quotes to mark this, which works but creates some typographical complexity. The Chinese simply shifts voice. This is an acceptable structural adaptation. **No issue.**

**Chinese:** 汝於如是正觀察時。若自了知戒蘊清淨。雖不作思我當發起清淨無悔。然其法爾。尸羅淨者定生如是清淨無悔。若起如是清淨無悔。雖不作思我起歡悅。然其法爾無有悔者定生歡悅。

**English:** "When you examine yourself thus and know that the aggregate of your discipline is pure — then, without even intending 'I shall generate non-regret,' by the very nature of things, pure discipline gives rise to non-regret. And when non-regret arises, without even intending 'I shall generate gladness,' by the very nature of things, one who is free of remorse naturally generates gladness."

- Accurate. 法爾 = "the nature of things" — correctly rendered. 歡悅 = "gladness" — correct per later distinction in the passage. 清淨無悔 = "pure non-regret" simplified to "non-regret" — acceptable since the purity is implicit. **No issue.**
- Glossary check: 無悔 = "non-regret" (*avipratisara*) — matches glossary. **No issue.**

**Chinese:** 如是且於一種歡悅所依處所。汝應生起清淨無悔為先歡悅。復於除障喜悅處所當生喜悅。謂我今者尸羅清淨。有力有能安住世尊所制學處。於現法中能得未得能觸未觸。能證未證。由是處所生喜悅意。

**English:** "'In this way, from the first basis of gladness, you should generate gladness preceded by non-regret. Then, at the further basis of obstacle-removing joy, generate joy: 'My discipline is pure. I have the power and capacity to abide in the training rules established by the Blessed One. In this very life, I can attain the unattained, touch the untouched, realize the unrealized.' From this, joyful delight arises.'"

- **(3) Slight nuance — minor:** 除障喜悅處所 = "the basis of obstacle-removing joy." The English has "obstacle-removing joy," which is correct. However, 除障 could also mean "joy that arises from the removal of obstacles" rather than "joy that removes obstacles." The distinction is subtle. **Severity: minor.**
- The three-fold formula 能得未得能觸未觸能證未證 is correctly rendered. **No issue.**

**Chinese:** 若汝獲得前後所證少分差別。即由如是增上力故。於他圓滿。所證差別。謂諸如來或聖弟子及自後時所證差別。當生信解發喜悅意。

**English:** "'If you have obtained even a small portion of realization, then through its force you should generate conviction and joyful delight regarding the full attainments of others — the Tathagatas and the noble disciples — as well as regarding your own future attainments.'"

- **(3) Slight nuance — minor:** 前後所證少分差別 = "a small difference/distinction between what was previously realized and what is later realized." The English "even a small portion of realization" compresses "the small distinction between earlier and later realization" into a simpler formulation. The Chinese emphasizes the *progress* (before vs. after), not just having "a small portion." Consider: "If you have gained even a slight advance in realization." **Severity: minor.**

**Chinese:** 如是行相諸適悅意。先名歡悅今名喜悅。總名悅意。如是名為取欣樂相。

**English:** "These forms of pleasant mental states are distinguished as follows: the first — arising from clean conscience — is called 'gladness'; the second — arising from confidence in attainment — is called 'joy.' Together they are called 'delight.' This is apprehending the sign of delight."

- **(2) Addition — minor:** The Chinese says simply 先名歡悅今名喜悅 = "the former is called gladness, the latter (lit. 'now') is called joy." The English adds interpretive glosses: "arising from clean conscience" and "arising from confidence in attainment." These are accurate inferences from the context (earlier passage: 歡悅 from non-regret based on pure discipline; 喜悅 from confidence in capacity to attain). However, these glosses are not in the Chinese text itself. This is a translator's addition for clarity. Should ideally be in a footnote or marked as such to maintain the distinction between Asanga's words and translator's interpretation. **Severity: minor.**
- **(4) Structural note:** The English correctly exits the quoted instruction to provide this summative observation, matching the Chinese's shift to a third-person summary voice. **No issue.**

### Section 32.4 (T30.0460b01)

**Chinese:** 取是相已復應教授告言。賢首。汝由如是厭離相故調練其心。復由如是欣樂相故滋潤其心。

**English:** "Having apprehended these signs, the teacher further instructs: 'Worthy One, through the sign of disenchantment you have disciplined your mind; through the sign of delight you have moistened it.'"

- Accurate. 調練 = "disciplined/trained" and 滋潤 = "moistened" — match established terminology from section 31.11. **No issue.**

**Chinese:** 汝於斷滅世間貪憂應多安住。隨於彼彼所緣境界勤修加行。或奢摩他品或毘鉢舍那品。即於彼彼所緣境界。當令心住內住等住。

**English:** "'You should dwell often in the state of having abandoned worldly desire and grief. Whatever the object, diligently practice — whether in the shamatha mode or the vipashyana mode — and regarding those objects, cause the mind to abide, abide inwardly, and abide evenly.'"

- **(3) Minor nuance:** 斷滅世間貪憂 = "having cut off/eliminated worldly desire and sorrow." The English "having abandoned" works, though 斷滅 is stronger than "abandoned" — it implies actual elimination. However, at this stage the practitioner is a beginner, so "abandoned" may reflect the aspiration rather than attainment. **Severity: minor.**
- 令心住內住等住 — three of the nine stages. Correctly identified in footnote. **No issue.**

**Chinese:** 汝當獲得身心輕安及一境性。

**English:** "You shall attain physical and mental pliancy and mental one-pointedness."

- Accurate. Glossary terms correctly used. **No issue.**

**Chinese:** 汝若如是背諸黑品向諸白品。由調練心滋潤心故。復應數數取過患相。

**English:** "When you have thus turned from the dark category toward the bright through disciplining and moistening the mind, you should then repeatedly apprehend the sign of danger"

- Accurate. 黑品/白品 = "dark category/bright category" — matches glossary. **No issue.**

**Chinese:** 謂於所有諸相尋思及隨煩惱取過患相。言諸相者。謂色等十相。言尋思者。謂欲等八。隨煩惱者。謂貪欲等五。

**English:** "that is, the dangers in all signs, seekings, and secondary afflictions. What are signs? The ten, beginning with form. What are seekings? The eight, beginning with desire. What are secondary afflictions? The five, beginning with sensual desire."

- **(3) Terminology question — minor:** 尋思 is rendered "seekings" here, but the glossary has "discursive thought" for 尋思 (*vitarka*). The footnote identifies them correctly. However, "seekings" (尋思) is an unusual rendering — in the context of the eight 尋思 (eight kinds of discursive thought: desire, anger, harm, relatives, homeland, immortality, contempt, livelihood), these are usually called "eight types of discursive thought" or "eight seekings/preoccupations." The use of "seekings" is not standard but is defensible if consistently maintained. The footnote should clarify this is the same term as 尋思 in the glossary. **Severity: minor.**

**Chinese:** 汝應於彼取過患相。如是諸相能令其心作用遽務。如是尋思能令其心思慕躁擾。如是隨煩惱能令其心恒不寂靜。若心作用諸相所作。思慕躁擾尋思所作。恒不寂靜隨煩惱所作。由是令心苦惱而住。

**English:** "You should apprehend the dangers in all of these. Signs cause the mind to become busy with activity. Seekings cause it to long and grow agitated. Secondary afflictions cause it to remain perpetually unsettled. The busy activity is the work of signs; the longing and agitation, the work of seekings; the perpetual unsettledness, the work of secondary afflictions. Through all three, the mind dwells in distress."

- **(3) Minor nuance:** 作用遽務 = "activity/function that is hurried/urgent." The English "busy with activity" is slightly softer than "hurried into action" but captures the meaning. **Severity: minor.**
- The parallel structure (three causes, then three effects restated in attribution) is well preserved. **No issue.**

**Chinese:** 是故如是諸相尋思及隨煩惱。是苦非聖能引。無義令心散動。令心躁擾。令心染污。汝應如是取過患相。

**English:** "Therefore these signs, seekings, and secondary afflictions are suffering, ignoble, drawing one toward what is without meaning — scattering the mind, agitating it, defiling it. You should apprehend the sign of danger in this way."

- **(3) Possible mistranslation — minor:** 是苦非聖能引無義 — this echoes the well-known formula from the four noble truths / right effort context: "suffering, ignoble, capable of drawing [one toward] what is without meaning." The English punctuation "suffering, ignoble, drawing one toward what is without meaning" reads this as three predicates. The Chinese could also be parsed: "is suffering, is not noble, draws one to meaninglessness." The rendering is acceptable. **Severity: minor.**
- 令心散動令心躁擾令心染污 = three effects (scatter, agitate, defile). All three correctly rendered. **No issue.**

**Chinese:** 又汝應依心一境性心安住性心無亂性。以六種行正取其相。何等為六。一無相想。二於無相中無作用想。三無分別想。四於無分別中無所思慕無躁擾想。五寂靜想。六於寂靜中離諸燒惱寂滅樂想。

**English:** "'Furthermore, relying on mental one-pointedness, mental stability, and mental non-disturbance, you should properly apprehend the sign through six aspects of contemplation: (1) The perception of signlessness. (2) Within signlessness, the perception of non-activity. (3) The perception of non-discrimination. (4) Within non-discrimination, the perception of being free from longing and agitation. (5) The perception of quiescence. (6) Within quiescence, the perception of peaceful bliss free from all torment.'"

- **(3) Terminology — minor:** 心安住性 = "mental stability" — this is fine. 心無亂性 = "mental non-disturbance" — also fine. 心一境性 = "mental one-pointedness" — matches glossary. Three bases correctly identified.
- Six perceptions (六想) all correctly translated and in correct order. **No issue.**
- **(4) Structural observation:** The Chinese lists these as 想 (perceptions/conceptions). The English renders as "perceptions" which is correct. The footnote helpfully identifies the three-pair structure. **No issue.**

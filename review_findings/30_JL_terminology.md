## Flags

### FLAG-1 — Potential Watchlist Conflict: "one-pointed attention" (Stage 8, section 30.10)

**Where:** Line 264, 286, 512 (footnote mapping)
**Issue:** The text renders 專注一趣 (Stage 8) as "one-pointed attention," which is correct per glossary entry 512. However, the terminology watchlist entry for 心一境性 / ekagrata (line 28 of terminology.md) stipulates: use "one-pointed mind / mental one-pointedness" (full phrase). The two Chinese terms are distinct — 專注一趣 = Stage 8 of the nine stages; 心一境性 = characteristic of samadhi generally.
**Verdict:** No error. The text correctly uses "one-pointed attention" for 專注一趣 (glossary 512) and does not use "one-pointedness" alone (the watchlist prohibition). The watchlist prohibition targets 一境性 / ekagrata, not 專注一趣 / ekotikarana. This distinction is well-maintained in the text.
**Action required:** None. But note for editors: the two terms look similar; the rendering difference is intentional and correct.

### FLAG-2 — "Unwholesome thoughts of desire, ill-will, and harmfulness" — no glossary entry for this triad as a unit

**Where:** Line 260 (section 30.10, stage 6 — Pacifying)
**Issue:** The triad "unwholesome thoughts — of desire, ill-will, and harmfulness" renders what in Sanskrit would be *vitarka* (applied thought) of the three types (kama-vitarka, vyapada-vitarka, vihimsa-vitarka). "Ill-will" (vyapada) and "harmfulness" (vihimsa) appear as doctrinal terms but are not individually listed in the glossary. "Ill-will" appears in the glossary only as part of the five lower fetters entry (line 115) and right thought entry (line 453), not as a standalone rendering. "Harmfulness" is absent from the glossary entirely.
**Severity:** Minor. The renderings are standard NTC/Bodhi convention and are contextually correct. But if these terms appear in other sections, they should be added to the glossary for consistency.
**Action recommended:** Add glossary entries for vyapada → "ill-will" and vihimsa → "harmfulness" to prevent future inconsistency.

### FLAG-3 — "Hindrance of sensual desire" — partial rendering check

**Where:** Line 260 (section 30.10, stage 6 — Pacifying)
**Issue:** The text says "the hindrance of sensual desire" as one specific named obstruction. The glossary entry for 蓋 / nivarana (line 220) gives "hindrance(s)" as the rendering and notes "the five hindrances that obstruct samadhi," but does not individually list the five hindrances or give a glossary entry for sensual desire as a hindrance specifically (欲貪蓋 / kamacchanda-nivarana). "Sensual desire" as a hindrance is consistent with standard Buddhist usage, but the specific compound is not in the glossary.
**Severity:** Minor. The rendering is correct and conventional. Not a watchlist violation.
**Action recommended:** Consider adding 欲貪蓋 → kamacchanda-nivarana → "hindrance of sensual desire" to the glossary for completeness, especially if this hindrance is named repeatedly in fascicle 30.

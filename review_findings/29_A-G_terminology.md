## Flags by Category

### Category 1: Terms NOT in the Glossary

**FLAG T-01 — "improper mental attention" / ayonisho-manaskara**
- Location: Section 29.1, body text: "there is certainly also improper mental attention (*ayonisho-manaskara*)."
- Current usage: "improper mental attention (*ayonisho-manaskara*)"
- Glossary status: NOT in glossary. The glossary has 作意 / manaskara = "attention" but NOT the compound 不如理作意 / ayonisho-manaskara as its own entry.
- Recommendation: Add to glossary as: 不如理作意 | ayoniso-manaskara | improper mental attention / unwise attention | improper mental attention. Note: Pali form is *ayoniso-manasikara*; Sanskrit *ayonisho-manaskara* is used correctly here. The term appears without prior gloss in the fascicle — add a first-use English gloss: "improper mental attention (*ayonisho-manaskara*, unwise attention)."

**FLAG T-02 — "nine aspects" (mindfulness factor)**
- Location: Section 29.3, body text: "Mindfulness (*smriti*): the nine aspects — because through the nine aspects one settles the mind and is able to hold it in the category of shamatha."
- Current usage: "nine aspects" (bare, no Sanskrit, no cross-reference to the full technical term)
- Glossary status: The glossary has 九種心住 / nava-akara-citta-sthiti = "nine stages of mental abiding" with a note "Listed in 26.5; detailed treatment in fascicle 30." This is referenced in the footnote [^eight_factors] as "the nine aspects (九種相), referring to the nine stages of mental abiding (*navakara-cittasthiti*)," but in the body text it appears without gloss or cross-reference.
- Recommendation: In the body text, write "the nine aspects (九種相, *nava-akara-citta-sthiti*; see the nine stages of mental abiding)" or simply "the nine-aspect [method of] mental abiding." Alternatively, add a parenthetical in-text: "the nine aspects — that is, the nine stages of mental abiding (*nava-akara-citta-sthiti*) —". The footnote handles this adequately, but the body text leaves "nine aspects" dangling. Severity: Minor. The footnote rescues it.

### Category 2: Terms Rendered Differently from Glossary

**FLAG T-03 — "pliancy" vs. "ease" for prashrabdhi**
- Location: Section 29.3 body text: "Ease (*prashrabdhi*): with pure faith as the foremost condition, the mind generates joy."
- Also: Section 29.3: "ease factor of awakening" (line 243 in source: "*prashrabdhi*")
- Current usage: "ease" throughout sections 29.3, 29.4, 29.9 (seven factors listing)
- Glossary status: 輕安 / prashrabdhi = **"pliancy, ease"** with **Preferred in text: "pliancy"**. The Fascicle 29 glossary sub-table (Fascicle 29 entries) also lists: 安覺支 / prashrabdhi-bodhyanga = "ease factor of awakening" with Preferred: "ease factor."
- Analysis: There is a genuine internal inconsistency in the glossary itself. The Core Terms table says preferred = "pliancy," while the Thirty-Seven Factors table says preferred = "ease factor." The translation uses "ease" consistently, which matches the Fascicle 29 sub-table but contradicts the Core Terms entry.
- Recommendation: This is a glossary-level inconsistency that predates this fascicle. The translation's consistent use of "ease" for *prashrabdhi* in the 29.3 abandonment-factor context is defensible given the Fascicle 29 sub-table. However, the MASTER_GLOSSARY Core Terms table lists "pliancy" as preferred. Flag for editorial decision: either (a) update Core Terms to list "ease" as acceptable in awakening-factor / abandonment-factor contexts, or (b) update the translation to use "pliancy" consistently. Currently, both "ease" and "pliancy" appear across fascicles — this needs normalization. Severity: Moderate.

**FLAG T-04 — "overestimation" (adhimana) — rendering consistent but first-use gloss missing**
- Location: Section 29.2: "one avoids overestimation (*adhimana*)"
- Current usage: "overestimation (*adhimana*)" — correct rendering; glossary entry confirmed.
- Glossary status: 增上慢 / adhimana = "overestimation" ✓
- Analysis: Used correctly. NOT a rendering mismatch. However, this is flagged under first-use gloss (see Category 5 below).

**FLAG T-05 — "vigorous right effort" vs. "vigorous effort"**
- Location: Section 29.1 body text, multiple instances: "vigorous right effort" (e.g., "one exerts vigorous right effort. This is called vigorous effort.")
- Current usage: Alternates between "vigorous right effort" and "vigorous effort" within the same section.
- Glossary status: 發勤精進 = "vigorous effort" (no qualifier "right" in the glossary entry).
- Analysis: The phrase "vigorous right effort" appears twice in 29.1 (lines 43 and 45), while "vigorous effort" (without "right") appears in lines 51, 55, 69, 71, 73 and throughout. This creates inconsistency within the same section. The glossary term is "vigorous effort." The addition of "right" in two instances appears to be a translation artifact (possibly from 正精進 appearing in the Chinese at those points).
- Recommendation: Standardize to "vigorous effort" throughout 29.1, or verify against the Chinese source whether 正 (right) appears in those two instances. If so, the addition is correct and intentional; if not, remove "right." Severity: Minor, pending source verification.

**FLAG T-06 — "indomitable" for bala**
- Location: Section 29.6: "Because this pure faith has the meaning of being indomitable, it is called the faith power (*shraddha-bala*)."
- Current usage: "indomitable" as the defining quality of "power" (bala)
- Glossary status: 力 / bala = "power, strength" with Preferred: "power." The text does not use "bala" as a standalone but defines it as "indomitable" — this is a gloss of the quality, not the term itself, so it is NOT a rendering mismatch. Correctly used.
- Analysis: No flag needed. This is definitional exposition, not a rendering of the term.

**FLAG T-07 — "sentient being" appears once in non-pudgala context**
- Location: Section 29.6: "or by any other beings in the world"
- Section 29.10: "never intentionally take the life of a sentient being"
- Current usage: "sentient being" (once in 29.10)
- Glossary status: The watchlist flags "sentient being (for 補特伽羅)" as WRONG. However, this instance in 29.10 refers to 有情 (sattva), not 補特伽羅 (pudgala). The glossary entry for 有情 / sattva = "sentient being(s), being(s)" with Preferred: "sentient beings."
- Analysis: NO violation. "Sentient being" for 有情/sattva is correct per glossary. The watchlist prohibition applies only when "sentient being" is used to render 補特伽羅. This instance is correct.

### Category 3: Deprecated Term Watchlist Violations

**FLAG T-08 — "absorptions" used without proper first-use pattern**
- Location: Section 29.7, body text: "the samadhi faculty and samadhi power are the right samadhi within the four dhyanas (meditative absorptions)"
- Current usage: "the four dhyanas (meditative absorptions)"
- Watchlist status: The watchlist says: "absorptions (for 靜慮) → dhyanas: First use: 'the four dhyanas (meditative absorptions)'; thereafter: dhyanas."
- Analysis: This IS the correct first-use pattern — "four dhyanas (meditative absorptions)" — so this is technically correct per the watchlist. No violation. However, "meditative absorptions" appears only here in sections 29.1–29.7; elsewhere dhyanas appears without gloss. If this is the first occurrence of "dhyanas" in this fascicle, the gloss is appropriately placed. Confirmed correct. NOT a flag.

**FLAG T-09 — "the four noble truths" — first-use gloss present? CHECK**
- Location: Section 29.7: "the right knowledge within the four noble truths"
- Current usage: "four noble truths" — no Sanskrit gloss
- Glossary status: 四聖諦 / catvary-arya-satyani = "four noble truths" — the preferred form is "four noble truths" (already English). No Sanskrit gloss required per the glossary; this is a standard English rendering. NOT a flag.

**CONFIRMED WATCHLIST VIOLATION:**

**FLAG T-10 — "defilement" used once**
- Location: Section 29.3: "the mind is free from defilement — mental equipoise."
- Current usage: "free from defilement"
- Watchlist status: "defilements (for 煩惱) → afflictions: NTC/Tibetan convention; 'defilements' is Theravada register"
- Analysis: The Chinese likely has 染 (staining/defilement) or 染污 here in the equanimity definition, which is distinct from 煩惱 (klesha/afflictions). The term here describes a mental quality (the mind being unstained/uncontaminated by objects), not the technical term 煩惱. However, "defilement" is on the watchlist as deprecated in favor of "afflictions." The nuance is that 染 ≠ 煩惱, so this may be a legitimate rendering of a different Chinese term. Nevertheless, the watchlist targets "defilements" broadly as Theravada register.
- Recommendation: Verify against Chinese source. If the Chinese has 染污 (defilement/staining), consider "the mind remains unstained" or "the mind is uncontaminated" rather than "free from defilement." If the Chinese has 煩惱, replace with "afflictions." Either way, "defilement" should be replaced. Severity: Minor (one instance, possibly correct for 染 rather than 煩惱, but avoid the register contamination).

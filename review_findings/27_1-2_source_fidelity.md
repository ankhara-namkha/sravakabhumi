## Findings

### S-1: Fire element internal definition — missing "煖煖所攝" phrase — Severity: Moderate
- **Location:** Line 70 (English) / Chinese 內火界者。謂此身中內別溫性。溫熱所攝煖煖所攝。親附執受。
- **Chinese:** 溫熱所攝煖煖所攝
- **English:** "included in the category of the warm and hot — which is personally appropriated"
- **Issue:** Omission. The Chinese has a two-part formula parallel to all other elements: 溫熱所攝 (included in the category of the warm and hot) AND 煖煖所攝 (included in the category of warmth/heat). The second phrase 煖煖所攝 is structurally parallel to 堅鞕所攝地地所攝 (earth), 濕潤所攝水水所攝 (water), and 風飄所攝 (wind). The translation collapses both into a single clause. For earth: "included in the category of the solid and hard, included in the category of earth"; for water: "included in the category of the moist and wet, included in the category of water". Fire should follow the same pattern.
- **Recommendation:** Render as: "included in the category of the warm and hot, included in the category of warmth — which is personally appropriated." Apply to both internal and external fire element definitions.

### S-2: Fire element external definition — same "煖煖所攝" omission — Severity: Moderate
- **Location:** Line 74 (English) / Chinese 外火界者。謂外溫性。溫熱所攝煖煖所攝。非親附非執受。
- **Chinese:** 溫熱所攝煖煖所攝
- **English:** "included in the category of the warm and hot — which is not personally appropriated"
- **Issue:** Same omission as S-1 for the external fire element. The second category phrase 煖煖所攝 is dropped.
- **Recommendation:** Same fix as S-1: add "included in the category of warmth" as the second structural phrase.

### S-3: Fire element external — "牛糞末等" rendered as "dried cow dung and other materials" — Severity: Minor
- **Location:** Line 76 (English) / Chinese 依鑽燧等牛糞末等以求其火
- **Chinese:** 牛糞末等
- **English:** "dried cow dung and other materials"
- **Issue:** Minor addition/interpretation. 末 means "powder" or "crumbs/fine material," not specifically "dried." The Chinese says "cow dung powder and such" (牛糞末等). "Dried" is a plausible inference but is not in the source. This is a minor interpretive addition.
- **Recommendation:** "Powdered cow dung and the like" or simply "cow dung powder and such materials."

### S-4: Fire element external — "或復所餘" omitted — Severity: Minor
- **Location:** Line 76 (English) / Chinese 或復所餘如是等類名外火界
- **Chinese:** 或復所餘
- **English:** "Such and similar things are called the external fire element."
- **Issue:** Omission. After listing villages/cities/countries, the Chinese adds 或復所餘 ("or whatever else remains"), then 如是等類 ("such and similar things"). The English skips 或復所餘 and goes straight to "Such and similar things." This is a very minor omission since 如是等類 already conveys the open-ended scope, but 或復所餘 is a distinct phrase.
- **Recommendation:** "Or other such things besides — these and similar things are called the external fire element."

### S-5: Wind element — "脇臥風" and "脊臥風" translation nuance — Severity: Minor
- **Location:** Line 84 (English) / Chinese 有脇臥風。有脊臥風。
- **Chinese:** 脇臥風 / 脊臥風
- **English:** "the flank wind, the back wind"
- **Issue:** Minor loss of nuance. The Chinese 脇臥 literally means "flank-resting" (lying on the side) and 脊臥 means "back-resting" (lying on the back). These wind names derive from the resting postures in which they are most active. The translation drops the 臥 (resting/lying) component. The footnote does say "the flank and back winds relate to resting postures," which partially compensates, but the running text loses the connection.
- **Recommendation:** Consider "the flank-resting wind" and "the back-resting wind," or note the lying-posture meaning more explicitly in the body text. Current rendering is acceptable if the footnote is considered sufficient; this is a judgment call. Flagged for awareness.

### S-6: Wind element external — "吠藍婆風" transliteration — Severity: Minor
- **Location:** Line 88 (English) / Chinese 有吠藍婆風
- **Chinese:** 吠藍婆風
- **English:** "the *vairamana* wind"
- **Issue:** Possible mistransliteration. 吠藍婆 is standardly reconstructed as *vairambha* (or *vairambbha*), not *vairamana*. The footnote gives 吠藍婆 but renders it "vairamana." Scholarly sources (e.g., Deleanu, the Sanskrit Shravakabhumi Shukla edition) give *vairambha* as the Sanskrit equivalent. The "na" ending appears to be an error for "bha."
- **Recommendation:** Correct to *vairambha* in both running text and footnote.

### S-7: Space element — structural phrase "由此吞咽。於此吞咽既吞咽已" rendered imprecisely — Severity: Moderate
- **Location:** Line 92 (English) / Chinese 由此吞咽。於此吞咽既吞咽已。由此孔穴便下漏泄。
- **Chinese:** 由此吞咽。於此吞咽既吞咽已。由此孔穴便下漏泄。
- **English:** "Through these one swallows; within these one swallows; and after swallowing, through these openings what has been consumed passes downward and is eliminated."
- **Issue:** The Chinese has a three-step formula: (1) 由此吞咽 — "through these [openings] one swallows"; (2) 於此吞咽 — "within these [openings] one swallows" (i.e., the food passes within them); (3) 既吞咽已。由此孔穴便下漏泄 — "once swallowed, through these openings it leaks downward and is discharged." The English "within these one swallows" is slightly awkward and obscures the Chinese meaning: the food passes *within* the openings (the throat, etc.). More critically, "what has been consumed" is an addition not in the Chinese — the subject is implied, and the Chinese simply says 便下漏泄 ("it leaks downward and is discharged").
- **Recommendation:** "Through these one swallows; within these [the food] is swallowed; and after swallowing, through these openings it passes downward and is discharged." The phrase "what has been consumed" could remain if marked as a clarifying addition, but currently it reads as if it is in the source.

### S-8: Consciousness element — "三種差別" rendered as "threefold distinction" — Severity: Minor
- **Location:** Line 96 (English) / Chinese 又心意識三種差別是名識界
- **Chinese:** 心意識三種差別
- **English:** "Furthermore, the threefold distinction of heart (*citta*), mind (*manas*), and consciousness (*vijnana*) is called the consciousness element."
- **Issue:** No real error, but a note: the Chinese 三種差別 means "threefold differentiation" or "three types of distinction." The translation "threefold distinction" is adequate. However, the rendering "heart" for 心 (*citta*) may surprise readers. The glossary entry for 識 gives "consciousness" and for 心意識 the footnote explains the three terms. The NTC convention would typically render citta as "mind" or leave it in Sanskrit. This is ultimately a glossary question, not a source-fidelity error. Flagged as minor for consistency review.
- **Recommendation:** No change required for source fidelity. The term "heart" for citta is a valid but non-standard choice that should be confirmed against glossary conventions.

### S-9: Application to pride-conduct — "無復高舉" omitted/conflated — Severity: Critical
- **Location:** Line 100 (English) / Chinese 便於身中離一合想得不淨想。無復高舉憍慢微薄。於諸慢行心得清淨。
- **Chinese:** 無復高舉憍慢微薄
- **English:** "They no longer hold themselves aloft; their pride becomes attenuated"
- **Issue:** The Chinese lists two distinct results: (1) 無復高舉 — "they no longer hold themselves aloft" (i.e., haughtiness ceases); (2) 憍慢微薄 — "arrogance and pride become attenuated." These are two conditions. 高舉 (holding aloft / exalting oneself) and 憍慢 (arrogance/pride) are distinct terms in Abhidharma. The English conflates them: "They no longer hold themselves aloft; their pride becomes attenuated" treats 高舉 and 憍慢 as a single process, when the Chinese separates them with a semicolon-equivalent. The word 憍 (arrogance) is dropped entirely — only "pride" (慢) is given.
- **Recommendation:** "They no longer exalt themselves; their arrogance and pride become attenuated, and their mind is purified from pride-conduct." This preserves both 高舉 and the 憍慢 compound.

### S-10: Application to pride-conduct — closing formula differs from Chinese — Severity: Critical
- **Location:** Line 100 (English) / Chinese 是名慢行補特伽羅。由界差別淨行所緣。
- **Chinese:** 是名慢行補特伽羅。由界差別淨行所緣。
- **English:** "This is the element-differentiation purifying-conduct object for persons of pride-conduct."
- **Issue:** Structural rearrangement. The Chinese closing formula is: "This is [how] persons of pride-conduct, by means of element-differentiation, [use] the purifying-conduct object." The English inverts the structure to "This is the element-differentiation purifying-conduct object for persons of pride-conduct" — making the object the grammatical subject rather than the person. While the meaning is similar, the Chinese emphatic structure puts the *person* (慢行補特伽羅) first, then states that element differentiation is their purifying-conduct object. This is a structural fidelity issue with the Q&A closing formula.
- **Recommendation:** "This is [how] persons of pride-conduct [employ] element differentiation as their purifying-conduct object." Or more literally: "This is called [the practice of] persons of pride-conduct; element differentiation is their purifying-conduct object."

### S-15: External winds — "多羅掌" rendering — Severity: Moderate
- **Location:** Line 88 (English) / Chinese 動衣搖扇及多羅掌
- **Chinese:** 多羅掌
- **English:** "palm fronds"
- **Issue:** Slight loss of specificity. 多羅 is the *tala* palm (Borassus flabellifer, the palmyra palm). 掌 here means "palm leaf" (used as a fan). "Palm fronds" is generically correct but loses the specific identification of the tala/palmyra palm. This is a minor issue since the meaning is clear.
- **Recommendation:** Consider "*tala* palm leaves" or "palmyra fronds" to preserve the specific reference; "palm fronds" is acceptable but less precise.

### S-16: Section 27.1 — "癡行增上補特伽羅" rendering — Severity: Moderate
- **Location:** Line 25 (English) / Chinese 癡行增上補特伽羅所有癡行皆得微薄
- **Chinese:** 癡行增上補特伽羅
- **English:** "a person of delusion-conduct"
- **Issue:** The Chinese is 癡行增上補特伽羅, which literally means "a person in whom delusion-conduct is predominant." The glossary gives 癡增上者 = "person dominated by delusion" (moha-carita). The translation uses "person of delusion-conduct" which drops 增上 ("predominant/dominant"). The fascicle 26 translation uses "person dominated by..." for the 增上 construction. This section should use the same rendering for consistency.
- **Recommendation:** Change to "a person dominated by delusion-conduct" or "a person in whom delusion-conduct predominates" to match the glossary convention. The same applies to 慢行補特伽羅 in 27.2, which is rendered as "a person of pride-conduct" rather than the glossary's "person dominated by pride" — though 27.2 uses 慢行 without 增上, so the shorter form is acceptable there.

### S-17: Section 27.1 — Q&A structural marker "謂" rendering — Severity: Moderate
- **Location:** Line 23 (English) / Chinese 云何緣性緣起所緣。謂於三世...
- **Chinese:** 謂
- **English:** (no marker — the answer begins directly after the question)
- **Issue:** Structural infidelity. The glossary specifies that 謂 should be rendered as "It is as follows:" or "That is to say:" per the Q&A structural conventions. In section 27.1, the question "What is the object of the nature of conditions and dependent origination?" is immediately followed by the answer without any 謂-marker. The Chinese reads: 云何緣性緣起所緣。謂於三世... The English goes: "What is the object of the nature of conditions and dependent origination? Throughout the three times..." — dropping the 謂 marker entirely.
- **Recommendation:** Insert "It is as follows:" or "That is to say:" after the question, before "Throughout the three times..."

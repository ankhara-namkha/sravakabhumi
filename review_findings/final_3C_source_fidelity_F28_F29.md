## 8. Mistranslation Check

### 8A. 中身 / 表身 in 35 aspects of body

Chinese: 中身表身 (core body, surface body)
English: "the core body and the surface body"
These are unusual terms. 中身 = inner/core body; 表身 = external/surface body. **Translation is reasonable. PASS.**

### 8B. 中庸身劣身。中身妙身 in 35 aspects of body

Chinese: 中庸身劣身 (ordinary body, inferior body), 中身妙身 (middling body, excellent body)
English: "the ordinary body and the inferior body; the middling body and the excellent body"

**Finding [S-m-2]:** The Chinese 中庸 means "ordinary/middling" and 劣 means "inferior." Then 中 means "middle" and 妙 means "excellent/subtle." The translation renders 中庸身 as "ordinary" and 中身 as "middling" -- these are close in meaning but the distinction is maintained. The Chinese appears to present two separate binary pairs: (ordinary vs. inferior) and (middling vs. excellent), creating a four-level quality scale. The English preserves this structure. **Acceptable. PASS.**

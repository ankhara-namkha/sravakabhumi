## Critical Findings (C)

### C-1. "World-Honored One" used for 世尊 (lines 581, 583)

The glossary entry for 世尊 (bhagavat) explicitly states: "the Blessed One" with the note "NOT 'World-Honored One' (Chinese-register calque)." The translation uses "World-Honored One" twice:

- Line 581: "the World-Honored One established the four foundations of mindfulness"
- Line 583: "The Buddha, the World-Honored One, within the contemplation of the body taught..."

**Required fix:** Replace both instances of "World-Honored One" with "the Blessed One."

### C-2. Inconsistent rendering of 等持等至 (line 29)

The translation reads: "meditative concentrations and attainments (*samapatti*)"

The glossary entries are:
- 等持 | samapatti | meditative attainment
- 等引 | samahita | meditative equipoise

The Chinese 等持等至 should be rendered as two distinct terms per the glossary. The current translation conflates 等持 with "meditative concentrations" (which is not a glossary-recognized rendering for any term) and then applies the *samapatti* label ambiguously. The standard Yogacara mapping is 等持 = samadhi, 等至 = samapatti, 等引 = samahita. Even using the glossary as-is, "meditative concentrations" does not appear as a rendering for 等持.

**Required fix:** Render per the glossary entries. The clause "all formless attainments and whatever other meditative concentrations and attainments (*samapatti*)" should be revised to distinguish the two Chinese terms clearly. At minimum, "meditative concentrations" should become a recognized glossary term or be replaced by one.

## Major Findings (M)

### M-1. "All-encompassing object" instead of "pervasive object" for 遍滿所緣 (line 525)

The glossary specifies: 遍滿所緣 | sarvatraga-alambana | **pervasive object**

Line 525 renders this as "the all-encompassing object." This is an inconsistent rendering that departs from the glossary without authorization. The same passage also uses variant phrasings for the other three meditation objects:
- "the object that purifies conduct" (glossary: "purifying-conduct object")
- "the object of skill" (glossary: "skillful object")
- "the object that purifies afflictions" (glossary: "affliction-purifying object")

While the latter three are paraphrases that convey the same meaning, the switch from "pervasive" to "all-encompassing" for the first object introduces a genuinely different term.

**Required fix:** Replace "the all-encompassing object" with "the pervasive object." Consider standardizing the other three to match glossary forms as well.

### M-2. "Faculty of concentration" / "power of concentration" / "concentration factor" for 定根/定力/定等覺支 (lines 499, 501, 503)

The glossary says 定 (samadhi) should use "samadhi" when it represents samadhi-in-general. In the five faculties, five powers, and seven factors of awakening, these are samadhi-faculties. Other Buddhist translation traditions render these as "faculty of samadhi" (定根), "power of samadhi" (定力), and "samadhi factor of awakening" (定等覺支). The choice of "concentration" here creates an inconsistency: the text uses "samadhi" everywhere else for 三摩地/定, but switches to "concentration" in the 37 factors.

Additionally, "right concentration" at line 505 (正定) in the eightfold path is the established canonical rendering and is widely recognized, so it may be justified. But the five faculties/powers/factors entries should be consistent with the broader terminological framework.

**Suggested fix:** Consider "faculty of samadhi," "power of samadhi," and "samadhi factor of awakening" for consistency. If "concentration" is retained, document the rationale in the glossary. The "right concentration" (正定) in the eightfold path may remain as-is given its canonical status.

### M-3. "Mental aggregates" used without "skandhas" (line 513)

Line 513 contains: "(11) the name-body [i.e., the mental aggregates]"

The watchlist says: "aggregates (without context) -> skandhas. First use: 'the five skandhas (aggregates)'; thereafter: skandhas." While this is a translator's bracket (not running text), using "aggregates" without "skandhas" introduces the deprecated term as an explanatory gloss. A reader encountering this first would learn the deprecated term.

**Suggested fix:** Revise to "[i.e., the mental skandhas]" or "[i.e., the non-material skandhas (aggregates)]."

### M-4. Four meditation objects inconsistently rendered vs. glossary (line 525)

Beyond M-1 (the "all-encompassing" issue), the full set of four meditation objects at line 525 uses descriptive paraphrases instead of the glossary's established compound forms:
- "the all-encompassing object" vs. "pervasive object"
- "the object that purifies conduct" vs. "purifying-conduct object"
- "the object of skill" vs. "skillful object"
- "the object that purifies afflictions" vs. "affliction-purifying object"

These four technical terms have established glossary renderings from the detailed treatment in fascicles 26-27. Using different phrasings in fascicle 28 undermines cross-fascicle consistency.

**Required fix:** Align all four to glossary forms.

### m-6. Missing first-use pattern for prajna (line 23)

Glossary says prajna can be glossed as "wisdom." First appearance at line 23: "the superior training in prajna (*adhiprajna-shiksha*)" -- the Sanskrit is given for the compound but "wisdom" is not given as a standalone English gloss for prajna.

### m-7. "Right concentration" at line 49 in gradual path formula

Line 49: "the mind attains right concentration"

The Chinese is 心得正定. In this context 正定 can be read as "right samadhi" (the technical term) rather than "right concentration." This is a minor concern because "right concentration" is the established canonical rendering of 正定 in the gradual path formula and is widely recognized. However, it creates a tension with the general principle of preserving "samadhi" for 定/三摩地.

No fix required -- this is a known convention tension.

### m-8. Pali text titles in footnotes

Lines 621, 661, 667, 671, 673 reference Pali canonical texts by their Pali names:
- "Satipatthana Sutta" (MN 10)
- "Mahasatipatthana Sutta" (DN 22)
- "Anapanasati Sutta" (MN 118)
- "Kimatthiya Sutta" (AN 11.1-2)
- "Salayatana-vibhanga Sutta" (MN 137)

The watchlist says "sutta (Pali form) -> sutra." However, these are proper names of Pali canonical texts used in scholarly cross-references. Using their conventional Pali titles is standard scholarly practice. This is **not a violation** but is noted for consistency awareness. An alternative approach would be to use Sanskritized forms (e.g., "Smrityupasthana Sutra") but this is non-standard for Pali canon references.

No fix required.

### m-9. "Signless concentration" vs. "signless samadhi" (line 179)

Line 179: "the signless concentration (*animitta-samadhi*)"

The Chinese is 無相定. Since the Sanskrit is provided as *animitta-samadhi*, using "concentration" rather than "samadhi" for the English rendering creates a minor inconsistency. The glossary says 三摩地 standalone = samadhi, not concentration. While 定 here could warrant "concentration" per the glossary's contextual rule, the parenthetical Sanskrit *samadhi* makes the inconsistency visible.

**Suggested fix:** Consider "the signless samadhi (*animitta-samadhi*)" for consistency.

### m-10. "Establishing concentration" header at line 37/39

Line 37: "Because of three functions: establishing concentration, providing the basis for knowledge, and accomplishing the purpose."
Line 39: "**(1) Establishing concentration** refers to the superior training in ethical discipline."

The Chinese is 建立定義 where 定 = samadhi. "Establishing concentration" could be "establishing samadhi" for consistency with the glossary preference for preserving "samadhi." The text at line 39 says "enabling the mind to attain samadhi" -- using "samadhi" in the explanation but "concentration" in the header creates an internal inconsistency within the same paragraph.

**Suggested fix:** Consider "establishing samadhi" to match line 39's usage.

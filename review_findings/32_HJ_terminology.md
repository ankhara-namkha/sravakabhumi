## Flag (1): Terms Not in Glossary

### 1a. `kaya-anupassana` (line 231, footnote)

**Location:** `[^thirtysix]` footnote in 32.9:
> "The thirty-six bodily substances (三十六物) is a standard list for contemplation of the body (*kaya-anupassana*)"

**Issue:** The Sanskrit term `kaya-anupassana` appears in the footnote as a technical designation for the body-contemplation practice, but it is NOT in `MASTER_GLOSSARY.md`. The glossary has the Chinese `身念住` / Sanskrit `kaya-smrityupasthana` (foundation of mindfulness of the body), which is the standard Sravakabhumi rendering. `kaya-anupassana` is the Pali-derived compound form used in the Theravada tradition (Satipatthana Sutta commentary), not the Yogacara/Sanskrit rendering.

**Assessment:** This is a double issue:
- Not in glossary (Category 1)
- The compound `kaya-anupassana` mixes Sanskrit `kaya` with Pali-register suffix `anupassana` (Sanskrit would be `kaya-anupashyana` or simply `kaya-smrityupasthana`). While this appears in a footnote (not running text), it is inconsistent with NTC convention for a scholarly translation.

**Recommendation:** Either replace with `kaya-smrityupasthana` (matching the glossary entry for 身念住), or add `kaya-anupassana` to the glossary with a note explaining its Theravada vs. Yogacara usage. The footnote context (cross-referencing Pali parallels) may justify the Theravada form if explicitly flagged.

## Flag (2): Rendered Differently from Glossary

### 2a. "four formless aggregates" vs. glossary rendering

**Location:** Section 32.10, line 243 (body text) and line 253 (footnote):
> "You should further contemplate the four formless aggregates"
> "The four formless aggregates (四無色蘊) are feeling (*vedana*), perception (*samjna*), formations (*samskara*), and consciousness (*vijnana*)"

**Issue:** The translation uses "four formless aggregates" for 四無色蘊. The glossary entry for 蘊 is:

| Chinese | Sanskrit | English Rendering | Preferred in text |
|---------|----------|------------------|-------------------|
| 蘊 | skandha | skandha(s) / aggregate(s) | **skandhas** |

The glossary specifies that the preferred in-text form is "skandhas" (not "aggregates"), with the note: "First use: 'the five skandhas (aggregates)'". The watchlist further specifies: "aggregates (without context) → skandhas: First use: 'the five skandhas (aggregates)'; thereafter: skandhas."

The translation uses "aggregates" (not "skandhas") in 32.10. This should be "the four formless skandhas" in running text, following the "thereafter: skandhas" convention.

**Assessment:** Moderate. The term `skandha` was first glossed early in the fascicle series. By fascicle 32, running text should use "skandhas" not "aggregates." The footnote correctly provides `vedana`, `samjna`, `samskara`, `vijnana` individually. The compound "formless skandhas" may read slightly less naturally than "formless aggregates" in English, but the glossary rule is clear.

**Recommendation:** Change "four formless aggregates" → "four formless skandhas" in body text (line 243 and line 249). The footnote parenthetical "(四無色蘊)" may retain the gloss note as is.

## CRITICAL

### C1 — "anapanasati" (Pali form) used in running text alongside "anapanasmriti" (Sanskrit form) [Line 101]

**Location:** Section 31.3, Meaning subsection
**Text (line 101):** "...this is the meaning of anapanasati."
**Contrast with line 99:** "How does one who diligently cultivates mindfulness of breathing (*anapanasmriti*) practice the six-aspect vipashyana?"
**Issue:** The translation correctly uses the Sanskrit form *anapanasmriti* at the section opening (line 99) with proper parenthetical gloss. However, line 101's concluding phrase of the Meaning subsection switches to "anapanasati" — the **Pali form**, which is prohibited by the watchlist. The watchlist rules: "Use Sanskrit forms per NTC convention." The glossary entry (row 111) registers the term as "anapanasmriti" (Sanskrit). "Anapanasati" is the Pali equivalent and must not appear in running translation text.
**Required fix:** Change line 101 "this is the meaning of anapanasati" → "this is the meaning of *anapanasmriti*" (or "this is the meaning of mindfulness of breathing (*anapanasmriti*)").

## MAJOR

### M1 — "fire is heat" — rendering diverges from glossary characterization [Line 73]

**Location:** Section 31.2, Own-Characteristic subsection
**Text:** "earth is solidity; water is moisture; fire is heat; wind is lightness and movement; consciousness is discernment"
**Glossary entry (row 194):** "火界 | tejas-dhatu | fire element | fire element | Characterized by warmth (溫性)"
**Issue:** The glossary specifies the characteristic of fire as "warmth" (溫性), cross-referenced from the Chinese 溫性. The translation renders this as "heat." While semantically close, "heat" and "warmth" are distinct renderings and the glossary records 溫性 explicitly as "warmth." This is a deviation from the registered characterization. "Heat" may convey a stronger, more intense quality than 溫性 implies. All other elements match their glossary characterizations: earth → solidity (堅性) ✓, water → moisture (濕性) ✓, wind → lightness and movement (輕性動性) ✓. Fire alone deviates.
**Required fix:** Change "fire is heat" → "fire is warmth" to match the glossary's registered characterization of tejas-dhatu.

### M2 — "jivitindriya" introduced without English gloss [Line 107]

**Location:** Section 31.3, Common Characteristic subsection
**Text:** "The movement of in-breaths and out-breaths depends upon the life-faculty (*jivitindriya*) and the sentient body."
**Issue:** The term *jivitindriya* appears here with its Sanskrit form in parentheses, but does not appear in the MASTER_GLOSSARY. The entry for 命根 (jivitindriya) is absent from the glossary. This is a glossary gap: an unglosssed Sanskrit term appears in translation. Additionally, the rendering "life-faculty" requires verification — the only glossary reference to it is tangential (row 128, mrtyu-mara: "Death and the destruction of the life faculty"). Per glossary conventions, every Sanskrit technical term needs a registered entry. The term "life-faculty" is not cross-referenced to any glossary row. The format follows the correct pattern (English rendering + parenthetical Sanskrit), but the term needs to be added to the glossary before use.
**Required fix:** (1) Add 命根 | jivitindriya | life faculty | life faculty to MASTER_GLOSSARY.md. (2) Once added, verify "life-faculty" matches the registered English rendering. The hyphenated form "life-faculty" versus "life faculty" should be standardized.

## MINOR

### m1 — "duhkha-skandha" used without prior first-use gloss [Line 49]

**Location:** Section 31.1, Reasoning from dependence subsection
**Text:** "...accumulates the entire mass of suffering (*duhkha-skandha*)."
**Issue:** The term *duhkha-skandha* is not in the MASTER_GLOSSARY. The glossary has entries for individual forms: 苦 | duhkha | suffering (row 58), and 蘊 | skandha (implied via five skandhas entries), and 行苦 | samskara-duhkhata (row 279), but no entry for 大苦蘊 / duhkha-skandha as a compound. The translation renders it "mass of suffering (*duhkha-skandha*)" — an unregistered compound with an unregistered Sanskrit form. While the rendering is semantically defensible ("the entire mass/heap of suffering"), the compound needs a glossary entry.
**Assessment:** Minor, as the rendering is transparent and plausible. However, per glossary protocol, any Sanskrit technical compound appearing in translation must be registered.
**Suggested fix:** Add 大苦蘊 | duhkha-skandha | mass of suffering | mass of suffering to MASTER_GLOSSARY.md. Current rendering in the text is acceptable pending glossary registration.

### m2 — "conflicted dharma" / "non-conflicted dharma" — unregistered renderings [Line 109]

**Location:** Section 31.3, Categories subsection
**Text:** "...this is inversion — belonging to the dark category, a conflicted dharma, as described above. The opposite is non-inversion — belonging to the bright category, a non-conflicted dharma."
**Issue:** "Conflicted dharma" and "non-conflicted dharma" are not registered in the MASTER_GLOSSARY. These likely correspond to 有漏法 (sasrava-dharma, literally "dharma with outflows/effluents") and 無漏法 (anasrava-dharma, "dharma without outflows"). If so, the translation uses a non-standard rendering. The glossary has no entries for 有漏 or 無漏. The conventional NTC rendering for 有漏/無漏 is "with/without afflictive outflows" or "conditioned/unconditioned" — but neither "conflicted" nor the concept of outflows (asrava) is addressed in the glossary.
**Assessment:** Minor, as the passage could be a different Chinese term (煩惱相應/anon-煩惱相應 = "associated with afflictions/not associated"), which would make "conflicted" defensible. The source Chinese should be verified. The rendering is unregistered regardless.
**Suggested fix:** Verify Chinese source. If 有漏/無漏: add glossary entries and reconsider "conflicted" vs. standard "with outflows." If 煩惱相應/非煩惱相應: "conflicted" is acceptable but should be registered.

### m3 — "superior observation" for vipashyana — unregistered locution [Line 129]

**Location:** Section 31.4
**Text:** "...repeatedly investigates what has been discerned through the superior observation of vipashyana."
**Issue:** The phrase "superior observation of vipashyana" is a non-standard formulation. "Superior observation" does not appear in the glossary. The glossary registers 增上慧法 (adhiprajna-dharma) as "superior wisdom-dharma" (row 175) and 增上慧學 (adhiprajna-shiksha) as "superior training in prajna" (row 387). However, "superior observation" as an alternative name for or modifier of vipashyana is not registered. The watchlist also flags "special insight" as a prohibited Tibetan calque — "superior observation" avoids that specific prohibition, but it is an unusual compound not found in established translations.
**Assessment:** Minor. The phrase reads plausibly and is not prohibited. However, it should be verified against the Chinese source (possibly 增上觀) and registered if used consistently. If the Chinese is 增上觀 (adhiprajna-vipashyana), then "superior vipashyana" or "superior insight" (the latter itself cautioned in the 30_AD review) would be closer to established NTC patterns. The current "superior observation of vipashyana" is redundant if 觀 = vipashyana.
**Suggested fix:** Verify Chinese. If 增上觀 (adhiprajna-vipashyana): change to "the higher vipashyana" or "superior vipashyana." Register the choice in the glossary.

### m4 — "six-aspect vipashyana" — term not registered in glossary [Lines 23, 57, 67, 91, 99, 123, 131]

**Location:** Throughout sections 31.1–31.4, recurring
**Text:** "practice the six-aspect vipashyana" / "This is the six-aspect vipashyana"
**Issue:** The phrase "six-aspect vipashyana" is the central technical structure of fascicle 31. It is used seven times in sections 31.1–31.4 alone. However, this compound is not registered in the MASTER_GLOSSARY. The six aspects (Meaning, Things, Own-Characteristic, Common Characteristic, Categories, Time) are also not individually registered. Compare: the "four types of reasoning" are individually registered (rows 168–171). The six-aspect framework deserves comparable glossary entries, especially given its prominence in the fascicle.
**Assessment:** Minor as a terminology issue (the rendering is consistent and clear), but this is a glossary gap that should be addressed before the fascicle is complete.
**Suggested fix:** Add entries for 六行觀 (or the appropriate Chinese term) → "six-aspect vipashyana" and for the six individual aspects (義 → meaning, 事 → things, 自相 → own-characteristic, 共相 → common characteristic, 品 → categories, 時 → time) to MASTER_GLOSSARY.md.

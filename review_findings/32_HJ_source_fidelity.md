## Section 32.8 — Graduated Contemplation: From One to the Limitless [T30.0461c01]

### Finding 1 (m) — Minor numerical sequence deviation

**Chinese:** 復應於二於三於四於五於十二十三十四十五十。或百青瘀或千青瘀。
**English:** "then expand: to two, three, four, five, ten, twenty, thirty, forty, fifty, a hundred, a thousand"

The Chinese reads: 二, 三, 四, 五, 十, 二十, 三十, 四十, 五十, 百, 千. The English matches this correctly. **No issue.** Verified accurate.

### Finding 2 (m) — Slight compression of transition formula

**Chinese:** 於趣入時應作是念。如我今者勝解所作。無量青瘀廣說乃至無量骨鎖。真實青瘀乃至骨鎖。其量過此不可數知。
**English:** "When making this transition, reflect: 'The limitless livid corpses — and so forth through to the skeletons — that my imaginative attention has produced: the *actual* livid corpses and skeletons far exceed these and cannot be counted."

The Chinese has 於趣入時應作是念 ("when entering, one should reflect thus") — rendered as "when making this transition, reflect." The "should" modality (應) is slightly softened to a bare imperative, but this is consistent with the translation's register throughout. **Acceptable.**

### Finding 3 (m) — Minor compression: "beginningless past" for 從前際來

**Chinese:** 從前際來於彼彼有彼彼趣中輪迴生死
**English:** "From the beginningless past, cycling through birth and death in various realms and destinies"

彼彼有彼彼趣 literally "in this and that existence, in this and that destiny" — the English "various realms and destinies" is a clean compression. 有 (existence/bhava) is rendered as "realm" — slightly loose but not wrong in context. **Acceptable.**

### Finding 4 (C) — CRITICAL: Omission of 尸骸 ("corpse") in corpse-gathering passage

**Chinese:** 假使有能攝聚如是所棄尸骸令不壞爛。一切大地亦不容受。
**English:** "Even if one could gather all these discarded corpses and prevent them from decaying, the entire great earth could not contain them."

This is **accurately translated.** No omission here. Verified.

### Finding 5 (m) — 廣大脅山 rendered as "great Mount Vipula"

**Chinese:** 其聚量等廣大脅山
**English:** "would form a heap equal to great Mount Vipula"

脅山 is identified in the footnote as Mount Vipula. This is a plausible identification. 廣大 is "vast/great" — rendered as "great." **Acceptable.**

### Finding 6 (m) — 又非修習如是青瘀乃至骨鎖毘鉢舍那。應頓觀察。 — slight restructuring

**Chinese:** 又非修習如是青瘀乃至骨鎖毘鉢舍那。應頓觀察。
Literally: "Furthermore, one should not practice this vipashyana on the livid through to the skeleton by observing all at once."

**English:** "Furthermore, this vipashyana — from the livid through to the skeleton — should not be practiced by observing all stages at once."

Accurate. The double negative structure (非...應頓) is correctly captured as "should not be practiced by observing all stages at once." **No issue.**

### Finding 7 (M) — MAJOR: Slight omission in alternation instruction

**Chinese:** 纔應於一尸骸青瘀起勝解已。尋復令心於內寂靜。乃至於此所緣境相喜樂明淨。無諸擾惱不強勵力。齊爾所時應於如是尸骸青瘀發起勝解。若纔於此乃至勵力方現在前。爾時於內應修寂靜。

Literally: "Only after generating imaginative attention regarding a single corpse's lividity, immediately return the mind to inner stillness. [Continue] until this meditative object is joyful, clear, bright, free from disturbance, and without strained effort — for that duration, generate imaginative attention on the corpse's lividity. If [the object is present] only with effort, then cultivate inner stillness."

**English:** "Generate imaginative attention regarding a single livid corpse, then immediately return the mind to inner stillness. Sustain the contemplation only so long as the object appears joyful, clear, bright, free from disturbance, and without strained effort. The moment effort is required, return inward to cultivate stillness."

The Chinese has a two-step instruction: (a) first generate then immediately return to stillness (纔...尋復), (b) the duration marker 齊爾所時 — meaning "for just that duration" when the five qualities are present, sustain the contemplation, (c) if effort is needed, return to stillness. The English captures the logic correctly but compresses the 齊爾所時 temporal marker. The word 尸骸 (corpse/cadaver) appears twice in the Chinese — "一尸骸青瘀" and "尸骸青瘀" — but the English has "a single livid corpse" and "the contemplation," dropping the second explicit reference to "corpse's lividity." This is a minor structural smoothing rather than a content omission. **Downgrade to (m) — minor.**

### Finding 8 (m) — "安置眾相不顯現中" — slightly loose rendering

**Chinese:** 內略其心方便除遣安置眾相不顯現。中不全棄捨有分別相。亦不分別。
**English:** "withdraw the mind inward. Skillfully remove the manifold signs and set them aside so they no longer appear — yet without fully abandoning the formed object, and without engaging in discrimination."

The Chinese 方便除遣安置眾相不顯現中 is a dense clause: "skillfully remove and set aside [into a state where] the manifold signs do not appear." The English "set them aside so they no longer appear" is a good rendering. 中 here functions as a locative ("in the state where..."), which the English captures with "so that."

不全棄捨有分別相 = "without fully abandoning the formed/discriminated sign" — English "without fully abandoning the formed object." 有分別相 is more precisely "discriminated sign" or "sign with discrimination." "Formed object" is interpretive but defensible. **Acceptable, minor.**

## Section 32.9 — Light-Perception Applied; Entering the Foundations of Mindfulness [T30.0462a01]

### Finding 9 (m) — 彼瑜伽師復應教授告言。賢首。 — voice transition

**Chinese:** 彼瑜伽師復應教授告言。賢首。
**English:** "The teacher further instructs: 'Worthy One...'"

The Chinese explicitly says 彼瑜伽師 ("that yoga teacher") — the English "the teacher" is accurate. The "告言" (addressing, saying) is compressed into the colon. **No issue.**

### Finding 10 (m) — 汝先所取諸光明相 — "signs" plural vs. singular

**Chinese:** 汝先所取諸光明相
**English:** "the signs of luminosity you previously apprehended"

諸光明相 is indeed plural "signs of luminosity" — correctly rendered. **No issue.**

### Finding 11 (m) — Light-perception instruction: accurate

**Chinese:** 若汝能以光明俱心。照了俱心。明淨俱心。無闇俱心。修奢摩他毘鉢舍那。如是乃為於奢摩他毘鉢舍那道修光明想。
**English:** "If you can cultivate shamatha and vipashyana with a mind accompanied by luminosity, accompanied by illumination, accompanied by clarity and brightness, free from darkness — this is what it means to cultivate the perception of luminosity on the paths of shamatha and vipashyana."

Accurate. Four Chinese terms → four English phrases. 光明想 = "perception of luminosity." **No issue.**

### Finding 12 (M) — MAJOR: Omission of 後所修習 connecting clause

**Chinese:** 若有最初於所緣境多不分明。數習勝解。其相闇昧。由是因緣後所修習所有勝解。亦不分明。雖多串習而相闇昧。
**English:** "If from the very beginning your meditation object is mostly unclear, and you repeatedly develop imaginative attention with an obscure sign, then all subsequent imaginative attention will likewise be unclear — even with much repeated effort, the sign remains dark."

The Chinese 由是因緣 ("due to this cause/condition") is rendered as "then" — acceptable compression. 後所修習所有勝解 is "all subsequently cultivated imaginative attention" — English "all subsequent imaginative attention" drops 所修習 ("cultivated/practiced") but the meaning is preserved. 雖多串習而相闇昧 = "even though much habituated, the sign remains dark" — English "even with much repeated effort, the sign remains dark." 串習 is "habitual practice/repeated familiarity" rather than "effort" (勵力). This is a minor semantic shift but not a mistranslation. **Downgrade to (m) — minor.**

### Finding 13 (m) — Five signs recap accurate

**Chinese:** 如是汝由善取如是厭離相故。善取如是欣樂相故。善取如是奢摩他相故。善取如是毘鉢舍那相故。善取如是光明相故。
**English:** "because you have well apprehended the sign of disenchantment, the sign of delight, the sign of shamatha, the sign of vipashyana, and the sign of luminosity"

The five are listed correctly. The repetitive 善取如是...相故 structure is compressed into a single enumeration — good register decision. **No issue.**

### Finding 14 (m) — 於時時中 doubled instruction

**Chinese:** 於時時中內以寂靜。於時時中由隨相行毘鉢舍那。思擇諸法。
**English:** "alternating from time to time between inner stillness and the investigation of dharmas through sign-following vipashyana"

The Chinese has two parallel clauses each beginning with 於時時中 ("from time to time"): (a) inwardly abide in stillness, (b) through sign-following vipashyana, investigate dharmas. The English compresses into "alternating from time to time between..." which captures the parallel structure elegantly. **Accurate.**

### Finding 15 (m) — 即於不淨正修加行增上力故 — "through the force of this correct practice of impurity"

**Chinese:** 即於不淨正修加行增上力故。於諸念住漸次趣入。
**English:** "through the force of this correct practice of impurity, you gradually enter the foundations of mindfulness."

正修加行 = "correct cultivation practice/preparatory practice." English "correct practice" drops 加行 (preliminary practice/preparatory practice) but captures the sense. **Minor.**

### Finding 16 (m) — 將趣入時 vs. "When entering"

**Chinese:** 將趣入時汝應先於內身所有三十六物。始從髮毛乃至小便。善取其相。
**English:** "When entering, first apprehend the signs of the thirty-six substances within the internal body — beginning with head-hair and body-hair through to urine."

將趣入時 = "when about to enter / on the verge of entering." English "when entering" is slightly less precise — the Chinese emphasizes the preparatory moment before full entry. **Very minor.**

### Finding 17 (m) — Internal body contemplation conclusion

**Chinese:** 如是名為於內身中修循身觀。依自身內而發起故。
**English:** "This is called contemplating the body within the internal body — because it arises based on one's own internal body."

Accurate. 循身觀 = "contemplation following/tracking the body" — rendered as "contemplating the body." **No issue.**

## Section 32.10 — The Four Foundations via Asubha [T30.0462b01]

### Finding 18 (m) — External body contemplation: minor compression

**Chinese:** 次應於外諸不淨物善取其相。汝當發起青瘀勝解。廣說乃至骨鎖勝解。或狹小勝解。或廣大勝解。或無量勝解。
**English:** "Next, well apprehend the signs of external impure things. Generate imaginative attention regarding the livid, and so forth through to the skeleton — whether limited, extensive, or limitless."

The Chinese has three scale terms: 狹小 (narrow/limited), 廣大 (vast/extensive), 無量 (limitless). English "limited, extensive, or limitless" — accurate. **No issue.**

### Finding 19 (M) — MAJOR: Omission of "又於他身內外不淨。善取其相。令心明了。" as separate instruction

**Chinese:** 後復應於自身內外諸不淨物。善取其相令心明了。又於他身內外不淨。善取其相。令心明了。於自所愛汝當發起如是勝解。
**English:** "After this, well apprehend the signs of impurity both internal and external to your own body, making the mind clear. Likewise apprehend the internal and external impurity of others' bodies. Regarding your own beloved body, generate imaginative attention as follows:"

The Chinese has two distinct instructions: (a) 自身內外不淨 — internal and external impurity of one's own body, making the mind clear; (b) 又於他身內外不淨 — also others' bodies' internal and external impurity, making the mind clear. The English "Likewise apprehend the internal and external impurity of others' bodies" captures (b), but drops 令心明了 ("making the mind clear") for the second instruction. The first instruction includes it ("making the mind clear"), but the Chinese repeats it for the second as well. This is a minor omission of a repeated qualifier. **Downgrade to (m) — minor.**

### Finding 20 (m) — 於自所愛 — "regarding your own beloved body"

**Chinese:** 於自所愛汝當發起如是勝解
**English:** "Regarding your own beloved body"

自所愛 is "that which one loves/cherishes in oneself" — could mean one's beloved body or one's beloved self. "Your own beloved body" is a reasonable interpretation given the context of body contemplation. **Acceptable.**

### Finding 21 (m) — Charnel ground sequence

**Chinese:** 復於死已出送塚間。至塚間已棄之在地。棄在地已至青瘀位。至膿爛位。廣說乃至至骨鎖位。
**English:** "after death, carried out to the charnel ground, discarded on the earth — reaching the livid stage, the festering stage, and so forth through to the skeleton."

復於死已 = "moreover, after dying" — English "after death" drops 復 ("moreover/further"). 出送塚間 = "carried out and sent to the charnel ground." English "carried out to the charnel ground" — accurate. **No issue.**

### Finding 22 (m) — 依自他身若內若外而發起故 — conclusion of internal-external body

**Chinese:** 如是名為於內外身修循身觀。依自他身若內若外而發起故。
**English:** "This is called contemplating the body within the internal-and-external body — because it arises based on one's own and others' bodies, whether internal or external."

Accurate. **No issue.**

### Finding 23 (m) — Four formless aggregates introduction

**Chinese:** 汝復應於四無色蘊由聞思增上力分別取相。於其三分發起勝解。一於奢摩他品。二於無散亂品。三於毘鉢舍那品。
**English:** "You should further contemplate the four formless aggregates — apprehending their signs through the force of hearing and reflection, and generating imaginative attention in three divisions: (1) shamatha, (2) non-distraction, (3) vipashyana."

分別取相 = "discriminating/distinguishing and apprehending signs" — English "apprehending their signs" drops 分別 (discriminating). Minor. The three divisions are correctly listed. **Minor.**

### Finding 24 (m) — Shamatha division: six perceptions listed

**Chinese:** 起無相無分別寂靜想行。及無作用無思慕無躁動。離諸燒惱寂滅樂想行。
**English:** "the six perceptions arise — signlessness, non-discrimination, quiescence, non-activity, freedom from longing and agitation, peaceful bliss free from torment."

The Chinese groups them as: (a) 無相無分別寂靜想行 (signlessness, non-discrimination, quiescence — perception-activities), (b) 無作用無思慕無躁動 (non-activity, no longing, no agitation), (c) 離諸燒惱寂滅樂想行 (free from torment, peaceful bliss — perception-activities). The English lists all six clearly. 想行 ("perception-activities") is rendered simply as "perceptions" — consistent with the translation throughout. **No issue.**

### Finding 25 (M) — MAJOR: 受等四無色蘊 — "feeling *and the other* four formless aggregates" vs. "four formless aggregates"

**Chinese:** 於所緣境無亂受等四無色蘊。剎那剎那展轉別異。
**English:** "The four formless aggregates that arise undisturbed regarding the meditative object are moment by moment continuously different"

受等四無色蘊 = "feeling and the rest of the four formless aggregates" — i.e., feeling (受), perception (想), formations (行), consciousness (識). The 等 indicates "feeling *etc.*" — meaning feeling is being singled out as the lead member. The English "the four formless aggregates" is accurate in denotation (it refers to the same four), but the Chinese emphasis on 受 (feeling) as the leading term is lost. This is consistent with the section title "contemplating feeling, mind, and dharmas" (受心法觀) where feeling leads. The same construction appears three times in this section. **Minor structural point, not a content error.** Downgrade to (m).

### Finding 26 (m) — 剎那剎那展轉別異。唯是新新而非故故相續流轉。

**Chinese:** 剎那剎那展轉別異。唯是新新而非故故相續流轉。
**English:** "moment by moment continuously different — only new, never the old, flowing in succession."

剎那剎那 = "moment by moment" (ksana-ksana). 展轉別異 = "successively/continuously different." 唯是新新而非故故 = "only new-new and not old-old." 相續流轉 = "continuing to flow/turn." English captures this well. The doubling 新新/故故 is elegantly rendered as "only new, never the old." **Accurate.**

### Finding 27 (M) — MAJOR: Non-distraction division — omission of 過去盡滅 and restructuring

**Chinese:** 謂汝於先取諸境界緣諸境界墮不定地。過去盡滅及今失念。心亂所生諸相尋思隨煩惱境。增上受等四無色蘊。
**English:** "previously, when you apprehended various objects while in an unfixed state, the disturbed mind gave rise to signs, discursive thoughts, and secondary afflictions. The four formless aggregates that arose in connection with those disturbances have passed away; yet now, through loss of mindfulness, similar states may arise again."

The Chinese is dense: "that previously, when apprehending and attending to various objects, you fell into the unfixed ground (不定地). [Those states] have passed away and been destroyed (過去盡滅). And now, through loss of mindfulness (今失念), the four formless aggregates that arise under the influence of signs, discursive thoughts, and secondary-affliction objects produced by the disturbed mind."

The English restructures this substantially:
- 墮不定地 = "fell into the unfixed ground/state" → "while in an unfixed state" — accurate
- 過去盡滅 = "have passed away and been utterly destroyed" → "have passed away" — 盡滅 (completely destroyed/extinguished) is weakened to "passed away"
- 及今失念 = "and now, through loss of mindfulness" → "yet now, through loss of mindfulness" — accurate
- The English adds "similar states may arise again" — this is an interpretive addition not explicit in the Chinese, though it is implied by the context (the purpose of the instruction is to address recurring disturbances)

The Chinese does NOT explicitly say "similar states may arise again" — it says the past states are destroyed, but now through loss of mindfulness, the aggregates influenced by these disturbance-objects [continue/recur]. The English interpretation is reasonable but adds explanatory content. **Major — addition of interpretive material.**

### Finding 28 (m) — 誑幻所作 rendering

**Chinese:** 如是諸法其性皆是誑幻所作。暫時而有率爾現前。多諸過患。其性無常不可保信。
**English:** "all such dharmas are by nature the work of deception and illusion — temporarily present, abruptly appearing, filled with dangers, impermanent, unreliable."

誑幻所作 = "the work of deception and illusion" — accurate. 暫時而有 = "temporarily existing" → "temporarily present" — accurate. 率爾現前 = "abruptly/suddenly appearing before one" → "abruptly appearing" — accurate. 多諸過患 = "having many dangers/faults" → "filled with dangers" — accurate. **No issue.**

### Finding 29 (m) — Vipashyana division: 有相有分別作意

**Chinese:** 住有相有分別作意。於有分別有相所緣。增上內所生受等四無色蘊。
**English:** "abide in attention that has signs and discrimination. The four formless aggregates that arise internally in connection with this discriminated object"

有相有分別作意 = "attention with signs and with discrimination." 於有分別有相所緣 = "regarding the discriminated, signed object." 增上內所生 = "arising internally under the influence of." The English captures this. **Accurate.**

### Finding 30 (m) — 如前所說發起勝解 — "generate imaginative attention as before"

**Chinese:** 如前所說發起勝解。
**English:** "Generate imaginative attention as before."

如前所說 = "as previously explained" — English "as before" is a slight compression but acceptable. **No issue.**

## Critical Issues

### CRIT-1: 靜慮 (dhyana) rendered as "meditative absorption" — deviates from glossary preferred form

**Severity:** Critical
**Lines:** 372, 390, 392, 394, 404, 409, 417, 422
**Glossary entry:** 靜慮 | dhyana | dhyana / meditative absorption | **Preferred in text: dhyana**. First use: "the dhyanas (meditative absorptions)"; thereafter: dhyanas.
**Actual usage:**
- Line 372: "they are able, within this, not to abandon their **meditative absorption**"
- Line 390 (heading): "**(d) Not abandoning meditative absorption**"
- Line 392: "how does the bhikshu yogin not abandon **meditative absorption**?"
- Line 394: "what is meant by the bhikshu yogin not abandoning **meditative absorption**"
- Line 404 (verse): "constantly upon the image in **meditative absorption**"
- Line 409: "Constantly upon the image in **meditative absorption**"
- Line 417 (verse): "in **meditative absorption** with constant, earnest mindfulness"
- Line 422: "In **meditative absorption** with constant, earnest mindfulness"

**Chinese source:** 不捨靜慮 = "not abandoning dhyana." 靜慮 appears eight times in this section.
**Required fix:** Replace "meditative absorption" with "dhyana" throughout. Section headings and verse renderings should match: "not abandoning dhyana." The glossary permits "meditative absorption" only as secondary, with "dhyana" as preferred.
**Note:** Line 356 correctly uses "first dhyana," "second dhyana," etc. The inconsistency is specifically in the Revata dialogue and verse commentary subsections.

### CRIT-3: 慈愍 rendered as "loving-kindness" — drops the 愍 (compassion) component without note

**Severity:** Critical
**Lines:** 326, 376, 428, 444
**Glossary entry:** 慈 | maitri | loving-kindness — "loving-kindness" is for 慈 alone.
**Chinese source:** Lines use 慈愍 (慈 + 愍 = maitri + karuna / loving-kindness + compassion). This is a compound.
**Actual usage:**
- Line 326: "impurity, **loving-kindness**, the nature of conditions..."
- Line 376: "they should settle the mind upon **loving-kindness**"
- Line 428 (fn): "Purifying-conduct objects (impurity, **loving-kindness**, etc.)"
- Line 444 (fn): "aversion → **loving-kindness**"

**Assessment:** The Chinese 慈愍 in these contexts is used as the unified meditative object for aversion-types (confirmed at source line 28 and 40). Rendering it as just "loving-kindness" silently drops the compassion element. Per design invariants ("no silent omission"), a translator's note or bracketed gloss is required at first occurrence, or the glossary should be updated to include 慈愍 as a compound entry. The current rendering is not flagrantly wrong but is incomplete and unglosarized.
**Required fix:** Add 慈愍 as a glossary entry: "loving-kindness and compassion" or "benevolent compassion" with note explaining the compound; OR add bracketed note at first use: "loving-kindness [and compassion] (*maitri-karuna*)."

### MOD-7: "formations" for 諸行 at line 378 — context ambiguity between skandha and general usage

**Severity:** Moderate
**Line:** 378
**Glossary entry:** 行 | samskara | formation(s) | formations | "In skandha context"
**Actual usage (line 378):** "confused about the individual characteristics of **formations**" (translating 諸行自相)
**Chinese source:** 若愚諸行自相 = "if confused about the individual characteristics of all conditioned phenomena/formations"
**Assessment:** The glossary specifies "formations" for 行 "in skandha context." At line 378, 諸行 refers to "all conditioned phenomena" broadly — not strictly the skandha of formations (行蘊). However, "formations" is the standard English for 諸行 as well, per Buddhist convention. This is technically correct but the glossary note "in skandha context" could be misread as restricting "formations" to only the skandha sense.
**Required fix:** Clarify the glossary entry: "formations" for 行/諸行 in both "the formation skandha" and "all conditioned phenomena" contexts. No change needed in translation text — usage is acceptable but the glossary should be clarified.

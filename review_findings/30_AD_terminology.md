## CRITICAL

### C1 — "superior insight" for 勝觀 (vipashyana) [Line 78]

**Location:** Section 30.3, teacher's praise speech
**Text:** "who accomplishes dhyana and grows in superior insight"
**Source:** 靜慮成就勝觀增長
**Issue:** 勝觀 is a standard Chinese rendering of *vipashyana*. The translation renders it "superior insight," which is not in the glossary and closely resembles the watchlist-prohibited "special insight" (banned as an "over-technical Tibetan calque"). The glossary entry for vipashyana is clear: preferred form in text is "vipashyana." At minimum, this passage requires a parenthetical on first occurrence: "vipashyana (insight)" or "superior vipashyana." The current rendering "superior insight" is an unregistered deviation that risks drift toward the watchlist-banned Tibetan calque.
**Required fix:** Change to "accomplishes dhyana and grows in vipashyana" or "accomplishes dhyana and deepens vipashyana practice." If "superior" is retained (as modifier of 勝觀's meaning), gloss: "superior vipashyana (insight)."

## MAJOR

### M1 — "moral discipline" for 尸羅/shila [Line 94]

**Location:** Section 30.4, first inquiry point
**Text:** "have you purified your moral discipline (*shila*) and established right view?"
**Source:** 善淨尸羅
**Issue:** The translation uses the compound "moral discipline" as the English equivalent, with *shila* in italics as parenthetical. The glossary specifies the preferred in-text form as "shila or discipline" — NOT "moral discipline." "Moral discipline" is a non-standard compound that does not match either registered rendering. The parenthetical format `(*shila*)` is also inconsistent with glossary convention, which introduces as "shila (ethical discipline)" on first occurrence in a fascicle.
**Required fix:** Render as "have you purified your shila (ethical discipline)" on first occurrence, or simply "have you purified your shila" thereafter. Drop "moral discipline" as a compound.

### M2 — "pure life" for 梵行 (brahmacarya) [Line 78]

**Location:** Section 30.3, teacher's praise speech
**Text:** "bringing no disgrace upon the wise companions in the pure life"
**Source:** 不辱有智同梵行者
**Issue:** 梵行 is registered in the glossary as *brahmacarya*, preferred rendering "pure conduct" (with note "celibate conduct" as alternate). "Pure life" is neither of these registered renderings. While semantically close, the unregistered deviation from "pure conduct" creates inconsistency with how 梵行 is rendered throughout the translation series.
**Required fix:** Change to "companions in the pure conduct" (*brahmacarya*), or at minimum "companions in pure conduct."

### M3 — First-use gloss absent for *shamatha* and *vipashyana*

**Location:** Fascicle 30 overall (sections 30.1–30.4 checked here)
**Issue:** Section 30.1's summary verse mentions "one-pointed mind" (心一境性), which is correctly rendered per the watchlist. However, the summary verse also mentions topics that will be treated in subsequent sections (shamatha and vipashyana by implication). More directly: this is the opening of Fascicle 30, and neither *shamatha* nor *vipashyana* appears with a first-use gloss in sections 30.1–30.4. The glossary requires: "shamatha (calm abiding)" and "vipashyana (insight)" on first occurrence in each fascicle. The source text (30.1 summary verse, footnote 30) references "one-pointed mind" and "cultivating attention" as topics implying these terms. When shamatha and vipashyana next appear in sections 30.5+ (the source text at T30.0450b27 explicitly names 奢摩他 and 毘鉢舍那), the translator must ensure first-use glosses are present.
**Status:** Not yet a defect in 30.1–30.4 (neither term appears in these sections), but this review flags it as a watch-item for 30.5 onward. No fix required here; verification required at 30.5.

## MINOR

### m1 — "noble one" as address form [Line 58]

**Location:** Section 30.3, teacher's opening address
**Text:** "Well done, well done, noble one!"
**Source:** 善哉善哉賢首 (lit. "Worthy chief" or "Virtuous one foremost")
**Issue:** The glossary defines 聖 (arya) as "noble one(s)" = those who have attained direct realization of the truths (stream-enterer through arhat). Here the Chinese has 賢首 (xian-shou), not 聖 (arya). The student being addressed is explicitly a *beginner* (初修業者), not an arya. "Noble one" could mislead readers into thinking this is a technical arya address. Alternative registered renderings might be "worthy one," "virtuous one," or "good friend."
**Assessment:** Minor, as "noble one" is used loosely in many Buddhist translations as a respectful address form (not always reserved for aryas). But the specific Chinese 賢首 should be noted.
**Suggested fix:** Change to "Well done, well done, worthy one!" to avoid conflating 賢首 with the technical arya category.

### m2 — "wisdom" for 智光明 (light of wisdom) [Line 76]

**Location:** Section 30.3, teacher's encouragement
**Text:** "you alone seek the great light of wisdom"
**Source:** 獨求獲得大智光明
**Issue:** "Wisdom" here is used in a general/poetic context (大智光明 = "great light of wisdom/knowledge"). The glossary notes that 慧 (prajna) is rendered "prajna or wisdom" depending on context, with prajna preferred when technical. In this poetic/rhetorical passage, "wisdom" is appropriate. No flag for usage, but the distinction should be maintained: this is 智 (jnana/wisdom), not 慧 (prajna). The rendering is acceptable; note for consistency tracking.
**Assessment:** Acceptable. No fix required. Flag for awareness.

### m3 — "birth and death" for 生死 (samsara) [Line 66]

**Location:** Section 30.3, teacher's encouragement
**Text:** "the perilous wilderness paths of birth and death"
**Source:** 生死曠野險道
**Issue:** 生死 (jati-marana / samsara) is rendered "birth and death" rather than "samsara." The glossary registers 輪迴/生死 as *samsara*, preferred "samsara." The compound "birth and death" is a literal translation acceptable in poetic/rhetorical contexts but diverges from the preferred glossary term. In a rhetorical praise passage, "the wilderness paths of samsara" would maintain both tone and terminological consistency.
**Assessment:** Minor deviation in a poetic context. Consistent with some prior precedents in the translation. Flag as a minor inconsistency.
**Suggested fix:** "the perilous wilderness paths of samsara" — or retain "birth and death" with awareness it is a poetic departure.

### m4 — Pali cross-references in footnote [Line 82, footnote]

**Location:** Footnote 4 (four floods)
**Text:** "Cf. SN 45.171; AN 4.10."
**Issue:** SN = Samyutta Nikaya (Pali canon), AN = Anguttara Nikaya (Pali canon). The design invariants prohibit using Pali-form terms in translation text (vipassana → vipashyana, etc.), but the restriction on Pali forms is primarily about Sanskrit/Pali term substitution, not cross-reference citation formats. However, citing Pali suttas as cross-references for a Chinese Abhidharma text raises the circularity concern: this is a Sarvastivada/Yogacara text, and citing Theravada Pali canon parallels could mislead readers about doctrinal lineage. More importantly, the reference numbers appear incorrect: SN 45 is the Magga-samyutta (Path Connected Discourses); "SN 45.171" does not correspond to a standard sutta on the four floods. The four floods are discussed in SN 45.171 is not a standard reference — the relevant text would be AN 4.10 (Ogha Sutta). The SN reference may be in error.
**Assessment:** The SN 45.171 reference should be verified or removed. AN 4.10 (Ogha Sutta) is the standard canonical reference for the four floods.
**Suggested fix:** Either correct to "Cf. AN 4.10 (Ogha Sutta)" alone, or expand with proper Taisho parallel references instead of Pali citations.

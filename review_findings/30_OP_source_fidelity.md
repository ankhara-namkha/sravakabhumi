## Vipashyana Applied to the Contemplation of Impurity

**Reviewer:** Agent S (Source Fidelity)

**English translation:** translation/fascicle_30_en.md, lines 366–430 (section 30.15)

## Section Count

The Chinese source for this section is a single continuous passage (0452a11–0453a28) covering the six-aspect vipashyana applied to impurity contemplation. The English translation covers it as a single section (30.15). The passage immediately following (0453a29) begins the loving-kindness contemplation, which is NOT included in section 30.15. **Section boundaries match.**

The six aspects enumerated in both Chinese and English are: (1) Meaning 義, (2) Things 事, (3) Characteristics 相 (subdivided into own-characteristic 自相 and common characteristic 共相), (4) Categories 品, (5) Time 時, (6) Reasoning 理 (subdivided into four reasonings). **All six aspects are present in the English. Structure matches.**

## Sentence-by-Sentence Comparison

### Opening (0452a11)

**Chinese:** 云何勤修不淨觀者。尋思六事差別所緣毘鉢舍那。
**English:** "How does one who diligently cultivates the contemplation of impurity (asubha) practice the six-aspect vipashyana?"
**Verdict:** Accurate. The Sanskrit *asubha* is a valid addition (not in Chinese but standard scholarly gloss). No issue.

### 1. Meaning 義 (0452a11–a16)

**Chinese:** 謂依不淨增上正法。聽聞受持增上力故。由等引地如理作意解了其義。知此不淨實為不淨深可厭逆。其性朽穢惡臭生臭。由如是等種種行相。於先所聞依諸不淨所說正法。解了其義。如是名為於諸不淨尋思其義。

**English:** "Based on the correct teachings concerning impurity — through the dominant force of hearing and retaining them — one enters meditative equipoise with wise attention and understands their meaning: impurity is truly impure, deeply repulsive, its nature decaying and putrid, rank with the stench of corruption. Through these and other aspects, one understands the meaning of the correct teachings on impurity previously heard."

**Findings:**
- Chinese 惡臭生臭 means "foul-smelling and raw-smelling" (two distinct types of stench). The English "rank with the stench of corruption" collapses both into one phrase. **Minor imprecision** — the Chinese distinguishes 惡臭 (putrid stench) from 生臭 (raw/flesh stench). The translation captures the general sense but loses the dual description.
- The closing 如是名為於諸不淨尋思其義 is not explicitly translated as a formula. The English implicitly conveys this but does not include a closing tag like "This is investigating the meaning of impurity." **Minor structural omission** — the six-aspect sections in the Chinese consistently end with 如是名為... closing formulas. The English sometimes includes them and sometimes omits them. Inconsistent handling; see pattern below.

### 2. Things 事 (0452a16–a18)

**Chinese:** 云何名為尋思彼事。謂彼如是解了義已。觀不淨物建立二分。謂內及外。如是名為尋思彼事。

**English:** "Having understood the meaning, one observes that impure things are established in two divisions: internal and external."

**Verdict:** Accurate and complete. The closing formula 如是名為尋思彼事 is omitted in the English, consistent with the compression pattern.

### 3. Own-Characteristic — Internal (自相, 0452a18–a25)

**Chinese:** 云何名為尋思自相。謂且於內身中所有穢朽不淨發起勝解。了知身中有髮毛等廣說乃至腦膜小便。復於如是身中所有多不淨物。攝為二界發起勝解。所謂地界及以水界。始於髮毛乃至大便起地勝解。始於淚污乃至小便起水勝解。如是名為依內不淨尋思自相。

**English:** "Regarding the internal body, one arouses the conviction of its impure, decaying contents. One knows that within the body there are hair, body-hair, and so forth through to brain-membrane and urine. One further classifies these many impure substances into two elements. From hair and body-hair through to feces, one develops the conviction of the earth element. From tears and filth through to urine, one develops the conviction of the water element. This is investigating the own-characteristic of internal impurity."

**Findings:**
- 髮毛: Chinese 髮 = head-hair, 毛 = body-hair. English "hair, body-hair" is correct.
- 廣說乃至 ("broadly explained, up to") = "and so forth through to" — acceptable.
- 大便 = feces. English "feces" — correct.
- 淚污 = tears and filth. English "tears and filth" — correct.
- Closing formula 如是名為依內不淨尋思自相 IS translated: "This is investigating the own-characteristic of internal impurity." Good.
- **No issues.**

### 4. Own-Characteristic — External (0452a25–0452c04)

This is the longest sub-section, covering the nine stages of corpse decomposition plus the two types of linked skeleton, the two ways of taking up the skeleton-sign, and the three types of deterioration.

#### Corpse-sign taking (0452a25–a29)

**Chinese:** 復於其外諸不淨物。由青瘀等種種行相發起勝解。謂先發起青瘀勝解。或親自見或從他聞。或由分別所有死屍。如是死屍。或男或女或非男女。或親或怨或是中庸。或劣或中或復是勝。或是少年或是中年或是老年。

**English:** "Regarding external impure things, one develops conviction through the stages of decomposition, beginning with lividity. First, one takes up the sign of a corpse — whether seen personally, heard about from others, or contemplated through mental imagery. This corpse may be male, female, or of indeterminate sex; a dear one, an enemy, or a stranger; of low, middle, or high status; young, middle-aged, or old."

**Findings:**
- 或由分別 = "or through discrimination/mental construction." English "contemplated through mental imagery" is a reasonable interpretation but slightly interpretive. The Chinese says "or through conceptual formation" (分別). **Minor** — acceptable rendering.
- 或劣或中或復是勝 = "inferior, middling, or superior." English "low, middle, or high status" — reasonable.
- **No significant issues.**

#### Nine stages of decomposition (0452a29–0452b22)

**(1) Lividity 青瘀 (0452a29–b02)**
**Chinese:** 取彼相已若此死屍死經一日。血流已盡未至膿爛。於是發起青瘀勝解。
**English:** "A corpse dead one day — the blood has dried but putrefaction has not begun. One develops the conviction of lividity."
- 血流已盡: "blood has finished flowing" / "blood has dried." English "blood has dried" is close. Chinese literally says "the flow of blood is exhausted." **Acceptable.**

**(2) Putrefaction 膿爛 (0452b02–b03)**
**Chinese:** 若此死屍死經二日。已至膿爛未生蟲蛆。於是發起膿爛勝解。
**English:** "Dead two days — putrefaction has begun but worms have not yet appeared."
- The conviction label (膿爛勝解) is omitted in the English — the reader infers it from the heading. **Acceptable compression.**

**(3) Decay and bloating 爛壞/[月*逄]脹 (0452b03–b05)**
**Chinese:** 若此死屍死經七日。已生蟲蛆身體已壞。於是發起爛壞勝解[月*逄]脹勝解。
**English:** "Dead seven days — worms have appeared and the body has broken down."
- The Chinese explicitly names TWO convictions here: 爛壞勝解 (decay conviction) AND [月*逄]脹勝解 (bloating conviction). The English heading says "Decay and bloating" but the text only says "the body has broken down" — it does not explicitly mention bloating as a separate observation. **Minor omission** — the bloating ([月*逄]脹) as a distinct conviction is somewhat absorbed into "broken down." The heading captures it but the body text does not.

**(4) Being devoured 食噉 (0452b05–b06)**
**Chinese:** 若此死屍為諸狐狼鵄梟鵰鷲烏鵲餓狗之所食噉。於是發起食噉勝解。
**English:** "The corpse is consumed by foxes, wolves, owls, vultures, crows, and hungry dogs."
- 鵄梟 = owls (specifically eagle-owls and screech-owls). English "owls" — correct.
- 鵰鷲 = eagles and vultures. English "vultures" — **Minor omission**: 鵰 (eagles/raptors) is dropped, only 鷲 (vultures) is rendered. The Chinese lists two distinct birds.
- 烏鵲 = crows and magpies. English "crows" — **Minor omission**: 鵲 (magpies) is dropped.
- These are minor but the Chinese does enumerate more animals than the English renders.

**(5) Stripped to sinew and bone 異赤 (0452b07–b08)**
**Chinese:** 即此死屍既被食已。皮肉血盡唯筋纏骨。於是發起異赤勝解。
**English:** "After being devoured, skin, flesh, and blood are gone; only sinew binds the bones."
- The conviction name 異赤勝解 is not translated or explained. 異赤 literally means "different-red" / "reddened" — referring to the reddish appearance of sinew-bound bones stripped of flesh. The English heading "Stripped to sinew and bone" is descriptive but loses the technical term. **Minor** — the heading conveys the content.

**(6) Scattering 分散 (0452b08–b10)**
**Chinese:** 若此死屍或被食噉。支節分離散在處處。或有其肉或無其肉或餘少肉。於是發起分散勝解。
**English:** "The devoured limbs lie scattered in various places — some with flesh, some without, some with traces of flesh remaining."
- **Accurate.** Good.

**(7) Bare bones 骨 (0452b10–b14)**
**Chinese:** 若此死屍骨節分散。手骨異處。足骨異處。膝骨異處。髀骨異處。髖骨異處。髆骨異處。臂骨異處。脊骨異處。頷輪異處。齒鬘異處。髑髏異處。見是事已起骨勝解。
**English:** "The bones of the corpse lie scattered: hand-bones in one place, foot-bones in another — knees, thighs, hips, shoulders, arms, spine, jaw, teeth, and skull, all separated."
- The Chinese lists 11 bone types: 手骨, 足骨, 膝骨, 髀骨, 髖骨, 髆骨, 臂骨, 脊骨, 頷輪, 齒鬘, 髑髏.
- The English lists 9: hand, foot, knees, thighs, hips, shoulders, arms, spine, jaw, teeth, skull. Wait — that is 11 items if we count them carefully: hand-bones, foot-bones, knees, thighs, hips, shoulders, arms, spine, jaw, teeth, skull = 11. **Correct match.** The enumeration is preserved.

**(8) Linked skeleton 鎖 (0452b14–b16)**
**Chinese:** 若復思惟如是骸骨共相連接而不分散。唯取麁相不委細取支節屈曲。如是爾時起鎖勝解。
**English:** "One contemplates the skeletal remains still connected, not yet scattered. Taking only the gross appearance without detailed attention to the joints and articulations, one develops the conviction of a linked skeleton."
- **Accurate.**

**(9) Bone-chain 骨鎖 (0452b16–b17)**
**Chinese:** 若委細取支節屈曲。爾時發起骨鎖勝解。
**English:** "When one attends in detail to the joints and their articulations, one develops the conviction of a bone-chain."
- **Accurate.**

#### Two types of linked skeleton (0452b17–b20)

**Chinese:** 又有二鎖。一形骸鎖。二支節鎖。形骸鎖者。謂從血鑊脊骨乃至髑髏所住。支節鎖者。謂臂髆等骨連鎖及髀膞等骨連鎖。此中形骸鎖說名為鎖。若支節鎖說名骨鎖。
**English:** "There are two types of linked skeleton: the trunk chain — running from the sacrum and spine up to the skull — and the limb chain — the linked bones of arms, shoulders, thighs, and shins. The trunk chain is called simply 'linked skeleton'; the limb chain is called 'bone-chain.'"

**Findings:**
- 血鑊 — This is a difficult term. It likely refers to the sacrum/pelvis area (blood-cauldron). English "sacrum" is a reasonable scholarly rendering. **Acceptable.**
- 膞 = shins/lower leg bones. English "shins" — correct.
- The distinction between 鎖 (linked skeleton = trunk) and 骨鎖 (bone-chain = limbs) is correctly preserved. Good.

#### Two ways of taking up the skeleton-sign (0452b20–b24)

**Chinese:** 復有二種取骨鎖相。一取假名綵畫木石泥等所作骨鎖相。二取真實骨鎖相。若思惟假名骨鎖相時。爾時唯名起鎖勝解。不名骨鎖。若思惟真實骨鎖相時。爾時名起骨鎖勝解。
**English:** "There are also two ways of taking up the skeleton-sign: from depictions — paintings, carvings in wood or stone, clay models — or from actual skeletal remains. Contemplating a depicted skeleton produces the conviction of a 'linked' representation. Contemplating an actual skeleton produces the conviction of a bone-chain."

**Findings:**
- 假名綵畫木石泥等所作 = "conventionally designated, made by painting, wood, stone, clay, etc." English "from depictions — paintings, carvings in wood or stone, clay models" — accurate and well rendered.
- The key doctrinal distinction: contemplating a representation (假名) gives only 鎖勝解 (linked conviction, not bone-chain), while contemplating actual remains gives 骨鎖勝解 (bone-chain conviction). **Correctly rendered.**

#### Three types of deterioration (0452b24–b28)

**Chinese:** 又即此外造色色相三種變壞。一自然變壞。二他所變壞。三俱品變壞。始從青瘀乃至[月*逄]脹。是自然變壞。始從食噉乃至分散。是他所變壞。若骨若鎖及以骨鎖。是俱品變壞。若能如是如實了知外不淨相。是名尋思外諸所有不淨自相。

**English:** "Furthermore, these external forms undergo three types of deterioration: (1) natural deterioration — from lividity through bloating; (2) deterioration caused by others — from being devoured through scattering; (3) combined deterioration — bare bones, linked skeleton, and bone-chain. Truly knowing external impurity in this way is investigating the own-characteristic of external impurity."

**Findings:**
- 外造色色相: "external derived-form appearances." The English "external forms" is adequate but drops 造色 (derived form / *rupya-rupa*). **Minor** — technical precision lost but meaning preserved.
- The three-fold classification and their ranges are all correctly mapped. Good.
- Closing formula is translated. Good.

### 5. Shared/Common Characteristic 共相 (0452b28–0452c05)

**Chinese:** 云何尋思不淨共相。謂若內身外淨色相未有變壞。若在外身不淨色相已有變壞。由在內身不淨色相平等法性相似法性發起勝解。能自了知我淨色相亦有如是同彼法性。若諸有情成就如是淨色相者。彼淨色相亦有如是同彼法性。譬如在外不淨色相。是名尋思不淨共相。

**English:** "One's own body, with its outwardly clean appearance, has not yet deteriorated. The external corpse has already deteriorated. Yet through the conviction that the impure nature of one's own body is equal to and of the same kind as the corpse, one can know: 'My apparently clean form possesses this same nature.' All beings who possess apparently clean forms — their clean forms likewise possess this same nature, just as the external impure forms. This is investigating the common characteristic of impurity."

**Findings:**
- 平等法性相似法性: "equal dharma-nature and similar dharma-nature." English "equal to and of the same kind as" captures the sense. **Accurate.**
- 由在內身不淨色相: The Chinese says the impure nature (不淨色相) is present within the internal body — i.e., even the living body already contains the impure nature. The English "the impure nature of one's own body" conveys this. **Correct.**
- The heading says "Shared Characteristic" while the closing tag says "common characteristic." **Inconsistency** — minor, but "common characteristic" is more standard for 共相. Recommend standardizing to "common characteristic" throughout.

### 6. Categories 品 (0452c05–c16)

**Chinese:** 云何名為尋思彼品。謂作是思。若我於彼諸淨色相不淨法性不如實知。於內於外諸淨色相發起貪欲便為顛倒。黑品所攝。是有諍法有苦有害。有諸災患有遍燒惱。由是因緣發起當來生老病死愁歎憂苦種種擾惱。若我於彼諸淨色相不淨法性。如實隨觀便無顛倒。白品所攝。是無諍法無苦無害。廣說乃至由此因緣能滅當來生老病死乃至擾惱。若諸黑品。我今於彼不應忍受應斷應遣。若諸白品。我今於彼未生應生。生已令住增長廣大。如是名為尋思彼品。

**English:** "One reflects: 'If I do not truly know the impure nature underlying apparently clean forms, and generate desire for clean forms internal and external, this is inversion — belonging to the black category: conflicted, painful, harmful, laden with calamity and burning distress. Through this, future birth, aging, sickness, death, grief, lamentation, and every sort of disturbance arise. If I truly observe the impure nature underlying these apparently clean forms, this is non-inversion — belonging to the white category: free from conflict, free from suffering and harm. Through this, future birth, aging, and all disturbance are extinguished. The black I should not tolerate but should cut off and drive away. The white, if not yet arisen, I should generate; once arisen, I should maintain and develop.'"

**Findings:**
- 有諍法有苦有害。有諸災患有遍燒惱: The Chinese lists five qualities — (1) 有諍 conflicted, (2) 有苦 painful, (3) 有害 harmful, (4) 有諸災患 with calamities, (5) 有遍燒惱 with pervasive burning distress. The English renders these as "conflicted, painful, harmful, laden with calamity and burning distress." **All five captured.** Good.
- 廣說乃至: "broadly explained, up to" — this is a formulaic abbreviation in the Chinese, indicating the white-category qualities mirror the black ones. English renders as "free from conflict, free from suffering and harm" and then "Through this, future birth, aging, and all disturbance are extinguished." This appropriately abbreviates while conveying the mirror pattern. **Acceptable.**
- 生已令住增長廣大: "once arisen, cause it to abide, increase, and expand." English "once arisen, I should maintain and develop" compresses 住增長廣大 (abide, increase, expand) into "maintain and develop." **Minor compression** — loses "expand" (廣大) but meaning preserved.
- 愁歎憂苦: "grief, lamentation, worry, suffering." English "grief, lamentation." **Minor omission** — 憂 (worry/sorrow) and 苦 (suffering) are partially absorbed into "every sort of disturbance" at the end but the specific terms are compressed.

### 7. Time 時 (0452c16–c23)

**Chinese:** 云何名為尋思彼時。謂作是思。若內所有諸淨色相在現在世。若外所有不淨色相亦現在世。諸過去世曾淨色相。彼於過去雖有淨相。而今現在如是次第種種不淨。諸現在世我之所有似淨色相。此淨色相於現在世雖有淨相。於未來世不當不淨如今現在外不淨色。無有是處。我此色身去來今世。曾如是相當如是相現如是相。不過如是不淨法性。如是名為尋思彼時。

**English:** "One reflects: 'My own apparently clean forms exist in the present. External impure forms also exist in the present. Forms that were clean in the past — though clean then — are now progressively impure in these various ways. My own seemingly clean forms in the present — though clean now — will inevitably become impure in the future, just like the external impure forms before me. My body, in past, future, and present, was, will be, and is of this same impure nature — it never transcends it.'"

**Findings:**
- 於未來世不當不淨如今現在外不淨色。無有是處。This is a double-negative rhetorical construction: "That in the future it should NOT become impure like the present external impure forms — there is no such possibility." The English "will inevitably become impure in the future" conveys the meaning correctly, converting the double-negative to a positive assertion. **Accurate rendering of meaning.**
- 曾如是相當如是相現如是相: "was of this nature, will be of this nature, is of this nature." English "was, will be, and is of this same impure nature" — **accurate**.
- 不過如是不淨法性: "does not go beyond this impure dharma-nature." English "it never transcends it" — **accurate**.
- Closing formula omitted. Consistent pattern.

### 8. Reasoning 理 (0452c23–0453a28)

#### 8a. Reasoning from dependence 觀待道理 (0452c23–0453a05)

**Chinese:** 云何名為尋思彼理。謂作是思。若內若外都無有我有情可得。或說為淨或說不淨。唯有色相唯有身形。於中假想施設言論。謂之為淨或為不淨。又如說言壽煖及與識。若棄捨身時離執持而臥無所思如木。既死沒已漸次變壞分位可知。謂青瘀等乃至骨鎖。今我此身先業煩惱之所引發。父母不淨和合所生。糜飯等食之所增長。此因此緣此由藉故。雖暫時有諸淨色相似。可了知而內身中若內若外。於常常時種種不淨皆悉充滿。如是名依世俗勝義及以因緣觀待道理。尋思彼理。

**English:** "'Neither internally nor externally is there any self or being who can be called pure or impure. There are only forms, only bodily shapes. Within these, purely by convention and designation, we speak of "pure" or "impure." As it is said: when life, warmth, and consciousness abandon the body, it lies bereft of its sustaining forces — without thought, like a log. After death, the body progressively deteriorates through knowable stages, from lividity through to bone-chain. My present body was brought into being by prior karma and afflictions, born from the union of my parents' impure substances, sustained and grown by food. For these reasons, although temporarily it appears to have clean forms, within the body — at all times — various impurities fill it completely.'"

**Findings:**
- 壽煖及與識: "life, warmth, and consciousness." English "life, warmth, and consciousness" — **correct**.
- 離執持而臥無所思如木: "separated from sustaining [forces], lying without thought, like wood." English "bereft of its sustaining forces — without thought, like a log" — **accurate** and natural.
- 糜飯等食: "congee, rice, and other food." English "food" — **minor compression**, loses the specific listing but this is a formulaic reference.
- 可了知 at the end of 諸淨色相似。可了知: The Chinese text here has a period/pause marker that may indicate "似可了知" (apparently knowable/discernible) — i.e., the clean appearances can seemingly be perceived. The English "it appears to have clean forms" captures this adequately.
- 若內若外: "whether internal or external." The English says "within the body" which absorbs the internal/external distinction. **Minor compression** — the Chinese emphasizes that both the interior and exterior of the body are filled with impurity.
- The closing formula 如是名依世俗勝義及以因緣觀待道理尋思彼理 classifies this reasoning under THREE sub-types: (1) conventional (世俗), (2) ultimate (勝義), and (3) causal (因緣) — all under the heading of reasoning from dependence (觀待道理). The English footnote [^dependence_impurity] mentions "conventional designation" and "dependent arising" but the explicit three-fold classification is not stated in the main text. **Minor omission** — the Chinese explicitly says this reasoning operates on three levels (conventional, ultimate, causal-conditional), which the footnote partially captures but the main text does not.

#### 8b. Reasoning from function 作用道理 (0453a05–a07)

**Chinese:** 復作是思。於此不淨若能如是善修善習善多修習能斷欲貪。如是欲貪定應當斷。如是名依作用道理尋思彼理。

**English:** "'If one thoroughly cultivates, practices, and extensively develops this contemplation of impurity, one can eradicate sensual desire. This sensual desire will surely be eradicated.'"

**Findings:**
- 善修善習善多修習: "well-cultivate, well-practice, well-and-extensively cultivate." English "thoroughly cultivates, practices, and extensively develops" — **accurate**.
- 定應當斷: "will certainly be eradicated." English "will surely be eradicated" — **correct**.
- Closing formula omitted. Consistent.

#### 8c. Reasoning from proof 證成道理 (0453a07–a17)

**Chinese:** 復作是思。如世尊說。若於不淨善修善習善多修習能斷欲貪。是至教量。我亦於內自能現見於諸不淨。如如作意思惟修習如是如是。令欲貪纏未生不生生已除遣。是現證量。比度量法亦有可得。謂作是思。云何今者作意思惟能對治法。可於能治所緣境界煩惱當生。如是名依證成道理尋思彼理。

**English:** "'The World-Honored One has taught: "If one thoroughly cultivates the contemplation of impurity, one can eradicate sensual desire." This is scriptural authority. I myself can directly perceive that when I attend to and contemplate impurity, the fetter of sensual desire does not arise when absent, and when present, it is driven away — this is direct realization. Inference is also available: how could attending to the antidote cause the very affliction it counters to arise?'"

**Findings:**
- **IMPORTANT — Mistranslation of the inference passage.** The Chinese says: 云何今者作意思惟能對治法。可於能治所緣境界煩惱當生。This means: "How could one, while directing attention to and contemplating the antidote dharma, [expect that] afflictions should arise regarding the object-field of the antidote?" — i.e., the rhetorical argument is that it is impossible for the very affliction to arise when one is attending to its antidote. The English "how could attending to the antidote cause the very affliction it counters to arise?" captures the sense correctly as a rhetorical question. **Actually accurate upon closer analysis.** The English restructures the Chinese syntax but preserves the rhetorical logic. No issue.

- However, there is a subtle precision issue: 能對治法 = "the antidote dharma" and 能治所緣境界 = "the object-field of the antidote." The Chinese distinguishes between the antidote itself and its object-field. The English collapses this. **Minor.**

- 如如作意思惟修習如是如是: The Chinese uses a correlative structure — "in whatever way one directs attention and contemplates, in that very way..." The English "when I attend to and contemplate" simplifies this correlative. **Minor compression.**

- 欲貪纏: "the fetter of sensual desire" (欲貪 = sensual desire, 纏 = fetter/entanglement). English "the fetter of sensual desire" — **correct**.

#### 8d. Reasoning from the nature of things 法爾道理 (0453a17–a22)

**Chinese:** 復作是思。如是之法成立法性。難思法性安住法性。謂修不淨能與欲貪作斷對治。不應思議不應分別唯應信解。如是名依法爾道理尋思彼理。

**English:** "'That cultivating impurity meditation serves as the antidote that eradicates sensual desire — this is the established nature of dharmas, their inconceivable nature, their abiding nature. This should not be further analyzed or elaborated upon, but only accepted with faith.'"

**Findings:**
- 成立法性。難思法性。安住法性: Three aspects — established dharma-nature, inconceivable dharma-nature, abiding dharma-nature. English "the established nature of dharmas, their inconceivable nature, their abiding nature" — **all three preserved**. Good.
- 不應思議不應分別唯應信解: "should not think about it, should not discriminate, should only believe/understand." English "should not be further analyzed or elaborated upon, but only accepted with faith" — **accurate**.

**Chinese:** 是名勤修不淨觀者。尋思六事差別所緣毘鉢舍那。

**English:** "This is the six-aspect vipashyana for one who diligently cultivates the contemplation of impurity."

**Verdict:** **Accurate.** Good closing.
